###  EQUAL EMPLOYMENT OPPORTUNITY (COMMONWEALTH AUTHORITIES) ACT 1987- SECT 1  Short title [_see_ Note 1] 
<dl compact="">

		This Act may be cited as the _Equal Employment Opportunity (Commonwealth Authorities) Act 1987_. 

 </dl>
###  EQUAL EMPLOYMENT OPPORTUNITY (COMMONWEALTH AUTHORITIES) ACT 1987- SECT 2  Commencement [_see_ Note 1] 
<dl compact="">

		This Act shall come into operation on a day to be fixed by Proclamation. 

 </dl>
###  EQUAL EMPLOYMENT OPPORTUNITY (COMMONWEALTH AUTHORITIES) ACT 1987- SECT 3  Interpretation 
<dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:

</dd> </dl>
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_Australia_ includes the Territories to which this Act extends. _authority_ means:  </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>an incorporated or unincorporated body or authority established for a

public purpose:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

	(i)	by an Act or by regulations made under an Act; or
 	(ii)	by or under a law of a Territory (other than a law of the Australian Capital Territory or the Northern Territory);

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		being a body whose staff consists of, or includes, persons who are not APS

employees; or

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>a company or other body corporate incorporated under a law of the

Commonwealth, of a State or of a Territory, being a company or body corporate

in which the Commonwealth has a controlling interest;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

but does not include a relevant employer as defined in section&#160;3 of the _Equal Opportunity for Women in the Workplace Act 1999_.

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_designated group_ means any of the following classes of persons:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>members of the Aboriginal race of Australia or persons who are descendants

of indigenous inhabitants of the Torres Strait Islands;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>persons who have migrated to Australia and whose first language is not

English, and the children of such persons;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>persons with a physical or mental disability;</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>any other class of persons prescribed by the regulations for the purposes

of this definition.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_discrimination_ means:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>discrimination that is unlawful under the _Racial Discrimination Act 1975_ or the _Sex Discrimination Act 1984_; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>discrimination by which a person with a physical or mental disability is,

because of the disability, treated less favourably than a person without the

disability.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_employee_ means a natural person appointed or engaged:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>under a contract of service, whether on a full-time, part-time, casual or

temporary basis; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>under a contract for services.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_employment matters_ includes:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>recruitment procedure, and selection criteria, for appointment or

engagement of persons as employees;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>promotion and transfer of employees;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>training and staff development for employees; and</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>conditions of service of employees.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_operative day_, in relation to an authority, means the day

specified in relation to that authority in subsection 5(2). _overseas_ means outside Australia. _program_, in relation to a relevant authority, means an equal employment opportunity program designed to ensure that appropriate action is taken by the authority:  </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>to eliminate discrimination by it against; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>to promote equal opportunity for;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

women and persons in designated groups in relation to employment matters.

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_relevant authority_ means an authority that employs 40 or more

employees in Australia. _responsible Minister_, for a relevant authority, means:  </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>if the regulations prescribe a Minister as responsible for the

authority&#151;that Minister; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>otherwise&#151;the Minister responsible for the authority.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_trade union_ means:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>an organisation of employees registered pursuant to the _Conciliation and Arbitration Act 1904_; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>a trade union within the meaning of a State Act or law of a Territory.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_woman_ means a member of the female sex irrespective of age.

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>A reference to discrimination in relation to employment matters does not

include a reference to discrimination that:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>is essential for the effective performance of the duties to which the

employment matters relate; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>is not unlawful under the _Racial Discrimination Act 1975_ or the

_Sex Discrimination Act 1984_.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>For the purposes of this Act, an authority employs an employee in

Australia if the contract, by which the employee is appointed or engaged, is

made in Australia, notwithstanding that the employee may perform duties

overseas. </dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>Nothing in this Act shall be taken to require any action incompatible with the principle that employment matters should be dealt with on the basis of merit.  </dd> </dl></dl>

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

###  EQUAL EMPLOYMENT OPPORTUNITY (COMMONWEALTH AUTHORITIES) ACT 1987- SECT 5  Relevant authorities required to develop etc. programs 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>A relevant authority shall commence the development and implementation of

a program on the operative day. </dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The operative day is: </dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>in relation to an authority that is a relevant authority on 1&#160;July

1987 or such later day as is prescribed&#151;that day or that later day; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>in any other case&#151;the day on which an authority becomes a relevant

authority. 

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>A reference in paragraph&#160;(2)(b) to an authority becoming a relevant

authority on a particular day includes a reference to an authority that comes

into existence on that day. </dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>Where, at any time, an authority ceases to be a relevant authority because the number of employees employed by it in Australia falls below 40, this Act continues to apply to the authority as if the authority was a relevant authority unless and until the number of employees employed by it in Australia falls below 30\.  </dd> </dl></dl>

**

###  EQUAL EMPLOYMENT OPPORTUNITY (COMMONWEALTH AUTHORITIES) ACT 1987- SECT 6  Contents of program 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		Without limiting the generality of the definition of _program_ in

subsection 3(1), the program of a relevant authority shall provide for action

to be taken:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>to inform employees of the contents of the program and of the results of

any monitoring and evaluation of the program under paragraph&#160;(h);</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>to confer responsibility for the development and implementation of the

program (including a continuous review of the program), on a person or persons

having sufficient authority and status within the management of the relevant

authority to enable the person or persons properly to develop and implement the

program;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>to consult with each trade union having members affected by the proposal

for the development and implementation of the program in accordance with this

Act;</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>to consult with employees of the relevant authority, particularly

employees who are women or persons in designated groups;</dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>for the collection and recording of statistics and related information

concerning employment by the relevant authority, including the number of, and

the types of jobs undertaken by, or job classifications of:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

	(i)	employees of either sex; and
 	(ii)	persons in designated groups;

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(f)</dt><dd>to consider policies, and examine practices, of the relevant authority, in

relation to employment matters to identify:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

	(i)	any policies or practices that discriminate against women or persons in designated groups; and
 	(ii)	any patterns (whether ascertained statistically or otherwise) of lack of equality of opportunity in respect of women or persons in designated groups;

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(g)</dt><dd>to set:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

	(i)	the particular objectives to be achieved by the program; and
 	(ii)	the quantitative and other indicators against which the effectiveness of the program is to be assessed;

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(h)</dt><dd>to monitor and evaluate the implementation of the program and:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

	(i)	to assess the achievement of those objectives; and
 	(ii)	to assess the effectiveness of the program by comparing statistics and information collected and recorded under paragraph&#160;(e) with the indicators against which the effectiveness of the program is to be assessed.  

**

###  EQUAL EMPLOYMENT OPPORTUNITY (COMMONWEALTH AUTHORITIES) ACT 1987- SECT 7  Regard to be had to program 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		A relevant authority shall take any action necessary to give effect to its

program and any person who exercises powers in relation to employment matters

in the authority shall have regard to the program in exercising those powers. 

 </dl></dl>

**

###  EQUAL EMPLOYMENT OPPORTUNITY (COMMONWEALTH AUTHORITIES) ACT 1987- SECT 9  Annual program report 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>A relevant authority shall:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>prepare a report (in this section called a _program report_)

on the development and implementation of its program during each period of 12

months commencing on the operative day in relation to the authority or the

anniversary of that day; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>lodge the report with the responsible Minister within 3 months after the

end of the period to which the report relates. 

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>A program report shall provide, in respect of the period to which it

relates:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a detailed analysis of the action taken by the relevant authority to

develop and implement its program;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the assessments referred to in paragraph 6(h); and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>particulars of each direction given by the Minister under section&#160;12\. 

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>Where a relevant authority has lodged a program report with the

responsible Minister, that Minister:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>shall, if the report fails to satisfy the requirements of this Act, by

notice in writing, request the authority to lodge a further report, within such

period as is specified in the notice; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>shall, if the report or further report, as the case may be, satisfies the

requirements of this Act, cause a copy of the report or further report to be

laid before each House of the Parliament within 15 sitting days of that House

after the day on which that Minister receives the report or further report. 

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(4)</dt><dd>If:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a relevant authority is required by an Act other than this Act to prepare

an annual report for presentation to the Parliament; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the annual report is lodged with the responsible Minister within 3 months

after the end of the period to which a program report relates;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

then the authority may, with the consent of the responsible Minister, include that program report in the annual report.

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(5)</dt><dd>Where an authority ceases to be a relevant authority, this section

continues to apply, in relation to the final period, as if:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>references to the relevant authority were references to the authority;

and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>a reference to a period of 12 months were a reference to the final period. 

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(6)</dt><dd>In subsection&#160;(5), _final period_ means the period

beginning on the last anniversary of the operative day before the day on which

the authority ceased to be a relevant authority and ending on that day. 

</dd> </dl></dl>

**

###  EQUAL EMPLOYMENT OPPORTUNITY (COMMONWEALTH AUTHORITIES) ACT 1987- SECT 10  Special report upon request 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The responsible Minister may, by notice in writing to a relevant

authority, require the authority to give the responsible Minister a special

report within the time specified in the notice.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>In this section, _special report_ means a report in writing (other than a report under section&#160;9) in relation to the development, implementation or review of the program of the authority. </dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>A notice under sub-section (1) may specify the particular aspects of the program of the authority that are to be dealt with in the special report. </dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>Where a relevant authority has lodged a special report with the responsible Minister, that Minister shall cause a copy of the report to be laid before each House of the Parliament within 15 sitting days of that House after the day on which he or she receives the report.  </dd> </dl></dl>

**

###  EQUAL EMPLOYMENT OPPORTUNITY (COMMONWEALTH AUTHORITIES) ACT 1987- SECT 11  Minister may make recommendations 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>After considering a report lodged by a relevant authority under this Part,

the responsible Minister may make a recommendation in writing to the authority

regarding the action to be taken by the authority to improve the effectiveness

of its program.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>If the authority does not concur in or adopt a recommendation made under subsection&#160;(1), the authority must, within a reasonable time, inform the responsible Minister in writing of the authority's reasons for not concurring in or adopting the recommendation. </dd> </dl></dl>

###  EQUAL EMPLOYMENT OPPORTUNITY (COMMONWEALTH AUTHORITIES) ACT 1987- SECT 12  Directions by Minister 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The responsible Minister for a relevant authority may give general

directions in writing to the authority about the performance of the authority's

obligations under this Act.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Where, under an Act other than this Act, a relevant authority submits a corporate plan to its responsible Minister, that Minister may have regard to that authority's obligations under this Act and, if that Minister considers that the corporate plan should be revised for the purposes of giving effect to those obligations, shall direct the authority in writing to revise the corporate plan accordingly. </dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>For the purposes of subsection&#160;(2), _corporate plan_ means a plan, however described, prepared by the relevant authority defining the principal objectives of the authority and outlining the policies or strategies to be pursued by the authority in achieving those objectives.  </dd> </dl></dl>
