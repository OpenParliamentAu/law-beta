### <name>ENVIRONMENT PROTECTION (ALLIGATOR RIVERS REGION) ACT 1978 - LONG TITLE </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> An Act to provide for the appointment of a Supervising Scientist for the purpose of protecting the environment in the Alligator Rivers Region of the Northern Territory from the effects of mining operations, and for other<lf>                                   purposes<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

**<b>

### <name>ENVIRONMENT PROTECTION (ALLIGATOR RIVERS REGION) ACT 1978 - SECT 1 Short title </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   1\. This Act may be cited as the  Environment Protection (Alligator Rivers Region) Act 1978.*1* SEE NOTES TO FIRST ARTICLE OF THIS CHAPTER . </sect>
**<b>

### <name>ENVIRONMENT PROTECTION (ALLIGATOR RIVERS REGION) ACT 1978 - SECT 2 Commencement </name>

## SECT
<sect>   2\. This Act shall come into operation on the day on which it receives the Royal Assent.*1* SEE NOTES TO FIRST ARTICLE OF THIS CHAPTER . </sect>

<sect>   3.(1) In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:<lf>   'Administrator of the Northern Territory' means the Administrator of the Northern Territory acting with the advice of the Executive Council of the Northern Territory;<lf>   'Alligator Rivers Region' or 'Region' means:<lf>    (a) the area shown on the map set out in the Schedule, being a copy of the<lf> map appearing on of land that is or was the subject of a pastoral lease) as is outside the area referred to in paragraph (a);<lf>    (ac) the area bounded on the south by the parallel of latitude 13 degrees<lf> 30' south and on all other sides by part of the boundary of the area referred to in paragraph (a);<lf>    (ad) the area bounded on the east by the meridian of longitude 133 degrees<lf> east, on the south by the parallel of latitude 13 degrees 30' south and on all other sides by part of the boundary of the area referred to in paragraph (a);<lf>    (b) the territorial sea adjacent to the area referred ### <name>ENVIRONMENT PROTECTION (ALLIGATOR RIVERS REGION) ACT 1978 - PART II  PART II-THE SUPERVISING SCIENTIST </name>
</b>** 
**<b>

### <name>ENVIRONMENT PROTECTION (ALLIGATOR RIVERS REGION) ACT 1978 - SECT 4 Supervising Scientist </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   4\. There shall be a Supervising Scientist for the Alligator Rivers Region. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>ENVIRONMENT PROTECTION (ALLIGATOR RIVERS REGION) ACT 1978 - SECT 5 Functions of Supervising Scientist in relation to uranium mining in the Region </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   5\. The Supervising Scientist has the following functions in relation to uranium mining operations in the Region:<lf>    (a) to devise and develop programs for research into, and programs for the<lf> collection and assessment of information relating to, the effects on the environment in the Region of uranium mining operations in the Region;<lf>    (b) to co-ordinate, and supervise, the carrying out of programs referred to<lf> in paragraph (a);<lf>    (c) to devise and develop, and to promote and assist in the devising and<lf> development of:<lf>          (i) standards, practices and procedures in relation to uranium mining<lf> operations in the Region for the protection of, or in so far as those standards, practices and procedures affect, the environment in the Region; and<lf>          (ii) measures for the protection and restoration of the environment in<lf> the Region from the effects of uranium mining operations in the Region;<lf>    (d) to co-ordinate, and supervise, the implementation, in relation to<lf> uranium mining operations in the Region, of requirements of or having effect under prescribed instruments in so far as those requirements relate to any matter affecting the environment in the Region;<lf>    (e) to advise the Minister with respect to:<lf>          (i) the effects on the environment in the Region of uranium mining<lf> operations in the Region;<lf>          (ii) standards, practices and procedures in relation to uranium mining<lf> operations in the Region for the protection of, or in so far as those standards, practices and procedures affect, the environment in the Region;<lf>          (iii) measures for the protection and restoration of the environment<lf> in the Region from the effects of uranium mining operations in the Region; and<lf>          (iv) requirements of or having effect under prescribed instruments in<lf> relation to uranium mining operations in the Region in so far as those requirements relate to any matter affecting the environment in the Region and the implementation of those requirements;<lf>    (f) to perform such other functions, in relation to uranium mining<lf> operations in the Region, as are conferred on him by or under a prescribed instrument (including this Act); and<lf>    (g) to do anything incidental or conducive to the performance of any of the<lf> foregoing functions. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>ENVIRONMENT PROTECTION (ALLIGATOR RIVERS REGION) ACT 1978 - SECT 5A Functions of Supervising Scientist in relation to general mining in a conservation zone </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   5A.(1) The Supervising Scientist has the following functions in relation to general mining operations in a conservation zone:<lf>    (a) to devise and develop programs for research into, and programs for the<lf> collection and assessment of information relating to, the effects on the environment in the zone of general mining operations in the zone;<lf>    (b) to co-ordinate, and supervise, the carrying out of programs referred to<lf> in paragraph (a);<lf>    (c) to devise and develop, and to promote and assist in the devising and<lf> development of:<lf>          (i) standards, practices and procedures in relation to general mining<lf> operations in the zone for the protection of, or in so far as those standards, practices and procedures affect, the environment in the zone; and<lf>          (ii) measures for the protection and restoration of the environment in<lf> the zone from the effects of general mining operations in the zone;<lf>    (d) to consider, and review, the implementation, in relation to general<lf> mining operations in the zone, of requirements of or having effect under prescribed instruments in so far as those requirements relate to any matter affecting the environment in the zone;<lf>    (e) to advise the Minister, or a person designated by the Minister in<lf> writing, with respect to:<lf>          (i) the effects on the environment of general mining operations in the<lf> zone;<lf>          (ii) standards, practices and procedures in relation to general mining<lf> operations in the zone for the protection of, or in so far as those standards, practices and procedures affect, the environment in the zone;<lf>          (iii) measures for the protection and restoration of the environment<lf> in the zone from the effects of general mining operations in the zone; and<lf>          (iv) requirements of or having effect under prescribed instruments in<lf> relation to general mining operations in the zone in so far as those requirements relate to any matter affecting the environment in the zone and the implementation of those requirements;<lf>    (f) to perform such other functions, in relation to general mining<lf> operations in the zone, as are conferred on him or her by or under a prescribed instrument (including this Act); and<lf>    (g) to do anything incidental or conducive to the performance of any of the<lf> foregoing functions.<lf>   (2) Where, whether before or after the commencement of this section:<lf>    (a) a conservation zone is declared under the National Parks and Wildlife<lf> Conservation Act 1975; and<lf>    (b) an area is subsequently excised from the zone<lf> under that Act;<lf> then, until the Minister otherwise directs under section 7, the Supervising Scientist continues to have the functions conferred by subsection (1) as if the area had not been excised. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>ENVIRONMENT PROTECTION (ALLIGATOR RIVERS REGION) ACT 1978 - SECT 6 Powers of Supervising Scientist </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   6\. The Supervising Scientist has power to do all things that are necessary or convenient to be done for, or in connexion with, the performance of his functions. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>ENVIRONMENT PROTECTION (ALLIGATOR RIVERS REGION) ACT 1978 - SECT 7 Directions of Minister etc. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   7\. The Supervising Scientist shall comply with directions given to him from time to time by the Minister in relation to the performance of his functions or the exercise of his powers and shall furnish to the Minister such information in relation to the operation of this Act as the Minister requires. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>ENVIRONMENT PROTECTION (ALLIGATOR RIVERS REGION) ACT 1978 - SECT 8 Appointment and term of office </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   8.(1) The Supervising Scientist shall be appointed by the Governor-General.<lf>   (2) Subject to subsection (4), a person appointed as the Supervising Scientist holds office for such period, not exceeding 3 years, as is specified in his instrument of appointment.<lf>   (3) A person appointed as the Supervising Scientist is eligible for re-appointment.<lf>   (4) A person who has attained the age of 65 years shall not be appointed or re-appointed as the Supervising Scientist and a person shall not be appointed or re-appointed as the Supervising Scientist for a period that extends beyond the date on which he will attain the age of 65 years.<lf>   (5) The Supervising Scientist holds office on such terms and conditions (if any) in respect of matters not provided for by this Act as are determined by the Governor-General. </lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>ENVIRONMENT PROTECTION (ALLIGATOR RIVERS REGION) ACT 1978 - SECT 9 Pecuniary interests </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   9.(1) A person shall not be appointed as the Supervising Scientist if he has a direct or indirect pecuniary interest in a business carried on in Australia, or in a body corporate carrying on such a business, being an interest that could be in conflict with his duties as Supervising Scientist if he were so appointed.<lf>   (2) The Supervising Scientist shall, to the best of his knowledge, disclose to the Minister any direct or indirect pecuniary interest that he has or acquires in a business carried on in Australia, or in a body corporate carrying on such a business, being an interest that could be in conflict with his duties as Supervising Scientist. </lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>ENVIRONMENT PROTECTION (ALLIGATOR RIVERS REGION) ACT 1978 - SECT 10 Remuneration and allowances </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   10.(1) The Supervising Scientist shall be paid such remuneration as is determined by the Remuneration Tribunal, but, if no determination of that remuneration by the Tribunal is in operation, he shall be paid such remuneration as is prescribed.<lf>   (2) The Supervising Scientist shall be paid such allowances as are prescribed.<lf>   (3) This section has effect subject to the Remuneration Tribunals Act 1973\. </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>ENVIRONMENT PROTECTION (ALLIGATOR RIVERS REGION) ACT 1978 - SECT 11 Leave of absence </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   11.(1) Subject to section 87E of the Public Service Act 1922, the Supervising Scientist has such recreation leave entitlements as are determined by the Remuneration Tribunal.<lf>   (2) The Minister may grant the Supervising Scientist leave of absence, other than recreation leave, on such terms and conditions as to remuneration or otherwise as the Minister determines. </lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>ENVIRONMENT PROTECTION (ALLIGATOR RIVERS REGION) ACT 1978 - SECT 12 Resignation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   12\. The Supervising Scientist may resign his office by writing under his hand delivered to the Governor-General. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>ENVIRONMENT PROTECTION (ALLIGATOR RIVERS REGION) ACT 1978 - SECT 13 Termination of appointment </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   13.(1)  The Governor-General may terminate the appointment of the Supervising Scientist for misbehaviour or physical or mental incapacity.<lf>   (2) If the Supervising Scientist:<lf>    (a) becomes bankrupt, applies to take the benefit of any law for the relief<lf> of bankrupt or insolvent debtors, compounds with his creditors or makes an assignment of his remuneration for their benefit;<lf>    (b) without reasonable excuse fails to comply with subsection 9 (2) or 20<lf> (2);<lf>    (c) engages in paid employment outside the duties of his office without the<lf> approval of the Minister; or<lf>    (d) is absent from duty, except on leave of absence, for 14 consecutive days<lf> or for 28 days in any 12 months;<lf> the Governor-General shall terminate his appointment. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>ENVIRONMENT PROTECTION (ALLIGATOR RIVERS REGION) ACT 1978 - SECT 14 Acting Supervising Scientist </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   14.(1) The Minister may appoint a person to act as the Supervising Scientist during any period, or during all periods, when:<lf>    (a) the Supervising Scientist is absent from duty or from Australia or is,<lf> for any other reason, unable to perform the functions of his office; or<lf>    (b) there is a vacancy in the office of Supervising Scientist, whether or<lf> not an appointment has previously been made to the office.<lf>   (2) A person acting as the Supervising Scientist by reason of a vacancy in the office of Supervising Scientist shall not continue so to act:<lf>    (a) if an appointment has not previously been made to the office-after the<lf> expiration of 12 months after the commencement of this Act; or<lf>    (b) in any other case-after the expiration of 12 months after the occurrence<lf> of the vacancy.<lf>   (3) A person appointed to act as the Supervising Scientist has, while acting as the Supervising Scientist, all the powers, functions and duties of the Supervising Scientist, and references in Parts III, IV and V to the Supervising Scientist shall, if a person is acting as the Supervising Scientist, be read as a reference to the person so acting.<lf>   (4) The Minister may:<lf>    (a) determine the terms and conditions of appointment of a person appointed<lf> to act as the Supervising Scientist; and<lf>    (b) at any time terminate such an appointment.<lf>   (5) If a person who holds an appointment to act as the Supervising Scientist fails, without reasonable excuse, to comply with subsection 9 (2) as it applies by reason of subsection (7) of this section or with subsection 20 (2), the Minister shall terminate his appointment.<lf>   (6) A person who holds an appointment to act as the Supervising Scientist may resign his appointment by writing under his hand delivered to the Minister.<lf>   (7) Subsection 9 (1) applies to and in relation to the appointment of a person to act as the Supervising Scientist in like manner as it applies to and in relation to the appointment of a person as the Supervising Scientist and subsection 9 (2) applies to and in relation to a person appointed to act as the Supervising Scientist in like manner as it applies to and in relation to the Supervising Scientist.<lf>   (8) The validity of an act done by a person appointed to act as the Supervising Scientist shall not be questioned in any proceedings on a ground arising from the fact that the occasion for the appointment, or for him to act under the appointment, had not arisen or that the appointment had ceased to have effect or the occasion for him to act under the appointment had passed. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>

### <name>ENVIRONMENT PROTECTION (ALLIGATOR RIVERS REGION) ACT 1978 - SECT 16 Co-ordinating Committee </name>
</b>** 

## SECT

**<b>

### <name>ENVIRONMENT PROTECTION (ALLIGATOR RIVERS REGION) ACT 1978 - SECT 17 Functions of Co-ordinating Committee </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   17\. The functions of the Co-ordinating Committee are:<lf>    (a) to consider programs for research into, and programs for the collection<lf> and assessment of information relating to, the effects on the environment in the Alligator Rivers Region of uranium mining operations in the Region;<lf>    (b) to keep under review programs, and the carrying out of programs,<lf> referred to in paragraph (a);<lf>    (c) to make recommendations to the Supervising Scientist with respect to<lf> programs referred to in paragraph (a);<lf>    (d) to consider, and keep under review:<lf>          (i) standards, practices and procedures in relation to uranium mining<lf> operations in the Region for the protection of, or in so far as those standards, practices and procedures affect, the environment in the Region; and<lf>          (ii) measures for the protection and restoration of the environment in<lf> the Region from the effects of uranium mining operations in the Region;<lf>    (e) to keep under review:<lf>          (i) requirements of or having effect under prescribed instruments in<lf> relation to uranium mining operations in the Region in so far as those requirements relate to any matter affecting the environment in the Region; and<lf>          (ii) the implementation of those requirements;<lf>    (f) to make recommendations to the Supervising Scientist with respect to<lf> matters referred to in paragraphs (d) and (e);<lf>    (g) to perform such other functions, in relation to uranium mining<lf> operations in the Region, as are conferred on it by or under a prescribed instrument; and<lf>    (h) to do anything incidental or conducive to the performance of any of the<lf> foregoing functions. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>

and<lf>    (b) subject to subsection (6), at any time terminate such an appointment.<lf>   (6) Where a person is appointed as the deputy of a member on the nomination of the Administrator of the Northern Territory or the appropriate Aboriginal Land Council, on the nomination of a Department, authority, incorporated company or other body, or on the nomination of the Director of National Parks and Wildlife, the Minister shall not terminate the appointment unless:<lf>    (a) he is required to do so by subsection 20 (1) as it applies by reason of<lf> subsection (8) of this section; ## SECT
<sect>   31.(1) Subsection (2) applies to every person who is or has been the Supervising Scientist or a member of the staff assisting the Supervising Scientist.<lf>   (2) Subject to subsection (3), a person to whom this subsection applies shall not, either directly or indirectly, except in the performance of a function or duty under or in connexion with this Act, make a record of, or divulge or communicate to any person, any information concerning the affairs of any other person acquired by him by reason of his office or employment under or for the purposes of this Act.<lf>   Penalty: $1,000 or imprisonment for 6 months, or both.<lf>   (3) Subsection (2) does not prevent the communication of information or the production of a document by the Supervising Scientist or a member of the staff assisting the Supervising Scientist authorized by him for that purpose:<lf>    (a) to the Minister, to the Permanent Head of the Department that deals with<lf> matters arising under this Act or to an officer of, or an employee in, that Department approved by the Permanent Head of that Department; or<lf>    (b) to a person to whom, in the opinion of the Minister, it is in the public<lf> interest that the information be communicated or the document produced.<lf>   (4) Neither the Permanent Head of the Department that deals with matters arising under this Act nor an officer of, nor an employee in, that Department approved by him for the purposes of subsection (3) shall, either directly or indirectly, except for the purpose of advising the Minister in connexion with this Act, make a record of, or divulge or communicate to any person, any information communicated to him by the Supervising Scientist or by a member of the staff assisting the Supervising Scientist, being information concerning the affairs of another person acquired by the Supervising Scientist or by a member of that staff by reason of his office or employment under or for the purposes of this Act.<lf>   Penalty: $1,000 or imprisonment for 6 months, or both.<lf>   (5) Nothing in this section shall be taken to affect the operation of section 9 of the Ombudsman Act 1976 or the operation of any similar provision in a law of the Northern Territory that confers functions on a person similar to functions conferred on the Ombudsman by that Act.<lf>   (6) A reference in this section to a member of the staff assisting the Supervising Scientist is a reference to a member of the staff referred to in subsection 26 (1), a person engaged under subsection 26 (4) or any other person to whom a delegation has been given under subsection 35 (1). </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
