### <name>EXPLOSIVES ACT 1961-1973 - SECT. 1\. Short title. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>                            EXPLOSIVES  ACT  1961-1973<lf> <lf>                          An Act relating to Explosives.<lf> <lf>                               PART  I-PRELIMINARY<lf> Short title amended; No. 32, 1918, s. 2.<lf>   1\. This Act may be cited as the Explosives Act 1961-1973.*<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>EXPLOSIVES ACT 1961-1973 - SECT. 2\. Commencement. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   2\. This Act shall come into operation on a date to be fixed by Proclamation.* <lf> Sections 3 and 4 repealed by No. 216, 1973, s. 3.<lf>                          *   *   *   *   *   *   *   *<lf> </lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>EXPLOSIVES ACT 1961-1973 - SECT. 5\. Definitions. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   5\. In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears-<lf> <lf>   ''explosives'' means-<lf> <lf>      (a)  substances or articles capable of producing an explosive, incendiary or pyrotechnic effect;<lf> <lf>      (b)  substances or articles prescribed by the regulations to be explosives; or<lf> <lf>      (c)  containers that have contained a substance or article to which either of the last two preceding paragraphs of this definition applies and have not been certified, in accordance with the regulations, to be free from explosives; <lf> <lf>   ''handling'' includes loading, unloading, discharging, stacking, stowing, storing, transporting and any operation incidental to, or arising out of, any of those operations;<lf> <lf>   ''vessel'' means a ship, boat or other vessel used for any purpose on the sea or in navigation.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>EXPLOSIVES ACT 1961-1973 - SECT. 6\. Act binds Crown. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Amended by No. 216, 1973, s. 3.<lf>   6\. This Act binds the Crown in right of the Commonwealth or of a State and any authority constituted by or under a law of the Commonwealth, of a State or of a Territory.<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>EXPLOSIVES ACT 1961-1973 - SECT. 7\. Act extends to Territories. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Sub-section (1) amended by No. 216, 1973, s. 3.<lf>   7\. (1) This Act extends to all the Territories.<lf> Amended by No. 216, 1973, s. 3\. 

  (2) Where a law, not being an Act, in force in a Territory is inconsistent with this Act or the regulations, or with an order under the regulations, that law is, to the extent of the inconsistency, of no effect.<lf> </lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>EXPLOSIVES ACT 1961-1973 - SECT. 8\. Heading substituted by No. 47, 1964, s. 4\. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>                        PART  II-HANDLING  OF  EXPLOSIVES<lf> Definition.<lf>   8\. In this Part, ''vessel'' does not include a vessel belonging to the naval forces of the Commonwealth or of another part of the Queen's dominions.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>EXPLOSIVES ACT 1961-1973 - SECT. 9\. Explosives to which this Part applies. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Amended by No. 47, 1964, s. 5; and No. 216, 1973, s. 3.<lf>   9\. This Part applies to explosives that-<lf> <lf>   (a)  are the property of, or are in the possession or control of, the Commonwealth;<lf> <lf>  (ab)  have been manufactured by the Commonwealth and, in pursuance of an arrangement made with the Commonwealth, are intended to be, or are being, exported from the Commonwealth; or<lf> <lf>   (b)  are the property of, or are in the possession or control of, the government or the naval, military or air forces of another country and are in the Commonwealth or a Territory with the approval of the Commonwealth for the purposes of, or a purpose related to, the defence of the Commonwealth.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>EXPLOSIVES ACT 1961-1973 - SECT. 10\. Regulations. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   10\. (1) The regulations may make provision-<lf> <lf>   (a)  for or in relation to, or for or in relation to any matter arising out of or incidental to, the handling of explosives to which this Part applies; and<lf> <lf>   (b)  for or in relation to safety measures to be observed at, in or upon a place, vehicle, aircraft or vessel at, in, upon or in the vicinity of which there are explosives to which this Part applies.<lf> Amended by No. 216, 1973, s. 3\. 

  (2) Before making regulations for or in relation to the handling in a port of explosives to which this Part applies, the Governor-General shall take into consideration any recommendations with respect to the proposed regulations made to the Minister by the committee known as the Council of the Association of Australian Port and Marine Authorities.<lf> </lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>EXPLOSIVES ACT 1961-1973 - SECT. 11\. Orders relating to the berthing of vessels. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   11\. (1) The regulations may empower a person to direct, by order, that a vessel in which explosives to which this Part applies are, or are to be, loaded may, for a purpose or purposes specified in the order, be berthed in a port so specified.<lf> 

  (2) The regulations may provide that where-<lf> <lf>   (a)  the port specified in an order referred to in the last preceding sub-section is a prescribed port; and<lf> <lf>   (b)  the vessel to which the order relates is not provided with a berth in the port or is provided with a berth in the port that, in the opinion of the person who made the order, is not suitable for the purpose or purposes specified in the order,<lf> that person may, by order, give such directions as are necessary for ensuring that the vessel is, for such period as is necessary, provided with a berth in the port that is so suitable.<lf> Added by No. 47, 1964, s. 6\. <p>  (3) Except in the case of explosives referred to in paragraph (ab) or in paragraph (b) of section nine of this Act, the powers conferred by regulations made by virtue of sub-section (1) of this section shall not be exercised otherwise than for a purpose related to the defence of the Commonwealth.<lf> </lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>EXPLOSIVES ACT 1961-1973 - SECT. 11A. Orders relating to transporting explosives by railway. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Inserted by No. 47, 1964, s. 7.<lf>   11A. (1) The regulations may empower a person to give, by order, such directions as are necessary for ensuring that such explosives to which this Part applies as are specified in the order are transported by railway between such places, on such day or days and in such manner as are so specified.<lf> 

  (2) Except in the case of explosives referred to in paragraph (ab) or in paragraph (b) of section nine of this Act, the powers conferred by regulations made by virtue of the last preceding sub-section shall not be exercised otherwise than for a purpose related to the defence of the Commonwealth.<lf> </lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>EXPLOSIVES ACT 1961-1973 - SECT. 12\. Indemnity. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   12\. (1) The Commonwealth may indemnify a person from and against all actions, proceedings, claims, demands, costs and expenses relating to any loss, damage or injury to any person or property attributable to explosives to which this Part applies or to the handling of explosives to which this Part applies.<lf> 

  (2) An indemnity given under section ten of the Explosives Act 1952, or that Act as amended, and in force immediately before the commencement of this Act has, after the commencement of this Act, the same force and effect as if-<lf> <lf>   (a)  the indemnity had been given under the last preceding sub-section;<lf> <lf>   (b)  any reference in the indemnity to the Explosives Act 1952 or any amendment of that Act were a reference to this Act; and<lf> <lf>   (c)  any reference in the indemnity to the regulations made under the Explosives Act 1952 or any amendment of that Act or to Explosives Regulations were a reference to regulations made under this Act for the purposes of this Part.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>EXPLOSIVES ACT 1961-1973 - SECT. 13\. Definitions. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>              PART  III-CONTROL  OF  COMMONWEALTH  EXPLOSIVES  AREAS<lf>   13\. In this Part-<lf> <lf>   ''Commonwealth explosives area'' means any Commonwealth land declared by the regulations to be an explosives area and, where any such land consists of a part of the bed of the sea, or of a bay, estuary or river, includes the waters above the land;<lf> <lf>   ''Commonwealth land'' means land the property of the Commonwealth and includes any other land-<lf> <lf>      (a)  in which the Commonwealth has a legal or equitable estate or interest; or<lf> <lf>      (b)  over or in connexion with which the Commonwealth has a right, power or privilege.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>EXPLOSIVES ACT 1961-1973 - SECT. 14\. Regulations. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   14\. (1) The regulations may make provision for ensuring the safety of persons who are, and the protection from damage of property that is, in or in the vicinity of a Commonwealth explosives area.<lf> 

  (2) Without limiting the generality of the last preceding sub-section, the regulations may make provision-<lf> <lf>   (a)  for or in relation to, or for or in relation to any matter arising out of or incidental to, the handling of explosives and other goods in or in the vicinity of a Commonwealth explosives area;<lf> <lf>   (b)  for or in relation to safety measures to be observed in or in the vicinity of a Commonwealth explosives area;<lf> <lf>   (c)  prohibiting or regulating the entry of persons, vehicles, aircraft and vessels into, and the movement of persons, vehicles, aircraft and vessels within or over, a Commonwealth explosives area;<lf> <lf>   (d)  for or in relation to the berthing of vessels in a Commonwealth explosives area; and<lf> <lf>   (e)  prescribing fees to be paid in respect of the use of facilities or services maintained, operated or provided by the Commonwealth in a Commonwealth explosives area.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>EXPLOSIVES ACT 1961-1973 - SECT. 15\. Orders. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>                              PART  IV-MISCELLANEOUS<lf>   15\. The regulations may empower a person to provide, by order, for any matter that may be provided for by the regulations.<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>EXPLOSIVES ACT 1961-1973 - SECT. 16\. General provisions applicable to orders. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   16\. (1) Orders made under the regulations are not Statutory Rules within the meaning of the Rules Publication Act 1903-1939.<lf> 

  (2) The provisions of section forty-eight (except paragraphs (a) and (b) of sub-section (1), and sub-section (2) ) and of section forty-nine of the Acts Interpretation Act 1901-1957 apply, by force of this section, to orders made under the regulations in like manner as those provisions apply to regulations.<lf> <p>  (3) The Acts Interpretation Act 1901-1957 applies, by force of this section, to the interpretation of orders made under the regulations in like manner as it applies to the interpretation of regulations, and, for the purposes of section forty-six of that Act, orders so made shall be deemed to be regulations.<lf> <p>  (4) Where the regulations confer power to make orders, the power includes, unless the contrary intention appears, power exercisable in the like manner, and subject to the like conditions (if any), to repeal, revoke, amend or vary an order so made.<lf> </lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>EXPLOSIVES ACT 1961-1973 - SECT. 17\. Date from which orders take effect. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   17\. An order made under the regulations shall take effect-<lf> <lf>   (a)  in the case of an order required by the regulations to be published in the Gazette-on the date of publication; and<lf> <lf>   (b)  in any other case-on the date on which the order is made,<lf> or on such later date as is prescribed or as is fixed under the regulations.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>EXPLOSIVES ACT 1961-1973 - SECT. 18\. Proof of instruments. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   18\. Evidence of an order or other instrument made or issued by a Minister or other person in pursuance of a provision contained in, or having effect under, the regulations may, in any legal proceedings, be given by the production of-<lf> <lf>   (a)  a document purporting to be that order or other instrument and to be signed by the Minister or that other person; or<lf> <lf>   (b)  a document purporting to be certified to be a true copy of that order or other instrument by or on behalf of the Minister or that other person.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>EXPLOSIVES ACT 1961-1973 - SECT. 19\. Committees of advice. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   19\. The Regulations may make provision for or in relation to the establishment, and the functions and powers, of committees to advise the Minister on matters relating to, or to the handling of, explosives.<lf> </lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>EXPLOSIVES ACT 1961-1973 - SECT. 20\. Offences. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Amended by No. 93, 1966, s. 3.<lf>   20\. A person who contravenes, or fails to comply with, a provision of the regulations or of an order made under the regulations is guilty of an offence against this Act punishable upon conviction by-<lf> <lf>   (a)  a fine not exceeding One thousand dollars or imprisonment for a term not exceeding six months, or both, and in addition, if the offence is a continuing offence, by a fine not exceeding Two hundred dollars for each day during which the offence continues; or<lf> <lf>   (b)  such lesser penalty as is prescribed.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>EXPLOSIVES ACT 1961-1973 - SECT. 21\. Regulations. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   21\. The Governor-General may make regulations, not inconsistent with this Act, prescribing all matters required or permitted by this Act to be prescribed, or necessary or convenient to be prescribed for carrying out or giving effect to this Act.<lf> ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ -- <lf> </lf></lf></sect>
