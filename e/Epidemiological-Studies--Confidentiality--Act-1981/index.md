###  EPIDEMIOLOGICAL STUDIES (CONFIDENTIALITY) ACT 1981- SECT 1  Short title [_see_ Note 1] 
This Act may be cited as the _Epidemiological Studies (Confidentiality) Act 1981_.

 
###  EPIDEMIOLOGICAL STUDIES (CONFIDENTIALITY) ACT 1981- SECT 2  Commencement [_see_ Note 1] 
This Act shall come into operation on the day on which it receives the Royal

Assent.

 
###  EPIDEMIOLOGICAL STUDIES (CONFIDENTIALITY) ACT 1981- SECT 3  Interpretation 
<dt>(1)</dt><dd>In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:

</dd> 
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_Commonwealth epidemiological study_ means an epidemiological study

conducted by, or on behalf of, the Commonwealth. _court_ includes a tribunal, authority or person having power to require the production of documents or the answering of questions, but does not include the Commonwealth Ombudsman. _document_ includes any record of information, however recorded or stored, whether in written or printed form, on film, by electronic means or otherwise and, without limiting the generality of the foregoing, includes an article containing an image produced by photography, x-rays or otherwise. _epidemiological study_ means a study of:  </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the incidence or distribution, within the population of a country, or a

part of a country, or within a particular group of persons, or within a sample

or sub-sample of such a population or group, of:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>a disease;</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>a physical or mental state; or</dd>

<dt>(iii)</dt><dd>a condition, circumstance, occurrence, activity, form of behaviour,

course of conduct, or state of affairs, that is or may be disadvantageous to,

or result in a disadvantage to, the person concerned or to the community; or

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the factors responsible for such an incidence or distribution;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

or both, and includes a series of such studies.

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_prescribed study_ means:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the Vietnam Veterans Study; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>a Commonwealth epidemiological study declared by the regulations to be a

study to which this Act applies.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

###  EPIDEMIOLOGICAL STUDIES (CONFIDENTIALITY) ACT 1981- SECT 3A  Application of the _Criminal Code_ 
**

 <dl compact="">

		Chapter&#160;2 of the _Criminal Code_ applies to all offences against

this Act.

 </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Note:	Chapter&#160;2 of the _Criminal Code_ sets out the

general principles of criminal responsibility.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  EPIDEMIOLOGICAL STUDIES (CONFIDENTIALITY) ACT 1981- SECT 4  Secrecy relating to prescribed studies 
**

 <dl compact="">

		Subject to sections&#160;5 and 7, a person who has assisted, or is assisting,

in the conduct of a prescribed study shall not, either directly or indirectly,

except for the purpose of the conduct of that study, make a record of, or

divulge or communicate to any person, any information concerning the affairs of

another person acquired by him by reason of his having assisted, or assisting,

in the conduct of that study.

 </dl>

Penalty:	$1,000 or imprisonment for 6 months. 

**

###  EPIDEMIOLOGICAL STUDIES (CONFIDENTIALITY) ACT 1981- SECT 5  Authorisation of use of documents 
**

 <dl compact="">

		Notwithstanding section&#160;4, the Minister may, by writing signed by him,

authorize the giving of access, in a form or forms specified in the instrument

of authorization, to documents prepared or obtained in the conduct of a

prescribed study to persons assisting in the conduct of another prescribed

study, and, where such an authorization is given, it is a defence to a

prosecution for a contravention of section&#160;4 that is constituted by the

giving of access to a document if the access was given in accordance with the

authorization.

 </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Note:	The defendant bears an evidential burden in relation to

establishing that the access was given in accordance with the authorization.

See subsection 13.3(3) of the _Criminal Code_.

 </dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

**

###  EPIDEMIOLOGICAL STUDIES (CONFIDENTIALITY) ACT 1981- SECT 6  Secrecy relating to certain documents 
**

 <dl compact="">

		Subject to section&#160;7, a person who has assisted, or is assisting, in the

conduct of a prescribed study and has been given access, by virtue of an

authorization under section&#160;5, to a document prepared or obtained in the

conduct of another prescribed study shall not, either directly or indirectly,

except for the purpose of the conduct of either of those studies, make a record

of, or divulge or communicate to any person, any information concerning the

affairs of another person acquired by him by reason of his having been given

access to that document.

 </dl>

Penalty:	$1,000 or imprisonment for 6 months. 

**

###  EPIDEMIOLOGICAL STUDIES (CONFIDENTIALITY) ACT 1981- SECT 7  Certain persons may be given information 
**

 <dl compact="">

		Sections&#160;4 and 6 do not prohibit a person who has assisted, or is

assisting, in the conduct of a prescribed study from giving information

concerning the affairs of another person to:

 </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the person who supplied the information;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>where the information concerns the affairs of only one person&#151;that

person;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>where the information concerns the affairs of 2 or more persons&#151;any of

those persons with the consent of the other person, or each other person, whose

affairs that information concerns; or</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>a person nominated by a person to whom the information may be given by

virtue of paragraph&#160;(a), (b) or (c) as a person to whom that information

may be given.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

**

###  EPIDEMIOLOGICAL STUDIES (CONFIDENTIALITY) ACT 1981- SECT 8  Documents not to be produced in court etc. 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>A person who has assisted, or is assisting, in the conduct of a prescribed

study shall not be required:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>to produce in a court, or permit a court to have access to, a document

prepared or obtained in the course of the conduct of that study, being a

document concerning the affairs of another person; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>to divulge or communicate to a court any information concerning the

affairs of another person acquired by him by reason of his having assisted, or

assisting, in the conduct of that study.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>A person who has assisted, or is assisting, in the conduct of a prescribed

study and has been given access, by virtue of an authorization under

section&#160;5, to a document shall not be required:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>to produce in a court, or permit a court to have access to, that document,

being a document concerning the affairs of another person; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>to divulge or communicate to a court any information concerning the

affairs of another person acquired by him by reason of his having had, or

having, that access.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

**

###  EPIDEMIOLOGICAL STUDIES (CONFIDENTIALITY) ACT 1981- SECT 9  Extension 
**

 <dl compact="">

		Without limiting the generality of sections&#160;4, 6 and 8, those sections

extend to a person who has assisted, or is assisting, in the conduct of an

epidemiological study:

 </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>as an officer of the Public Service of a State or of the Northern

Territory;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>as an employee of a body corporate, or of another person, involved in the

conduct of the study; or</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>in accordance with a contract for the provision of his services;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

and extend to a person whether or not he received or receives, or was or is entitled to receive, remuneration in respect of the assistance provided by him. 

**

###  EPIDEMIOLOGICAL STUDIES (CONFIDENTIALITY) ACT 1981- SECT 10  Oaths and declarations of secrecy 
**

 <dl compact="">

		A person who is, or is about to become, a person to whom section&#160;4 or 6

applies shall, if and when required to do so by the Minister, or by a person

authorized in writing by the Minister for the purposes of this section, take an

oath, or make an affirmation or declaration, in a manner and form, and before a

person, prescribed by the regulations, not to make a record of, or divulge or

communicate, information in contravention of this Act.

 </dl>

Penalty:	$1,000 or imprisonment for 6 months. 

**

###  EPIDEMIOLOGICAL STUDIES (CONFIDENTIALITY) ACT 1981- SECT 11  Publication of results of studies etc. 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Nothing in this Act prohibits the publication of conclusions based on,

statistics derived from, or particulars of procedures used in, a prescribed

study, but such conclusions, statistics or particulars shall not be published

in a manner that enables the identification of an individual person (including

a deceased person).</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>In this section, _publication_, in relation to conclusions, statistics or particulars, includes: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the divulging or communication to a court of the conclusions, statistics

or particulars; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the production to a court of, or the permitting of a court to have access

to, a document containing the conclusions, statistics or particulars.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

**

###  EPIDEMIOLOGICAL STUDIES (CONFIDENTIALITY) ACT 1981- SECT 12  Application 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>For the purposes of the application of this Act in relation to the Vietnam

Veterans Study, this Act extends to information acquired, and documents

prepared or obtained, before the commencement of this Act.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>For the purposes of the application of this Act in relation to a Commonwealth epidemiological study declared by the regulations to be a study to which this Act applies, this Act extends to information acquired, and documents prepared or obtained, before the commencement of those regulations. </dd> </dl>

**

###  EPIDEMIOLOGICAL STUDIES (CONFIDENTIALITY) ACT 1981- SECT 13  Regulations 
**

 <dl compact="">

		The Governor-General may make regulations, not inconsistent with this Act,

prescribing all matters required or permitted by this Act to be prescribed or

necessary or convenient to be prescribed for carrying out or giving effect to

this Act.

 </dl>
