###  SEAFARERS REHABILITATION AND COMPENSATION LEVY COLLECTION ACT 1992 
**


**

###  Compilation Information

**

###  Long Title 
**
Seafarers Rehabilitation and Compensation Levy Act 1992


###  SEAFARERS REHABILITATION AND COMPENSATION LEVY COLLECTION ACT 1992- SECT 1  Short title [_see_ Note 1] 
<dl compact="">

		This Act may be cited as the _Seafarers Rehabilitation and Compensation Levy Collection Act 1992_.

 </dl>
###  SEAFARERS REHABILITATION AND COMPENSATION LEVY COLLECTION ACT 1992- SECT 2  Commencement [_see_ Note 1] 
<dl compact="">

		This Act commences on the day on which section&#160;2 of the Compensation Act

commences.

 </dl>
###  SEAFARERS REHABILITATION AND COMPENSATION LEVY COLLECTION ACT 1992- SECT 3  Interpretation 
<dl compact="">

		In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:

 </dl>
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_authorised person_ means a person appointed as an authorised

person under section&#160;11\. _Authority_ has the same meaning as in the Compensation Act. _Comcare_ means the body established under section&#160;68 of the _Safety, Rehabilitation and Compensation Act 1988_. _Compensation Act_ means the _Seafarers Rehabilitation and Compensation Act 1992_. _employer_ means a person who employs or engages one or more seafarers on a prescribed ship. _levy_ means an amount of levy imposed by the _Seafarers Rehabilitation and Compensation Levy Act 1992_. _premises_ includes:  </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a structure, building, vehicle or vessel; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>a place (whether enclosed or built on or not); and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>a part of premises (including premises of a kind referred to in

paragraph&#160;(a) or (b)).

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_prescribed ship_ has the same meaning as in the Compensation

Act. _quarter_ means:  </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the period beginning on the day on which this Act commences and ending on

the last day of March, June, September or December, whichever occurs first

after the commencement of this Act; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>each later period of 3 months.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_seafarer_ has the same meaning as in the Compensation Act. _seafarer berth_ has the same meaning as in the Compensation Act.  </dl></dl>

**

###  SEAFARERS REHABILITATION AND COMPENSATION LEVY COLLECTION ACT 1992- SECT 3A  Application of _Criminal Code_ 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		Chapter 2 of the _Criminal Code_ applies to all offences against this

Act.

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Note:	Chapter 2 of the _Criminal Code_ sets out the general

principles of criminal responsibility.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  SEAFARERS REHABILITATION AND COMPENSATION LEVY COLLECTION ACT 1992- SECT 4  Number of seafarer berths on which levy is payable 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		A reference in this Act to the number of seafarer berths on a prescribed ship

is a reference to the number of seafarer berths on the ship on the first day of

each quarter.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  SEAFARERS REHABILITATION AND COMPENSATION LEVY COLLECTION ACT 1992- SECT 5  Due date for payment 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		Levy on a seafarer berth is payable at the end of the period within which an

employer is required by this Act to give a return under section&#160;6.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  SEAFARERS REHABILITATION AND COMPENSATION LEVY COLLECTION ACT 1992- SECT 6  Employers to give returns and information 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		An employer must, within 14 days after the beginning of each quarter, give to

the person prescribed by the regulations a return in accordance with a form

approved by the Chief Executive Officer of Comcare containing:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a statement of the number of seafarer berths on each prescribed ship in

respect of which the employer employed or engaged seafarers on the first day of

the quarter; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>such other information, relating to those berths as is specified in the

form.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

**

###  SEAFARERS REHABILITATION AND COMPENSATION LEVY COLLECTION ACT 1992- SECT 7  Offences relating to returns 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>A person must not refuse or fail to give a return that he or she is

required to give under section&#160;6.

</dd> </dl></dl>

Penalty:	5 penalty units.

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1A)</dt><dd>Subsection (1) does not apply if the person has a reasonable excuse.

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Note:	A defendant bears an evidential burden in relation to the

matter in subsection (1A) (see subsection 13.3(3) of the _Criminal Code_).

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1B)</dt><dd>For the purposes of an offence against subsection (1), strict liability

applies to the physical element, that the person is required to give a return

under section 6.

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Note:	For _strict liability_, see section 6.1 of the

_Criminal Code_.

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>A person must not give a return that does not contain all or any of the

information required by section 6 to be included in the return.

</dd> </dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

Penalty:	20 penalty units.

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2A)</dt><dd>Subsection (2) does not apply if the person has a reasonable excuse.

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Note:	A defendant bears an evidential burden in relation to the

matter in subsection (2A) (see subsection 13.3(3) of the _Criminal Code_).

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2B)</dt><dd>For the purposes of an offence against subsection (2), strict liability

applies to the physical element, that the person is required under section 6 to

include information in a return.

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Note:	For _strict liability_, see section 6.1 of the

_Criminal Code_.

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>A person is not excused from giving a return on the ground that the return

might tend to incriminate the person, but any return given, and any information

or thing (including any document) obtained as a direct or indirect consequence

of the giving of the return, is not admissible in evidence against the person

in criminal proceedings, other than proceedings for an offence against

subsection&#160;(2) or section 137.1 or 137.2 of the _Criminal Code_ in

relation to giving the return.

</dd> </dl></dl>

**

###  SEAFARERS REHABILITATION AND COMPENSATION LEVY COLLECTION ACT 1992- SECT 8  Recovery of levy 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		Levy payable on a seafarer berth may be recovered by the Commonwealth as a

debt due to the Commonwealth.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  SEAFARERS REHABILITATION AND COMPENSATION LEVY COLLECTION ACT 1992- SECT 9  Detention of ship 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		If any levy imposed on a seafarer berth on a prescribed ship remains unpaid

after it becomes payable the ship may be detained by an authorised person until

the levy is paid.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  SEAFARERS REHABILITATION AND COMPENSATION LEVY COLLECTION ACT 1992- SECT 10  Levy taken to be levy in relation to a ship for certain purposes of the Admiralty Act 
**

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		Levy is taken to be a levy in relation to a ship for the purposes of

paragraph 4(3)(q) of the _Admiralty Act 1988_.

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Note:	paragraph 4(j)(q) of the _Admiralty Act 1988_ provides

that certain claims in relation to a ship may be dealt with as claims _in rem_.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  SEAFARERS REHABILITATION AND COMPENSATION LEVY COLLECTION ACT 1992- SECT 11  Appointment of authorised persons 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		The Chief Executive Officer of Comcare may, in writing, appoint the Deputy

Chief Executive Officer of Comcare, or a member of Comcare's staff:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>who is an SES employee or acting SES employee; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>whose classification level appears in Group 7 or 8 of Schedule&#160;1 to

the Classification Rules under the _Public Service Act 1999_; or</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>who is acting in a position usually occupied by a person with a

classification level of the kind mentioned in paragraph&#160;(b);

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

to be an authorised person for the purposes of a specified provision of this Act. 

**

###  SEAFARERS REHABILITATION AND COMPENSATION LEVY COLLECTION ACT 1992- SECT 12  Access to premises and books <center>[Back to Top](#top)</center> 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>An authorised person may, with the consent of the occupier of premises, or

in accordance with a warrant issued under subsection 13(2), enter the premises

for the purpose of exercising the powers of an authorised person under this

section.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The powers of an authorised person under this section are to: </dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>search for, examine, take extracts from and make copies of any document;

and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>search for and examine a thing;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

relating to a berth on a prescribed ship, on which levy is, or may be, payable. 

**

###  SEAFARERS REHABILITATION AND COMPENSATION LEVY COLLECTION ACT 1992- SECT 13  Warrants to enter premises 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>If an authorised person suspects on reasonable grounds that there is on

particular premises a book, document or thing relating to a seafarer berth, the

person may apply to a Magistrate for a warrant under this section.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>If the Magistrate is satisfied, by information on oath or affirmation, that: </dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>there are reasonable grounds for believing that there is on the premises a

book, document or thing relating to a berth on a prescribed ship, on which levy

is, or may be, payable; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the issue of a warrant is reasonably required for the purposes of this Act;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

the Magistrate may issue a warrant authorising the applicant to enter the premises with such assistance, and using such force, as is necessary and reasonable for the purpose of exercising some or all of the powers of an authorised person under subsection 12(2).

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>The Magistrate must not issue a warrant unless:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the informant or some other person has given to the Magistrate, either

orally or by affidavit, such further information (if any) as the Magistrate

requires concerning the grounds on which the issue of the warrant is being

sought; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the Magistrate is satisfied that there are reasonable grounds for issuing

the warrant.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(4)</dt><dd>There must be stated in the warrant:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the purpose for which the warrant is issued; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the powers exercisable under subsection 12(2) by the authorised person to

whom the warrant is issued; and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>a day (not more than 7 days after the day of issue of the warrant) on

which the warrant ceases to have effect; and</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>whether entry is authorised to be made at any time of the day or night, or

during specified hours of the day or night; and</dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>a description of the book, document or thing that the applicant suspects

are on the premises.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

**

###  SEAFARERS REHABILITATION AND COMPENSATION LEVY COLLECTION ACT 1992- SECT 14  Identity cards 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Chief Executive Officer of Comcare may cause an identity card to be

issued to an authorised person.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>An identity card must: </dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>contain a recent photograph of the authorised person to whom it is issued;

and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>be in a form approved by the Chief Executive Officer of Comcare.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>If an authorised person proposes to enter premises otherwise than in

accordance with a warrant issued under subsection 13(2), the authorised person

must produce his or her identity card to the occupier of the premises for the

occupier's inspection and, if the authorised person fails to do so, the

authorised person is not entitled to enter the premises under subsection

12(1).</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>If a person to whom an identity card has been issued ceases to be an authorised person, the person must immediately return the identity card to a person designated, in writing, by the Chief Executive Officer of Comcare.</dd> <dt>(5)</dt><dd>A person who contravenes subsection&#160;(4) is guilty of an offence punishable upon conviction by a fine not exceeding one penalty unit.</dd> <dt>(6)</dt><dd>Subsection (5) does not apply if the person has a reasonable excuse. </dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Note:	A defendant bears an evidential burden in relation to the

matter in subsection (6) (see subsection 13.3(3) of the _Criminal Code_).

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(7)</dt><dd>Subsection (5) is an offence of strict liability.

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Note:	For _strict liability_, see section 6.1 of the

_Criminal Code_.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  SEAFARERS REHABILITATION AND COMPENSATION LEVY COLLECTION ACT 1992- SECT 15  Delegation by the CEO of Comcare 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Chief Executive Officer of Comcare may, in writing, delegate all or

any of his or her functions and powers under this Act (other than powers under

section&#160;11) to:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the Deputy Chief Executive Officer of Comcare; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>a member of Comcare's staff.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>A delegate is, in the exercise of a delegated power, subject to the

directions of the Chief Executive Officer of Comcare.

</dd> </dl></dl>

**

###  SEAFARERS REHABILITATION AND COMPENSATION LEVY COLLECTION ACT 1992- SECT 16  Regulations 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Governor-General may make regulations prescribing matters:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>required or permitted by this Act to be prescribed; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>necessary or convenient to be prescribed for carrying out or giving effect

to this Act or for facilitating the collection or recovery of amounts of levy

payable to the Commonwealth under section&#160;8.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>The matters that may be prescribed under subsection&#160;(1) include but

are not limited to:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>providing for the manner of payment of levy to the Commonwealth under

section&#160;8; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>providing for the repayment of overpayments; and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>requiring employers to keep records relating to seafarer berths on

prescribed ships; and</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>requiring employers to give information relating to seafarer berths on

prescribed ships to such persons as are prescribed; and</dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>the form of warrant for the purposes of section l 3; and</dd>

<dt>(f)</dt><dd>penalties, not exceeding 10 penalty units, for offences against the

regulations.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>
