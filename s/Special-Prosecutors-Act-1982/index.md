**

###  SPECIAL PROSECUTORS ACT 1982 
**


**

###  Compilation Information 
**






**

###  Long Title 
**
###  SPECIAL PROSECUTORS ACT 1982- SECT 1  Short title [_see_ Note 1] 
This Act may be cited as the _Special Prosecutors Act 1982_.

 
###  SPECIAL PROSECUTORS ACT 1982- SECT 2  Commencement [_see_ Note 1] 
This Act shall come into operation on the day on which it receives the Royal

Assent.

 
###  SPECIAL PROSECUTORS ACT 1982- SECT 3  Interpretation 
<dt>(1)</dt><dd>In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:

</dd> 
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_appoint_ includes re-appoint. _legal practitioner_ means a barrister, a solicitor, a barrister and solicitor, or a legal practitioner, of the High Court or of the Supreme Court of a State or Territory. _State_ includes the Northern Territory. _Territory_ does not include the Northern Territory.  </dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>A reference in this Act to a prosecution for an offence against a law of

the Commonwealth includes a reference to a proceeding by the Crown in right of

the Commonwealth for the recovery of a pecuniary penalty under a law of the

Commonwealth.

</dd> </dl>

**

###  SPECIAL PROSECUTORS ACT 1982- SECT 4  Extension to external Territories 
**

 <dl compact="">

		This Act extends to all the external Territories.

 </dl>

**

###  SPECIAL PROSECUTORS ACT 1982- SECT 5  The Special Prosecutors 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Governor-General may appoint such number of persons as he considers

necessary to be Special Prosecutors.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>A person shall not be appointed as a Special Prosecutor unless he is enrolled as a legal practitioner and has been so enrolled for not less than 5 years. </dd> </dl>

**

###  SPECIAL PROSECUTORS ACT 1982- SECT 6  Functions 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The functions of a Special Prosecutor are:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>in respect of such matters or classes of matters as the Attorney-General,

by instrument in writing published in the _Gazette_, from time to time

specifies in relation to that Special Prosecutor for the purposes of this

paragraph:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>to institute prosecutions in any court for offences against the laws of

the Commonwealth or of the Territories; and</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>to carry on prosecutions (whether or not instituted by him) in any court

for offences against the laws of the Commonwealth or of the Territories; and

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>in respect of such matters or classes of matters as the Attorney-General,

by instrument in writing published in the _Gazette_, from time to time

specifies in relation to that Special Prosecutor for the purposes of this

paragraph, to take, or to coordinate or supervise the taking of, civil remedies

on behalf of the Commonwealth and its authorities.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>The institution or carrying on by a Special Prosecutor of a prosecution

for an offence against a law of the Commonwealth or of a Territory, or any

other act or thing done by a Special Prosecutor in relation to such a

prosecution, shall not be challenged or called in question in any court on the

ground that the prosecution did not relate to a matter specified by the

Attorney-General under paragraph (1)(a).</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>The taking by a Special Prosecutor of a civil remedy on behalf of the Commonwealth or an authority of the Commonwealth, or any other act or thing done by a Special Prosecutor in relation to the taking of such a civil remedy, shall not be challenged or called in question in any court on the ground that the taking of the civil remedy did not relate to a matter specified by the Attorney-General under paragraph (1)(b).</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>In addition to his functions under subsection (1), a Special Prosecutor shall perform any functions in relation to the institution or carrying on of prosecutions for offences against the laws of a State that are conferred upon him by or under any State Act.</dd> <dt>(5)</dt><dd>Nothing in this Act affects the right of any person other than a Special Prosecutor to institute or carry on a prosecution for an offence against a law of the Commonwealth or of a Territory or to take a civil remedy on behalf of the Commonwealth or an authority of the Commonwealth. </dd> </dl>

**

###  SPECIAL PROSECUTORS ACT 1982- SECT 7  Directions and guidelines to Special Prosecutor 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Attorney-General may, by notice in writing, give directions or furnish

guidelines to a Special Prosecutor with respect to the performance of the

functions of that Special Prosecutor but the Attorney-General is not entitled

to give directions or furnish guidelines in relation to a particular case.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Where the Attorney-General gives any directions or furnishes any guidelines to a Special Prosecutor under subsection (1), the Attorney-General shall cause a copy of the directions or guidelines to be published in the _Gazette_. </dd> </dl>

**

###  SPECIAL PROSECUTORS ACT 1982- SECT 8  Powers of Special Prosecutor 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>For the purposes of the performance of his functions, a Special Prosecutor

is entitled to prosecute by indictment in his own name indictable offences

against the laws of the Commonwealth or of the Territories but nothing in this

subsection prevents a Special Prosecutor from prosecuting an offence against

the laws of the Commonwealth or of the Territories in any other manner.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Where a person is under commitment on a charge of an indictable offence against the laws of the Commonwealth or of a Territory, being a prosecution instituted or carried on by a Special Prosecutor, that Special Prosecutor may decline to proceed further in the prosecution and may, if the person is in custody, by warrant under his hand direct the discharge of the person from custody, and where such a direction is given, the person shall be discharged accordingly.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>Nothing in this Act affects, or shall be taken to have affected: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the power of the Attorney-General, or of a person appointed by the

Governor-General, to prosecute by indictment in his own name indictable

offences against the laws of the Commonwealth;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the power of the Attorney-General, or of a person appointed by the

Attorney-General, to prosecute by information in his own name an indictable

offence triable before the Supreme Court of the Australian Capital Territory;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>the power of the Director of Public Prosecutions:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>to prosecute by indictment in his official name indictable offences

against the laws of the Commonwealth; or</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>to prosecute by information in his official name an indictable offence

triable before the Supreme Court of the Australian Capital Territory;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>the power under section 61 of the _Judiciary Act 1903_ of the

Attorney-General or of a person appointed by the Attorney-General;</dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>the power under section 71 of the _Judiciary Act 1903_ of the

Attorney-General or of a person appointed by the Governor-General;</dd>

<dt>(f)</dt><dd>the power under subsection 53(6) of the _Australian Capital Territory Supreme Court Act 1933_ of the Attorney-General or of a person appointed by

the Attorney-General; or</dd>

<dt>(g)</dt><dd>the power of the Director of Public Prosecutions under subsection 9(4) of

the _Director of Public Prosecutions ActÂ 1983_.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(4)</dt><dd>In this section, _Special Prosecutor_ includes a person acting

as a Special Prosecutor.

</dd> </dl>

**

###  SPECIAL PROSECUTORS ACT 1982- SECT 9  Special Prosecutor may appear personally or by counsel or solicitor 
**

<dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>In any proceeding instituted or carried on by a Special Prosecutor, or any

proceeding by way of appeal from or otherwise arising out of a proceeding

instituted or carried on by a Special Prosecutor, that Special Prosecutor may

appear in person or may be represented by counsel or solicitor.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>A Special Prosecutor is, in his official capacity, entitled to practise as a barrister in any federal court or court exercising federal jurisdiction and in any court of a Territory and is entitled to all the rights and privileges of a barrister in every such court whether or not he would, but for this section, be entitled to practise in that court.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>In this section: </dd> </dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_Special Prosecutor_ includes a person acting as a Special

Prosecutor. _Territory_ includes the Northern Territory.  </dl></dl>

**

###  SPECIAL PROSECUTORS ACT 1982- SECT 10  Terms and conditions of appointment 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>A Special Prosecutor holds office for such period, not exceeding 5 years,

as is specified in the instrument of his appointment, but is eligible for

re-appointment.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>A Special Prosecutor holds office on such terms and conditions (if&#160;any) in respect of matters not provided for by this Act as are determined by the Governor-General. </dd> </dl>

**

###  SPECIAL PROSECUTORS ACT 1982- SECT 11  Remuneration and allowances 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>A Special Prosecutor shall be paid such remuneration and allowances as are

determined by the Attorney-General.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>An office of Special Prosecutor is not a public office for the purposes of Part II of the _Remuneration Tribunal Act 1973_. </dd> </dl>

**

###  SPECIAL PROSECUTORS ACT 1982- SECT 12  Leave of absence 
**

 <dl compact="">

		The Attorney-General may grant to a Special Prosecutor leave of absence from

duty on such terms and conditions as to remuneration or otherwise as the

Attorney-General determines.

 </dl>

**

###  SPECIAL PROSECUTORS ACT 1982- SECT 13  Resignation 
**

 <dl compact="">

		A Special Prosecutor may resign his office by writing signed by him and

delivered to the Governor-General.

 </dl>

**

###  SPECIAL PROSECUTORS ACT 1982- SECT 14  Disclosure of interests 
**

 <dl compact="">

		A Special Prosecutor shall give written notice to the Attorney-General of all

direct or indirect pecuniary interests that he has or acquires in any business

or in any body corporate carrying on any business.

 </dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

**

###  SPECIAL PROSECUTORS ACT 1982- SECT 15  Termination of appointment 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Governor-General may terminate the appointment of a Special Prosecutor

for misbehaviour or physical or mental incapacity.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Where the Governor-General is satisfied, having regard to any report made to the Attorney-General by a Special Prosecutor under section 20, that that Special Prosecutor has discharged his functions in relation to the matters or classes of matters specified in relation to him by the Attorney-General under section 6, the Governor-General may terminate the appointment of that Special Prosecutor.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>If a Special Prosecutor: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>becomes bankrupt, applies to take the benefit of any law for the relief of

bankrupt or insolvent debtors, compounds with his creditors or makes an

assignment of his remuneration for their benefit;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>is absent from duty except on leave of absence granted by the

Attorney-General for 14 consecutive days or for 28 days in any 12 months;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>without the consent of the Attorney-General engages in practice as a

barrister or solicitor, or engages in paid employment, outside the duties of

his office; or</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>fails, without reasonable excuse, to comply with his obligations under

section 14;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

the Governor-General shall terminate the appointment of that Special Prosecutor. 

**

###  SPECIAL PROSECUTORS ACT 1982- SECT 16  Acting Special Prosecutor 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Attorney-General may appoint a person to act as a Special Prosecutor

during any period, or during all periods, when a Special Prosecutor is absent

from duty or from Australia or is, for any reason, unable to perform the

functions of his office.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Attorney-General may: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>determine the terms and conditions of appointment, including remuneration

and allowances, of a person acting as a Special Prosecutor; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>at any time terminate such an appointment.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>The appointment of a person to act as a Special Prosecutor ceases to have

effect if he resigns the appointment by writing signed by him and delivered to

the Attorney-General.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>While a person is acting as a Special Prosecutor, he has, and may exercise, all the powers and shall perform all the functions of the Special Prosecutor in whose place he is acting.</dd> <dt>(5)</dt><dd>The validity of anything done by or in relation to a person purporting to act under subsection (1) shall not be called in question on the ground that the occasion for his appointment had not arisen, that there is a defect or irregularity in or in connection with his appointment, that the appointment had ceased to have effect or that the occasion for him to act had not arisen or had passed. </dd> </dl>

**

###  SPECIAL PROSECUTORS ACT 1982- SECT 17  Appointment of staff, consultants etc. 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>A Special Prosecutor may, on behalf of the Commonwealth and with the

approval of the Attorney-General, employ, under agreements in writing, such

persons as he thinks necessary for the purposes of the performance of his

functions.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>A Special Prosecutor may, on behalf of the Commonwealth and with the approval of the Attorney-General, engage, under agreements in writing, persons having suitable qualifications and experience as consultants to, or to perform services for, that Special Prosecutor.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>Where a Special Prosecutor employs or engages a person under this section, the terms and conditions of the person's employment or engagement are such as are from time to time determined by the Special Prosecutor.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>In this section, _Special Prosecutor_ includes a person acting as a Special Prosecutor. </dd> </dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

**

###  SPECIAL PROSECUTORS ACT 1982- SECT 18  Staff on secondment to Special Prosecutor 
**

 <dl compact="">

		In addition to persons employed or engaged under section 17, a Special

Prosecutor shall be assisted by:

 </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>persons appointed or engaged under the _Public Service ActÂ 1999_

whose services are made available to that Special Prosecutor;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>members of the Australian Federal Police whose services are made available

to that Special Prosecutor;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>officers and employees of authorities of the Commonwealth whose services

are made available to that Special Prosecutor; and</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>persons whose services are made available to that Special Prosecutor

pursuant to arrangements made under section 19.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

**

###  SPECIAL PROSECUTORS ACT 1982- SECT 19  Administrative arrangements 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Attorney-General may make an arrangement with the appropriate Minister

of a State or of the Northern Territory under which the State or Territory

will, from time to time as agreed upon under the arrangement, make available a

person who is an officer or employee of the State or Territory or of an

authority of the State or Territory or a member of the Police Force of the

State or Territory, or persons who are such officers, employees or members, to

perform services for a Special Prosecutor.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>An arrangement under subsection (1) may provide for the Commonwealth to reimburse a State or the Northern Territory with respect to the services of a person or persons to whom the arrangement relates.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>In this section, _State_ does not include the Northern Territory. </dd> </dl>

**

###  SPECIAL PROSECUTORS ACT 1982- SECT 20  Annual report 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Each Special Prosecutor shall, as soon as practicable after each

30&#160;June, prepare and furnish to the Attorney-General a report of his

operations under this Act during the year that ended on that 30&#160;June.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Attorney-General shall cause a copy of every report furnished to him by a Special Prosecutor under this section to be laid before each House of the Parliament within 15 sitting days of that House after the report is received by him. </dd> </dl>

**

###  SPECIAL PROSECUTORS ACT 1982- SECT 21  Regulations 
**

 <dl compact="">

		The Governor-General may make regulations, not inconsistent with this Act,

prescribing matters necessary or convenient to be prescribed for carrying out

or giving effect to this Act.

 </dl>
