###  SHIPPING REGISTRATION ACT 1981- SECT 1  Short title [_see_ Note 1] 
This Act may be cited as the _Shipping Registration Act 1981_.

 
###  SHIPPING REGISTRATION ACT 1981- SECT 2  Commencement [_see_ Note 1] 
This Act shall come into operation on a date to be fixed by Proclamation.

###  SHIPPING REGISTRATION ACT 1981- SECT 5  Act to bind the Crown 
**

 <dl compact="">

of the Northern Territory and of Norfolk Island, but nothing in this Act

 </dl>

###  SHIPPING REGISTRATION ACT 1981- SECT 8  Australian-owned ships 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>A reference in this Act to an Australian-owned ship shall be read as a

reference to a ship that:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>is owned by an Australian national or Australian nationals, and by no

other person;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>is owned (otherwise than as described in paragraph (c)) by 3&#160;or more

persons as joint owners, where the majority of those persons are Australian

nationals; or</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>is owned by 2 or more persons as owners in common, where more than half of

the shares in the ship are owned by an Australian national or Australian

nationals.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>For the purposes of paragraph (1)(c), where 2 or more persons are joint

owners of a share or shares in a ship:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>in the case of 2 or more particular shares that are owned by the same

persons&#151;the interest of each owner in the shares shall be ascertained by

dividing the number of the shares by the number of the owners of the shares;

and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>in the case of a share to which paragraph (a) does not apply&#151;the interest

of each owner in the share shall be ascertained by dividing the number one by

the number of the owners of the share;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

and, if the sum of the interests so ascertained in respect of all jointly-owned shares in the ship as being interests of an Australian national or Australian nationals is a whole number or a whole number and a fraction, such number of those shares as is equal to that whole number shall be deemed to be owned by an Australian national or Australian nationals.

###  SHIPPING REGISTRATION ACT 1981- SECT 11  Registration of property in ships 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>For the purposes of the registration of a ship:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the property in the ship shall be divided into 64 shares;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>subject to paragraph (c), subsection (2) and section 37, the number of

persons registered as owners of the ship shall not at any time exceed 64, but

nothing in this paragraph affects the beneficial interests of any number of

persons or of a body corporate represented by or claiming under or through a

registered owner (including a registered joint owner);</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>any number of persons not exceeding 5 may be registered as joint owners of

the ship or of a share or shares in the ship;</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>a joint owner of the ship or of a share or shares in the ship is not

entitled to dispose of his or her interest in severalty;</dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>a person shall not be registered as the owner of a fractional part of a

share in the ship; and</dd>

<dt>(f)</dt><dd>a body corporate shall be registered as owner by its corporate name.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>For the purposes of paragraph (1)(b), where a share in a ship is jointly

owned, or 2 or more shares in a ship are jointly owned by the same persons, the

joint owners of that share, or of those shares, as the case may be, shall be

treated as constituting one person.

</dd> </dl>

###  SHIPPING REGISTRATION ACT 1981- SECT 12  Obligation to register Australian-owned ships 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Subject to this Part, every Australian-owned ship shall be registered

under this Act.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Where an Australian-owned ship is operated by a foreign resident under a demise charter, the Authority may, by instrument in writing, exempt the ship during the term of the charter from the requirement to be registered.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>Where a ship required to be registered is not registered, the owner of the ship is, in respect of each day on which the ship remains unregistered (including the day of a conviction for an offence against this subsection or any subsequent day), guilty of an offence, and, whether or not proceedings are instituted for such an offence, the ship may be detained until registration is effected.</dd> <dt>(3A)</dt><dd>An offence under subsection&#160;(3) is an offence of strict liability. </dd> </dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Note:	For _strict liability_, see section&#160;6.1 of

the _Criminal Code_.

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(4)</dt><dd>It is not a defence to a prosecution under subsection (3) that the ship

required to be registered is registered under the law of a foreign country.

</dd> </dl>

**

###  SHIPPING REGISTRATION ACT 1981- SECT 13  Exemptions from registration 
**

 <dl compact="">

		Ships less than 24 metres in tonnage length, Government ships, fishing

vessels and pleasure craft are exempt from the requirement to be registered

under section 12.

 </dl>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>small craft wholly owned by residents of Australia or by residents of

Australia and Australian nationals;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>small craft operated solely by residents of Australia or Australian

nationals or both;</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>ships on demise charter to Australian-based operators.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

 <dl compact="">

under the law of a foreign country.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Where, in relation to a ship that has, at any time, been registered under the law of a foreign country, application is made for the registration of the ship under this Act, the application shall be accompanied by such evidence as the Registrar requires to establish: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>that the ship is no longer registered under the law of that country; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>that steps have been taken, or are proposed to be taken, to close the

registration of the ship under this Act.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

###  SHIPPING REGISTRATION ACT 1981- SECT 20  Custody of registration certificate 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The registration certificate of a ship shall not be used except for the

purpose of the lawful navigation of the ship, and shall not be subject to

detention by reason of a claim by an owner, mortgagee, charterer, operator or

any other person to any title to, lien or charge on, or interest in, the

ship.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>A person having possession or control of a registration certificate shall not fail to deliver the certificate on demand to the person entitled to the custody of it for the purposes of the lawful navigation of the ship, or to the Registrar, a Deputy Registrar, a proper officer or any other person entitled by law to require its delivery.</dd> <dt>(2A)</dt><dd>Subsection&#160;(2) does not apply if the person has a reasonable excuse. </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Note:	A defendant bears an evidential burden in relation to the

matter in subsection&#160;(2A) (see subsection 13.3(3) of the _Criminal Code_).

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(2B)</dt><dd>An offence based on subsection&#160;(2) is an offence of strict liability.

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Note:	For _strict liability_, see section&#160;6.1 of

the _Criminal Code_.

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>If, in any proceedings in respect of an offence against this section in

relation to a registration certificate, the court is of the opinion that the

certificate is lost, the court shall make an order to that effect and cause an

office copy of the order to be served on the Registrar.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>In this section, _registration certificate_ includes a provisional registration certificate. </dd> </dl>

###  SHIPPING REGISTRATION ACT 1981- SECT 40  Mortgage not to change ownership 
**

 <dl compact="">

mortgagee becoming, or the mortgagor ceasing to be, owner of the ship or share

security under the mortgage.

 </dl>

###  SHIPPING REGISTRATION ACT 1981- SECT 41  Mortgagee to have power of disposal 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>A mortgagee of a ship or of a share in a ship has power absolutely to

dispose of the ship or share and to give effectual receipts in respect of the

disposal, but where there are 2 or more mortgages of the same ship or share, a

subsequent mortgagee shall not, except under the order of a court of competent

jurisdiction, dispose of the ship or share without the consent of every prior

mortgagee.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>A mortgagee of a ship or of a share in a ship who intends to dispose of the ship or the share shall not dispose of the ship or share unless he or she has first given notice in writing to the Registrar of his or her intention so to dispose of the ship or share.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>Where the Registrar is notified by a mortgagee of the intention of that mortgagee to dispose of a ship or of a share in a ship, the Registrar shall forthwith inform the subsequent mortgagee (if any) or each subsequent mortgagee (if more than one) of that ship or share whose interest in that ship or share is entered in the Register of the matter so notified to him or her.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>The disposal of a ship or of a share in a ship is not invalidated because of any failure to comply with a requirement of subsection&#160;(2) in relation to the disposal. </dd> </dl>

###  SHIPPING REGISTRATION ACT 1981- SECT 42  Transfer of mortgage

 <dl compact="">
