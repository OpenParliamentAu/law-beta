### <name>TRADE COMMISSIONERS ACT 1933 - SECT. 1\. Short title. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Commonwealth and for other purposes.<lf> Short title amended; No. 32, 1918, s. 2.<lf>   1\. This Act may be cited as the Trade Commissioners Act 1933.*<lf> </lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>TRADE COMMISSIONERS ACT 1933 - SECT. 2\. Definitions. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   2\. In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears-<lf> <lf>   ''Assistant Trade Commissioner'' means an Assistant Trade Commissioner appointed pursuant to this Act;<lf> <lf>   ''the Public Service Board'' means the Board of Commissioners appointed in pursuance of the Commonwealth Public Service Act 1922,* or in pursuance of that Act as subsequently amended;<lf> <lf>   ''Trade Commissioner'' means a Trade Commissioner appointed pursuant to this Act.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>TRADE COMMISSIONERS ACT 1933 - SECT. 3\. Appointment of Trade Commissioners and Assistant Trade Commissioners. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Amended by No. 64, 1936, s. 2.<lf>   3\. The Governor-General may appoint one or more Trade Commissioners and Assistant Trade Commissioners of the Commonwealth, who shall, subject to this Act, hold office for such periods and upon such conditions as are prescribed or as the Governor-General in any particular case determines.<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>TRADE COMMISSIONERS ACT 1933 - SECT. 4\. Trade Commissioners or Assistant Trade Commissioners who were not previously officers of Public Service. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   4\. (1) A Trade Commissioner or an Assistant Trade Commissioner who was not, immediately prior to his appointment as Trade Commissioner or Assistant Trade Commissioner, as the case may be, an officer of the Public Service of the Commonwealth shall not be subject to the provisions of the Commonwealth Public Service Act 1922-1932.*<lf> Amended by No. 64, 1936, s. 3\. 

  (2) A person to whom this section applies shall be paid such salary, allowances and expenses as are prescribed or as the Governor-General in any particular case determines.<lf> </lf>
</lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>TRADE COMMISSIONERS ACT 1933 - SECT. 5\. Removal of Trade Commissioners and Assistant Trade Commissioners. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   5\. (1) A Trade Commissioner or an Assistant Trade Commissioner to whom the last preceding section applies shall not be removed from office except by the Governor-General on the grounds of proved misbehaviour or incapacity.<lf> Amended by No. 216, 1973, s. 3\. 

  (2) A Trade Commissioner or Assistant Trade Commissioner shall be deemed to have vacated his office if he becomes bankrupt, or compounds with his creditors or makes an assignment of his salary for their benefit.<lf> </lf>
</lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>TRADE COMMISSIONERS ACT 1933 - SECT. 6\. Officers of Public Service appointed Trade Commissioners or Assistant Trade Commissioners. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Sub-section (1) amended by No. 64, 1936, s. 4.<lf>   6\. (1) An officer of the Public Service of the Commonwealth who is appointed a Trade Commissioner or an Assistant Trade Commissioner-<lf> <lf>   (a)  shall hold office during the period for which he is appointed a Trade Commissioner or an Assistant Trade Commissioner, and during that period, shall be subject to the provisions of the Commonwealth Public Service Act 1922-1932;* <lf> <lf>   (b)  shall retain all his existing and accruing rights;<lf> <lf>   (c)  shall not by reason of his appointment under this Act, be required to resign from the Public Service of the Commonwealth, but may be granted leave of absence for the period of his appointment under this Act, and the period of leave so granted shall, for all purposes, be included as part of the officer's period of service; and<lf> <lf>   (d)  shall, unless he has been dismissed for misconduct or has attained the maximum age for retirement fixed by the Commonwealth Public Service Act 1922-1934,* be entitled, upon the termination of his appointment under this Act, to re-appointment to a position in the Public Service of the Commonwealth with such advancement in status and salary, beyond those held and received by him in that Service immediately prior to this appointment under this Act, as the Public Service Board in the circumstances thinks just.<lf> Inserted by No. 64, 1936, s. 4; amended by No. 216, 1973, s. 3\. 

  (1A) For the purpose of determining the existing and accruing rights of an officer under paragraph (b) of the last preceding sub-section, the officer's service as a Trade Commissioner or an Assistant Trade Commissioner shall be taken into account as if it were service in the Public Service of the Commonwealth. 

  (2) In determining the status and salary to which an officer shall be advanced, the Public Service Board shall take into consideration the period of the officer's appointment under this Act.<lf> Amended by No. 64, 1936, s. 4\. <p>  (3) An officer to whom this section applies shall, during the period during which he holds office as Trade Commissioner or as Assistant Trade Commissioner, be paid salary at the same rate as he was being paid immediately prior to his appointment as Trade Commissioner or as Assistant Trade Commissioner together with such additional remuneration and allowances as are prescribed or as the Governor-General in any particular case determines.<lf> </lf></p></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>TRADE COMMISSIONERS ACT 1933 - SECT. 7\. Duties of Trade Commissioners and Assistant Trade Commissioners. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   7\. Every Trade Commissioner or Assistant Trade Commissioner shall-<lf> <lf>   (a)  act as Trade Commissioner or Assistant Trade Commissioner of the Commonwealth in such place as the Governor-General determines and shall have and may exercise such powers and perform such duties as the Governor-General confers or imposes upon him; and<lf> <lf>   (b)  carry out such instructions as he receives from the Minister relating to the commercial, trading, financial and general interests of the Commonwealth, whether in such place or elsewhere.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>TRADE COMMISSIONERS ACT 1933 - SECT. 8\. Designation of Trade Commissioners and Assistant Trade Commissioners. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   8\. A Trade Commissioner or an Assistant Trade Commissioner, in respect of whom a direction is given under this section, shall have, in relation to the performance of his functions under this Act, such designation as the Minister directs.<lf> </lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>TRADE COMMISSIONERS ACT 1933 - SECT. 9\. Trade Commissioners and Assistant Trade Commissioners not to engage in business. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> <lf>   9\. A Trade Commissioner or an Assistant Trade Commissioner shall not during his tenure of office, whether within or without the Commonwealth, and whether for reward or otherwise, be or act as a director or agent of or hold any office in any company or syndicate whether incorporated or unincorporated or hold any other employment, or engage in any business.<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>TRADE COMMISSIONERS ACT 1933 - SECT. 10\. Trade Commissioners and Assistant Trade Commissioners may appoint officers. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   10\. (1) A Trade Commissioner or an Assistant Trade Commissioner may, subject to the regulations and subject to such directions as are given from time to time by the Minister, appoint such officers as he thinks necessary for the purposes of this Act.<lf> 

  (2) Officers appointed by a Trade Commissioner or Assistant Trade Commissioner in pursuance of this section shall not be subject to the provisions of the Commonwealth Public Service Act 1922-1932* or of the Superannuation Act 1922-1930, but shall be engaged for such periods and shall be subject to such conditions, as are prescribed, or as, in prescribed cases, the Minister determines.<lf> </lf>
</lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>TRADE COMMISSIONERS ACT 1933 - SECT. 11\. Payments of salaries, &c., out of moneys appropriated. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   11\. The Minister shall, out of moneys from time to time appropriated by the Parliament for the purpose, pay all such moneys as are necessary for payment of the salaries, remuneration, allowances and expenses of Trade Commissioners and Assistant Trade Commissioners, and of officers appointed under section ten of this Act.<lf> </lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>TRADE COMMISSIONERS ACT 1933 - SECT. 12\. Regulations. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   12\. The Governor-General may make regulations, not inconsistent with this Act, prescribing all matters which by this Act are required or permitted to be prescribed, or which are necessary or convenient to be prescribed, for giving effect to this Act.<lf> ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ -- <lf> </lf></lf></sect>
