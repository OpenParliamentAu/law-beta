###  WITNESS PROTECTION ACT 1994- SECT 4  Establishment of the National Witness Protection Program 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Commissioner is to maintain a program, to be known as the National

Witness Protection Program, under which the Commissioner, and persons who hold

or occupy designated positions, arrange or provide protection and other

assistance for witnesses.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>That protection and assistance may include things done as a result of powers and functions conferred on the Commissioner under a complementary witness protection law. </dd> </dl></dl>

**

###  WITNESS PROTECTION ACT 1994- SECT 5  Inclusion in NWPP not to be done as a reward for giving evidence etc. 
**

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		The inclusion of a witness in the NWPP is not to be done as a reward or as a

means of persuading or encouraging the witness to give evidence or to make a

statement.

 </dl></dl>

###  WITNESS PROTECTION ACT 1994- SECT 7  Witness to disclose certain matters before being included in the NWPP 
**

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Commissioner is not to include a witness in the NWPP unless the

Commissioner is satisfied that the witness has provided the Commissioner with

all information necessary for the Commissioner to decide whether the witness

should be included.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Without limiting the generality of subsection&#160;(1), a witness must: </dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>disclose to the Commissioner details of all outstanding legal obligations

of the witness; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>disclose to the Commissioner details of any outstanding debts of the

witness, including amounts outstanding for any tax, including a tax under a law

of a State or Territory; and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>disclose to the Commissioner details of the witness's criminal history;

and</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>disclose to the Commissioner details of any civil proceedings that have

been instituted by or against the witness; and</dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>disclose to the Commissioner details of any bankruptcy proceedings that

have been instituted against the witness; and</dd>

<dt>(f)</dt><dd>inform the Commissioner whether the witness is an undischarged bankrupt

under the _Bankruptcy Act 1966_ and, if the witness is, give to the

Commissioner copies of all documents relating to the bankruptcy; and</dd>

<dt>(g)</dt><dd>inform the Commissioner whether the witness has entered into or intends to

enter into a personal insolvency agreement under Part&#160;X of the

_Bankruptcy Act 1966_ and, if the witness has done or intends to do such a

thing, give to the Commissioner copies of all documents relating to that thing;

and</dd>

<dt>(h)</dt><dd>inform the Commissioner whether there are any restrictions on the

witness's holding positions in companies, whether public or private and, if

there are, give to the Commissioner copies of all documents relating to those

restrictions; and</dd>

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>disclose to the Commissioner details of the witness's immigration status;

and</dd>

<dt>(j)</dt><dd>disclose to the Commissioner details of financial liabilities and assets

(whether real or personal) of the witness in relation to which:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>a record is kept under a law of a State or Territory; or</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>the witness has entered into a contractual arrangement; and

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(k)</dt><dd>disclose to the Commissioner details of any cash held by the witness,

whether in accounts or otherwise; and</dd>

<dt>(l)</dt><dd>disclose to the Commissioner details of any reparation order that is in

force against the witness; and</dd>

<dt>(m)</dt><dd>inform the Commissioner whether any of the witness's property (whether

real or personal) is liable to forfeiture or confiscation or is subject to

restraint under a law of the Commonwealth or of a State or Territory; and</dd>

<dt>(n)</dt><dd>inform the Commissioner of the witness's general medical condition; and</dd>

<dt>(o)</dt><dd>disclose to the Commissioner details of any relevant court orders or

arrangements relating to custody or access to children; and</dd>

<dt>(p)</dt><dd>disclose to the Commissioner details of any business dealings in which the

witness is involved; and</dd>

<dt>(q)</dt><dd>disclose to the Commissioner details of court orders relating to sentences

imposed on the witness to which the witness is subject in relation to criminal

prosecutions; and</dd>

<dt>(r)</dt><dd>disclose to the Commissioner details of any parole or licence to which the

witness is subject; and</dd>

<dt>(s)</dt><dd>give to the Commissioner copies of any documents relating to any such

orders, parole or licence; and</dd>

<dt>(t)</dt><dd>disclose to the Commissioner details of any arrangements that the witness

has made for:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>the service of documents on the witness; or</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>representation in proceedings in a court; or</dd>

<dt>(iii)</dt><dd>enforcement of judgments in the witness's favour; or</dd>

<dt>(iv)</dt><dd>compliance with the enforcement of judgments against the witness.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>The Commissioner may also:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>require the witness to undergo:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>medical tests or examinations; or</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>psychological or psychiatric examinations;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		and to make the results available to the Commissioner; or

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>make such other inquiries and investigations as the Commissioner considers

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

for the purposes of assessing whether the witness should be included in the NWPP.

###  WITNESS PROTECTION ACT 1994- SECT 8  Selection for inclusion in the NWPP 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Commissioner has the sole responsibility of deciding whether to

include a witness in the NWPP, including cases where an approved authority has

requested that a witness be included in the NWPP.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>A witness may be included in the NWPP only if: </dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the Commissioner has decided that the witness be included; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the witness agrees to be included; and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>the witness signs a memorandum of understanding in accordance with

section&#160;9 or:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>if the witness is under 18 years&#151;a parent or guardian of the witness

signs such a memorandum; or</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>if the witness otherwise lacks legal capacity to sign the memorandum&#151;a

guardian or other person who is usually responsible for the care and control of

the witness signs such a memorandum.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>The Commissioner must, in deciding whether to include a witness in the

NWPP, have regard to:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>whether the witness has a criminal record, particularly in respect of

crimes of violence, and whether that record indicates a risk to the public if

the witness is included in the NWPP; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>if a psychological or psychiatric examination or evaluation of the witness

has been conducted to determine the witness's suitability for inclusion in the

NWPP&#151;that examination or evaluation; and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>the seriousness of the offence to which any relevant evidence or statement

relates; and</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>the nature and importance of any relevant evidence or statement; and</dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>whether there are viable alternative methods of protecting the witness;

and</dd>

<dt>(f)</dt><dd>the nature of the perceived danger to the witness; and</dd>

<dt>(g)</dt><dd>the nature of the witness's relationship to other witnesses being assessed

for inclusion in the NWPP;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

and may have regard to such other matters as the Commissioner considers relevant.

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(4)</dt><dd>The Commissioner must not include a witness in the NWPP if the

Commissioner does not, in his or her opinion, have enough information to assess

the matters referred to in subsection&#160;(3) in relation to the witness.</dd> <dt>(5)</dt><dd>If: </dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a parent or guardian of a witness signs a memorandum of understanding

because the witness was under 18 years; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the witness is included in the NWPP and remains a participant until after

he or she turns 18;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

the Commissioner may require the participant to sign another memorandum of understanding.

###  WITNESS PROTECTION ACT 1994- SECT 9  Memorandum of understanding 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>A memorandum of understanding is to:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>set out the basis on which a participant is included in the NWPP and

details of the protection and assistance that is to be provided; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>contain a provision to the effect that protection and assistance under the

NWPP may be terminated if the participant breaches a term of the memorandum of

understanding.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>A memorandum of understanding in relation to a participant may also

contain:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the terms and conditions on which protection and assistance is to be

provided to the participant, which may include a condition that protection and

assistance may be withdrawn if the participant:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>commits an offence against a law of the Commonwealth or of a State or

Territory; or</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>engages in activities of a kind specified in the memorandum of

understanding; or</dd>

<dt>(iii)</dt><dd>compromises the integrity of the NWPP; and

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>an agreement by or on behalf of the participant not to compromise,

directly or indirectly, the security of, or any other aspect of, the protection

or assistance being provided; and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>an agreement by or on behalf of the participant that the participant

comply with all reasonable directions of the Commissioner in relation to the

protection and assistance provided to the participant; and</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>an agreement by or on behalf of the participant that the participant will,

if required to do so by the Commissioner:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>undergo medical, psychological or psychiatric tests or examinations and

make the results available to the Commissioner; or</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>undergo drug or alcohol counselling or treatment; and

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>a list of:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>the outstanding legal obligations (including family maintenance

obligations and taxation obligations) of the participant; and</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>any other obligations of the participant;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		and an agreement by or on behalf of the participant as to how those

obligations are to be met; and

<dt>(f)</dt><dd>a financial support arrangement; and</dd>

<dt>(g)</dt><dd>an agreement by or on behalf of the participant that the participant will

disclose to the Commissioner details of any criminal charges that are made

against, and any civil or bankruptcy proceedings that are made in relation to,

the participant after the participant is included in the NWPP.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>A memorandum of understanding must be signed by or on behalf of the

witness in the presence of a person who holds or occupies a designated

position.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>A witness becomes included in the NWPP when the Commissioner signs the memorandum of understanding.</dd> <dt>(5)</dt><dd>The Commissioner must, as soon as practicable after signing a memorandum of understanding, notify the relevant participant that it has been signed.</dd> <dt>(6)</dt><dd>Subject to subsection&#160;(7), the Commissioner may, by notice in writing given to a participant, vary the memorandum of understanding, and a variation takes effect on the day on which the notice is received by the participant.</dd> <dt>(7)</dt><dd>A variation must not have the effect of removing from the memorandum of understanding the provisions referred to in subsection&#160;(1). </dd> </dl></dl>

###  WITNESS PROTECTION ACT 1994- SECT 10  Inclusion of foreign nationals or residents in NWPP at the request of foreign law enforcement agencies 
**

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>If:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the Minister receives a request from an appropriate authority of a foreign

country (the _agency_) for the inclusion of a person (the

_nominated person_) who is a citizen or a resident of that country

in the NWPP; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the Minister is satisfied that:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>the agency has provided all material that is necessary to support the

request; and</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>it is appropriate to do so in all the circumstances;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

the Minister is to refer the request to the Commissioner.

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Commissioner is to consider including the nominated person in the NWPP

in the same way as the Commissioner would consider including another person in

the NWPP.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>The Commissioner may, if he or she thinks it appropriate to do so, seek further information about the nominated person from the agency.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>If: </dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the Commissioner decides that the nominated person is suitable for

inclusion in the NWPP; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the Minister, after considering a report from the Commissioner

recommending the inclusion of the person in the NWPP, has decided that it is

appropriate in all the circumstances that the person be included in the NWPP;

and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>the Commissioner has entered into an arrangement with the agency for the

purpose of making services under the NWPP available to the agency; and</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>the nominated person has been granted a visa for entry to Australia;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

the Commissioner is to include the nominated person in the NWPP.

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(5)</dt><dd>An arrangement referred to in paragraph&#160;(4)(c) must include

procedures under which the agency pays the costs associated with providing

protection for the nominated person and any associated persons, including:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the costs of travel by those persons and the costs of associated travel by

members; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>any costs that will be incurred if protection and assistance under the

NWPP to the nominated person is terminated; and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>such other costs as the Commissioner determines.

</dd>

that has come to the notice of the person in relation to this Act;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

except where it is necessary to do so for the purpose of carrying the provisions of this Act into effect.

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>If it is essential to the determination of legal proceedings under or in

###  WITNESS PROTECTION ACT 1994- SECT 27  Requirement where participant becomes a witness in criminal proceedings 
**

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>the person is to be a witness in a criminal proceeding under that

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>the person has a criminal record under his or her former identity;

</dd> </dl></dl>

###  WITNESS PROTECTION ACT 1994- SECT 28  Identity of participant not to be disclosed in court proceedings etc. 
**

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		If, in any proceedings in a court, a tribunal or a Royal Commission or other

commission of inquiry, the identity of a person who is a Commonwealth

participant is in issue or may be disclosed, the court, tribunal or commission

must, unless it considers that the interests of justice require otherwise:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>hold that part of the proceedings that relate to the identity of the

participant in private; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>make such order relating to the suppression of publication of evidence

given before the court, tribunal or commission as, in its opinion, will ensure

that the identity of the participant is not disclosed.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>
