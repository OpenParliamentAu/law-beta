### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - LONG TITLE </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>          An Act relating to a Rural Adjustment Scheme<lf> </lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - PART 1  PART 1 - PRELIMINARY </name>
</b>** 
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 1 Short title </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   1\. This Act may be cited as the Rural Adjustment Act 1992.*1* SEE NOTES TO FIRST ARTICLE OF THIS CHAPTER . </sect>
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 2 Commencement </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   2\. This Act commences on 1 January 1993\. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 3 Objectives of this Act </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   3\. (1) The objectives of this Act, which are to be pursued on both a national basis and a regional basis, are:<lf>   (a) to foster the development of a more profitable farm sector that is able to operate competitively in a deregulated financial and market environment; and<lf>   (b) to improve the competitiveness of the farm sector in a sustainable manner. 

<lf>   (2) In order to achieve those objectives, this Act is intended:<lf>   (a) to promote a better financial, technical and management performance from the farm sector; and<lf>   (b) to provide support, either directly or indirectly, to farmers who have prospects of sustainable long-term profitability with a view to improving the productivity of their farm units; and<lf>   (c) in the case of direct support to farmers - to provide that support in a way that ensures that the farmers who are supported become financially independent of that support within a reasonable period; and<lf>   (d) to provide that support through, but not limited to:<lf>     (i) grants for the purposes of subsidies for interest payable<lf> on, and associated costs of, loans, whether the loans are provided by a State or by another person; and<lf>     (ii) grants for the purposes of farm training, planning,<lf> appraisal, support services and rural adjustment research; and<lf>   (e) to support farmers who do not have prospects of sustainable long-term profitability to leave the farm sector; and<lf>   (f) to enable grants of money to be made to persons other than farmers for purposes relating to rural adjustment; and<lf>   (g) to enable loans of money (whether secured or unsecured) to be made to farmers or other persons for purposes relating to rural adjustment. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 4 Interpretation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   4\. In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:<lf>   "Chairperson" means the Chairperson of the Council;<lf>   "Commonwealth member" means a member referred to in paragraph 6 (3) (b);<lf>   "Council" means the Rural Adjustment Scheme Advisory Council;<lf>   "member" means a member of the Council, and includes the Chairperson;<lf>   "person" includes:<lf>   (a) an association, authority, body, institution or other organisation, whether incorporated or unincorporated; and<lf>   (b) a partnership;<lf>   "Rural Adjustment Scheme" means:<lf>   (a) the State component of the Rural Adjustment Scheme<lf>   (b) the scheme embodied in Division 1 of Part 3; and<lf>   (c) agreements and memoranda under Division 1 of Part 3;<lf>   "State" includes the Australian Capital Territory and the Northern Territory;<lf>   "State component of the Rural Adjustment Scheme" means the schemes of support relating to rural adjustment that are, from time to time, in force under agreements between the Commonwealth and one or more of the States. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - PART 2  PART 2 - THE RURAL ADJUSTMENT SCHEME ADVISORY COUNCIL </name>
</b>** 
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - DIVISION 1  Division 1 - Establishment of the Council </name>
</b>** 
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 5 Establishment of the Council </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   5\. A council called the Rural Adjustment Scheme Advisory Council is established. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 6 How the Council is to be constituted </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   6\. (1) The Council is to consist of:<lf>   (a) a Chairperson; and<lf>   (b) not more than 7 other members. 

<lf>   (2) The members are to be appointed by the Minister, and are to be appointed on a part-time basis. <p><lf>   (3) Of the members referred to in paragraph (1) (b):<lf>   (a) at least one is to be appointed, after consultation with State Ministers, to represent the States; and<lf>   (b) at least one is to be an officer of the Department appointed to represent the Commonwealth; and<lf>   (c) one is to be appointed to represent the National Farmers' Federation; and<lf>   (d) the others are to be appointed because of their expertise in economics, financial administration, banking, sustainable agriculture, regional adjustment, regional development, farm management or training. <p><lf>   (4) The performance of the functions of the Council is not affected by a vacancy in its membership. </lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 7 Period of appointment of members </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   7\. (1) A member holds office for the period, not exceeding 3 years, specified in the instrument of appointment. 

<lf>   (2) A person may, on one occasion only, be re-appointed as a member. </lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - DIVISION 2  Division 2 - Functions of the Council </name>
</b>** 
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 8 Functions of the Council </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   8\. The functions of the Council are:<lf>   (a) to recommend to the Minister, after consultation with all interested parties:<lf>     (i) an ongoing 3 year plan for broad strategic directions for<lf> the Rural Adjustment Scheme; and<lf>     (ii) a proposed annual program and budget for the Rural<lf> Adjustment Scheme; and<lf>   (b) to provide to the Minister a strategic and national view of the operations of the Rural Adjustment Scheme; and<lf>   (ba) to provide to the Minister regional perspectives on the operations of the Rural Adjustment Scheme; and<lf>   (c) to advise the Minister, after consultation with all interested parties, on the appropriate level of funding for the Rural Adjustment Scheme and the appropriate level of funding as between the States under the State component of the Rural Adjustment Scheme; and<lf>   (d) at the request of the Minister, to provide advice to the Minister, after consultation with all interested parties, on matters that the Minister requests advice on and, in particular, whether, under the State component of the Rural Adjustment Scheme, exceptional circumstances exist in relation to the farm sector and, if exceptional circumstances do, in the opinion of the Council, exist, on whether changes should be made to the funding arrangements under the State component of the Rural Adjustment Scheme as a result of those circumstances. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 9 Committees </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   9\. The Council may form specialist committees to assist it in the performance of its functions. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 10 Staff </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   10\. The Chairperson may make arrangements with the Secretary to the Department for the services of officers or employees of the Department to be made available to the Council. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - DIVISION 3  Division 3 - Administrative provisions </name>
</b>** 
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 11 Remuneration and allowances </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   11\. (1) A member is to be paid such remuneration as is determined by the Remuneration Tribunal but, if no determination is in operation, the member is to be paid such remuneration as is prescribed. 

<lf>   (2) A member is to be paid such allowances as are prescribed. <p><lf>   (3) This section has effect subject to the Remuneration Tribunal Act 1973\. <p><lf>   (4) This section does not apply to a Commonwealth member. </lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 12 Terms and conditions of appointment not provided for by Act </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   12\. A member holds office on such terms and conditions (if any) in relation to matters not provided for by this Act as are determined in writing by the Minister. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 13 Leave of absence </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   13\. (1) The Minister may grant leave of absence to the Chairperson on such terms and conditions as the Minister considers appropriate. 

<lf>   (2) The Chairperson may grant leave of absence to another member on such terms and conditions as the Chairperson considers appropriate. </lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 14 Resignation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   14\. A member may resign by giving to the Minister a signed notice of resignation. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 15 Acting appointments </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   15\. (1) If:<lf>   (a) there is a vacancy in the office of Chairperson, whether or not an appointment has previously been made to the office; or<lf>   (b) the Chairperson is absent from duty or from Australia or is otherwise unable to perform the duties of the office of Chairperson;<lf> the Minister may appoint another member to act as the Chairperson during the vacancy, absence or inability. 

<lf>   (2) The Minister may appoint a person to act in the office of a member (other than the Chairperson):<lf>   (a) during a vacancy in the office, whether or not an appointment has previously been made to the office; or<lf>   (b) during any period, or during all periods, when the occupant of the office is absent from duty or from Australia or is otherwise unable to perform the duties of the office, including a period when the member is acting as the Chairperson. <p><lf>   (3) A person appointed to act during a vacancy must not continue to act for more than 12 months. <p><lf>   (4) A person acting in an office under this section is entitled to the same remuneration and allowances that are payable to the holder of the office in lieu of his or her own remuneration and allowances. <p><lf>   (5) Anything done by a person purporting to act under an appointment under this section is not invalid merely because:<lf>   (a) the occasion for the appointment had not arisen; or<lf>   (b) there was a defect or irregularity in connection with the appointment; or<lf>   (c) the appointment had ceased to have effect; or<lf>   (d) the occasion for the person to act had not arisen or had ceased. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 16 Disclosure of interests </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   16\. (1) If:<lf>   (a) a member has a direct or indirect pecuniary interest in a matter being considered, or about to be considered, by the Council; and<lf>   (b) the interest could conflict with the proper performance of the member's duties in relation to the consideration of the matter;<lf> the member must, as soon as practicable after the relevant facts have come to the member's knowledge, disclose the nature of the interest at a meeting of the Council. 

<lf>   (2) A disclosure under subsection (1) must be recorded in the minutes of the meeting. </lf>
</lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 17 Termination of appointment </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   17\. (1) The Minister may terminate the appointment of a member for:<lf>   (a) misbehaviour or physical or mental incapacity; or<lf>   (b) inefficiency or incompetence. 

<lf>   (2) If:<lf>   (a) a member becomes bankrupt, applies to take the benefit of any law for the relief of bankrupt or insolvent debtors, compounds with his or her creditors or makes an assignment of his or her remuneration for their benefit; or<lf>   (b) a member, without reasonable excuse, contravenes section 16; or<lf>   (c) the Chairperson is absent, except with the leave of the Minister, from 3 consecutive meetings of the Council; or<lf>   (d) a member is absent, except with the leave of the Chairperson, from 3 consecutive meetings of the Council;<lf> the Minister may terminate the appointment of the member. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 18 Meetings </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   18\. (1) Subject to subsection (2), meetings of the Council are to be held at such times and places as the Council from time to time determines. 

<lf>   (2) The Chairperson may at any time convene a meeting of the Council. <p><lf>   (3) The Chairperson is to preside at all meetings of the Council at which the Chairperson is present. <p><lf>   (4) If the Chairperson is not present at a meeting of the Council, the members present are to appoint one of their number to preside at the meeting. <p><lf>   (5) At a meeting of the Council:<lf>   (a) 4 members constitute a quorum; and<lf>   (b) a question is to be decided by a majority of the votes of the members present and voting; and<lf>   (c) the member presiding has only a deliberative vote. <p><lf>   (6) The Council must keep minutes of its proceedings. <p><lf>   (7) The Council may invite a person to attend a meeting for the purpose of advising or informing it on any matter. </lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 19 Resolutions without meetings </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   19\. If the Council so determines, a resolution is to be taken to have been passed at a meeting of the Council if:<lf>   (a) without meeting, a majority of the number of members indicate agreement with the resolution in accordance with the method determined by the Council; and<lf>   (b) that majority would, if present at a meeting of the Council, have constituted a quorum under subsection 18 (5); and<lf>   (c) all members were informed of the proposed resolution, or reasonable efforts had been made to inform all members of the proposed resolution. </lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 20 Annual report </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   20\. (1) As soon as practicable after 30 June in each year, the Chairperson must:<lf>   (a) prepare a report on the Council's operations during the year ending on that 30 June; and<lf>   (b) give the report to the Minister.<lf> The report must include a report on the operation of the Rural Adjustment Scheme during the year. 

<lf>   (2) The Minister must cause the report to be tabled in each House of the Parliament within 15 sitting days of that House after the Minister received the report. </lf>
</lf></lf></lf></sect>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - DIVISION 1  Division 1 - Agreements with persons other than States </name>

**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 20A Agreements with persons other than States </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Grants of money<lf>   20A. (1) The Minister may, on behalf of the Commonwealth, enter into a written agreement with a person (other than a State) for the Commonwealth to make one or more grants of money to the person for purposes relating to rural adjustment. A grant is subject to such terms and conditions as are set out in the agreement. 

<lf> Loans of money<lf>   (2) The Minister may, on behalf of the Commonwealth, enter into a written agreement with a person (other than a State) for the Commonwealth to make one or more loans of money (whether secured or unsecured) to the person for purposes relating to rural adjustment. A loan is subject to such terms and conditions as are set out in the agreement. <p><lf> Payments to be made out of separate appropriation<lf>   (3) Payments by the Commonwealth under agreements made under this section are to be made out of money appropriated by the Parliament for the purposes of this section. <p><lf> Performance requirements<lf>   (4) Unless the Minister otherwise determines, an agreement under this section must include provisions relating to the following matters:<lf>   (a) performance requirements;<lf>   (b) outcomes and/or targets;<lf>   (c) performance indicators;<lf>   (d) review and evaluation.<lf> This subsection does not, by implication, limit the generality of subsection (1) or (2). <p><lf> Compliance with section 20B<lf>   (5) This section has effect subject to section 20B. </lf></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf></p></lf></lf>
</lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 20B Memorandum of understanding about section 20A powers </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   20B. (1) The Commonwealth may enter into a memorandum of understanding with a State about the exercise of the powers conferred on the Minister by section 20A. 

<lf>   (2) The Minister may enter into an agreement under section 20A that relates, in whole or in part, to an activity carried on, or proposed to be carried on, in a particular State only if a memorandum of understanding with that State is in force under subsection (1). <p><lf>   (3) In exercising a power conferred on the Minister by section 20A, the Minister must comply with the terms of any applicable memorandum of understanding in force under subsection (1). <p><lf>   (4) A memorandum of understanding under subsection (1) may be set out in the same document as an agreement under section 20E. </lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 20C Delegation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   20C. The Minister may, by writing, delegate all or any of his or her powers under section 20A to an officer of the Department who holds or performs the duties of a Senior Executive Service office. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 20D Repayment of grants and loans, payment of interest etc. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   20D. An amount payable or repayable by a person to the Commonwealth under an agreement under section 20A is a debt due by the person to the Commonwealth. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - DIVISION 2  Division 2 - State component of the Rural Adjustment Scheme </name>
</b>** 
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 20E Agreements with States </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   20E. The Commonwealth may enter into an agreement with a State relating to rural adjustment. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 21 Payments by the Commonwealth under an agreement with a State </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   21\. (1) The payments (including advances) by the Commonwealth to a State provided for in an agreement between the Commonwealth and that State relating to rural adjustment may be made to that State, by way of financial assistance, on the terms and conditions contained in the agreement and out of money appropriated by the Parliament for the purpose. 

<lf>   (2) Subject to subsections (3) and (4), the financial assistance is to be provided on the basis that the Commonwealth contributes 90%, and the State 10%, of the assistance provided to farmers under the State component of the Rural Adjustment Scheme. <p><lf>   (3) Subject to subsection (4), in a case where interest subsidies are provided to farmers under the State component of the Rural Adjustment Scheme to overcome difficulties due to exceptional circumstances, the Commonwealth and the State are to share equally the costs of so much of those subsidies as exceeds 50% of the interest and associated costs of the loans. <p><lf>   (4) The Commonwealth and a State may jointly determine that subsection (2) or (3), as the case requires, does not apply in relation to a particular matter arising under an agreement. </lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 21A Performance requirements </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   21A. (1) Unless the Minister otherwise determines, an agreement under this Division must include provisions relating to the following matters:<lf>   (a) performance requirements;<lf>   (b) outcomes and/or targets;<lf>   (c) performance indicators;<lf>   (d) review and evaluation. 

<lf>   (2) The Minister may, by writing, delegate all or any of his or her powers under subsection (1) to an officer of the Department who holds or performs the duties of a Senior Executive Service office. <p><lf>   (3) This section does not, by implication, limit the generality of section 20E or 21\. </lf></p></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 21B Repayment of grants etc. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   21B. An amount repayable by a State to the Commonwealth under an agreement under this Division is a debt due by the State to the Commonwealth. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - PART 4  PART 4 - SUPPORT TO BE INALIENABLE </name>

**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 22 Support under the Rural Adjustment Scheme to be inalienable </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   22\. Support provided directly to a farmer under the Rural Adjustment Scheme is to be inalienable, whether by way of or in consequence of sale, assignment, execution, charge, bankruptcy or otherwise. </sect>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 22A Other payment powers not limited </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   22A. This Act does not, by implication, limit the power of the Commonwealth to make payments otherwise than under this Act. </sect>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - PART 6  PART 6 - REGULATIONS </name>
</b>** 
**<b>

### <name>RURAL ADJUSTMENT ACT 1992 - SECT 23 Regulations </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   23\. The Governor-General may make regulations prescribing matters:<lf>   (a) required or permitted to be prescribed by this Act; or<lf>   (b) necessary or convenient to be prescribed for carrying out or giving effect to this Act. </lf></lf></sect>
