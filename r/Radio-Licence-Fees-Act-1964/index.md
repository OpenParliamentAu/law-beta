### <name>RADIO LICENCE FEES ACT 1964 - LONG TITLE </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>           An Act to provide for the payment of fees in respect<lf>                        of certain radio licences<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>RADIO LICENCE FEES ACT 1964 - SECT 1 Short title </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   1\. This Act may be cited as the Radio Licence Fees Act 1964.*1* SEE NOTES TO FIRST ARTICLE OF THIS CHAPTER . </sect>
**<b>

### <name>RADIO LICENCE FEES ACT 1964 - SECT 2 Commencement </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   2\. This Act shall come into operation on the day on which it receives the Royal Assent.*1* SEE NOTES TO FIRST ARTICLE OF THIS CHAPTER . </sect>
**<b>

### <name>RADIO LICENCE FEES ACT 1964 - SECT 3 Incorporation of Broadcasting Act </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   3\. The Broadcasting Services Act 1992 is incorporated and shall be read as one with this Act. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>RADIO LICENCE FEES ACT 1964 - SECT 4 Interpretation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   4\. (1) In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:<lf>   "FM access fee" means the fee payable in respect of a licence under section 6B;<lf>   "gross earnings", in respect of a licence in respect of a period, means the gross earnings of the licensee during that period from the broadcasting, pursuant to the licence, of advertisements or other material;<lf>   "licence" means a commercial radio broadcasting licence referred to in paragraph 5 (1) (b), (d) or (f) of the Transitional Provisions Act;<lf>   "Transitional Provisions Act" means the  Broadcasting Services (Transitional Provisions and Consequential Amendments) Act 1992\. 

  (2) Where, in connexion with any transaction, any consideration is paid or given otherwise than in cash, the money value of that consideration shall, for the purposes of this Act, be deemed to have been paid or given. 
</lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>RADIO LICENCE FEES ACT 1964 - SECT 5 Licence fees </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   5\. There is payable to the Commonwealth by a licensee, by way of tax in respect of his licence, fees in accordance with sections 6 and 6A. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>RADIO LICENCE FEES ACT 1964 - SECT 6 Amount of fees </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   6\. (1) There is payable on the grant of a licence, not being a grant by way of the renewal of a licence, a fee of $500\. 

  (2) Subject to section 6A, there is payable by a licensee:<lf>   (a) on each 31 December that occurs during the period of the licence; and<lf>   (b) on:<lf>     (i) if the licence's period ends on a 31 December or a day<lf> within the first 6 months of a calendar year - the first 31 December after the licence's period; or<lf>     (ii) if the licence's period ends on any other day in a<lf> calendar year - each 31 December that occurs during the 18 months immediately following the licence's period;<lf> a fee of an amount equal to the relevant percentage of the gross earnings in respect of the licence during the period of one year ending on the 30 June last preceding the 31 December. <p>  (2A) In subsection (2), "relevant percentage", in relation to the gross earnings in respect of a licence during a period, means:<lf>   (a) where those gross earnings are less than $5,000,000 - the percentage ascertained in accordance with the formula:<lf>            0.25 + (0.3 x     A    )<lf>                   (      1,000,000);<lf>   (b) where those gross earnings are not less than $5,000,000 but are less than $6,000,000 - the percentage ascertained in accordance with the formula:<lf>            1.75 + (0.45 x A - 5,000,000)<lf>                   (         1,000,000  );<lf>   (c) where those gross earnings are not less than $6,000,000 but are less than $7,000,000 - the percentage ascertained in accordance with the formula:<lf>            2.2 + (0.4 x A - 6,000,000)<lf>                  (        1,000,000  );<lf>   (d) where those gross earnings are not less than $7,000,000 but are less than $10,000,000 - the percentage ascertained in accordance with the formula:<lf>            2.65 + (0.15 x A - 7,000,000)<lf>                   (         1,000,000  );<lf>   (e) where those gross earnings are not less than $10,000,000 - whichever is the lesser of 3.25% or the percentage ascertained in accordance with the formula:<lf>            3.1 + (0.1 x A - 10,000,000)<lf>                  (        1,000,000   );<lf> where A is the number of dollars in those gross earnings. <p>  (3) If a licensee:<lf>   (a) had, before the commencement of the Transitional Provisions Act, in accordance with the Broadcasting Act 1942; or<lf>   (b) has, in accordance with subsection 123 (2) of the Broadcasting Act 1942 as applied by section 22 of the Transitional Provisions Act;<lf> adopted an accounting period ending on a day other than 30 June, the reference in subsection (2) of this section to 30 June shall, in relation to the licence, be read as a reference to that other day. <p>  (4) Where the amount of the gross earnings in respect of a licence during a period consists of a number of dollars and a number of cents, then, for the purposes of this section, the cents shall be disregarded. </p><p>  (5) For the purposes of subsection (2), the period of a licence shall be taken to include any period of renewal or further renewal of the licence. </p></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>

and<lf>   (c) by reason of a change in the accounting period used by the licensee, the second fee period commences before or does not commence immediately after the end of the first fee period;<lf> section 6 applies in relation to the fee payable on the anniversary referred to in paragraph (b) as if:<lf>   (d) references in that section to a period of one year ending on a day were references to the period commencing immediately after the end of the first fee period and ending on the day on which the second fee period ends; ### <name>RADIO LICENCE FEES ACT 1964 - SECT 6B FM access fees </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   6B. If a person is given:<lf>   (a) a notice under section 83G of the Broadcasting Act in respect of the granting of a non-metropolitan FM commercial radio licence within the meaning of Part IIIB of the Broadcasting Act; or<lf>   (b) a notice under section 83H of that Act in respect of the granting of a non-metropolitan supplementary radio licence within the meaning of that Part; or<lf>   (c) a notice under section 83J of that Act in respect of the conversion to FM of a non-metropolitan AM commercial radio licence within the meaning of that Part;<lf> then, on the day the notice is given, there is payable to the Commonwealth by that person, by way of tax in respect of the licence, a fee, to be known as an FM access fee, of an amount determined under section 6BA and specified in the notice. </lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>RADIO LICENCE FEES ACT 1964 - SECT 6BA Amount of FM access fee </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   6BA. (1) In this section:<lf>   "area licence", in relation to a proposed licence, means a commercial radio licence, or a supplementary radio licence, whose service area is substantially the same as the service area of the proposed licence and, if the proposed licence is a converted licence, includes the proposed licence;<lf>   "commencement day" means:<lf>   (a) in relation to a licence or proposed licence, other than a converted licence - the day on which the service provided under the licence commences in accordance with a determination made under section 96 of the Broadcasting Act; or<lf>   (b) in relation to a converted licence - the day on which the service authorised by the licence's varied licence warrant commences in accordance with a determination made under section 96 of the Broadcasting Act;<lf>   "converted licence" means a licence described in paragraph 6B (c);<lf>   "declared gross earnings", in relation to a licence, means the amount of annual gross earnings for that licence stated in an earnings declaration;<lf>   "declared most recent gross earnings", in relation to a licence, means the declared gross earnings for the licence in respect of the most recent 12 months for which the holder of the licence is required to give an earnings declaration;<lf>   "declared next most recent gross earnings", in relation to a licence, means the declared gross earnings for the licence in respect of the 12 months immediately before the most recent 12 months for which the holder of the licence is required to give an earnings declaration;<lf>   "earnings declaration" means a statutory declaration given to the Tribunal under paragraph 123 (1) (c) of the Broadcasting Act;<lf>   "existing licence", in relation to a proposed licence, means an area licence, other than an area licence whose commencement date, in the opinion of the Tribunal, is likely to be within the 28 days immediately before the proposed licence's commencement day;<lf>   "most recent census results" means the results of the most recent census count of the Australian population taken and published by the Australian Statistician;<lf>   "new licence" means a licence described in paragraph 6B (a);<lf>   "prescribed time" means:<lf>   (a) in relation to a proposed licence that is a new licence - the time at which a notice under subsection 82 (1) of the Broadcasting Act was published in the Gazette to invite interested persons to apply for the grant of the licence; or<lf>   (b) in relation to a proposed licence that is a supplementary licence - the time at which the Minister referred the application for the licence to the Tribunal under paragraph 82A (4) (a) of the Broadcasting Act; or<lf>   (c) in relation to a proposed licence that is a converted licence - the time at which the Minister gave a notice to the Tribunal in respect of the conversion under subsection 89D (6A) of the Broadcasting Act;<lf>   "proposed licence" means a licence in respect of which an FM access fee is to be payable;<lf>   "supplementary licence" means a licence referred to in paragraph 6B (b). 

  (2) For the purposes of this section, the service area of a supplementary radio licence is taken to be substantially the same as another licence's service area if it is smaller than, but wholly within the other licence's service area. 

  (3) The Tribunal must determine, in writing, the amount of FM access fee in respect of a proposed licence in accordance with the formula:<lf>       (AGE + ((  AGE  )2 x 1,000)) x NPL<lf>       (20    ((200,000)         ))   NCL;<lf> where:<lf>   "AGE" (Average Gross Earnings) is the amount determined under subsection (4) or (7);<lf>   "NPL" (Number of Pre-commencement Licences) is:<lf>   (a) if, in the Tribunal's opinion, there is at least one existing licence under which service will be provided immediately before the day that, in the Tribunal's opinion, is likely to be the proposed licence's commencement day - the number of those existing licences; or<lf>   (b) if there are no such existing licences - 1;<lf>   "NCL" (Number of Commencement Licences) is:<lf>   (a) if the proposed licence is not a converted licence - the sum of 1 plus the number that, in the Tribunal's opinion, is likely to be the number of area licences under which services will be provided on, or within 28 days after, the day that, in the Tribunal's opinion, is likely to be the proposed licence's commencement day; or<lf>   (b) if the proposed licence is a converted licence - subject to subsection (6), the number that, in the Tribunal's opinion, is likely to be the number of area licences under which services will be provided on, or within 28 days after, the day that, in the Tribunal's opinion, is likely to be the proposed licence's commencement day. <p>  (4) If, at the prescribed time for the proposed licence, there is at least one area licence, AGE is the amount determined by the Tribunal in accordance with the formula:<lf>          TGE<lf>          NPL;<lf> where:<lf>   "TGE" (Total Gross Earnings) is the sum of the calculation gross earnings of the area licences;<lf>   "NPL" (Number of Pre-commencement Licences) has the same meaning as in subsection (3). <p>  (5) In subsection (4):<lf>   "calculation gross earnings", in relation to an area licence, means:<lf>   (a) if there are declared most recent gross earnings for the licence - the amount of those earnings; or<lf>   (b) if there are no such earnings - an amount determined by the Tribunal to be the likely amount of the declared most recent gross earnings for the licence, having regard to either or both of the following:<lf>     (i) any declared next most recent gross earnings for the<lf> licence;<lf>     (ii) any declared most recent gross earnings, or any declared<lf> next most recent gross earnings, for any other area licences. <p>  (6) For the purposes of subsection (3), if:<lf>   (a) the proposed licence is a converted licence; and<lf>   (b) the Tribunal is satisfied that, but for this subsection, NCL should be 1; and<lf>   (c) there is at least one commercial FM radio licence or supplementary radio licence whose service area overlaps the proposed licence's service area (in this subsection called an "overlapping licence");<lf> NCL is to be 1 plus, in respect of each overlapping licence, a number that is determined by the Tribunal in accordance with the formula:<lf>            OP<lf>            TP;<lf> where:<lf>   "OP" (Overlapping Population) is the population, in the Tribunal's opinion, of the proposed licence's service area that is overlapped by the service area of the overlapping licence, having regard to the most recent census results; and<lf>   "TP" (Total Population) is the population, in the Tribunal's opinion, of the proposed licence's service area, having regard to the most recent census results. <p>  (7) If, at the prescribed time for the proposed licence, there is no area licence, AGE is the amount determined by the Tribunal in accordance with the formula:<lf>             NGE<lf>             NPL;<lf> where:<lf>   "NGE" (Notional Gross Earnings) is the amount determined by the Tribunal as what, if there had been an area licence, would have been the amount of the declared most recent gross earnings for the area licence, having regard to:<lf>   (a) the declared most recent gross earnings, or declared next most recent gross earnings, for any radio licences whose service areas overlap the proposed licence's service area; and<lf>   (b) the declared most recent gross earnings, or declared next most recent gross earnings, for any radio licence whose service area is of a similar size, or has a similar level of population, as that of the proposed licence's service area; and<lf>   (c) such other matters as the Tribunal considers relevant;<lf>   "NPL" (Number of Pre-commencement Licences) has the same meaning as in subsection (3). <p>  (8) In deciding NPL or NCL for the purposes of this section, the Tribunal may have regard to the following matters:<lf>   (a) the number of existing area licences;<lf>   (b) the likely grant by the Tribunal of any area licences;<lf>   (c) the likely commencement date of the proposed licence and licences mentioned in paragraph (b);<lf>   (d) any other matters that the Tribunal considers relevant. <p>  (9) Nothing in this section empowers the Tribunal to make a determination amending or in substitution for a determination made under subsection (3). </p></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>

### <name>RADIO LICENCE FEES ACT 1964 - SECT 6C Fee in respect of licence on conversion from AM to FM as part of the National Metropolitan Radio Plan </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   6C. (1) Where the Minister converts an AM commercial radio licence to FM pursuant to an application made under section 89DAE of the Broadcasting Act, there is payable in respect of the licence, upon the conversion, a fee of an amount equal to the amount ascertained in accordance with the formula:<lf>                    B - V<lf> where:<lf>   B is the amount of the bid made by the licensee, pursuant to paragraph 89DAE (2) (c) of the Broadcasting Act, for conversion of the licence to FM; and<lf>   V is the transmission facilities value. 

  (2) The amount of the deposit paid by the licensee pursuant to subsection 89DAE (8) of the Broadcasting Act in relation to the application shall be set off against the licensee's liability for the fee imposed by subsection (1). 
</lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>RADIO LICENCE FEES ACT 1964 - SECT 7 Earnings of subsidiary companies etc. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   7\. Where the Minister is of the opinion that:<lf>   (a) an amount, or part of an amount, earned during any period by a person other than a licensee would, if the licensee and that person were the same person, form part of the gross earnings in respect of the licence in respect of that period for the purposes of this Act; and<lf>   (b) a relationship exists between the licensee and the other person (whether by reason of any shareholding or of any agreement or arrangement, or for any other reason) of such a kind that the amount or the part of the amount, as the case may be, should, for the purposes of this Act, be treated as part of the gross earnings in respect of the licence in respect of that period;<lf> the Minister may direct that the amount or the part of the amount, as the case may be, shall be so treated. </lf></lf></lf></sect>
