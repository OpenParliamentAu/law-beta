###  ROYAL COMMISSIONS ACT 1902- SECT 1  Short title [_see_ Note 1] 
<dl compact="">

		This Act may be cited as the _Royal Commissions Act 1902_.

 </dl>
###  ROYAL COMMISSIONS ACT 1902- SECT 1A  Power to issue Royal Commission 
<dl compact="">

		Without in any way prejudicing, limiting, or derogating from the power of the

King, or of the Governor-General, to make or authorize any inquiry, or to issue

any commission to make any inquiry, it is hereby enacted and declared that the

Governor-General may, by Letters Patent in the name of the King, issue such

commissions, directed to such person or persons, as he thinks fit, requiring or

authorizing him or them or any of them to make inquiry into and report upon any

matter specified in the Letters Patent, and which relates to or is connected

with the peace, order, and good government of the Commonwealth, or any public

purpose or any power of the Commonwealth.

 </dl>
###  ROYAL COMMISSIONS ACT 1902- SECT 1B  Definitions 
<dl compact="">

		In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:

 </dl>
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_Commission_ and _Royal Commission_ means any

Commission of inquiry issued by the Governor-General by Letters Patent in

pursuance of this Act or of any other power, and includes the members of the

Commission, or a quorum thereof, or the sole Commissioner, sitting for the

purposes of the inquiry. _document_ includes any book, register or other record of information, however compiled, recorded or stored. _legal practitioner_ means a barrister, a solicitor, a barrister and solicitor, or a legal practitioner, of the High Court or of the Supreme Court of a State or Territory. _member_, in relation to a Commission, means:  </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>in the case of a Commission constituted by one person&#151;that person; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>in the case of a Commission constituted by 2 or more persons&#151;each of

those persons.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_reasonable excuse_ in relation to any act or omission by a witness

or a person summoned as a witness before a Commission means an excuse which

would excuse an act or omission of a similar nature by a witness or a person

summoned as a witness before a court of law. _relevant Commission_ means a Commission established by Letters Patent that declare that the Commission is a relevant Commission for the purposes of the provision in which the expression appears.  </dl></dl>

###  ROYAL COMMISSIONS ACT 1902- SECT 2  Power to summon witnesses and take evidence 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>A member of a Commission may summon a person to appear before the

Commission at a hearing to do either or both of the following:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>to give evidence;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>to produce the documents, or other things, specified in the summons.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>The member of a Commission presiding at a hearing of the Commission may

require a person appearing at the hearing to produce a document or other

thing.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>A Commission may, at a hearing, take evidence on oath or affirmation and for that purpose: </dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a member of the Commission may require a person appearing at the hearing

to give evidence either to take an oath or to make an affirmation in a form

approved by the member of the Commission presiding at the hearing; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>a member of the Commission, or a person who is an authorized person in

relation to the Commission, may administer an oath or affirmation to a person

so appearing at the hearing.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(3A)</dt><dd>A member of a Commission may, by written notice served (as prescribed) on

a person, require the person to produce a document or thing specified in the

notice to a person, and at the time and place, specified in the notice.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>In this section, a reference to a person who is an authorized person in relation to a Commission is a reference to a person authorized in writing, or a person included in a class of persons authorized in writing, for the purposes of this section by the President or Chairman of that Commission, or by the sole Commissioner, as the case may be. </dd> </dl></dl>

###  ROYAL COMMISSIONS ACT 1902- SECT 3  Failure of witnesses to attend or produce documents 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>A person served, as prescribed, with a summons to appear as a witness at a

hearing before a Commission shall not:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>fail to attend as required by the summons; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>fail to attend from day to day unless excused, or released from further

attendance, by a member of the Commission.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

Penalty:	$1,000 or imprisonment for 6 months.

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1A)</dt><dd>Subsection&#160;(1) is an offence of strict liability.

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Note:	For strict liability, see section&#160;6.1 of the

_Criminal Code_.

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1B)</dt><dd>Subsection&#160;(1) does not apply if the person has a reasonable

excuse.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>A person appearing as a witness at a hearing before a Commission shall not fail to produce a document or other thing that the person was required to produce by a summons under this Act served on him or her as prescribed or that the person was required to produce by the member of the Commission presiding at the hearing. </dd> </dl></dl>

Penalty:	$1,000 or imprisonment for 6 months.

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2A)</dt><dd>Subsection&#160;(2) is an offence of strict liability.</dd> <dt>(2B)</dt><dd>Subsection&#160;(2) does not apply if the person has a reasonable excuse.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>It is a defence to a prosecution for an offence against subsection&#160;(2) constituted by a failure to produce a document or other thing to a Commission if the document or other thing was not relevant to the matters into which the Commission was inquiring. </dd> </dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Note:	A defendant bears an evidential burden in relation to the

matters in subsections&#160;(1B), (2B) and (3) (see subsection 13.3(3) of the

_Criminal Code_).

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(4)</dt><dd>A person served with a notice under subsection 2(3A) must not refuse or

fail to produce a document or other thing that the person was required to

produce in accordance with the notice.

</dd> </dl></dl>

Penalty:	$1,000 or imprisonment for 6 months.

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(5)</dt><dd>Subsection&#160;(4) does not apply if the person has a reasonable

excuse.</dd> <dt>(6)</dt><dd>It is a defence to a prosecution for an offence against subsection&#160;(4) constituted by a refusal or failure to produce a document or other thing if the document or other thing was not relevant to the matters into which the Commission was inquiring. </dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Note:	A defendant bears an evidential burden in relation to the

matters in subsections&#160;(5) and (6) (see subsection 13.3(3) of the

_Criminal Code_).

 </dl></dl>

###  ROYAL COMMISSIONS ACT 1902- SECT 4  Search warrants 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1A)</dt><dd>A relevant Commission may authorise:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a member of the relevant Commission; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>a member of the Australian Federal Police, or of the Police Force of a

State or Territory, who is assisting the relevant Commission;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

to apply for search warrants under subsection&#160;(3) in relation to matters into which the relevant Commission is inquiring. The authorisation must be in writing.

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Where:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a relevant Commission, or a person authorised by a relevant Commission

under subsection&#160;(1A), has reasonable grounds for suspecting that there

may be, at that time or within the next following 24 hours, upon any land or

upon or in any premises, vessel, aircraft or vehicle, a thing or things of a

particular kind connected with a matter into which the relevant Commission is

inquiring (in this section referred to as _things of the relevant kind_); and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the relevant Commission, or the person, believes on reasonable grounds

that, if a summons were issued for the production of the thing or things, the

thing or things might be concealed, lost, mutilated or destroyed;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

the relevant Commission, or the person, may apply to a Judge of a prescribed court for the issue of a search warrant under subsection&#160;(3).

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>Where an application under subsection&#160;(1) is made to a Judge of a

prescribed court, the Judge may, if he or she is satisfied that there are

reasonable grounds for issuing the warrant, issue a search warrant authorizing

a member of the Australian Federal Police or of the Police Force of a State or

of the Northern Territory, or any other person, named in the warrant, with such

assistance as the member or person thinks necessary and if necessary by force:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>to enter upon the land or upon or into the premises, vessel, aircraft or

vehicle;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>to search the land, premises, vessel, aircraft or vehicle for things of

the relevant kind; and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>to seize any things of the relevant kind found upon the land or upon or in

the premises, vessel, aircraft or vehicle and deliver things so seized to the

relevant Commission.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(4)</dt><dd>There shall be stated in a warrant issued under this section:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a statement of the purpose for which the warrant is issued, which shall

include a reference to the matter into which the relevant Commission is

inquiring and with which the things of the relevant kind are connected;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>whether entry is authorized to be made at any time of the day or night or

during specified hours of the day or night;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>a description of the kind of things authorized to be seized; and</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>a date, not being later than one month after the date of issue of the

warrant, upon which the warrant ceases to have effect.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(5)</dt><dd>If, in the course of searching, in accordance with a warrant issued under

this section, for things of a particular kind connected with a matter into

which a relevant Commission is inquiring, the person executing the warrant

finds:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>any thing of another kind that he or she believes on reasonable grounds to

be connected with that matter; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>any thing that he or she believes on reasonable grounds to be connected

with another matter into which the relevant Commission is inquiring;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

and he or she believes on reasonable grounds that it is necessary to seize that thing in order to prevent its concealment, loss, mutilation or destruction, the warrant shall be deemed to authorize the person to seize that thing.

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(6)</dt><dd>A reference in this section to a Judge of a prescribed court shall be

construed as a reference to:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a Judge of the Federal Court of Australia; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>a Judge of a court of a State or Territory.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(7)</dt><dd>In this section, _thing_ includes a document.

</dd> </dl></dl>

**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

because of circumstances of urgency.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Where a Judge issues a search warrant upon an application made by telephone, the Judge shall: </dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>complete and sign that warrant;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>inform the applicant of the terms of the warrant and the date on which and

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>forward a copy of the warrant to the applicant.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>Where a search warrant is issued upon an application made by telephone, a

###  ROYAL COMMISSIONS ACT 1902- SECT 6  Penalty for refusing to be sworn or to give evidence 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>If any person appearing as a witness before the Commission refuses to be

sworn or to make an affirmation or to answer any question relevant to the

guilty of an offence.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The penalty for an offence under subsection&#160;(1) is a fine not exceeding $1,000 or imprisonment for a period not exceeding 6&#160;months.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>Subsection&#160;(1) is an offence of strict liability. </dd> </dl></dl>

###  ROYAL COMMISSIONS ACT 1902- SECT 6A  Self incrimination 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>It is not a reasonable excuse for the purposes of subsection 3(2B) or (5)

for a natural person to refuse or fail to produce a document or other thing

that the production of the document or other thing might tend to:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>incriminate the person; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>make the person liable to a penalty.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>A natural person is not excused from answering a question that the person

is required to answer by a member of a Commission on the ground that answering

the question might tend to:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>incriminate the person; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>make the person liable to a penalty.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>Subsections&#160;(1) and (2) do not apply to the production of a document

or other thing, or the answer to a question, if:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the production or answer might tend to incriminate the person in relation

to an offence; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the person has been charged with that offence; and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>the charge has not been finally dealt with by a court or otherwise

disposed of.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(4)</dt><dd>Subsections&#160;(1) and (2) do not apply to the production of a document

or other thing, or the answer to a question, if:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the production or answer might tend to make the person liable to a

penalty; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>proceedings in respect of the penalty have commenced; and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>those proceedings have not been finally dealt with by a court or otherwise

disposed of.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

###  ROYAL COMMISSIONS ACT 1902- SECT 6B  Arrest of witness failing to appear 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>If any person served with a summons to attend a Royal Commission as a

witness fails to attend the Commission in answer to the summons, the President

or Chairman may, on proof by statutory declaration of the service of the

summons, issue a warrant for the person's apprehension.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The warrant shall authorize the apprehension of the witness and the witness being brought before the Commission, and the witness' detention in custody for that purpose until he or she is released by order of the President or Chairman.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>The warrant may be executed by any member of the Australian Federal Police or of the Police Force of a State or of the Northern Territory, or by any person to whom it is addressed, and the person executing it shall have power to break and enter any place building or vessel for the purpose of executing it.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>The apprehension of any witness under this section shall not relieve him or her from any liability incurred by the witness by reason of his or her non-compliance with the summons. </dd> </dl></dl>

###  ROYAL COMMISSIONS ACT 1902- SECT 6C  Acts or omissions on different days to constitute separate offences 
**

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		Where any person has on any day done or omitted to do something, and the

person's act or omission amounts to an offence against section 3 or 6, and does

or omits to do the same thing at any meeting of the Commission held on some

other day, each such act or omission shall be a separate offence.

###  ROYAL COMMISSIONS ACT 1902- SECT 6D  Rights of witness 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Nothing in this Act shall make it compulsory for any witness before a

Royal Commission to disclose to the Commission any secret process of

manufacture.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>If any witness before a Royal Commission requests that the witness' evidence relating to a particular subject be taken in private on the ground that the evidence relates to the profits or financial position of any person, and that the taking of the evidence in public would be unfairly prejudicial to the interests of that person, the Commission may, if it thinks proper, take that evidence in private, and no person who is not expressly authorized by the Commission to be present shall be present during the taking of that evidence.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>The Commission may direct that: </dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>any evidence given before it;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the contents of any document, or a description of any thing:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>produced before, or delivered to, the Commission; or</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>produced under a notice under subsection 2(3A); or

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>any information that might enable a person who has given evidence before

the Commission to be identified;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

shall not be published, or shall not be published except in such manner, and to such persons, as the Commission specifies.

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(4)</dt><dd>A person who makes any publication in contravention of any direction given

under subsection&#160;(3) is guilty of an offence punishable, upon summary

conviction, by a fine not exceeding $2,000 or imprisonment for a period not

exceeding 12 months.</dd> <dt>(5)</dt><dd>This section shall be read as in aid of and not as in derogation of the Commission's general powers to order that any evidence may be taken in private. </dd> </dl></dl>

###  ROYAL COMMISSIONS ACT 1902- SECT 6FA  Examination of witnesses by counsel etc. 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		Any legal practitioner appointed by the Attorney-General to assist a

Commission, any person authorized by a Commission to appear before it, or any

legal practitioner authorized by a Commission to appear before it for the

purpose of representing any person, may, so far as the Commission thinks

proper, examine or cross-examine any witness on any matter which the Commission

deems relevant to the inquiry, and any witness so examined or cross-examined

shall have the same protection and be subject to the same liabilities as if

examined by any of the Commissioners, or by the sole Commissioner, as the case

may be.

 </dl></dl>

###  ROYAL COMMISSIONS ACT 1902- SECT 6G  Witness to be paid expenses 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Any witness appearing before a Royal Commission shall be paid a reasonable

sum for the expenses of his or her attendance in accordance with the prescribed

scale.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>In the absence of a prescribed scale, the President or Chairman of the Commission, or the sole Commissioner, may authorize the payment of such sum as he or she deems reasonable. </dd> </dl></dl>

###  ROYAL COMMISSIONS ACT 1902- SECT 6H  False or misleading evidence 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>A person shall not, at a hearing before a Commission, intentionally give

evidence that the person knows to be false or misleading with respect to any

matter, being a matter that is material to the inquiry being made by the

Commission.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>An offence against subsection&#160;(1) is an indictable offence and, subject to this section, is punishable on conviction by imprisonment for a period not exceeding 5 years or by a fine not exceeding $20,000.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>Notwithstanding that an offence against subsection&#160;(1) is an indictable offence, a court of summary jurisdiction may hear and determine proceedings in respect of such an offence if the court is satisfied that it is proper to do so and the defendant and the prosecutor consent.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>Where, in accordance with subsection&#160;(3), a court of summary jurisdiction convicts a person of an offence against subsection&#160;(1), the penalty that the court may impose is a fine not exceeding $2,000 or imprisonment for a period not exceeding 12 months. </dd> </dl></dl>

###  ROYAL COMMISSIONS ACT 1902- SECT 6I  Bribery of witness <center>[Back to Top](#top)</center>

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Any person who:

</dd> </dl></dl>

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

shall be guilty of an indictable offence.

Penalty:	Imprisonment for five years.

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>Any person who:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>gives, confers, or procures, or promises or offers to give or confer, or

to procure or attempt to procure, any property or benefit of any kind to, upon,

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>attempts by any means to induce any person who is required to produce a

document or other thing pursuant to a summons, requirement or notice under

section&#160;2 not to comply with the requirement; or</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>asks, receives or obtains, or agrees to receive or obtain any property or

benefit of any kind for himself, or any other person, upon any agreement or

understanding that any person who is required to produce a document or other

thing pursuant to a summons, requirement or notice under section&#160;2 will

not comply with the requirement;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

is guilty of an indictable offence.

Penalty:	Imprisonment for 5 years. 

**

###  ROYAL COMMISSIONS ACT 1902- SECT 6J  Fraud on witness 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Any person who practises any fraud or deceit, or intentionally makes or

exhibits any statement, representation, token, or writing, knowing it to be

false, to any person called or to be called as a witness before any Royal

Commission with intent to affect the testimony of that person as a witness,

shall be guilty of an indictable offence.

</dd> </dl></dl>

Penalty:	Imprisonment for two years.

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>Any person who practises any fraud or deceit, or intentionally makes or

exhibits any statement, representation, token, or writing, knowing it to be

false, to any person with intent that any person who is required to produce a

document or other thing pursuant to a summons, requirement or notice under

section&#160;2 will not comply with the requirement, is guilty of an indictable

offence.

</dd> </dl></dl>

Penalty:	Imprisonment for 2 years. 

**

###  ROYAL COMMISSIONS ACT 1902- SECT 6K  Destroying documents or other things 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>A person commits an offence if:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the person acts or omits to act; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the act or omission results in a document or other thing being:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>concealed, mutilated or destroyed; or</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>rendered incapable of identification; or</dd>

<dt>(iii)</dt><dd>in the case of a document, rendered illegible or indecipherable; and

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>the person knows, or is reckless as to whether, the document or thing is

one that:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>is or may be required in evidence before a Commission; or</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>a person has been, or is likely to be, required to produce pursuant to a

summons, requirement or notice under section&#160;2.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>An offence under subsection&#160;(1) is an indictable offence and, subject

to this section, is punishable on conviction by imprisonment for a period not

exceeding 2 years or by a fine not exceeding $10,000.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>Notwithstanding that an offence under subsection&#160;(1) is an indictable offence, a court of summary jurisdiction may hear and determine proceedings in respect of such an offence if the court is satisfied that it is proper to do so and the defendant and the prosecutor consent.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>Where, in accordance with subsection&#160;(3), a court of summary jurisdiction convicts a person of an offence under subsection&#160;(1), the penalty that the court may impose is a fine not exceeding $2,000 or imprisonment for a period not exceeding 12 months. </dd> </dl></dl>

**

###  ROYAL COMMISSIONS ACT 1902- SECT 6L  Preventing witness from attending 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Any person who intentionally prevents any person who has been summoned to

attend as a witness before any Royal Commission from attending as a witness or

from producing anything in evidence pursuant to the summons to attend shall be

guilty of an indictable offence.

</dd> </dl></dl>

Penalty:	Imprisonment for one year.

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>Any person who intentionally prevents any person who is required to

produce a document or other thing pursuant to a notice under subsection 2(3A)

from producing that document or thing in accordance with the notice is guilty

of an indictable offence.

</dd> </dl></dl>

Penalty:	Imprisonment for 1 year. 

**

###  ROYAL COMMISSIONS ACT 1902- SECT 6M  Injury to witness 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		Any person who uses, causes or inflicts, any violence, punishment, damage,

loss, or disadvantage to any person for or on account of:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the person having appeared as a witness before any Royal Commission; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>any evidence given by him or her before any Royal Commission; or</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>the person having produced a document or thing pursuant to a summons,

requirement or notice under section&#160;2;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

is guilty of an indictable offence.

Penalty:	$1,000, or imprisonment for 1 year. 

**

###  ROYAL COMMISSIONS ACT 1902- SECT 6N  Dismissal by employers of witness 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Any employer who dismisses any employee from his or her employment, or

prejudices any employee in his or her employment, for or on account of the

employee having:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>appeared as a witness before a Royal Commission; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>given evidence before a Royal Commission; or</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>produced a document or thing pursuant to a summons, requirement or notice

under section&#160;2;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

is guilty of an indictable offence.

Penalty:	$1,000, or imprisonment for 1 year.

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>Subsection&#160;(1) does not apply if the employee was dismissed or

prejudiced in his or her employment for some reason other than the reasons

mentioned in subsection&#160;(1).

</dd> </dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Note:	A defendant bears an evidential burden in relation to the

matters in subsection&#160;(2) (see subsection 13.3(3) of the _Criminal Code_).

 </dl></dl>

**

###  ROYAL COMMISSIONS ACT 1902- SECT 6O  Contempt of Royal Commission 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Any person who intentionally insults or disturbs a Royal Commission, or

interrupts the proceedings of a Royal Commission, or uses any insulting

language towards a Royal Commission, or by writing or speech uses words false

and defamatory of a Royal Commission, or is in any manner guilty of any

intentional contempt of a Royal Commission, shall be guilty of an offence.

</dd> </dl></dl>

Penalty:	Two hundred dollars, or imprisonment for three months.

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>If the President or Chairman of a Royal Commission or the sole

Commissioner is a Justice of the High Court, or a Judge of any other Federal

Court, of the Supreme Court of a Territory or of the Supreme Court or County

Court or District Court of a State, he or she shall, in relation to any offence

against subsection&#160;(1) of this section committed in the face of the

Commission, have all the powers of a Justice of the High Court sitting in open

Court in relation to a contempt committed in face of the Court, except that any

punishment inflicted shall not exceed the punishment provided by

subsection&#160;(1) of this section.

</dd> </dl></dl>

**

###  ROYAL COMMISSIONS ACT 1902- SECT 6P  Commission may communicate information 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Where, in the course of inquiring into a matter, a Commission obtains

information that relates, or that may relate, to a contravention of a law, or

evidence of a contravention of a law, of the Commonwealth, of a State or of a

Territory, the Commission may, if in the opinion of the Commission it is

appropriate so to do, communicate the information or furnish the evidence, as

the case may be, to:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the Attorney-General of the Commonwealth, of a State, of the Australian

Capital Territory or of the Northern Territory; or</dd>

<dt>(aa)</dt><dd>the Director of Public Prosecutions; or</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>a Special Prosecutor appointed under the _Special Prosecutors Act 1982_; or</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>the Commissioner of the Australian Federal Police or of the Police Force

of a State or of the Northern Territory; or</dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>the authority or person responsible for the administration or enforcement

of that law.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1A)</dt><dd>A reference in subsection&#160;(1) to a contravention of a law is a

reference to a contravention for which a person may be liable to:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a criminal penalty; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>a civil or administrative penalty.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>Where, in the course of inquiring into a matter, a Commission:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>obtains information;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>takes evidence; or</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>receives a document or thing;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

that, in the opinion of the Commission, relates or may relate to a matter into which another Commission is required or authorized to inquire, the first-mentioned Commission may, if in its opinion it is appropriate so to do, communicate the information or furnish the evidence, document or thing, as the case may be, to that other Commission.

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2A)</dt><dd>Where, in the course of inquiry into a matter, a Commission:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>obtains information;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>takes evidence; or</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>receives a document or thing;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

that, in the opinion of the Commission, relates or may relate to the performance of the functions of the Australian Crime Commission, the Commission may, if in its opinion it is appropriate so to do, communicate the information or furnish the evidence, document or thing, as the case may be, to the Chief Executive Officer of the Australian Crime Commission.

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>A reference in subsection&#160;(2) or (2A) to the furnishing of a document

or thing includes a reference to the furnishing of the contents of the document

or a description of the thing.

</dd> </dl></dl>

**

###  ROYAL COMMISSIONS ACT 1902- SECT 7  Protection to Commissioners etc. 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Every such Commissioner shall in the exercise of his or her duty as

Commissioner have the same protection and immunity as a Justice of the High

Court.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Every witness summoned to attend or appearing before the Commission shall have the same protection, and shall in addition to the penalties provided by this Act be subject to the same liabilities in any civil or criminal proceeding, as a witness in any case tried in the High Court.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>A legal practitioner assisting a Commission or appearing on behalf of a person at a hearing before a Commission has the same protection and immunity as a barrister has in appearing for a party in proceedings in the High Court. </dd> </dl></dl>

**

###  ROYAL COMMISSIONS ACT 1902- SECT 7AA  Commission may have concurrent functions and powers under State laws 
**

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		If, with the consent of the Minister, any functions or powers are conferred

on:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a sole Commissioner; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>all the members of a Commission;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

by the Governor of a State or a Minister of a State, the sole Commissioner, or the members of the Commission, as the case may be, may perform those functions or exercise those powers in conjunction with the performance or exercise by the sole Commissioner, or by the members of the Commission, as the case may be, of the Commissioner's or members' functions or powers under this Act. 

**

###  ROYAL COMMISSIONS ACT 1902- SECT 7A  Effect of Royal Commissioner having authority to inquire under foreign law 
**

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		Where:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>by Letters Patent, a commission to make inquiry into and report upon the

matters specified in the Letters Patent has been issued to a person or persons

under section&#160;1A;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>an authority to inquire into, and take evidence in relation to, specified

matters is granted by, or issued under, a law of another country, to the

person, or to any one or more of the persons, referred to in

paragraph&#160;(a);</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>in the opinion of the Attorney-General, the matters specified:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>in the case of an authority granted by a law of another country&#151;in that

law; or</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>in the case of an authority issued under a law of another country&#151;in the

instrument by which the authority is issued;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		are the same or substantially the same as the matters specified in the

Letters Patent or are significantly related to those last-mentioned matters;

and

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>arrangements have been made between Australia and that other country in

relation to the performance of functions and the exercise of powers under the

authority by the person or persons referred to in paragraph&#160;(b);

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

any information obtained, and any evidence taken, by the person or persons referred to in paragraph&#160;(b), in the course of the performance of a function, or the exercise of a power, under the authority, being information or evidence that relates to the matters specified in the Letters Patent, may, subject to:

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>any conditions or restrictions subject to which the authority was granted

or issued; and</dd>

<dt>(f)</dt><dd>any provision of the arrangements;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

be used by the person or persons referred to in paragraph&#160;(a) for the purposes of the performance of any function, or the exercise of any power, under the commission. 

**

###  ROYAL COMMISSIONS ACT 1902- SECT 7B  Commission may take evidence outside Australia 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		Where:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>by Letters Patent, a commission has been issued to a person or persons (in

this section referred to as the _Commission_) under

section&#160;1A; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>arrangements have been made between Australia and another country in

relation to the taking of evidence in that country by the Commission;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

the Commission may, in that other country, subject to any provision of the arrangements and to the laws of that other country, administer an oath or affirmation to any person appearing as a witness before the Commission and take evidence given by that witness on oath or affirmation, and any evidence taken in that country by the Commission in accordance with those arrangements may be used by the Commission in Australia for the purpose of the performance of any function, or the exercise of any power, under the commission. 

**

###  ROYAL COMMISSIONS ACT 1902- SECT 7C  Statements made by witness not admissible in evidence against the witness 
**

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		A statement or disclosure made by any witness in the course of giving

evidence, being evidence that is taken as mentioned in section&#160;7A or 7B,

is not (except in proceedings for an offence against this Act) admissible in

evidence against that witness in any civil or criminal proceedings in any court

of the Commonwealth, of a State or of a Territory.

 </dl></dl>

**

</dd> </dl></dl>

###  ROYAL COMMISSIONS ACT 1902- SECT 8  Allowances to witnesses

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Governor-General may make regulations prescribing a scale of

###  ROYAL COMMISSIONS ACT 1902- SECT 16  Evidence of issue of Commission etc.

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>a certificate signed by the Minister for Foreign Affairs, stating that

certificate in relation to the performance of functions, and the exercise of

</dd>

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>A certificate signed by the Attorney-General stating that, in his or her

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the matters specified in a law of another country by which an authority to

inquire into, and take evidence in relation to, those matters is granted to a

person, being a law referred to in the certificate; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the matters specified in an instrument by which an authority to inquire

into, and take evidence in relation to, those matters is issued under the law

of another country, being an authority referred to in the certificate;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

are the same or substantially the same as the matters specified in the Letters Patent by which a commission referred to in the certificate has been issued under section&#160;1A, or are significantly related to those last-mentioned matters, is evidence that the Attorney-General is of that opinion.
