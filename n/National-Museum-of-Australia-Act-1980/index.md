### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 3 Interpretation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   3.*2* (1) In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:<lf>   "appoint" includes re-appoint;<lf>   "Australian history" includes:<lf>   (a) the natural history of Australia; and<lf>   (b) the history of the interaction of man with the Australian natural environment;<lf>   "Australian natural environment" includes all aspects of the surroundings of man in Australia, whether affecting him as an individual or in his social groupings;<lf>   "Chairman" means the Chairman of the Council;<lf>   "Council" means the Council of the National Museum of Australia;<lf>   "Deputy Chairman" means the Deputy Chairman of the Council;<lf>   "deputy part-time member" means a person appointed under section 15 to be the deputy of a part-time member;<lf>   "Director" means the Director of the National Museum of Australia;<lf>   "historical material" means material (whether in written form or in any other form) relating to Australian history;<lf>   "member" means a member of the Council;<lf>   "Museum" means the National Museum of Australia established by this Act;<lf>   "national historical collection" means:<lf>   (a) all historical material that was in the ownership of the Museum at the time when the Statute Law (Miscellaneous Provisions) Act (No. 2) 1985 received the Royal Assent other than any such material that the Council determines should not be in the national historical collection;<lf>   (b) such historical material that was in the possession of the Museum at the time referred to in paragraph (a) as the Council determines should be included in the national historical collection; and<lf>   (c) such historical material that comes into the ownership or possession of the Museum after the time referred to in paragraph (a) as the Council determines should be included in the national historical collection;<lf>   "part-time member" means a member appointed to be a part-time member. 

<lf>   (2) For the purposes of the definitions of "Australian history" and "Australian natural environment" in subsection (1), "Australia" includes:<lf>   (a) every external Territory; and<lf>   (b) the continental shelf, within the meaning of Convention on the Continental Shelf, that is adjacent to the coast of Australia or of an external Territory. <p><lf>   (3) For the purposes of subsection (2), "external Territory" includes any territory that was formerly a Territory referred to in section 122 of the Constitution (including a Territory administered by Australia under a Trusteeship Agreement) but has ceased to be a Territory referred to in that section. <p><lf>   (4) Any determination made by the Council for the purposes of the definition of "national historical collection" in subsection (1) shall be based upon criteria, and made in accordance with guidelines, approved by the Minister. *2* Section 3 is amended by section 15 and Schedule 1 only of the Maritime Legislation Amendment Act 1994\. ### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - PART II  PART II - THE NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA </name>

**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 4 Establishment of Museum </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   4\. (1) There is established by this Act a National Museum of Australia. 

<lf>   (2) The Museum:<lf>   (a) is a body corporate, with perpetual succession;<lf>   (b) shall have a common seal;<lf>   (c) may acquire, hold and dispose of real and personal property; and<lf>   (d) may sue and be sued in its corporate name. <p><lf>   (3) The common seal of the Museum shall be kept in such custody as the Council directs and shall not be used except as authorized by the Council. <p><lf>   (4) All courts, judges and persons acting judicially shall take judicial notice of the common seal of the Museum affixed to a document and shall presume that it was duly affixed. </lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 5 Gallery of Aboriginal Australia </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   5\. (1) There shall be a Gallery of Aboriginal Australia, which shall form part of the Museum. 

<lf>   (2) The Gallery shall consist of:<lf>   (a) a collection of historical material owned by the Museum that relates to Aboriginals and Torres Strait Islanders; and<lf>   (b) the land and premises used in connection with the exhibition of that material. <p><lf>   (3) Nothing in subsection (2) prevents the exhibition in the Gallery of historical material relating to Aboriginals and Torres Strait Islanders that is in the possession of the Museum otherwise than as part of the national historical collection. <p><lf>   (4) In the performance of its functions in relation to the Gallery, the Council shall pursue a policy directed towards securing, under the Council, the development and maintenance of the Gallery, and the exhibition of historical material referred to in subsections (2) and (3), by persons who are Aboriginals, Torres Strait Islanders or descendants of Aboriginals or Torres Strait Islanders. </lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 6 Functions of Museum </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   6\. (1) The functions of the Museum are:<lf>   (a) to develop and maintain a national collection of historical material;<lf>   (b) to exhibit, or to make available for exhibition by others, historical material from the national historical collection or historical material that is otherwise in the possession of the Museum;<lf>   (ba) from time to time as the occasion requires, to exhibit, by itself or in collaboration with others, in Australia or elsewhere, material, whether in written form or in any other form and whether relating to Australia or to a foreign country;<lf>   (c) to conduct, arrange for or assist in research into matters pertaining to Australian history; and<lf>   (d) to disseminate information relating to Australian history and information relating to the Museum and its functions. 

<lf>   (2) The Museum shall use every endeavour to make the most advantageous use of the national collection in the national interest. </lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 7 Powers of Museum </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   7\. (1) Subject to this Act, the Museum has power to do all things necessary or convenient to be done for or in connection with the performance of its functions. 

<lf>   (2) Without limiting the generality of subsection (1), the powers of the Museum referred to in that subsection include power:<lf>   (a) to purchase or take on hire, or to accept as a gift or on deposit or loan, historical material;<lf>   (b) to lend or hire out or otherwise deal with (otherwise than by way of disposal) historical material;<lf>   (c) to accept gifts, devises, bequests or assignments made to the Museum, whether on trust or otherwise, and whether unconditionally or subject to a condition and, if a gift, devise, bequest or assignment is accepted by the Museum on trust or subject to a condition, to act as trustee or to comply with the condition, as the case may be;<lf>   (d) to collect, and make available (whether in writing or in any other form and whether by sale or otherwise), information relating to Australian history;<lf>   (e) to make available (whether by sale or otherwise) reproductions, replicas or other representations (whether in writing or in any other form) of historical material;<lf>   (f) to make available (whether in writing or in any other form and whether by sale or otherwise) information relating to the Museum and its functions;<lf>   (g) to occupy, use and control any land or building owned or held under lease by the Commonwealth and made available to the Museum under section 8;<lf>   (h) to erect buildings;<lf>   (j) to purchase or take on hire, or to accept as a gift or on deposit or loan, and to dispose of or otherwise deal with, furnishings, equipment and other goods;<lf>   (k) to act as trustee of moneys or other property vested in the Museum on trust; and<lf>   (m) to act on behalf of the Commonwealth or of an authority of the Commonwealth in the administration of a trust relating to historical material or related matters. <p><lf>   (3) The Museum shall not dispose of historical material except in accordance with section 9 or 9A. <p><lf>   (4) Notwithstanding anything contained in this Act, any money or other property held by the Museum upon trust or accepted by the Museum subject to a condition shall not be dealt with except in accordance with the obligations of the Museum as trustee of the trust or as the person who has accepted the money or other property subject to the condition, as the case may be. <p><lf>   (5) Nothing in this Act requires the Museum to perform its functions or exercise its powers in relation to historical material that is owned or otherwise in the possession of an authority of the Commonwealth, being historical material that is used for the purposes of the authority. </lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 8 Land and historical material for Museum </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   8\. (1) The Minister may make available to the Museum for the purposes of the Museum such land owned or held under lease by the Commonwealth as is specified by the Minister by notice in the Gazette and any building, structure or other improvements on that land. 

<lf>   (2) Where the Council so requests, the Minister may:<lf>   (a) transfer or cause to be transferred to the Museum, for inclusion in the national historical collection, historical material owned by the Commonwealth; and<lf>   (b) transfer or cause to be transferred to the Museum such other goods or equipment owned by the Commonwealth as he considers would be of use to the Museum. <p><lf>   (3) The Minister shall transfer or cause to be transferred to the Museum, for inclusion in the national historical collection, the specimens and animals that are the property of the Commonwealth by virtue of:<lf>   (a) the agreement a copy of which was set forth in the Schedule to the Zoological Museum Agreement Act 1924; or<lf>   (b) the agreement specified in paragraph (a) as varied by the agreement a copy of which was set forth in the Schedule to the Australian Institute of Anatomy Agreement Act 1931\. </lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 9 Disposal of historical material in national historical collection </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   9\. (1) Subject to subsection (3), where the Council is satisfied that historical material in the national historical collection:<lf>   (a) is unfit for the collection; or<lf>   (b) is not required as part of the collection;<lf> the Council may resolve that the historical material be disposed of by sale, gift or destruction. 

<lf>   (2) Where the Council is satisfied that the exchange of historical material in the national historical collection for other historical material would be advantageous to the collection, the Council may resolve that the first-mentioned historical material be disposed of in exchange for that other historical material. <p><lf>   (3) The Council shall not resolve that historical material be disposed of by destruction unless the Council is satisfied that the material has no saleable value. <p><lf>   (4) Where:<lf>   (a) the Council has resolved, in accordance with this section, that historical material be disposed of; and<lf>   (b) if the value of that historical material exceeds $20,000, the Minister has approved of that disposal;<lf> the Museum may dispose of that historical material accordingly. <p><lf>   (5) The Museum must, in each report prepared under section 63M of the Audit Act 1901 (as that section applies by virtue of section 38), include particulars of any disposals of historic material under this section or section 9A during the financial year to which the report relates. </lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 9A Disposal of historical material not in national historical collection </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   9A. (1) Subject to subsection (2), the Council may resolve that historical material owned by or in the possession of the Museum, but not in the national historical collection, be disposed of by sale, gift, exchange for other historical material, or destruction. 

<lf>   (2) The Council shall not resolve that historical material be disposed of by destruction unless the Council is satisfied that the material has no saleable value and cannot be disposed of in any other way. <p><lf>   (3) Where:<lf>   (a) the Council has resolved, in accordance with this section, that historical material be disposed of; and<lf>   (b) if the value of the historical material exceeds $20,000, the Minister has approved of the disposal;<lf> the Museum may dispose of the historical material accordingly. </lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - PART III  PART III - THE COUNCIL OF THE MUSEUM </name>
</b>** 
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 10 Establishment of Council </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   10\. (1) There is established by this section a Council by the name of the Council of the National Museum of Australia. 

<lf>   (2) The Council is responsible for the conduct and control of the affairs of the Museum and, subject to sections 5 and 12, the policy of the Museum with respect to any matter shall be determined by the Council. <p><lf>   (3) All acts and things done in the name of, or on behalf of, the Museum by the Council or with the authority of the Council shall be deemed to have been done by the Museum. <p><lf>   (4) The Council shall commence to function when the Minister has declared, by notice published in the Gazette, that the first members of the Council have been duly appointed. </lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 12 Directions by Minister </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   12\. The Council shall perform its functions and exercise its powers in accordance with such written directions (if any) as are given to it from time to time by the Minister. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 13 Membership of Council </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   13\. (1) The Council shall consist of:<lf>   (a) a Chairman;<lf>   (b) the Director; and<lf>   (c) not less than 7 nor more than 10 other members. 

<lf>   (2) The members, other than the Director, shall be appointed by the Governor-General having regard to their knowledge and experience with respect to matters relating to Australian history or with respect to any other area of knowledge relevant to the affairs of the Museum. <p><lf>   (3) A member, other than the Director:<lf>   (a) shall be appointed to be a part-time member; and<lf>   (b) holds office for such term, not exceeding 3 years, as is fixed by the Governor-General in the instrument of his appointment. <p><lf>   (4) The performance of the functions, or the exercise of the powers, of the Council is not affected by reason only of there being a vacancy or vacancies in the membership of the Council. <p><lf>   (5) The appointment of a person as a part-time member is not invalidated, and shall not be called in question, by reason of a defect or irregularity in or in connection with his appointment. </lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></p></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 14 Deputy Chairman </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   14\. (1) There shall be a Deputy Chairman of the Council who shall be elected by the members from among the members referred to in paragraph 13 (1) (c). 

<lf>   (2) A member elected as the Deputy Chairman:<lf>   (a) holds office as the Deputy Chairman for the period fixed by the Council at the time of his election, but that period shall not extend beyond the expiration of the term for which he is holding office as a member at that time; and<lf>   (b) is eligible for re-election. <p><lf>   (3) The Deputy Chairman may resign his office by writing signed by him and delivered to the Chairman. <p><lf>   (4) The election of a person as the Deputy Chairman is not invalidated, and shall not be called in question, by reason of a defect or irregularity in or in connection with his election. <p><lf>   (5) The validity of anything done by a person purporting to be the Deputy Chairman shall not be called in question on the ground that there is a defect or irregularity in or in connection with his election as the Deputy Chairman, that he has ceased to be the Deputy Chairman or that the occasion for him to perform the functions, or exercise the powers, of the Deputy Chairman had not arisen or had ceased. </lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 15 Deputies of part-time members </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   15\. (1) A part-time member may, with the approval of the Minister, appoint a person, other than a member, to be his deputy. 

<lf>   (2) A part-time member may revoke the appointment of a person as his deputy, but the revocation is not effective until the member has given notice of it, in writing, to the Minister. <p><lf>   (3) A deputy part-time member may resign his office by writing signed by him and delivered to the Minister. <p><lf>   (4) A deputy part-time member is entitled, in the event of the absence of the member of whom he is the deputy from a meeting of the Council, to attend that meeting and, when so attending, shall be deemed to be a member. <p><lf>   (5) The appointment of a person as a deputy part-time member is not invalidated, and shall not be called in question, by reason of a defect or irregularity in or in connection with his appointment. </lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 16 Remuneration etc. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   16\. (1) This section applies to a person who is:<lf>   (a) a part-time member;<lf>   (b) a deputy part-time member; or<lf>   (c) a member of a committee constituted under section 21 other than a member of the Council. 

<lf>   (2) A person to whom this section applies shall be paid such remuneration as is determined by the Remuneration Tribunal, but, if no determination of that remuneration by the tribunal is in operation, he shall be paid such remuneration as is prescribed. <p><lf>   (3) A person to whom this section applies shall be paid such allowances as are prescribed. <p><lf>   (4) This section has effect subject to the  Remuneration Tribunals Act 1973\. </lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 17 Termination of appointment </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   17\. (1) The Governor-General may terminate the appointment of a part-time member by reason of the misbehaviour or physical or mental incapacity of that member. 

<lf>   (2) If a part-time member:<lf>   (a) becomes bankrupt, applies to take the benefit of any law for the relief of bankrupt or insolvent debtors, compounds with his creditors or makes an assignment of his remuneration for their benefit;<lf>   (b) is absent, except on leave granted by the Council, from 3 consecutive meetings of the Council otherwise than on business of the Council undertaken with the approval of the Council; or<lf>   (c) fails, without reasonable excuse, to comply with his obligations under section 19;<lf> the Governor-General shall terminate the appointment of that member. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 18 Resignation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   18\. A part-time member may resign his office by writing signed by him and delivered to the Governor-General. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 19 Disclosure of interests by members etc. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   19\. (1) A member who has a direct or indirect pecuniary interest in a matter being considered or about to be considered by the Council shall, as soon as possible after the relevant facts have come to his knowledge, disclose the nature of his interest at a meeting of the Council. 

<lf>   (2) A disclosure under subsection (1) shall be recorded in the minutes of the meeting of the Council and the member shall not:<lf>   (a) be present during any deliberation of the Council with respect to that matter; or<lf>   (b) take part in any decision of the Council with respect to that matter. <p><lf>   (3) The preceding provisions of this section apply to and in relation to a deputy part-time member in like manner as they apply to and in relation to a member of the Council. </lf></p></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 20 Meetings of Council </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   20\. (1) The Chairman or, if for any reason the Chairman is unable to act, the Deputy Chairman:<lf>   (a) shall convene such meetings of the Council as he considers necessary for the efficient conduct of its business; and<lf>   (b) shall, on receipt of a written request signed by not less than 4 members, convene a meeting of the Council. 

<lf>   (2) The Minister may at any time convene a meeting of the Council. <p><lf>   (3) The Chairman shall preside at all meetings of the Council at which he is present. <p><lf>   (4) In the event of the absence of the Chairman from a meeting of the Council, the Deputy Chairman shall preside at that meeting. <p><lf>   (5) In the event of the absence of both the Chairman and the Deputy Chairman from a meeting of the Council, the members present shall elect one of their number to preside at that meeting. <p><lf>   (6) At a meeting of the Council, 7 members constitute a quorum. <p><lf>   (7) Subject to subsection (8), all questions arising at a meeting of the Council shall be decided by a majority of the votes of the members present and voting, including the member presiding. <p><lf>   (8) In the event of an equality of votes on a resolution proposed at a meeting of the Council, the resolution shall be taken not to be passed but, if the same resolution is proposed at the first meeting of the Council held after the date of that first-mentioned meeting and there is again an equality of votes, the member presiding has a casting vote on the resolution. <p><lf>   (9) The Council may regulate the conduct of proceedings at its meetings as it thinks fit and shall keep minutes of those proceedings. <p><lf>   (10) If the Council so determines, a member of the Council may participate in, and form part of a quorum at, a meeting of the Council by means of any of the following methods of communication:<lf>   (a) telephone;<lf>   (b) closed circuit television;<lf>   (c) another method of communication determined by the Council. <p><lf>   (11) A determination of the Council under subsection (10) may be made in respect of a particular meeting, or in respect of all meetings of the Council. <p><lf>   (12) A member of the Council who participates in a meeting as provided by subsection (10) shall be taken for the purposes of this Part to be present at the meeting. <p><lf>   (13) If the Council so determines, a resolution shall be taken to have been passed at a meeting of the Council if, without meeting, a majority of the number of members of the Council who would, if present at a meeting of the Council and entitled to vote on the resolution at that meeting, have constituted a quorum of the Council indicate agreement with the resolution in accordance with the method determined by the Council. </lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 21 Committees of Council </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   21\. (1) Subject to this section, the Council may constitute such committees as it thinks necessary for the purposes of this Act. 

<lf>   (2) A committee may be constituted wholly by members of the Council or partly by members of the Council and partly by other persons. </lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 22 Delegation by Council </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   22\. (1) Subject to this section, the Council may, by resolution, either generally or as otherwise provided by the resolution, delegate all or any of its powers under this Act (except this power of delegation) to the Director, to any other member or to a member of the staff of the Museum. 

<lf>   (2) A power so delegated, when exercised by the delegate, shall, for the purposes of this Act, be deemed to have been exercised by the Council. <p><lf>   (3) A delegate is, in the exercise of a power so delegated, subject to the directions of the Council. <p><lf>   (4) A delegation of a power under this section:<lf>   (a) may be revoked by resolution of the Council (whether or not constituted by the persons constituting the Council at the time the power was delegated);<lf>   (b) does not prevent the exercise of the power by the Council; and<lf>   (c) continues in force notwithstanding a change in the membership of the Council. <p><lf>   (5) Section 34A of the Acts Interpretation Act 1901 applies in relation to a delegation under this section as if the Council were a person. <p><lf>   (6) A certificate signed by the Chairman stating any matter with respect to the delegation of a power under this section is prima facie  evidence of that matter. <p><lf>   (7) A document purporting to be a certificate mentioned in subsection (6) shall, unless the contrary is established, be deemed to be such a certificate and to have been duly given. </lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</sect>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - PART IV  PART IV - THE DIRECTOR OF THE MUSEUM </name>
</b>** 
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 23 Office of Director </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   23\. (1) There shall be a Director of the National Museum of Australia, who shall be appointed by the Governor-General. 

<lf>   (2) The Director is the executive member of the Council and shall, subject to and in accordance with the general directions of the Council, manage the affairs of the Museum. </lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 24 Terms and conditions of appointment etc. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   24\. (1) The Director holds office for such period, not exceeding 7 years, as is specified in the instrument of his appointment. 

<lf>   (2) A person who has attained the age of 65 years shall not be appointed as the Director and a person shall not be appointed as the Director for a period that extends beyond the day on which he will attain the age of 65 years. <p><lf>   (3) The Director holds office on such terms and conditions (if any) in respect of matters not provided for by this Act as are determined by the Governor-General. </lf></p></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 25 Remuneration etc. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   25\. (1) The Director shall be paid such remuneration as is determined by the Remuneration Tribunal, but, if no determination of that remuneration by the Tribunal is in operation, he shall be paid such remuneration as is prescribed. 

<lf>   (2) The Director shall be paid such allowances as are prescribed. <p><lf>   (3) This section has effect subject to the Remuneration Tribunals Act 1973\. </lf></p></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 26 Leave of absence </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   26\. (1) Subject to section 87E of the Public Service Act 1922, the Director has such recreation leave entitlements as are determined by the Remuneration Tribunal. 

<lf>   (2) The Minister may grant the Director leave of absence, other than recreation leave, on such terms and conditions as to remuneration or otherwise as the Minister determines. </lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 27 Termination of appointment </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   27\. (1) The Governor-General may terminate the appointment of the Director by reason of the misbehaviour or physical or mental incapacity of the Director. 

<lf>   (2) If the Director:<lf>   (a) becomes bankrupt, applies to take the benefit of any law for the relief of bankrupt or insolvent debtors, compounds with his creditors or makes an assignment of his remuneration for their benefit;<lf>   (b) is absent from duty, except on leave of absence, for 14 consecutive days or for 28 days in any 12 months;<lf>   (c) is absent, except on leave granted by the Council, from 3 consecutive meetings of the Council otherwise than on business of the Council undertaken with the approval of the Council;<lf>   (d) engages in paid employment outside the duties of his office without the approval of the Minister; or<lf>   (e) fails to comply with his obligations under section 19;<lf> the Governor-General shall terminate the appointment of the Director. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 28 Resignation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   28\. The Director may resign his office by writing signed by him and delivered to the Governor-General. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 29 Acting Director </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   29\. (1) The Minister may appoint a person to act in the place of the Director:<lf>   (a) during a vacancy in the office of the Director, whether or not an appointment has previously been made to the office; or<lf>   (b) during any period, or during all periods, when the Director is absent from duty or from Australia, or is, for any other reason, unable to perform the functions of his office;<lf> but a person appointed to act during a vacancy shall not continue so to act for more than 12 months. 

<lf>   (2) An appointment of a person under subsection (1) may be expressed to have effect only in such circumstances as are specified in the instrument of appointment. <p><lf>   (3) The Minister may:<lf>   (a) subject to this Act, determine the terms and conditions of appointment, including remuneration and allowances, of a person acting as Director; and<lf>   (b) terminate such an appointment at any time. <p><lf>   (4) Where a person is acting as Director in accordance with paragraph (1) (b) and the office of Director becomes vacant while that person is so acting, that person may continue so to act until the Minister otherwise directs, the vacancy is filled or a period of 12 months from the date on which the vacancy occurred expires, whichever first happens. <p><lf>   (5) The appointment of a person to act as Director ceases to have effect if he resigns his appointment by writing signed by him and delivered to the Minister. <p><lf>   (6) While a person is acting as Director, he has and may exercise all the powers, and shall perform all the functions, of the Director under this Act. <p><lf>   (7) A person who is acting as Director may attend a meeting of the Council and, in relation to a meeting that he so attends, shall, for the purposes of this Act, be deemed to be the Director. <p><lf>   (8) The appointment of a person under subsection (1) is not invalidated, and shall not be called in question, by reason of a defect or irregularity in or in connection with his appointment. <p><lf>   (9) The validity of anything done by a person purporting to act under subsection (1) shall not be called in question on the ground that the occasion for his appointment had not arisen, that there is a defect or irregularity in or in connection with his appointment, that the appointment had ceased to have effect or that the occasion for him to act had not arisen or had ceased. </lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></p></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></sect>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - PART V  PART V - STAFF AND CONSULTANTS </name>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 30 Staff </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   30\. (1) Subject to section 31, the staff of the Museum shall be persons appointed or employed under the Public Service Act 1922\. 

<lf>   (2) The Director has all the powers of, or exercisable by, a Secretary under the Public Service Act 1922, in so far as those powers relate to the branch of the Australian Public Service comprising the staff referred to in subsection (1), as if that branch were a separate Department of the Australian Public Service. <p><lf>   (3) For the purposes of section 26 of the Public Service Act 1922, the Director shall be deemed to be a Secretary. <p><lf>   (4) The members of the staff referred to in subsection (1) shall perform their functions and duties in accordance with the directions of the Director. </lf></p></lf></p></lf>

**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 31 Arrangements relating to staff </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   31\. (1) The Council may arrange with the Secretary of a Department of the Australian Public Service or with a body (other than a company or association) established for a public purpose by or under a law of the Commonwealth for the services of officers or employees of the body to be made available to the Museum. 

<lf>   (2) The Council may enter into an arrangement with the appropriate authority of a State or Territory for the services of officers or employees of the Public Service of the State or Territory, or of a body established for a public purpose by or under a law of the State or Territory, to be made available to the Museum. </lf>

</sect>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - PART VI  PART VI - FINANCE </name>
</b>** 
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 33 Moneys payable to Museum </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   33\. (1) There are payable to the Museum such moneys as are appropriated, from time to time, by the Parliament for the purposes of the Museum. 

<lf>   (2) The Minister for Finance may give directions as to the amounts in which, and the times at which, moneys referred to in subsection (1) are to be paid to the Museum. </lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 34 Estimates </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   34\. (1) The Council shall prepare estimates, in such form as the Minister directs, of the expenditure of the Museum out of moneys payable to the Museum under section 33 for each financial year and, if the Minister so directs, for any other period specified by the Minister, and shall submit those estimates to the Minister not later than such date as the Minister directs. 

<lf>   (2) Moneys paid to the Museum under section 33 shall not be expended by the Museum otherwise than in accordance with estimates of expenditure approved by the Minister. </lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 35 Application of moneys held by Museum </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   35\. Moneys held by the Museum otherwise than upon trust shall be applied only:<lf>   (a) in payment or discharge of the costs, expenses and other obligations incurred by the Museum in the performance of its functions or the exercise of its powers under this Act; and<lf>   (b) in payment of any remuneration and allowances payable to any person under this Act. </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 36 Investment </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   36\. Moneys to which section 35 applies that are not immediately required to be expended may be invested:<lf>   (a) on deposit with a bank that is an approved bank for the purposes of section 63J of the Audit Act 1901;<lf>   (b) in securities of the Commonwealth; or<lf>   (c) in any other manner approved by the Treasurer. </lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 37 Limit on contracts </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   37\. (1) Subject to subsection 9 (4), the Museum shall not, without the approval of the Minister, enter into a contract under which the Museum is to pay or receive:<lf>   (a) in the case of a contract for the acquisition of any historical material - an amount exceeding $250,000 or, if a higher amount is for the time being prescribed for the purposes of this paragraph, that higher amount; or<lf>   (b) in any other case - an amount exceeding $250,000 or, if a higher amount is for the time being prescribed for the purposes of this paragraph, that higher amount. 

<lf>   (2) Paragraph (1) (b) does not apply to the investment of money by the Museum in accordance with section 36\. </lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 38 Application of Division 3 of Part XI of Audit Act </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   38\. (1) It is hereby declared that the Museum is a public authority to which Division 3 of Part XI of the Audit Act 1901 applies. 

<lf>   (2) Division 3 of Part XI of the Audit Act 1901 applies in relation to the Museum subject to the modifications contained in this section. <p><lf>   (3) The first report to be prepared by the Museum in pursuance of section 63M of the Audit Act 1901 shall be prepared as soon as practicable after 30 June 1981 and shall relate to the period commencing on the date of commencement of this Act and ending on 30 June 1981\. <p><lf>   (4) A report prepared by the Museum in pursuance of section 63M of the Audit Act 1901 shall set out all directions given by the Minister, during the period to which the report relates, to the Museum or to the Council. </lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1980 - SECT 39 Exemption from taxation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   39\. The Museum is not subject to taxation under a law of Australia or of a State or Territory. </sect>

The Governor-General may make regulations, not inconsistent with this Act, prescribing all matters that by this Act are required or permitted to be prescribed, or are necessary or convenient to be prescribed for carrying out or giving effect to this Act, and, in particular:<lf>   (a) for regulating, restricting or prohibiting the entry of persons onto any land or building, or any part of any land or building, owned by or under the control of the Museum, and for regulating the conduct of persons on any such land or building, or any part of any such land or building, including making provision for and in relation to the removal of persons from any such land or building, or any part of any such land or building;<lf>   (b) for fixing charges for entry onto any land, or into any building, owned by, or under the control of, the Museum;<lf>   (c) for regulating, restricting or prohibiting the making, using, printing, publishing, exhibiting, selling or offering for sale of replicas, photographs, representations or copies of historical material forming part of the national historical collection; and<lf>   (d) for prescribing penalties not exceeding a fine of $500 for offences against the regulations. </lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
