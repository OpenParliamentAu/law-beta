###  NATIONAL HEALTH AND MEDICAL RESEARCH COUNCIL ACT 1992- SECT 1  Short title [_see_ Note 1] 
This Act may be cited as the _National Health and Medical Research Council Act 1992_.

 
###  NATIONAL HEALTH AND MEDICAL RESEARCH COUNCIL ACT 1992- SECT 2  Commencement [_see_ Note 1] 
<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Subject to subsection (2), the provisions of this Act commence on a day or

days to be fixed by Proclamation.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>If a provision of this Act does not commence under subsection (1) within the period of 6 months commencing on the day on which this Act receives the Royal Assent, it commences on the first day after the end of that period. </dd> 
###  NATIONAL HEALTH AND MEDICAL RESEARCH COUNCIL ACT 1992- SECT 3  Object of the Act etc. 
<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The object of this Act is to make provision for a national body to pursue

activities designed:

</dd> 
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>to raise the standard of individual and public health throughout

Australia; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>to foster the development of consistent health standards between the

various States and Territories; and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>to foster medical research and training and public health research and

training throughout Australia; and</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>to foster consideration of ethical issues relating to health.

</dd>

</dl></dl>
<dt>(2)</dt><dd>It is the intention of the Parliament that, to the extent that it is

practicable to do so, the Council should adopt a policy of public consultation

in relation to individual and public health matters being considered by it from

time to time.

</dd>

**

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>There is established by this section a council to be known as the National

Health and Medical Research Council.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Council: </dd> </dl>

</dl></dl></dl>

###  NATIONAL HEALTH AND MEDICAL RESEARCH COUNCIL ACT 1992- SECT 7  Functions of the Council 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The functions of the Council are:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>to inquire into, issue guidelines on, and advise the community on, matters

relating to:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>the improvement of health; and</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>the prevention, diagnosis and treatment of disease; and</dd>

<dt>(iii)</dt><dd>the provision of health care; and</dd>

<dt>(iv)</dt><dd>public health research and medical research; and</dd>

<dt>(v)</dt><dd>ethical issues relating to health; and

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>to advise, and make recommendations to, the Commonwealth, the States and

Territories on the matters referred to in paragraph (a); and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>to make recommendations to the Commonwealth on expenditure:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>on public health research and training; and</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>on medical research and training;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>any functions incidental to any of the foregoing.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>Subject to the direction of the Minister, the Council has the general

</dd> </dl>

###  NATIONAL HEALTH AND MEDICAL RESEARCH COUNCIL ACT 1992- SECT 8  Ethics guidelines 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Without limiting any of the matters on which the Council may issue

guidelines under subparagraph 7(1)(a)(v), the Council must issue guidelines

under that subparagraph for the conduct of medical research involving humans.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The guidelines for the conduct of medical research involving humans must be issued precisely as developed by the Principal Committee known as the Australian Health Ethics Committee and provided to the Council for the purpose.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>Sections 13, 14, 14A and 14B and the procedures developed and published by the Council under section 15 apply to the activities of the Australian Health Ethics Committee in developing guidelines for the conduct of medical research involving humans as if the references in sections 13, 14, 14A and 14B to the Council were references to that Committee and as if the procedures under section 15 had been developed to assist the Committee. </dd> </dl>

 <dl compact="">

###  NATIONAL HEALTH AND MEDICAL RESEARCH COUNCIL ACT 1992- SECT 10  Minister may give directions to the Council 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Subject to subsection (2), the Minister may give written directions to the

Council as to the performance of its functions or the exercise of its powers.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Directions given by the Minister under subsection (1) must be of a general nature only, and, in particular, the Minister is not entitled to direct the Council: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>to recommend the allocation of research funds to a particular person,

organisation, State or Territory; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>as to the manner of the Council's treatment of particular scientific,

technical or ethical issues.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>The Council must comply with any direction given by the Minister under

subsection (1).</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>If the Minister gives a direction to the Council, the Minister must cause a statement setting out particulars of, and of the reasons for, the direction to be laid before each House of the Parliament within 15 sitting days of that House after giving the direction. </dd> </dl>

###  NATIONAL HEALTH AND MEDICAL RESEARCH COUNCIL ACT 1992- SECT 56  Functions of Commissioner 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The functions of the Commissioner are:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>to investigate complaints concerning reviewable action made to the

Commissioner in accordance with this Part; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>to perform any other function that is conferred on the Commissioner by

this Part.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Commissioner is not to enquire into the merits of a recommendation or

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>a delegate of the Council; or</dd>

in the course of a reviewable action.

###  NATIONAL HEALTH AND MEDICAL RESEARCH COUNCIL ACT 1992- SECT 57  Who may complain 
**

 <dl compact="">

		A person whose interests are affected by a reviewable action may apply to the

Commissioner to investigate a complaint concerning the action made on one or

more of the grounds set out in section 58.

 </dl>

**

###  NATIONAL HEALTH AND MEDICAL RESEARCH COUNCIL ACT 1992- SECT 58  Grounds for complaint 
**

 <dl compact="">

		The grounds for complaint concerning a reviewable action are the following:

 </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>that the action involved a breach of the rules of natural justice;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>that the action was induced or affected by fraud;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>that there was no evidence or other material to justify the action;</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>that an irrelevant consideration was taken into account in relation to the

action;</dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>that a relevant consideration was not taken into account in relation to

the action;</dd>

<dt>(f)</dt><dd>that in the course of the action a discretionary power was exercised for a

purpose other than the purpose for which the power is conferred;</dd>

<dt>(g)</dt><dd>that the action involved the exercise of a discretionary power in bad

faith;</dd>

<dt>(h)</dt><dd>that, in the course of the action, a personal discretionary power was

exercised at the direction of another person;</dd>

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>that the action involved the exercise of a discretionary power in

accordance with a rule or policy without regard to the merits of the particular

case;</dd>

<dt>(j)</dt><dd>that the action involved any other exercise of a power in a way that

constitutes abuse of the power.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

**

###  NATIONAL HEALTH AND MEDICAL RESEARCH COUNCIL ACT 1992- SECT 59  How to make a complaint 
**

 <dl compact="">

		A complaint must:

 </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>be in writing; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>be signed by the complainant; and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>describe the action complained about; and</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>specify the nature of and grounds for the complaint; and</dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>be lodged with the Commissioner.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

**
