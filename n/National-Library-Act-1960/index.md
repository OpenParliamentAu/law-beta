### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - LONG TITLE </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>          An Act relating to the National Library of Australia<lf> </lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - PART I  PART I-PRELIMINARY </name>
</b>** 
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 1 Short title </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   1\. This Act may be cited as the National Library Act 1960.*1* SEE NOTES TO FIRST ARTICLE OF THIS CHAPTER . </sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 2 Commencement </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   2\. This Act shall come into operation on a date to be fixed by Proclamation.*1* SEE NOTES TO FIRST ARTICLE OF THIS CHAPTER . </sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 4 Interpretation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   4\. In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:<lf>   "library material" includes books, periodicals, newspapers, manuscripts, films, sound recordings, musical scores, maps, plans, pictures, photographs, prints and other recorded material, whether in writing or in some other form;<lf>   "member" means a member of the Council;<lf>   "the Council" means the Council of the National Library of Australia established by this Act;<lf>   "the Director-General" means the Director-General of the Library;<lf>   "the Library" means the National Library of Australia established by this Act. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - PART II  PART II-ESTABLISHMENT OF THE NATIONAL LIBRARY OF AUSTRALIA </name>
</b>** 
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 5 Establishment of National Library </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   5\. (1) A body corporate is hereby established under the name "National Library of Australia". 

  (2) The Library:<lf>   (a) shall have a seal;<lf>   (b) has power to acquire, hold and dispose of real and personal property; and<lf>   (c) may sue and be sued in its corporate name. <p>  (3) All courts, judges and persons acting judicially shall take judicial notice of the seal of the Library affixed to a document and shall presume that it was duly affixed. </p></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 6 Functions of Library </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   6\. The functions of the Library are, on behalf of the Commonwealth:<lf>   (a) to maintain and develop a national collection of library material, including a comprehensive collection of library material relating to Australia and the Australian people;<lf>   (b) to make library material in the national collection available to such persons and institutions, and in such manner and subject to such conditions, as the Council determines with a view to the most advantageous use of that collection in the national interest;<lf>   (c) to make available such other services in relation to library matters and library material (including bibliographical services) as the Council thinks fit, and, in particular, services for the purposes of:<lf>     (i) the library of the Parliament;<lf>     (ii) the Departments and authorities of the Commonwealth; and<lf>     (iii) the Territories; and<lf>   (d) to co-operate in library matters (including the advancement of library science) with authorities or persons, whether in Australia or elsewhere, concerned with library matters. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 7 Powers of the Library </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   7\. (1) The Library has power to do all things necessary or convenient to be done for or in connexion with the performance of its functions. 

  (2) Without limiting the generality of subsection (1), the powers of the Library referred to in that subsection include power:<lf>   (a) to purchase or take on hire, or to accept on deposit or loan, library material, and also furnishings, equipment and goods needed for the purposes of the Library;<lf>   (b) to dispose of, lend or hire out library material or other goods the property of the Library;<lf>   (c) to purchase or take on lease land or buildings, and to erect buildings, necessary for the purposes of the Library;<lf>   (d) to dispose of, or grant leases of, land or buildings vested in the Library;<lf>   (e) to occupy, use and control any land or building owned or held under lease by the Commonwealth and made available for the purposes of the Library;<lf>   (f) to accept gifts, devises, bequests and assignments made to the Library (whether on trust or otherwise); and<lf>   (g) to act as trustee of moneys, library material or other property vested in the Library upon trust, or to act on behalf of the Commonwealth or an authority of the Commonwealth in the administration of a trust relating to library material or library matters. <p>  (3) Notwithstanding anything contained in this Act, any money or property held by the Library upon trust shall be dealt with in accordance with the powers and duties of the Library as trustee. </p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 7A Power to purchase and dispose of assets </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   7A. (1) The Library shall not, without the approval of the Minister:<lf>   (a) acquire any property, right or privilege for a consideration exceeding in amount or value $250,000 or, if a higher amount is prescribed, that higher amount;<lf>   (b) dispose of any property, right or privilege where the amount or value of the consideration for the disposal, or the value of the property, right or privilege, exceeds $250,000 or, if a higher amount is prescribed, that higher amount;<lf>   (c) enter into a contract for the construction of a building for the Library, being a contract under which the Library is to pay an amount exceeding $250,000 or, if a higher amount is prescribed, that higher amount; or<lf>   (d) enter into a lease of land for a period exceeding 10 years. </lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 8 Transfer of library material etc. to Library </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   8\. The Minister may, at the request of the Council, make arrangements with the appropriate authority for the transfer to the Library of the ownership, use or custody of any library material, or of any equipment or goods, belonging to the Commonwealth. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 9 Land and buildings </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   9\. The Governor-General may make available, for the purposes of the Library, any land or buildings owned or held under lease by the Commonwealth. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - PART III  PART III-MANAGEMENT OF THE LIBRARY </name>
</b>** 
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 10 Constitution of Council </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   10\. (1) The affairs of the Library shall be conducted by a Council to be known as the Council of the National Library of Australia. 

  (2) The Council shall consist of:<lf>   (aa) the Director-General;<lf>   (a) one Senator elected by the Senate;<lf>   (b) one member of the House of Representatives elected by that House; and<lf>   (c) 9 members appointed by the Governor-General, being persons who, in the opinion of the Governor-General, by their knowledge and experience can advance the full development of the Library. <p>  (3) A member of the Council elected by either House of the Parliament holds office, subject to this Act, for such period, not exceeding 3 years, as is fixed by that House at the time of his election. </p><p>  (4) A member of the Council appointed by the Governor-General holds office, subject to this Act, for such period, not exceeding 3 years, as is fixed by the Governor-General at the time of his appointment. </p><p>  (5) A member of the Council elected by either House of the Parliament or appointed by the Governor-General is eligible for re-election or re-appointment. </p><p>  (6) The exercise or performance of the powers or functions of the Council is not affected by reason only of there being a vacancy or vacancies in the membership of the Council. </p></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 11 Acting members </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   11\. (1) The Minister may appoint a person to act as a member appointed by the Governor-General:<lf>   (a) during a vacancy in the office of such a member; or<lf>   (b) during any period, or during all periods, when such a member is absent from duty or from Australia or is, for any other reason, unable to perform the functions of his or her office;<lf> but a person appointed to act during a vacancy shall not continue so to act for more than 12 months. 

  (2) An appointment under this section may be expressed to have effect only in such circumstances as are specified in the instrument of appointment. 

  (3) The Minister may:<lf>   (a) determine the terms and conditions of appointment, including remuneration and allowances, of a person appointed under this section; and<lf>   (b) terminate such an appointment at any time. <p>  (4) Where a person is acting as a member appointed by the Governor-General in accordance with paragraph (1) (b) and the office of that member becomes vacant while that person is so acting, then, subject to subsection (2), that person may continue so to act until the Minister otherwise directs, the vacancy is filled or a period of 12 months from the day on which the vacancy occurred expires, whichever first happens. </p><p>  (5) A person appointed to act under this section may resign his or her appointment by writing signed by the person and delivered to the Minister. </p><p>  (6) Where a person is acting as a member appointed by the Governor-General, that person has and may exercise all the powers, and shall perform all the functions, of that member. </p><p>  (7) The validity of anything done by a person purporting to act under subsection (1) shall not be called in question on the ground that the occasion for his or her appointment had not arisen, or that there is a defect or irregularity in, or in connection with, the appointment, that the appointment had ceased to have effect or that the occasion for the person to act had not arisen or had ceased. </p></lf></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 12 Chairman and Deputy Chairman </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   12\. (1) The Governor-General shall appoint one of the members appointed by the Governor-General to be the Chairman of the Council during the pleasure of the Governor-General. 

  (2) There shall be a Deputy Chairman of the Council, who shall be a member elected by the members from time to time. 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 13 Remuneration and allowances </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   13\. (1) The members of the Council shall be paid such remuneration as is determined by the Remuneration Tribunal. 

  (2) The members of the Council shall be paid such allowances as are prescribed. 

  (3) This section has effect subject to the Remuneration Tribunals Act 1973\. 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 14 Termination of office </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   14\. (1) The Governor-General may terminate the appointment of a member, being a member appointed by the Governor-General, for inability, inefficiency or misbehaviour. 

  (2) A member elected by either House of the Parliament may be removed from office by that House. 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 15 Vacation of office </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   15\. (1) If a member other than the Director-General:<lf>   (a) becomes bankrupt, applies to take the benefit of any law for the relief of bankrupt or insolvent debtors, compounds with his creditors or makes an assignment of his remuneration for their benefit;<lf>   (c) in the case of a member elected by either House of the Parliament-ceases to be a member of that House;<lf>   (d) is absent, except on leave granted by the Council, from 3 consecutive meetings of the Council; or<lf>   (e) fails, without reasonable excuse, to comply with his or her obligations under section 15B;<lf> the Governor-General shall terminate the appointment of the member. 

  (2) For the purposes of paragraph (1) (c), a member of either House of the Parliament shall be deemed not to have ceased to be a member of that House while he continues to be entitled to the Parliamentary allowance that became payable to him as such a member. 
</lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 15A Resignation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   15A. A member other than the Director-General may resign his or her office by signed notice delivered to:<lf>   (a) in the case of a member appointed by the Governor-General-the Governor-General; or<lf>   (b) in the case of a member elected by the Senate or the House of Representatives-the President of the Senate or the Speaker of the House of Representatives, as the case may be. </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 15B Disclosure of interests </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   15B. (1) A member who has a direct or indirect pecuniary interest in a matter being considered or about to be considered by the Council must, as soon as possible after the relevant facts have come to the member's knowledge, disclose the nature of the interest at a meeting of the Council. 

  (2) A disclosure under subsection (1) must be recorded in the minutes of the meeting of the Council and the member must not:<lf>   (a) be present during any deliberations of the Council with respect to the matter; or<lf>   (b) take part in a decision of the Council with respect to the matter. </lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 16 Meetings of Council </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   16\. (1) The Chairman, or if, for any reason, the Chairman is unable to act, the Deputy Chairman:<lf>   (a) shall convene such meetings of the Council as he considers necessary for the efficient conduct of its business; and<lf>   (b) shall, on receipt of a written request signed by not less than 4 members, convene a meeting of the Council. 

  (2) The Minister may at any time convene a meeting of the Council. 

  (3) The Chairman shall preside at all meetings of the Council at which he is present. 

  (4) In the event of the absence of the Chairman from a meeting of the Council, the Deputy Chairman shall preside at that meeting. 

  (5) In the event of the absence of both the Chairman and the Deputy Chairman from a meeting of the Council, the members present shall appoint one of their number to preside at that meeting. 

  (6) At a meeting of the Council, 5 members constitute a quorum. 

  (7) Subject to subsection (8), all questions arising at a meeting of the Council shall be decided by a majority of the votes of the members present and voting, and for this purpose the member presiding at the meeting has a deliberative vote. 

  (8) In the event of an equality of votes on a resolution proposed at a meeting of the Council, the resolution shall be taken not to be passed, but if the same resolution is proposed at the next meeting of the Council held at a later date and there is again an equality of votes, the member presiding at that meeting has a casting vote on the proposed resolution. 
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 17 Director-General </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   17\. (1) For the purposes of this Act, there shall be a Director-General of the National Library. 

  (2) The Director-General shall be appointed by the Governor-General. 

  (3) The Director-General is the executive officer of the Council and has, under the Council, the conduct of the affairs of the Library. 

  (4) The Director-General has all the powers of, or exercisable by, a Secretary under the Public Service Act 1922 so far as those powers relate to the branch of the Public Service of the Commonwealth comprising the staff of the National Library, as if that branch were a separate Department. 

  (5) The Director-General may, by instrument in writing, and either generally or to the extent provided in the instrument, delegate to an officer employed under the Public Service Act 1922 all or any of his powers and functions under this Act (including his powers referred to in subsection (4) but not including this power of delegation). 

  (6) The power of the Director-General to delegate all or any of his powers and functions, other than the powers referred to in subsection (4) of this section, is subject to any directions of the Council. 

  (7) A power or function delegated under this section may be exercised or performed by the delegate as fully and effectually as by the Director-General. 

  (8) A delegation under this section is revocable at will and does not prevent the exercise of a power or the performance of a function by the Director-General. 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 17A Terms and conditions of appointment </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   17A. (1) The Director-General holds office for such period, not exceeding 7 years, as is specified in the instrument of his appointment, but is eligible for re-appointment. 

  (2) A person who has attained the age of 65 years shall not be appointed or re-appointed as Director-General, and a person shall not be appointed or re-appointed as Director-General for a period that extends beyond the date on which he will attain the age of 65 years. 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 17B Leave of absence </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   17B. (1) Subject to section 87E of the Public Service Act 1922, a full-time member has such recreation leave entitlements as are determined by the Remuneration Tribunal. 

  (2) The Minister, or the Council acting with the Minister's authority, may grant a full-time member leave of absence, other than recreation leave, on such terms and conditions as to remuneration or otherwise as the Minister or the Council, acting with the Minister's authority, determines. 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 17C Dismissal </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   17C. The Governor-General may terminate the appointment of the Director-General for misbehaviour or physical or mental incapacity. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 17D Resignation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   17D. The Director-General may resign his office by writing under his hand addressed to the Governor-General. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 17E Vacation of office </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   17E. (1) If the Director-General:<lf>   (a) engages in paid employment outside the duties of his office without the approval of the Minister; or<lf>   (aa) fails, without reasonable excuse, to comply with section 15B or subsection (3) of this section; or<lf>   (b) becomes bankrupt, applies to take the benefit of any law for the relief of bankrupt or insolvent debtors, compounds with his creditors or makes an assignment of his remuneration for their benefit;<lf> the Governor-General shall terminate the appointment of the Director-General. 

  (2) The Minister shall not give an approval for the purposes of paragraph (1) (a) unless he is satisfied that the paid employment is of a minor nature and will not interfere with the performance of the duties of the Director-General under this Act. 

  (3) The Director-General must give written notice to the Minister of all direct or indirect pecuniary interests that the Director-General has or acquires in any business or in any body corporate carrying on any business. 
</lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 17H Application of Superannuation Act </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   17H. For the purposes of subsections 4 (3A) and (4) of the Superannuation Act 1922, the Director-General shall be deemed to be required, by the terms of his appointment, to give the whole of his time to the duties of his office. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 17J Acting Director-General </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   17J. (1) The Minister may appoint a person to act as Director-General:<lf>   (a) during a vacancy in the office;<lf>   (b) during any period, or during all periods, when the Director-General is absent from duty or from Australia or is, for any other reason, unable to perform the functions of the office of Director-General;<lf> but a person appointed to act during a vacancy shall not continue so to act for more than 12 months. 

  (2) An appointment under this section may be expressed to have effect only in such circumstances as are specified in the instrument of appointment. 

  (3) The Minister may:<lf>   (a) determine the terms and conditions of appointment, including remuneration and allowances, of a person appointed under this section; and<lf>   (b) terminate such an appointment at any time. <p>  (4) Where a person is acting as Director-General in accordance with paragraph (1) (b) and the office of Director-General becomes vacant while that person is so acting, then, subject to subsection (2), that person may continue so to act until the Minister otherwise directs, the vacancy is filled or a period of 12 months from the day on which the vacancy occurred expires, whichever first happens. </p><p>  (5) A person appointed to act under this section may resign his or her appointment by writing signed by the person and delivered to the Minister. </p><p>  (6) While a person is acting as Director-General, that person has and may exercise all the powers, and shall perform all the functions, of the Director-General. </p><p>  (7) The validity of anything done by a person purporting to act under subsection (1) shall not be called in question on the ground that the occasion for his or her appointment had not arisen, that there is a defect or irregularity in or in connection with the appointment, that the appointment had ceased to have effect or that the occasion for the person to act had not arisen or had ceased. </p><p>  (8) Section 17B applies in relation to a person appointed under this section in like manner as it applies in relation to the Director-General. </p></lf></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></sect>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - PART IV  PART IV-FINANCE </name>
</b>** 
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 20 Moneys payable to Library </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   20\. (1) There are payable to the Library such moneys as are appropriated by the Parliament for the purposes of the Library. 

  (2) The Minister for Finance may give directions as to the amounts in which, and the times at which, moneys referred to in subsection (1) are to be paid to the Library. 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 21 Application of Division 3 of Part XI of the Audit Act 1901 </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   21\. The Library is a public authority to which Division 3 of Part XI of the Audit Act 1901 applies. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 21A Investment </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   21A. Money held by the Library otherwise than on trust that is not immediately required for the purposes of the Library may be invested:<lf>   (a) on deposit with a bank that is an approved bank for the purposes of section 63J of the Audit Act 1901; or<lf>   (b) in Commonwealth securities; or<lf>   (c) in any other manner approved in writing by the Treasurer. </lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 22 Application of moneys of Library </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   22\. The moneys of the Library shall be applied only:<lf>   (a) in payment or discharge of the costs and expenses of the Library under this Act; and<lf>   (b) in payment of the remuneration, allowances and expenses of the members of the Council. </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 23 Estimates </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   23\. (1) The Council shall prepare estimates, in such form as the Minister directs, of receipts and expenditure of the Library for each financial year and, if the Minister so directs, for any other period specified by the Minister, and the Council shall submit estimates so prepared to the Minister not later than such date as the Minister directs. 

  (2) Moneys of the Library shall not be expended otherwise than in accordance with estimates of expenditure approved by the Minister. 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 26 Exemption from taxation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   26\. The Library is not subject to taxation under any law of the Commonwealth or of a State or Territory. </sect>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 27A Supply of liquor on premises of Library </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   27A. (1) The regulations may make provision for and in relation to the sale, supply, disposal, possession or control of liquor on premises in the Australian Capital Territory owned by or under the control of the Library. 

  (2) The law of the Australian Capital Territory relating to the sale, supply and disposal of liquor does not apply with respect to premises in respect of which regulations are in force under subsection (1). 

  (3) In this section:<lf>   "liquor" means wine, spirits, ale, beer, porter, cider, perry or any liquid containing alcohol ordinarily used or fit for use as a beverage. </lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960 - SECT 27B Control of Library land and buildings </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   27B. (1) The regulations may make provision for and in relation to:<lf>   (a) regulating, restricting or prohibiting the entry of persons on to any land or building owned by or under the control of the Library; or<lf>   (b) regulating the conduct of persons on any such land or building; or<lf>   (c) the removal of persons from any such land or building. 

  (2) A reference in this section to any land or building owned by or under the control of the Library includes a reference to a part of any such land or any such building, as the case may be. 
</lf></lf></lf></sect>
