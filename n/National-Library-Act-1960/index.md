## SECT
<sect>                        NATIONAL  LIBRARY  ACT  1960-1973<lf> <lf>              An Act relating to the National Library of Australia.<lf> <lf>                               PART  I-PRELIMINARY<lf> Short title amended; No. 32, 1918, s. 2.<lf>   1\. This Act may be cited as the National Library Act 1960-1973.*<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960-1973 - SECT. 4\. Definitions. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Amended by No. 217, 1973, s. 4.<lf>   4\. In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears-<lf> <lf>   ''library material'' includes books, periodicals, newspapers, manuscripts, films, sound recordings, musical scores, maps, plans, pictures, photographs, prints and other recorded material, whether in writing or in some other form;<lf> <lf>   ''member'' means a member of the Council;<lf> <lf>   ''the Council'' means the Council of the National Library of Australia established by this Act;<lf> <lf>   ''the Director-General'' means the Director-General of the Library;<lf> <lf>   ''the Library'' means the National Library of Australia established by this Act.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960-1973 - SECT. 5\. Establishment of National Library. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>        PART  II-ESTABLISHMENT  OF  THE  NATIONAL  LIBRARY  OF  AUSTRALIA<lf>   5\. (1) A body corporate is hereby established under the name ''National Library of Australia''.<lf> 

  (2) The Library-<lf> <lf>   (a)  shall have a seal;<lf> <lf>   (b)  has power to acquire, hold and dispose of real and personal property; and <lf> <lf>   (c)  may sue and be sued in its corporate name.<lf> <p>  (3) All courts, judges and persons acting judicially shall take judicial notice of the seal of the Library affixed to a document and shall presume that it was duly affixed.<lf> </lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960-1973 - SECT. 6\. Functions of Library. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Amended by No. 216, 1973, s. 3.<lf>   6\. The functions of the Library are, on behalf of the Commonwealth-<lf> <lf>   (a)  to maintain and develop a national collection of library material, including a comprehensive collection of library material relating to Australia and the Australian people;<lf> <lf>   (b)  to make library material in the national collection available to such persons and institutions, and in such manner and subject to such conditions, as the Council determines with a view to the most advantageous use of that collection in the national interest;<lf> <lf>   (c)  to make available such other services in relation to library matters and library material (including bibliographical services) as the Council thinks fit, and, in particular, services for the purposes of-<lf> <lf>       (i)  the library of the Parliament;<lf> <lf>      (ii)  the Departments and authorities of the Commonwealth; and<lf> <lf>      (iii)  the Territories; and<lf> <lf>   (d)  to co-operate in library matters (including the advancement of library science) with authorities or persons, whether in Australia or elsewhere, concerned with library matters.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960-1973 - SECT. 7\. Powers of the Library. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   7\. (1) The Library has power to do all things necessary or convenient to be done for or in connexion with the performance of its functions.<lf> 

  (2) Without limiting the generality of the last preceding sub-section, the powers of the Library referred to in that sub-section include power-<lf> <lf>   (a)  to purchase or take on hire, or to accept on deposit or loan, library material, and also furnishings, equipment and goods needed for the purposes of the Library;<lf> <lf>   (b)  to dispose of, lend or hire out library material or other goods the property of the Library;<lf> <lf>   (c)  to purchase or take on lease land or buildings, and to erect buildings, necessary for the purposes of the Library;<lf> <lf>   (d)  to dispose of, or grant leases of, land or buildings vested in the Library;<lf> <lf>   (e)  to occupy, use and control any land or building owned or held under lease by the Commonwealth and made available for the purposes of the Library;<lf> <lf>   (f)  to accept gifts, devises, bequests and assignments made to the Library (whether on trust or otherwise); and<lf> <lf>   (g)  to act as trustee of moneys, library material or other property vested in the Library upon trust, or to act on behalf of the Commonwealth or an authority of the Commonwealth in the administration of a trust relating to library material or library matters.<lf> <p>  (3) Notwithstanding anything contained in this Act, any money or property held by the Library upon trust shall be dealt with in accordance with the powers and duties of the Library as trustee.<lf> </lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960-1973 - SECT. 7A. Power to purchase and dispose of assets. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Inserted by No. 217, 1973, s. 5.<lf>   7A. (1) The Library shall not, without the approval of the Minister-<lf> <lf>   (a)  acquire any property, right or privilege for a consideration exceeding in amount or value Fifty thousand dollars or, if a higher amount is prescribed, that higher amount;<lf> <lf>   (b)  dispose of any property, right or privilege where the amount or value of the consideration for the disposal, or the value of the property, right or privilege, exceeds Fifty thousand dollars or, if a higher amount is prescribed, that higher amount;<lf> <lf>   (c)  enter into a contract for the construction of a building for the Library, being a contract under which the Library is to pay an amount exceeding Fifty thousand dollars or, if a higher amount is prescribed, that higher amount; or<lf> <lf>   (d)  enter into a lease of land for a period exceeding ten years.<lf> 

  (2) Except as otherwise approved by the Minister administering the Lands Acquisition Act 1955-1966, the purchase or the taking on lease of land or buildings required for the purposes of the Library shall be effected under that Act.<lf> </lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960-1973 - SECT. 8\. Transfer of library material, &c., to Library. </name>

## SECT

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960-1973 - SECT. 9\. Land and buildings. </name>

## SECT

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960-1973 - SECT. 10\. Constitution of Council. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>                      PART  III-MANAGEMENT  OF  THE  LIBRARY<lf>   10\. (1) The affairs of the Library shall be conducted by a Council to be known as the Council of the National Library of Australia.<lf> Amended by No. 217, 1973, s. 6\. 

  (2)  The Council shall consist of-<lf> <lf>  (aa)  the Director-General;<lf> <lf>   (a)  one Senator elected by the Senate;<lf> <lf>   (b)  one member of the House of Representatives elected by that House; and<lf> <lf>   (c)  nine members appointed by the Governor-General, being persons who, in the opinion of the Governor-General, by their knowledge and experience can advance the full development of the Library.<lf> <p>  (3) A member of the Council elected by either House of the Parliament holds office, subject to this Act, for such period, not exceeding three years, as is fixed by that House at the time of his election.<lf> <p>  (4) A member of the Council appointed by the Governor-General holds office, subject to this Act, for such period, not exceeding three years, as is fixed by the Governor-General at the time of his appointment.<lf> Amended by No. 217, 1973, s. 6\. <p>  (5) A member of the Council elected by either House of the Parliament or appointed by the Governor-General is eligible for re-election or re-appointment. <lf> <p>  (6) The exercise or performance of the powers or functions of the Council is not affected by reason only of there being a vacancy or vacancies in the membership of the Council.<lf> </lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</lf></lf></sect>

  (2) The appointment of an acting member may be terminated at any time by the Minister.<lf> </lf>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960-1973 - SECT. 16\. Meetings of Council. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   16\. (1) The Chairman, or if, for any reason, the Chairman is unable to act, the Deputy Chairman-<lf> <lf>   (a)  shall convene such meetings of the Council as he considers necessary for the efficient conduct of its business; and<lf> <lf>   (b)  shall, on receipt of a written request signed by not less than four members, convene a meeting of the Council.<lf> 

  (2) The Minister may at any time convene a meeting of the Council.<lf> <p>  (3) The Chairman shall preside at all meetings of the Council at which he is present.<lf> <p>  (4) In the event of the absence of the Chairman from a meeting of the Council, the Deputy Chairman shall preside at that meeting.<lf> <p>  (5) In the event of the absence of both the Chairman and the Deputy Chairman from a meeting of the Council, the members present shall appoint one of their number to preside at that meeting.<lf> <p>  (6) At a meeting of the Council, five members constitute a quorum.<lf> <p>  (7) Subject to the next succeeding sub-section, all questions arising at a meeting of the Council shall be decided by a majority of the votes of the members present and voting, and for this purpose the member presiding at the meeting has a deliberative vote.<lf> <p>  (8) In the event of an equality of votes on a resolution proposed at a meeting of the Council, the resolution shall be taken not to be passed, but if the same resolution is proposed at the next meeting of the Council held at a later date and there is again an equality of votes, the member presiding at that meeting has a casting vote on the proposed resolution.<lf> </lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960-1973 - SECT. 21\. Bank accounts. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Substituted by No. 217, 1973, s. 15.<lf>   21\. (1) The Library may open and maintain an account or accounts with an approved bank or approved banks and shall maintain at all times at least one such account.<lf> 

  (2) The Library shall pay all the moneys of the Library into an account referred to in this section.<lf> <p>  (3) In this section, ''approved bank'' means the Reserve Bank of Australia or another bank approved by the Treasurer.<lf> </lf></p></lf>

**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960-1973 - SECT. 22\. Application of moneys of Library. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   22\. The moneys of the Library shall be applied only-<lf> <lf>   (a)  in payment or discharge of the costs and expenses of the Library under this Act; and<lf> <lf>   (b)  in payment of the remuneration, allowances and expenses of the members of the Council.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960-1973 - SECT. 23\. Estimates. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Substituted by No. 217, 1973, s. 16.<lf>   23\. (1) The Council shall prepare estimates, in such form as the Minister directs, of receipts and expenditure of the Library for each financial year and, if the Minister so directs, for any other period specified by the Minister, and the Council shall submit estimates so prepared to the Minister not later than such date as the Minister directs.<lf> 

  (2) Moneys of the Library shall not be expended otherwise than in accordance with estimates of expenditure approved by the Minister.<lf> </lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960-1973 - SECT. 24\. Proper accounts to be kept. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   24\. The Council shall cause to be kept proper accounts and records of the transactions and affairs of the Library and shall do all things necessary to ensure that all payments out of the moneys of the Library are correctly made and properly authorized and that adequate control is maintained over the assets of, or in the custody of, the Library and the incurring of liabilities by the Library.<lf> </lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960-1973 - SECT. 25\. Audit. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Substituted by No. 217, 1973, s. 17.<lf>   25\. (1) The Auditor-General shall inspect and audit the accounts and records of financial transactions of the Library and records relating to assets of, or in the custody of, the Library and shall forthwith draw the attention of the Minister to any irregularity disclosed by the inspection and audit that is, in the opinion of the Auditor-General, of sufficient importance to justify his so doing.<lf> 

  (2) The Auditor-General may, at his discretion, dispense with all or any of the detailed inspection and audit of any accounts or records referred to in sub-section (1).<lf> <p>  (3) The Auditor-General shall, at least once in each year, report to the Minister the results of the inspection and audit carried out under sub-section (1).<lf> <p>  (4) The Auditor-General or an officer authorized by him is entitled at all reasonable times to full and free access to all accounts, records, documents and papers of the Library relating directly or indirectly to the receipt or payment of moneys by the Library or to the acquisition, receipt, custody or disposal of assets by the Library.<lf> <p>  (5) The Auditor-General or an officer authorized by him may make copies of, or take extracts from, any such accounts, records, documents or papers.<lf> <p>  (6) The Auditor-General or an officer authorized by him may require any person to furnish him with such information in the possession of the person or to which the person has access as the Auditor-General or authorized officer considers necessary for the purpose of the functions of the Auditor-General under this Act, and the person shall comply with the requirement.<lf> <p>  (7) A person who contravenes sub-section (6) is guilty of an offence punishable, upon conviction, by a fine not exceeding Two hundred dollars.<lf> </lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LIBRARY ACT 1960-1973 - SECT. 26\. Exemption from taxation. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Substituted by No. 217, 1973, s. 17.<lf>   26\. The Library is not subject to taxation under any law of the Commonwealth or of a State or Territory.<lf> </lf></lf></sect>

</b>** 

## SECT
