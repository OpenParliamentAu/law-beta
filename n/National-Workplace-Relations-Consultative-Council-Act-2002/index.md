**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LABOUR CONSULTATIVE COUNCIL ACT 1977 - LONG TITLE </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>     An Act to establish a National Labour Consultative Council<lf> </lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LABOUR CONSULTATIVE COUNCIL ACT 1977 - SECT 1 Short title </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   1\. This Act may be cited as the National Labour Consultative Council Act 1977.*1* SEE NOTES TO FIRST ARTICLE OF THIS CHAPTER . </sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LABOUR CONSULTATIVE COUNCIL ACT 1977 - SECT 2 Commencement </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   2\. This Act shall come into operation on the day on which it receives the Royal Assent.*1* SEE NOTES TO FIRST ARTICLE OF THIS CHAPTER . </sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LABOUR CONSULTATIVE COUNCIL ACT 1977 - SECT 3 Definitions </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   3\. In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:<lf>   appointed member means a member appointed by the Minister under subsection 6(2).<lf>   "Council" means the National Labour Consultative Council established by this Act;<lf>   "member" means a member of the Council. </lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LABOUR CONSULTATIVE COUNCIL ACT 1977 - SECT 4 National Labour Consultative Council </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   4\. There is established by this Act a council by the name of the National Labour Consultative Council. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LABOUR CONSULTATIVE COUNCIL ACT 1977 - SECT 5 Purpose of Council </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   5\. (1) The purpose of the Council is to provide, in the public interest, a regular and organized means by which representatives of the Government of the Commonwealth, of employers and of employees, and, when the Minister considers it appropriate, representatives of other persons, bodies and organisations may consult together on industrial relations matters, and manpower matters, of national concern. 

<lf>   (2) It is the intention of this Act that:<lf>   (a) meetings of the Council shall be conducted on a non-political basis;<lf>   (b) the Council will not interfere with the proper performance of the functions of industrial tribunals; and<lf>   (c) subject to the rights of persons participating in meetings of the Council to report to the persons, bodies and organisations by which they are nominated and to the right of the Council to make announcements that those persons agree are in the public interest, the views expressed at those meetings will be kept confidential. </lf></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LABOUR CONSULTATIVE COUNCIL ACT 1977 - SECT 6 Membership of Council </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   6\. (1) The Council is to consist of these 15 members:<lf>   (a) the Minister, who is to be the Chairperson of the Council;<lf>   (b) 7 members nominated by the Australian Council of Trade Unions;<lf>   (c) 3 members nominated by the Australian Chamber of Commerce and Industry;<lf>   (d) one member nominated by the Australian Chamber of Manufactures;<lf>   (e) one member nominated by the Metal Trades Industry Association of Australia;<lf>   (f) one member nominated by the National Farmers' Federation;<lf>   (g) one member nominated by Master Builders Australia. 

<lf>   (2) The Minister appoints the other members of the Council. <p><lf>   (3) Subject to this Act, a member (except the Minister) holds office for such period, not exceeding 2 years, as is specified in the instrument of appointment, but is eligible for re-appointment. <p><lf>   (4) A person who was a member of the Council immediately before the commencement of this section ceases at that commencement to be such a member, but is eligible for re-appointment. </lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LABOUR CONSULTATIVE COUNCIL ACT 1977 - SECT 7 Resignation of members </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   7\. An appointed member may resign his membership by writing signed by him and delivered to the Minister. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LABOUR CONSULTATIVE COUNCIL ACT 1977 - SECT 8 Termination of appointment of members </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   8\. (1) If an organization  which has nominated a member under subsection 6(1) requests the termination of the appointment of a member nominated by it, the Minister shall terminate the appointment of that member. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LABOUR CONSULTATIVE COUNCIL ACT 1977 - SECT 9 Travelling allowance for members </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   9\. A member is not entitled to remuneration, but is entitled, in the appropriate circumstances, to payment of travelling allowance at a rate equal to the highest rate of travelling allowance payable to officers of the Australian Public Service other than Secretaries within the meaning of the Public Service Act 1922\. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LABOUR CONSULTATIVE COUNCIL ACT 1977 - SECT 10 Substitute members </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   10\. (1) The Minister may nominate a person to attend a meeting of the Council in his or her place. 

<lf>   (2) If an appointed member is unable to be present at a meeting of the Council, the organization which nominated that member may nominate a person to attend the meeting in that member's place. <p><lf>   (3) A person nominated under subsection (1) or (2) to attend a meeting of the Council in the place of a member of the Council has, and may exercise, at that meeting all the rights of that member. </lf></p></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LABOUR CONSULTATIVE COUNCIL ACT 1977 - SECT 10A Invited representatives </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   10A.*2* (1) The Minister may, after consulting the Confederation of Australian Industry, the Australian Council of Trade Unions and the Metal Trades Industry Association of Australia, invite a person, body or organisation to nominate a representative for the purposes of this section. 

<lf>   (2) A representative so invited may participate in a meeting or meetings of the Council and its committees, in accordance with the invitation, but does not become a member of the Council or of a committee. <p><lf>   (3) If at any time the Minister terminates an invitation, the representative concerned ceases to be entitled to participate in meetings of the Council and its committees. <p><lf>   (4) While a representative is entitled to attend a meeting or meetings of the Council, the representative is entitled to payment of travelling allowance as if he or she were a member. *2* Section 10A (1)-Schedule 11, item 7 of the Industrial Relations and other Legislation Amendment Act 1995 provides as follows:<lf>   Omit "the Confederation of Australian Industry, the Australian Council of Trade Unions and the Metal Trades Industry Association,", substitute "the members of the Council,".<lf>   The proposed amendment was misdescribed and is not incorporated in this reprint. </lf></lf></lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LABOUR CONSULTATIVE COUNCIL ACT 1977 - SECT 11 Meetings of Council </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   11\. (1) The Council must meet:<lf>   (a) at least once each year during the period that begins on 1 January and ends on 30 June; and<lf>   (b) at least once each year during the period that begins on 1 July and ends on 31 December. 

<lf>   (2) The Minister may, at any time, convene a meeting of the Council and shall do so whenever the holding of a meeting is necesary to comply with subsection (1). <p><lf>   (3) The Minister shall convene a meeting of the Council whenever requested to do so by a majority of the members of the Council. <p><lf>   (4) The Minister shall preside at all meetings of the Council at which he is present, and, in his absence from a meeting, the person nominated under subsection 10 (1) to attend that meeting in the place of the Minister shall preside. <p><lf>   (5) Subject to this section, the procedure to be followed at a meeting of the Council shall be determined by the Council. </lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>NATIONAL LABOUR CONSULTATIVE COUNCIL ACT 1977 - SECT 12 Committees </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   12\. (1) The Council may, at any time, cause to be constituted a committee to consider, and report to the Council on, any matter relevant to the purpose of the Council referred to it by the Council. 

<lf>   (2) A member of a committee is not entitled to remuneration, but is entitled, in the appropriate circumstances, to payment of travelling allowance at a rate equal to the highest rate of travelling allowance payable to officers of the Australian Public Service other than Secretaries within the meaning of the Public Service Act 1922\. </lf>
</sect>
