###  Long Title

###  GOVERNOR-GENERAL ACT 1974- SECT 1  Short title [_see_ Note 1] 
<dl compact="">

		This Act may be cited as the _Governor-General Act 1974_.

 </dl>
###  GOVERNOR-GENERAL ACT 1974- SECT 2  Commencement [_see_ Note 1] 
<dl compact="">

		This Act shall come into operation on the day on which it receives the Royal

Assent.

 </dl>
###  GOVERNOR-GENERAL ACT 1974- SECT 2A  Interpretation 
<dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>References in sections 6, 7, 11, 12 and 18 to the Governor-General shall

be construed as references to the Governor-General acting with the advice of

the Executive Council.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>In this Act: </dd> </dl>
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_assessment_ has the same meaning as in the _Superannuation Contributions Tax (Assessment and Collection) Act 1997._ _basic rate_, at a particular time, in relation to a particular person, means 60% of the salary of the Chief Justice of Australia at that time, reduced by the amount of any pension or retiring allowance payable to that person at that time, whether by virtue of a law or otherwise out of money provided in whole or part by Australia, a State or a Territory. _notice of assessment_, in respect of a person, means a notice given under subsection 15(8) of the _Superannuation Contributions Tax (Assessment and Collection) Act 1997_ to the person or to the person's spouse, or to the legal personal representative of the person or the person's spouse, stating that a person is liable to pay an amount of surcharge on surchargeable contributions for the person. _Scheme_ means the scheme constituted by section 4 for the provision of retirement and other benefits to and in respect of the Governor-General. _surcharge_ has the same meaning as in the _Superannuation Contributions Tax (Assessment and Collection) Act 1997._ _surchargeable contributions_, for a person, means surchargeable contributions within the meaning of the _Superannuation Contributions Tax (Assessment and Collection) Act 1997_ that are attributable to the operation of this Act in respect of the person. _surcharge debt account,_ in relation to a person who has held office as Governor-General, means the surcharge debt account that was kept for the person (while he or she held that office) under section 16 of the _Superannuation Contributions Tax (Assessment and Collection) Act 1997_. _surcharge deduction amount_, in relation to a person:  </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>who has held office as Governor-General; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>to or in respect of whom an allowance is payable under this Act;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

means:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>for the purposes of calculating the prescribed percentage before the

payment of the allowance has commenced&#151;the amount by which the person's

surcharge debt account was in debit when the allowance became payable; or</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>for the purposes of calculating the prescribed percentage after the

</dd>

###  GOVERNOR-GENERAL ACT 1974- SECT 2B  Marital relationship 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>In this section and section 2C:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_deceased person_ means a person who was, at the time of his or her

death, the Governor-General or a person who has at any time after the

commencement of this Act held that office.

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>For the purposes of this Act, a person had a _marital relationship_ with a deceased person at a particular time if the person

ordinarily lived with the deceased person as the deceased person's husband or

wife on a permanent and _bona fide_ domestic basis at that time.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>For the purpose of subsection (2), a person is to be regarded as ordinarily living with a deceased person as the deceased person's husband or wife on a permanent and _bona fide_ domestic basis at a particular time only if: </dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the person had been living with the deceased person as the deceased

person's husband or wife for a continuous period of at least 3 years up to that

time; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the person had been living with the deceased person as the deceased

person's husband or wife for a continuous period of less than 3 years up to

that time and the Commissioner, having regard to any relevant evidence, is of

the opinion that the person ordinarily lived with the deceased person as the

deceased person's husband or wife on a permanent and _bona fide_ domestic

basis at that time;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

whether or not the person was legally married to the deceased person.

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(4)</dt><dd>For the purpose of subsection (3), relevant evidence includes, but is not

limited to, evidence establishing any of the following:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the person was wholly or substantially dependent on the deceased person at

the time;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the persons were legally married to each other at the time;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>the persons had a child who was:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>born of the relationship between the persons; or</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>adopted by the persons during the period of the relationship;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>the persons jointly owned a home which was their usual residence.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(5)</dt><dd>For the purposes of this section, a person is taken to be living with the

deceased person if the Commissioner is satisfied that the person would have

been living with the deceased person except for a period of:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>temporary absence; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>absence because of special circumstances (for example, absence because of

the person's illness or infirmity).

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

###  GOVERNOR-GENERAL ACT 1974- SECT 2C  Spouse of a deceased person 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		For the purposes of this Act, a person is a spouse of a deceased person if:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the person had a marital relationship with the deceased person at the time

of the deceased person's death; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the person:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>had previously had a marital relationship with the deceased person; and</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>did not, at the time of the death, have a marital relationship with the

deceased person but was legally married to the deceased person; and</dd>

<dt>(iii)</dt><dd>in the Commissioner's opinion, was wholly or substantially dependent on

the deceased person at the time of the death.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

**

###  GOVERNOR-GENERAL ACT 1974- SECT 3  Salary of Governor-General 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		The annual sum payable out of the Consolidated Revenue Fund for the salary of

the Governor-General shall be $365,000.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  GOVERNOR-GENERAL ACT 1974- SECT 4  Allowances 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Subject to subsection (4), where, after the commencement of this Act, a

person ceases to hold office as Governor-General, an allowance is payable under

this section to him or her during his or her life-time at such rate as is from

time to time payable under subsection (3).</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Subject to subsection (4) and section 4A, where, after the commencement of this Act, a person who is Governor-General dies, or a person who has at any time after the commencement of this Act held office as Governor-General dies, an allowance is payable under this section to a spouse of the person during the life-time of the spouse at such rate as is from time to time payable under subsection (3A).</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>The rate of the allowance payable at any time (_relevant time_) to a person who has held office as Governor-General is: </dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>if the person's surcharge debt account was in debit when the allowance

became payable to the person&#151;a rate equal to 85% of the basic rate or the

prescribed percentage of the basic rate at the relevant time, whichever is

higher; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>if the person's surcharge debt account was not in debit when the allowance

became payable to the person, whichever of the following rates is applicable:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>if a notice of assessment in respect of the person has been given before

the relevant time&#151;a rate equal to 85% of the basic rate or the prescribed

percentage of the basic rate at that time, whichever is higher;</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>if a notice of assessment in respect of the person has not been given

before the relevant time, and subsection&#160;(3AA) does not apply to the

person at that time&#151;a rate equal to 85% of the basic rate at that time;</dd>

<dt>(iii)</dt><dd>if a notice of assessment in respect of the person has not been given

before the relevant time, and subsection&#160;(3AA) applies to the person at

that time&#151;the basic rate at that time.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Note:	For _prescribed percentage_, see

subsection&#160;(3B).

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(3AA)</dt><dd>This subsection applies to a person at a particular time (the

_relevant time_) if, before the relevant time:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the trustee of the Scheme (within the meaning of section&#160;5A); and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the person or, if the person is deceased, the person's spouse or spouses

or legal personal representative;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

have reached a  en agreement that a notice of assessment is not likely to be given in respect of the person.

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(3A)</dt><dd>The rate of the allowance payable at any time (the _relevant time_) to a spouse of a deceased person who held office as

Governor-General is the amount worked out using the formula:

where:

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_adjustment amount_ means the amount of any pension or retiring

allowance that would have been payable to the deceased person at the relevant

time if the deceased person had not died, whether by virtue of a law or

otherwise out of money provided in whole or part by Australia, a State or a

Territory. _allowance rate_ means:  </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>if the person died while holding office as Governor-General&#151;the rate that

would be applicable at the relevant time to the deceased person under

subsection&#160;(3) if he or she had not died but had ceased to hold that

office; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>if the deceased person died after ceasing to hold office as

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_reduction amount_ means the amount of any pension or retiring

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

</dd>

the trustee of the Scheme (within the meaning of section&#160;5A) is liable to pay the surcharge under the assessment mentioned in paragraph&#160;(b), and any general interest charge in respect of the surcharge, to the Commissioner of Taxation, and the liability of any other person to pay the surcharge or general interest charge, as the case requires, is discharged.

<dt>(5)</dt><dd>Despite subsection&#160;(4), the trustee of the Scheme is not liable to

###  GOVERNOR-GENERAL ACT 1974- SECT 4A  Allowance payable when there is more than one spouse 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>If a person who held office or had held office as the Governor-General

(_deceased person_) dies leaving more than one spouse, the

Commissioner must allocate any allowance payable to a spouse of the deceased

person under this Act among the spouses.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Subject to subsection (3), an allowance is only payable to each spouse in accordance with the allocation.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>In making the allocation, the Commissioner must: </dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>have regard to the respective needs of each of the spouses; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>ensure that the aggregate of the rates of allowance applicable to the

spouses will not exceed:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>if the deceased person died while holding office as Governor-General&#151;100%

of the rate of allowance that would have been applicable to the deceased person

under subsection 4(3) if he or she had not died but had ceased to hold that

office; or</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>if the deceased person died after ceasing to hold office as

Governor-General&#151;100% of the rate of allowance that would have been applicable

to the deceased person under subsection 4(3) if he or she had not died; and

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>ensure that the rate of allowance applicable to each spouse will not

exceed the rate specified in subsection 4(3A).

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

###  GOVERNOR-GENERAL ACT 1974- SECT 5  Appropriation 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

which is appropriated accordingly.

 </dl></dl>

###  GOVERNOR-GENERAL ACT 1974- SECT 5A  Trustee of Scheme for the purposes of the _Superannuation Contributions Tax (Assessment and Collection) Act 1997_ 
**

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>For the purposes of the definition of _trustee_ in section 43

of the _Superannuation Contributions Tax (Assessment and Collection) Act 1997_:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the Secretary to the Department; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>if the Secretary to the Department has declared in writing an officer of

the Department to be the designated officer for the purposes of this

section&#151;that officer;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

is taken to be the person who manages the Scheme.

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Note:	The definitions of _public sector superannuation scheme_, _superannuation fund_ and _trustee_ in

section 43 of the _Superannuation Contributions Tax (Assessment and Collection) Act 1997_ are relevant to this section.

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>Section 4 of the _Superannuation Contributions Tax (Application to the CommonwealthReduction of Benefits) Act 1997_ does not apply to the person

who (because of subsection (1)) is the trustee of the Scheme for the purpose of

the _Superannuation Contributions Tax (Assessment and Collection) Act 1997_.

</dd> </dl></dl>

###  GOVERNOR-GENERAL ACT 1974- SECT 6  Official Secretary 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>There shall be an Official Secretary, who shall be appointed by the

Governor-General.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Official Secretary, together with the staff employed under section 13, constitute the Office of Official Secretary to the Governor-General.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>The function of the Office is to assist the Governor-General. </dd> </dl></dl>

**

###  GOVERNOR-GENERAL ACT 1974- SECT 7  Terms and conditions of appointment 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		The Official Secretary holds office on such terms and conditions (if any) in

respect of matters not provided for by this Act as are determined by the

Governor-General.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  GOVERNOR-GENERAL ACT 1974- SECT 8  Remuneration of Official Secretary 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Official Secretary shall be paid such remuneration as is determined by

the Remuneration Tribunal, but, if no determination of that remuneration by the

Remuneration Tribunal is in operation, the Official Secretary shall be paid

such remuneration as is prescribed.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Official Secretary shall be paid such allowances as are prescribed.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>This section has effect subject to the _Remuneration Tribunal Act 1973_. </dd> </dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

**

###  GOVERNOR-GENERAL ACT 1974- SECT 10  Resignation 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		The Official Secretary may resign from the office of Official Secretary by

writing delivered to the Governor-General.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  GOVERNOR-GENERAL ACT 1974- SECT 11  Termination of appointment 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		The Governor-General may at any time terminate the appointment of a person as

Official Secretary.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  GOVERNOR-GENERAL ACT 1974- SECT 12  Acting Official Secretary 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Governor-General may appoint a person to act as Official Secretary:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>during a vacancy in the office of Official Secretary, whether or not an

appointment has previously been made to the office; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>during any period, or during all periods, when the Official Secretary is

absent from duty or from Australia, or is, for any other reason, unable to

perform the functions of the office of Official Secretary;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

but a person appointed to act during a vacancy shall not continue so to act for more than 12 months.

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>An appointment of a person under subsection (1) may be expressed to have

effect only in such circumstances as are specified in the instrument of

appointment.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>The Governor-General may: </dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>determine the terms and conditions of appointment, including remuneration

and allowances, of a person acting as Official Secretary; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>terminate such an appointment at any time.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(4)</dt><dd>Where a person is acting as Official Secretary in circumstances referred

to in paragraph (1)(b) and the office of Official Secretary becomes vacant

while that person is so acting, then, subject to subsection (2), that person

may continue so to act until the Governor-General otherwise directs, the

vacancy is filled or a period of 12 months from the date on which the vacancy

occurred expires, whichever first happens.</dd> <dt>(5)</dt><dd>The appointment of a person to act as Official Secretary ceases to have effect if the person resigns the appointment by writing signed by the person and delivered to the Governor-General.</dd> <dt>(6)</dt><dd>While a person is acting as Official Secretary, the person has and may exercise all the powers and shall perform all the functions of the Official Secretary under this Act.</dd> <dt>(7)</dt><dd>The validity of anything done by a person purporting to act pursuant to an appointment made under subsection (1) shall not be called in question on the ground that the occasion for the person's appointment had not arisen, that there is a defect or irregularity in or in connection with the appointment, that the appointment had ceased to have effect or that the occasion for the person to act had not arisen or had ceased. </dd> </dl></dl>

**

###  GOVERNOR-GENERAL ACT 1974- SECT 13  Official Secretary may employ staff 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		The Official Secretary may, on behalf of the Commonwealth, employ, under an

agreement in writing, a person as a member of the Governor-General's staff.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  GOVERNOR-GENERAL ACT 1974- SECT 14  Remuneration and other conditions of employees 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		The remuneration and other conditions of employment of a person employed

under section 13 are as determined by the Official Secretary.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  GOVERNOR-GENERAL ACT 1974- SECT 15  Termination of employment <center>[Back to Top](#top)</center> 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Official Secretary may at any time, by notice in writing given to a

person employed under section 13, terminate the person's employment.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>A person employed by the Official Secretary under section 13 may at any time, by notice in writing given to the Official Secretary, terminate the person's employment. </dd> </dl></dl>

**

###  GOVERNOR-GENERAL ACT 1974- SECT 19  Annual Report 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Official Secretary shall, as soon as practicable after 30 June in each

year, prepare and furnish to the Minister a report on the performance of the

functions and duties of the Official Secretary during that year.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Minister shall cause a copy of a report furnished to him or her under subsection (1) to be laid before each House of the Parliament within 15 sitting days of that House after the day on which he or she receives the report. </dd> </dl></dl>

**

###  GOVERNOR-GENERAL ACT 1974- SECT 20  Regulations 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		The Governor-General may make regulations, not inconsistent with this Act,

prescribing matters:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>required or permitted by this Act to be prescribed; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>necessary or convenient to be prescribed for carrying out or giving effect

to this Act.

</dd>
