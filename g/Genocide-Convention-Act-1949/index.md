### <name>GENOCIDE CONVENTION ACT 1949 - LONG TITLE </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> An Act to approve of Ratification by Australia of the Convention on the Prevention and Punishment of the Crime of Genocide, and for other purposes<lf> </lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>GENOCIDE CONVENTION ACT 1949 - SECT 1 Short title </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   1\. This Act may be cited as the Genocide Convention Act 1949.*1* 

SEE NOTES TO FIRST ARTICLE OF THIS CHAPTER . 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>GENOCIDE CONVENTION ACT 1949 - SECT 2 Commencement </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   2\. This Act shall come into operation on the day on which it receives the Royal Assent.*1* 

SEE NOTES TO FIRST ARTICLE OF THIS CHAPTER . 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>GENOCIDE CONVENTION ACT 1949 - SECT 3 Interpretation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   3\. In this Act-<lf>   "the Genocide Convention" means the Convention on the Prevention and Punishment of the Crime of Genocide approved by the General Assembly of the United Nations at Paris on the ninth day of December, One thousand nine hundred and forty-eight, the text of which convention in the English language is set out in the Schedule to this Act. </lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>GENOCIDE CONVENTION ACT 1949 - SECT 4 Approval of ratification </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   4\. Approval is hereby given to the depositing with the Secretary-General of the United Nations of an instrument of ratification of the Genocide Convention by Australia. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>GENOCIDE CONVENTION ACT 1949 - SECT 5 Approval of extension to Territories </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   5\. Approval is hereby given to the depositing with the Secretary-General of the United Nations of a notification by Australia, in accordance with Article twelve of the Genocide Convention, extending the application of the Genocide Convention to all the territories for the conduct of whose foreign relations Australia is responsible.

### <name>GENOCIDE CONVENTION ACT 1949 - SCHEDULE 1 </name>
</b>** 

## SCH
<sch>                                  THE  SCHEDULE<lf>                                                                        Section 3 <lf>  CONVENTION  ON  THE  PREVENTION  AND  PUNISHMENT  OF  THE  CRIME  OF GENOCIDE<lf> THE  CONTRACTING  PARTIES,<lf>   HAVING CONSIDERED the declaration made by the General Assembly of the United<lf> Nations in its resolution 96 (1) dated 11 December 1946 that genocide is a crime <lf> under international law, contrary to the spirit and aims of the United Nations<lf> and condemned by the civilized world;<lf>   RECOGNIZING that at all periods of history genocide has inflicted great losses <lf> on humanity; and<lf>   BEING CONVINCED that, in order to liberate mankind from such an odious<lf> scourge, international co-operation is required;<lf>   HEREBY AGREE AS HEREINAFTER PROVIDED:<lf>                                    Article I<lf>   The Contracting Parties confirm that genocide, whether committed in time of<lf> peace or in time of war, is a crime under international law which they undertake <lf> to prevent and to punish.<lf> 

                                   Article II<lf>   In the present Convention, genocide means any of the following acts committed<lf> with intent to destroy, in whole or in part, a national, ethnical, racial or<lf> religious group, as such:<lf>   (a)  Killing members of the group;<lf>          (b)  Causing serious bodily or mental harm to members of the group;<lf>          (c)  Deliberately inflicting on the group conditions of life calculated to<lf>        bring about its physical destruction in whole or in part;<lf>          (d)  Imposing measures intended to prevent births within the group;<lf>          (e)  Forcibly transferring children of the group to another group.<lf>  <p>                                         Article III<lf>          The following acts shall be punishable:<lf>          (a)  Genocide;<lf>          (b)  Conspiracy to commit genocide;<lf>          (c)  Direct and public incitement to commit genocide;<lf>          (d)  Attempt to commit genocide;<lf>          (e)  Complicity in genocide.<lf>  <p>                                          Article IV<lf>   Persons committing genocide or any of the other acts enumerated in article III <lf> shall be punished, whether they are constitutionally responsible rulers, public<lf> officials or private individuals.<lf> <p>                                   Article V<lf>   The Contracting Parties undertake to enact, in accordance with their<lf> respective Constitutions, the necessary legislation to give effect to the<lf> provisions of the present Convention and, in particular, to provide effective<lf> penalties for persons guilty of genocide or any of the other acts enumerated in<lf> article III.<lf> <p>                                   Article VI<lf>   Persons charged with genocide or any of the other acts enumerated in article<lf> III shall be tried by a competent tribunal of the State in the territory of<lf> which the act was committed, or by such international penal tribunal as may have <lf> jurisdiction with respect to those Contracting Parties which shall have accepted <lf> its jurisdiction.<lf> <p>                                  Article VII<lf>   Genocide and the other acts enumerated in article III shall not be considered<lf> as political crimes for the purpose of extradition.<lf> <p>  The Contracting Parties pledge themselves in such cases to grant extradition<lf> in accordance with their laws and treaties in force.<lf> <p>                                  Article VIII<lf>   Any Contracting Party may call upon the competent organs of the United Nations <lf> to take such action under the Charter of the United Nations as they consider<lf> appropriate for the prevention and suppression of acts of genocide or any of the <lf> other acts enumerated in article III.<lf> <p>                                   Article IX<lf>   Disputes between the Contracting Parties relating to the interpretation,<lf> application or fulfilment of the present Convention, including those relating to <lf> the responsibility of a State for genocide or any of the other acts enumerated<lf> in article III, shall be submitted to the International Court of Justice at the<lf> request of any of the parties to the dispute.<lf> <p>                                   Article X<lf>   The present Convention, of which the Chinese, English, French, Russian and<lf> Spanish texts are equally authentic, shall bear the date of 9 December 1948.<lf> <p>                                   Article XI<lf>   The present Convention shall be open until 31 December 1949 for signature on<lf> behalf of any Member of the United Nations and of any non-member State to which<lf> an invitation to sign has been addressed by the General Assembly.<lf> <p>  The present Convention shall be ratified, and the instruments of ratification<lf> shall be deposited with the Secretary-General of the United Nations.<lf> <p>  After 1 January 1950 the present Convention may be acceded to on behalf of any <lf> Member of the United Nations and of any non-member State which has received an<lf> invitation as aforesaid.<lf> <p>  Instruments of accession shall be deposited with the Secretary-General of the<lf> United Nations.<lf> <p>                                  Article XII<lf>   Any Contracting Party may at any time, by notification addressed to the<lf> Secretary-General of the United Nations, extend the application of the present<lf> Convention to all or any of the territories for the conduct of whose foreign<lf> relations that Contracting Party is responsible.<lf> <p>                                  Article XIII<lf>   On the day when the first twenty instruments of ratification or accession have <lf> been deposited, the Secretary-General shall draw up a proces-verbal and transmit <lf> a copy thereof to each Member of the United Nations and to each of the<lf> non-member States contemplated in article XI.<lf> <p>  The present Convention shall come into force on the ninetieth day following<lf> the date of deposit of the twentieth instrument of ratification or accession.<lf> <p>  Any ratification or accession effected subsequent to the latter date shall<lf> become effective on the ninetieth day following the deposit of the instrument of <lf> ratification or accession.<lf> <p>                                  Article XIV<lf>   The present Convention shall remain in effect for a period of ten years as<lf> from the date of its coming into force.<lf> <p>  It shall thereafter remain in force for successive periods of five years for<lf> such Contracting Parties as have not denounced it at least six months before the <lf> expiration of the current period.<lf> <p>  Denunciation shall be effected by a written notification addressed to the<lf> Secretary-General of the United Nations.<lf> <p>                                   Article XV<lf>   If, as a result of denunciations, the number of Parties to the present<lf> Convention should become less than sixteen, the Convention shall cease to be in<lf> force as from the date on which the last of these denunciations shall become<lf> effective.<lf> <p>                                  Article XVI<lf>   A request for the revision of the present Convention may be made at any time<lf> by any Contracting Party by means of a notification in writing addressed to the<lf> Secretary-General.<lf> <p>  The General Assembly shall decide upon the steps, if any, to be taken in<lf> respect of such request.<lf> <p>                                  Article XVII<lf>   The Secretary-General of the United Nations shall notify all Members of the<lf> United Nations and the non-member States contemplated in Article XI of the<lf> following:<lf>   (a)  Signatures, ratifications and accessions received in accordance with<lf>       Article XI;<lf>         (b)  Notifications received in accordance with Article XII;<lf>         (c)  The date upon which the present Convention comes into force in accordance<lf>        with Article XIII;<lf>          (d)  Denunciations received in accordance with Article XIV;<lf>          (e)  The abrogation of the Convention in accordance with Article XV;<lf>          (f)  Notifications received in accordance with Article XVI.<lf>  <p>                                        Article XVIII<lf>          The original of the present Convention shall be deposited in the archives of<lf>        the United Nations.<lf>  <p>         A certified copy of the Convention shall be transmitted to each Member of the<lf>        United Nations and to each of the non-member States contemplated in Article XI.<lf>  <p>                                         Article XIX<lf>          The present Convention shall be registered by the Secretary-General of the<lf>        United Nations on the date of its coming into force.<lf>
