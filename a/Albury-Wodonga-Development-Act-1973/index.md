## Part III—Establishment, functions and powers of the Albury-Wodonga Development Corporation<o:p></o:p>

##### <span style="mso-bookmark: _Toc98133334"><a id="7"></a>7<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>Albury</span>-Wodonga Development Corporation<o:p></o:p>

##### <span style="mso-bookmark: _Toc98133335"><a id="8"></a>8<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>Functions</span> and powers of Corporation<o:p></o:p>

             (1)  The functions of the Corporation under this Act are:<o:p></o:p>

                     (a)  to prepare the Corporation, over a period ascertained in accordance with the Winding-up Agreement, for abolition by a future Act; and<o:p></o:p>

                     (b)  for the purposes of preparing the Corporation for abolition as mentioned in paragraph (a), to dispose of the assets of the Corporation; and<o:p></o:p>

                     (c)  to assist the Commonwealth and other persons in connection with the winding-up of the Corporation and the joint Commonwealth/State scheme; and<o:p></o:p>

                     (d)  for the purposes of facilitating the winding-up of the Corporation and the joint Commonwealth/State scheme:<o:p></o:p>

                              (i)  to acquire assets that were previously held by the Albury-Wodonga (<st1:state><st1:place style="BACKGROUND-POSITION: left bottom; BACKGROUND-IMAGE: url(res://ietag.dll/#34/#1001); BACKGROUND-REPEAT: repeat-x">New South Wales</st1:place></st1:state>) Corporation or the Albury-Wodonga (Victoria) Corporation; and<o:p></o:p>

                             (ii)  to hold such assets; and<o:p></o:p>

                            (iii)  to prepare such assets for disposal (including by way of developing or improving such assets); and<o:p></o:p>

                            (iv)  to dispose of such assets; and<o:p></o:p>

                     (e)  if requested by or on behalf of the Commonwealth, or by or on behalf of an authority of the Commonwealth, to do so—to act as the agent of the Commonwealth, or the authority of the Commonwealth, in relation to the disposal of land in the Albury-Wodonga region.<o:p></o:p>

             (2)  The Corporation has power to do all things necessary or convenient to be done for or in connexion with, or as incidental to, the performance of its functions and, in particular, without limiting the generality of the foregoing, has power:<o:p></o:p>

                     (a)  to enter into contracts and agreements; and<o:p></o:p>

                     (b)  either directly or by arrangement with other persons, to construct buildings and works and carry on services.<o:p></o:p>

             (3)  Where it appears to the Minister that an Act of the Parliament of the State of New South Wales or of the State of Victoria or part of such an Act confers or imposes on the Corporation functions, powers or duties for the purposes of the Winding-up Agreement or for purposes that are otherwise complementary to this Act, the Minister may, by notice published in the _Gazette_, declare that Act or that part of that Act, as the case may be, to be complementary to this Act.<o:p></o:p>

             (4)  It is hereby declared to be the intention of the Parliament that the Corporation may have and be subject to functions, powers and duties specified by an Act or part of an Act for the time being declared under subsection (3) to be complementary to this Act.<o:p></o:p>

             (5)  For the purposes of subsection (3), each of the following purposes is taken to be a purpose that is complementary to this Act:<o:p></o:p>

                     (a)  the purpose of facilitating the disposal of the assets of the Corporation;<o:p></o:p>

                     (b)  the purpose of facilitating the winding-up of the Corporation;<o:p></o:p>

                     (c)  the purpose of facilitating the winding-up of the joint Commonwealth/State scheme.<o:p></o:p>

          (5A)  For the purposes of subsection (3), if an Act of New South Wales, or a part of such an Act, confers or imposes on the Corporation functions, powers or duties of a kind that were previously conferred or imposed on the Albury-Wodonga (New South Wales) Corporation, that Act or that part of that Act, as the case may be, is taken to confer or impose those functions, powers or duties for purposes that are complementary to this Act.<o:p></o:p>

          (5B)  For the purposes of subsection (3), if an Act of Victoria, or a part of such an Act, confers or imposes on the Corporation functions, powers or duties of a kind that were previously conferred or imposed on the Albury-Wodonga (Victoria) Corporation, that Act or that part of that Act, as the case may be, is taken to confer or impose those functions, powers or duties for purposes that are complementary to this Act.<o:p></o:p>

             (6)  The Corporation shall comply with any written directions given to it by the Minister with respect to the performance of its functions, the exercise of its powers and its procedures.<o:p></o:p>

             (7)  The Corporation shall comply in all respects with the provisions of the Winding-up Agreement that are applicable to it.<o:p></o:p>

             (8)  This section shall not be read as limiting the matters that may be dealt with by Departments or authorities of the Commonwealth, other than the Corporation.<o:p></o:p>

             (9)  To avoid doubt, a reference in subsection (1) to:<o:p></o:p>

                     (a)  the abolition or winding-up of the Corporation; or<o:p></o:p>

                     (b)  the winding-up of the joint Commonwealth/State scheme;<o:p></o:p>

does not, by implication:<o:p></o:p>

                     (c)  require the Corporation to dispose of all of its assets; or<o:p></o:p>

                     (d)  prevent functions, powers or duties being conferred or imposed on the Corporation by a law of <st1:state><st1:place style="BACKGROUND-POSITION: left bottom; BACKGROUND-IMAGE: url(res://ietag.dll/#34/#1001); BACKGROUND-REPEAT: repeat-x">New South Wales</st1:place></st1:state> or <st1:state><st1:place style="BACKGROUND-POSITION: left bottom; BACKGROUND-IMAGE: url(res://ietag.dll/#34/#1001); BACKGROUND-REPEAT: repeat-x">Victoria</st1:place></st1:state>.<o:p></o:p>

           (10)  It is the intention of the Parliament that subsection (1) is not to apply to the exclusion of a law of <st1:state><st1:place style="BACKGROUND-POSITION: left bottom; BACKGROUND-IMAGE: url(res://ietag.dll/#34/#1001); BACKGROUND-REPEAT: repeat-x">New South Wales</st1:place></st1:state> or <st1:state><st1:place style="BACKGROUND-POSITION: left bottom; BACKGROUND-IMAGE: url(res://ietag.dll/#34/#1001); BACKGROUND-REPEAT: repeat-x">Victoria</st1:place></st1:state> to the extent to which that law is capable of operating concurrently with that subsection.<o:p></o:p>

##### <span style="mso-bookmark: _Toc98133338"><a id="9"></a>9<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>Nature</span> of Corporation <o:p></o:p>

##### <span style="mso-bookmark: _Toc98133339"><a id="9A"></a>9A<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>_Commonwealth_</span>_Authorities and Companies Act 1997_ does not apply to Corporation<o:p></o:p>

##### <span style="mso-bookmark: _Toc98133340"><a id="10"></a>10<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>Composition</span> of Corporation<o:p></o:p>

             (1)  The Corporation consists of:<o:p></o:p>

                     (a)  the Chairperson; and<o:p></o:p>

                     (e)  2 other members; and<o:p></o:p>

                      (f)  the chief executive officer.<o:p></o:p>

             (2)  The appointed members are to be appointed by the Governor-General.<o:p></o:p>

             (3)  A person is not to be appointed as:<o:p></o:p>

                     (a)  the Chairperson; or<o:p></o:p>

                     (b)  a member referred to in paragraph (1)(e);<o:p></o:p>

unless he or she appears to the Minister to be qualified for appointment because of his or her knowledge and understanding of issues relating to the development of the Albury-Wodonga region.<o:p></o:p>

             (6)  The exercise of the functions or powers of the Corporation is not affected by reason of there being a vacancy or vacancies in the membership of the Corporation.<o:p></o:p>

##### <span style="mso-bookmark: _Toc98133341"><a id="10A"></a>10A<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>Appointment</span> of chief executive officer<o:p></o:p>

##### <span style="mso-bookmark: _Toc98133342"><a id="11"></a>11<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>Period</span> of appointment of appointed members<o:p></o:p>

##### <span style="mso-bookmark: _Toc98133343"><a id="12"></a>12<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>Remuneration</span> and allowances for appointed members<o:p></o:p>

             (1)  An appointed member shall be paid such remuneration as is determined by the Remuneration Tribunal, but, if no determination of that remuneration by the Tribunal is in operation, a member shall be paid such remuneration as is prescribed.<o:p></o:p>

             (2)  An appointed member shall be paid such allowances as are prescribed.<o:p></o:p>

             (3)  If an appointed member is a person who, if he or she were paid remuneration or allowances under this section, would cease to hold an office or position or cease to be entitled to a pension or other moneys, other than a pension or moneys payable under the _Social Security Act 1991_, the _Veterans’ Entitlements Act 1986_ or a prescribed law of the Commonwealth or of a State or Territory, the member shall not be paid remuneration or allowances under this section, but shall, subject to the approval of the Minister, be reimbursed such expenses as are reasonably incurred by reason of the person being a member.<o:p></o:p>

             (4)  This section has effect subject to the _Remuneration Tribunal Act 1973_.<o:p></o:p>

##### <span style="mso-bookmark: _Toc98133344"><a id="14"></a>14<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>Resignation</span> of members<o:p></o:p>

##### <span style="mso-bookmark: _Toc98133345"><a id="15"></a>15<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>Dismissal</span> of appointed members<o:p></o:p>

             (1)  The Governor-General may terminate the appointment of an appointed member for inability, inefficiency, misbehaviour or physical or mental incapacity.<o:p></o:p>

             (2)  If an appointed member:<o:p></o:p>

                     (c)  is absent, except on leave granted by the Corporation, from 3 consecutive meetings of the Corporation;<o:p></o:p>

                     (d)  becomes bankrupt or applies to take the benefit of any law for the relief of bankrupt or insolvent debtors, compounds with his or her creditors or makes an assignment of his or her remuneration for their benefit;<o:p></o:p>

                     (e)  is or becomes an officer or employee of the Corporation;<o:p></o:p>

                      (f)  in any way, otherwise than as a member, and in common with other members, of an incorporated company consisting of not fewer than twenty-five persons:<o:p></o:p>

                              (i)  is or becomes directly or indirectly interested in a contract made or proposed to be made by the Corporation; or<o:p></o:p>

                             (ii)  participates or claims to participate in the profits of any such contract or in any benefit arising from any such contract; or<o:p></o:p>

                     (g)  fails to comply with section 16;<o:p></o:p>

the Governor-General shall terminate the appointment of the member.<o:p></o:p>

##### <span style="mso-bookmark: _Toc98133346"><a id="16"></a>16<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>Disclosure</span> of interest by members<o:p></o:p>

             (1)  Without limiting paragraph 15(2)(f), a member who is directly or indirectly interested in:<o:p></o:p>

                     (a)  a contract made or proposed to be made by the Corporation;<o:p></o:p>

                     (b)  land in the Albury-Wodonga region; or<o:p></o:p>

                     (c)  an existing or proposed project of the Corporation;<o:p></o:p>

shall, as soon as possible after the relevant facts have come to his or her knowledge, disclose the nature of his or her interest at a meeting of the Corporation.<o:p></o:p>

             (2)  Without limiting paragraph 15(2)(f), a disclosure under subsection (1) shall be recorded in the minutes of the Corporation, and, unless the Minister otherwise directs, the member:<o:p></o:p>

                     (a)  shall not take part after the disclosure in any deliberation or decision of the Corporation relating to the contract, land or project, as the case requires; and<o:p></o:p>

                     (b)  shall be disregarded for the purpose of constituting the quorum for any such deliberation or decision.<o:p></o:p>

             (3)  A member who fails to comply with subsection (1) or paragraph (2)(a) is guilty of an offence and is punishable, upon conviction, by a fine not exceeding $500.<o:p></o:p>

             (4)  An offence under subsection (3) is an offence of strict liability.<o:p></o:p>

Note:          For **_strict liability_**, see section 6.1 of the _Criminal Code_.<o:p></o:p>

##### <span style="mso-bookmark: _Toc98133347"><a id="17"></a>17<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>Condition</span> of service—appointed member<o:p></o:p>

                   Subject to this Act, the appointed members hold office on such terms and conditions as the Minister determines.<o:p></o:p>

##### <span style="mso-bookmark: _Toc98133348"><a id="18"></a>18<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>Acting</span> appointments<o:p></o:p>

             (1)  Where the Chairperson is, or is expected to be, absent from duty or from <st1:country-region><st1:place style="BACKGROUND-POSITION: left bottom; BACKGROUND-IMAGE: url(res://ietag.dll/#34/#1001); BACKGROUND-REPEAT: repeat-x">Australia</st1:place></st1:country-region> or there is a vacancy in the office of Chairperson, the Minister may appoint a person to be acting Chairperson during the absence or until the filling of the vacancy.<o:p></o:p>

             (3)  An acting Chairperson appointed in the event of a vacancy shall not continue in office after the expiration of 12 months after the occurrence of the vacancy.<o:p></o:p>

             (4)  An acting Chairperson has all the functions, powers and duties of the Chairperson.<o:p></o:p>

             (5)  In the event of the inability of a member referred to in paragraph 10(1)(e) (whether on account of illness or otherwise) to attend the meetings of the Corporation, the Minister may appoint a person to be an acting member during that inability, and the person so appointed has all the functions, powers and duties of a member referred to in paragraph 10(1)(e).<o:p></o:p>

             (7)  The Minister may, at any time, terminate an appointment under this section.<o:p></o:p>

             (8)  Subject to this section, a person appointed under this section holds office on such terms and conditions as the Minister determines.<o:p></o:p>

             (9)  The validity of an act done by the Corporation shall not be questioned in any proceedings on a ground arising from the fact that the occasion for the appointment of a person purporting to be appointed under this section had not arisen or that an appointment under this section had ceased to have effect.<o:p></o:p>

##### <span style="mso-bookmark: _Toc98133349"><a id="19"></a>19<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>Meetings</span><o:p></o:p>

             (1)  The Corporation shall hold such meetings as are necessary for the performance of its functions.<o:p></o:p>

                     (b)  with the approval of the Finance Minister, borrow moneys from an approved bank, or any other lender, for the purposes of the Corporation in connexion with the performance of its functions; and<o:p></o:p>

                     (c)  give security over any of its assets for the purposes of paragraph (b).<o:p></o:p>

             (2)  The Corporation shall not borrow moneys otherwise than in accordance with this section.<o:p></o:p>

             (3)  The Finance Minister may, on behalf of the Commonwealth, guarantee the repayment of amounts borrowed in accordance with paragraph (1)(b) and the payment of interest on amounts so borrowed.<o:p></o:p>

##### <span style="mso-bookmark: _Toc98133367"><a id="27"></a>27<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>Bank</span> accounts<o:p></o:p>

             (1)  The Corporation may open and maintain an account or accounts with an approved bank or approved banks and shall maintain at all times at least one such account.<o:p></o:p>

             (2)  The Corporation shall pay all moneys of the Corporation, including moneys borrowed by the Corporation, into an account referred to in subsection (1).<o:p></o:p>

##### <span style="mso-bookmark: _Toc98133368"><a id="28"></a>28<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>Application</span> of moneys<o:p></o:p>

             (1)  The moneys of the Corporation may be applied by the Corporation:<o:p></o:p>

                     (a)  in payment or discharge of the costs, expenses and other obligations of the Corporation under this Act (including the Winding-up Agreement); or<o:p></o:p>

                     (b)  in payment of any remuneration or allowances payable to any person under this Act (including the Winding-up Agreement);<o:p></o:p>

but not otherwise.<o:p></o:p>

             (2)  Moneys of the Corporation not immediately required for the purposes of the Corporation may be invested:<o:p></o:p>

                     (a)  on fixed deposit with an approved bank;<o:p></o:p>

                     (b)  in securities of the Commonwealth; or<o:p></o:p>

                     (c)  in any other manner approved by the Finance Minister;<o:p></o:p>

but not otherwise.<o:p></o:p>

##### <span style="mso-bookmark: _Toc98133369"><a id="30"></a>30<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>Audit</span><o:p></o:p>

             (1)  The Auditor-General shall inspect and audit the accounts and records of financial transactions of the Corporation and records relating to assets of, or in the custody of, the Corporation and shall forthwith draw the attention of the Minister to any irregularity disclosed by the inspection and audit that is, in the opinion of the Auditor-General, of sufficient importance to justify his so doing.<o:p></o:p>

             (2)  The Auditor-General may dispense with all or any part of the detailed inspection and audit of any accounts or records referred to in subsection (1).<o:p></o:p>

             (3)  The Auditor-General shall, at least once in each year, report to the Minister the results of the inspection and audit carried out under subsection (1).<o:p></o:p>

             (5)  The Auditor-General or a person authorized by the Auditor-General is entitled at all reasonable times to full and free access to all accounts, records, documents and papers of the Corporation relating directly or indirectly to the receipt or payment of moneys by the Corporation or to the acquisition, receipt, custody or disposal of assets by the Corporation.<o:p></o:p>

             (6)  The Auditor-General or a person authorized by the Auditor-General may make copies of, or take extracts from, any such accounts, records, documents or papers.<o:p></o:p>

             (7)  The Auditor-General or a person authorized by the Auditor-General may require any person to provide such information in the possession of the person or to which the person has access as the Auditor-General or authorized person considers necessary for the purposes of the functions of the Auditor-General under this Act, and the person shall comply with the requirement.<o:p></o:p>

             (8)  A person who contravenes subsection (7) is guilty of an offence punishable, upon conviction, by a fine not exceeding $200.<o:p></o:p>

             (9)  An offence under subsection (8) is an offence of strict liability.<o:p></o:p>

Note:          For **_strict liability_**, see section 6.1 of the _Criminal Code_.<o:p></o:p>

##### <span style="mso-bookmark: _Toc98133370"><a id="31"></a>31<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>Liability</span> to taxation<o:p></o:p>

             (1)  Subject to subsection (2), the Corporation is not subject to taxation under any law of the Commonwealth or of a State or Territory.<o:p></o:p>

             (2)  The regulations may provide that subsection (1) does not apply in relation to taxation under a specified law.<o:p></o:p>

## Part VII—Miscellaneous<o:p></o:p>

##### <span style="mso-bookmark: _Toc98133372"><a id="31A"></a>31A<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>Delegation</span> by Minister<o:p></o:p>

             (1)  The Minister may, by writing, delegate to:<o:p></o:p>

                     (a)  the Secretary to the Department; or<o:p></o:p>

                     (b)  a person holding or performing the duties of a Senior Executive Service office (within the meaning of the _Public Service Act 1922_); or<o:p></o:p>

                     (c)  an SES employee or acting SES employee;<o:p></o:p>

all or any of the Minister’s powers under this Act (other than section 5A).<o:p></o:p>

             (2)  The delegate is, in the exercise of the power delegated under subsection (1), subject to the directions of the Minister.<o:p></o:p>

##### <span style="mso-bookmark: _Toc98133373"><a id="31B"></a>31B<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>Delegation</span> by Finance Minister

             (1)  The Finance Minister may, by written instrument, delegate any of the Finance Minister’s powers or functions under section 3 (definition of **_approved bank_**), 26 or 28 to an official (within the meaning of the _Financial Management and Accountability Act 1997_).

             (2)  In exercising powers or functions under a delegation, the official must comply with any directions of the Finance Minister.

##### <span style="mso-bookmark: _Toc98133374"><a id="32"></a>32<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>Annual</span> Report<o:p></o:p>

                   The Corporation shall, as soon as practicable after each financial year:<o:p></o:p>

                     (a)  prepare a report, in the form approved by the Finance Minister, of the Corporation’s operations during that year together with financial statements in respect of that year; and<o:p></o:p>

                     (b)  submit a copy of the report and the financial statements to the Minister, for presentation to the Parliament.<o:p></o:p>

##### <span style="mso-bookmark: _Toc98133375"><a id="33"></a>33<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>Annual</span> financial statement<o:p></o:p>

                   Before furnishing the Corporation’s financial statements to the Minister, the Corporation shall submit them to the Auditor-General, who shall report to the Minister:<o:p></o:p>

                     (a)  whether the statements are based on proper accounts and records;<o:p></o:p>

                     (b)  whether the statements are in agreement with the accounts and records;<o:p></o:p>

                     (c)  whether the receipt, expenditure and investment of moneys, and the acquisition and disposal of assets, by the Corporation during the relevant year have been in accordance with this Act; and<o:p></o:p>

                     (d)  such other matters arising out of the statements as the Auditor-General considers should be reported to the Minister.<o:p></o:p>

##### <span style="mso-bookmark: _Toc98133376"><a id="34"></a>34 <span style="mso-spacerun: yes"> </span>Regulations</span><o:p></o:p>

             (1)  The Governor-General may make regulations, not inconsistent with this Act, prescribing all matters required or permitted by this Act to be prescribed, or necessary or convenient to be prescribed for carrying out or giving effect to this Act.<o:p></o:p>

### Application, saving or transitional provisions<o:p></o:p>

_Albury-Wodonga Development Amendment Act 2000 (No. 46, 2000)<o:p></o:p>_

## Schedule 1<o:p></o:p>

## 65<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>Application—subsection 10(3) of the _Albury-Wodonga Development Act 1973_

Subsection 10(3) of the _Albury-Wodonga Development Act 1973_ as amended by this Act applies to appointments made after the commencement of this item.<o:p></o:p>

## 66<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>Application—subsection 10(7) of the _Albury-Wodonga Development Act 1973_

Despite the repeal of subsection 10(7) of the _Albury-Wodonga Development Act 1973_ by this Schedule, that subsection continues to apply, in relation to appointments made before the commencement of this item, as if that repeal had not happened.<o:p></o:p>

## 67<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>Transitional—chief executive officer 

To avoid doubt, the amendments of the _Albury-Wodonga Development Act 1973_ made by this Schedule do not affect the continuity of anything done under section 10A of that Act before the commencement of this item.<o:p></o:p>

## 68<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>Transitional—section 17 of the _Albury-Wodonga Development Act 1973_<o:p></o:p>

(1)        This item applies to a determination that was in force under section 17 of the _Albury-Wodonga Development Act 1973_ immediately before the commencement of this item.<o:p></o:p>

(2)        The determination has effect, after the commencement of this item, as if it were a determination made by the Minister under section 17 of the _Albury-Wodonga Development Act 1973_ as amended by this Act.<o:p></o:p>

## 69<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>Transitional—subsection 18(8) of the _Albury-Wodonga Development Act 1973_<o:p></o:p>

(1)        This item applies to a determination that was in force under subsection 18(8) of the _Albury-Wodonga Development Act 1973_ immediately before the commencement of this item.<o:p></o:p>

(2)        The determination has effect, after the commencement of this item, as if it were a determination made by the Minister under subsection 18(8) of the _Albury-Wodonga Development Act 1973_ as amended by this Act.<o:p></o:p>

## 70<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>Transitional—subsection 20B(1) of the _Albury-Wodonga Development Act 1973_<o:p></o:p>

Subsection 20B(1) of the _Albury-Wodonga Development Act 1973_ applies to a financial year that commences after the commencement of this item.<o:p></o:p>

## 71<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>Application—paragraph 32(a) of the _Albury-Wodonga Development Act 1973_

Despite the amendment of paragraph 32(a) of the _Albury-Wodonga Development Act 1973_ made by this Schedule, that paragraph continues to apply, in relation to a financial year during any part of which a State Corporation is in existence, as if that amendment had not been made.<o:p></o:p>

##### <span style="mso-bookmark: _Toc98133378"><a id="4"></a>4<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>Application</span> of Amendments<o:p></o:p>

             (1)  Each amendment made by this Act applies to acts and omissions that take place after the amendment commences.<o:p></o:p>

             (2)  For the purposes of this section, if an act or omission is alleged to have taken place between 2 dates, one before and one on or after the day on which a particular amendment commences, the act or omission is alleged to have taken place before the amendment commences.<o:p></o:p>

## Schedule 2

## 174<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">  </span>Saving provision—provisions that formerly referred to the Treasurer

(1)        Any thing that:

                     (a)  was done by the Treasurer, or by a delegate of the Treasurer, before the commencing time under an affected provision; and

                     (b)  was in effect immediately before the commencing time;

continues to have effect after the commencing time as if it had been done by the Finance Minister under the affected provision.

(2)        In this item:

**_affected_****_provision_** means a provision that is amended by an item in this Schedule so as to replace references to the Treasurer with references to the Finance Minister.

**_commencing_****_time_** means the day this Act receives the Royal Assent.

**_Finance Minister_** means the Minister who administers the _Financial Management and Accountability Act 1997_.
