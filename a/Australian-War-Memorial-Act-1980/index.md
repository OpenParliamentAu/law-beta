###  AUSTRALIAN WAR MEMORIAL ACT 1980<part>Part I&#151;Preliminary </part>
**
###  AUSTRALIAN WAR MEMORIAL ACT 1980- SECT 1  Short title [_see_ Note 1] 
This Act may be cited as the _Australian War Memorial Act 1980_.

 
###  AUSTRALIAN WAR MEMORIAL ACT 1980- SECT 2  Commencement [_see_ Note 1] 
This Act shall come into operation on a date to be fixed by Proclamation.

 
###  AUSTRALIAN WAR MEMORIAL ACT 1980- SECT 3  Interpretation 
In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:

 
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_active service_ means active service in war or in warlike

operations by members of the Defence Force. _appoint_ includes re-appoint. _appointed member_ means a member of the Council appointed by the Governor-General. _Australian military history_ means the history of:  </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>wars and warlike operations in which Australians have been on active

service, including the events leading up to, and the aftermath of, such wars

and warlike operations; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the Defence Force.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_Chair_ means the Chair of the Council. _Council_ means the Council of the Australian War Memorial. _Defence Force_ includes any naval or military force of the Crown raised in Australia before the establishment of the Commonwealth. _Director_ means the Director of the Australian War Memorial. _Fund_ means the Australian War Memorial Fund referred to in section 32\. _historical material_ means material (whether in writing or in any other form) relating to Australian military history. _member_ means a member of the Council. _Memorial_ means the corporation established by subsection 4(1) under the name of the Australian War Memorial. _memorial collection_ means all historical material that is owned by the Memorial from time to time.  </dl></dl>

###  AUSTRALIAN WAR MEMORIAL ACT 1980- SECT 5  Functions of Memorial 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The functions of the Memorial are:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>to maintain and develop the national memorial referred to in subsection

6(1) of the _Australian War Memorial Act 1962_ as a national memorial of

Australians who have died:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>on or as a result of active service; or</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>as a result of any war or warlike operations in which Australians have

been on active service;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>to develop and maintain, as an integral part of the national memorial

referred to in paragraph (a), a national collection of historical material;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>to exhibit, or to make available for exhibition by others, historical

material from the memorial collection or historical material that is otherwise

in the possession of the Memorial;</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>to conduct, arrange for and assist in research into matters pertaining to

Australian military history; and</dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>to disseminate information relating to:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>Australian military history;</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>the national memorial referred to in paragraph (a);</dd>

<dt>(iii)</dt><dd>the memorial collection; and</dd>

<dt>(iv)</dt><dd>the Memorial and its functions.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Memorial shall use every endeavour to make the most advantageous use

of the memorial collection in the national interest.

</dd> </dl>

###  AUSTRALIAN WAR MEMORIAL ACT 1980- SECT 6  Powers of Memorial 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Subject to this Act, the Memorial has power to do all things necessary or

convenient to be done for or in connection with the performance of its

functions.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Without limiting the generality of subsection (1), the powers of the Memorial include power: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>to purchase or take on hire, or to accept as a gift or on deposit or loan,

historical material;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>to lend or hire out or otherwise deal with (otherwise than by way of

disposal) historical material;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>to accept gifts, devises, bequests or assignments made to the Memorial,

whether on trust or otherwise, and whether unconditionally or subject to a

condition and, if a gift, devise, bequest or assignment is accepted by the

Memorial on trust or subject to a condition, to act as trustee or to comply

with the condition, as the case may be;</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>to collect, and make available (whether in writing or in any other form

and whether by sale or otherwise), information relating to Australian military

history;</dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>to make available (whether by sale or otherwise) reproductions, replicas

or other representations (whether in writing or in any other form) of

historical material;</dd>

<dt>(f)</dt><dd>to make available (whether in writing or in any other form and whether by

sale or otherwise) information relating to the Memorial and its functions;</dd>

<dt>(g)</dt><dd>to provide facilities to stimulate interest in Australian military

history;</dd>

<dt>(h)</dt><dd>to assist educational institutions in matters relating to Australian

military history;</dd>

<dt>(j)</dt><dd>to train members of the staff of the Memorial, and such other persons as

the Council approves, in developing, caring for and undertaking research in

relation to the memorial collection;</dd>

<dt>(k)</dt><dd>to assist, on request, in the creation and maintenance of military museums

in Defence Force establishments;</dd>

<dt>(m)</dt><dd>to occupy, use and control any land or building owned or held under lease

by the Commonwealth and made available to the Memorial under section 7;</dd>

<dt>(n)</dt><dd>to erect buildings;</dd>

<dt>(o)</dt><dd>to purchase or take on hire, or to accept as a gift or on deposit or loan,

and to dispose of or otherwise deal with, furnishings, equipment and other

goods;</dd>

<dt>(p)</dt><dd>to act as trustee of moneys or other property vested in the Memorial on

trust; and</dd>

<dt>(q)</dt><dd>to act on behalf of the Commonwealth or of an authority of the

Commonwealth in the administration of a trust relating to historical material

or related matters.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>The Memorial shall not dispose of historical material except in accordance

with section 8.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>Notwithstanding anything contained in this Act, any money or other property held by the Memorial upon trust or accepted by the Memorial subject to a condition shall not be dealt with except in accordance with the obligations of the Memorial as trustee of the trust or as the person who has accepted the money or other property subject to the condition, as the case may be. </dd> </dl>

**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Minister may make available to the Memorial for the purposes of the

specified by the Minister by notice in the _Gazette_ and any building,

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the transfer of the ownership of historical material from the Commonwealth

to the Memorial for the purpose of the inclusion of the material in the

memorial collection;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the deposit in the custody of the Memorial of historical material owned by

the Commonwealth; and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>the transfer to the Memorial of the ownership of, or the deposit in the

custody of the Memorial of, such other goods or equipment owned by the

Commonwealth as he or she considers to be of use to the Memorial;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

upon such terms and conditions as are specified in the arrangement.

###  AUSTRALIAN WAR MEMORIAL ACT 1980- SECT 8  Disposal of historical material in memorial collection 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Subject to subsection (3), where the Council is satisfied that historical

material in the memorial collection:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>is unfit for the collection; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>is not required as part of the collection;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

the Council may resolve that the historical material be disposed of by sale, gift or destruction.

<dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>Where the Council is satisfied that the exchange of historical material in

the memorial collection for other historical material would be advantageous to

the collection, the Council may resolve that the first-mentioned historical

material be disposed of in exchange for that other historical material.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>The Council shall not resolve that historical material be disposed of by destruction unless the Council is satisfied that the material has no saleable value.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>Where: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the Council has resolved, in accordance with this section, that historical

material be disposed of; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>if the value of that historical material exceeds $5,000, the Minister has

approved of that disposal;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

the Memorial may dispose of that historical material accordingly.

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>The Chair may resign his or her office by writing signed by him or her and

###  AUSTRALIAN WAR MEMORIAL ACT 1980- SECT 17  Meetings of Council

###  AUSTRALIAN WAR MEMORIAL ACT 1980- SECT 19  Delegation by Council 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Subject to this section, the Council may, by resolution, either generally

or as otherwise provided by the resolution, delegate all or any of its powers

under this Act (except this power of delegation) to a member of the Council, to

the Director or to a member of the staff of the Memorial.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>A power so delegated, when exercised by the delegate, shall, for the purposes of this Act, be deemed to have been exercised by the Council.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>A delegate is, in the exercise of a power so delegated, subject to the directions of the Council.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>A delegation of a power under this section: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>may be revoked by resolution of the Council (whether or not constituted by

the persons constituting the Council at the time the power was delegated);</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>does not prevent the exercise of the power by the Council; and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>continues in force notwithstanding a change in the membership of the

Council.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(5)</dt><dd>Section 34A of the _Acts Interpretation Act 1901_ applies in relation

to a delegation under this section as if the Council were a person.</dd> <dt>(6)</dt><dd>A certificate signed by the Chair stating any matter with respect to the delegation of a power under this section is _prima facie_ evidence of that matter.</dd> <dt>(7)</dt><dd>A document purporting to be a certificate mentioned in subsection (6) shall, unless the contrary is established, be deemed to be such a certificate and to have been duly given. </dd> </dl>

###  AUSTRALIAN WAR MEMORIAL ACT 1980- SECT 26  Acting Director 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Minister may appoint a person to act in the place of the Director:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>during a vacancy in the office of the Director, whether or not an

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>during any period, or during all periods, when the Director is absent from

functions of his or her office;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

but a person appointed to act during a vacancy shall not continue so to act for more than 12 months.

<dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>An appointment of a person under subsection (1) may be expressed to have

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>subject to this Act, determine the terms and conditions of appointment,

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>terminate such an appointment at any time.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(4)</dt><dd>Where a person is acting as Director in accordance with paragraph (1)(b)

available to the Memorial.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Council may enter into an arrangement with the appropriate authority of a State or Territory for the services of officers or employees of the Public Service of the State or Territory, or of a body established for a public purpose by or under a law of the State or Territory, to be made available to the Memorial. </dd> </dl>

###  AUSTRALIAN WAR MEMORIAL ACT 1980- SECT 32  Australian War Memorial Fund 
**

 <dl compact="">

_Australian War Memorial Act 1962_ is continued in existence under that

name and vested in the Memorial.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The moneys and investments constituting the Fund immediately before the commencement of this Act shall continue to form part of the Fund.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>There shall be paid into the Fund all moneys received by the Memorial other than: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>moneys paid to the Memorial under section 30; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>moneys held by the Memorial upon trust.

</dd>

<dt>(4)</dt><dd>The payment of moneys into a bank account maintained by the Memorial in

accordance with subsection 18(2) of the _Commonwealth Authorities and Companies Act 1997_ under the name of the Australian War Memorial Fund shall

into the Fund.

</dd> </dl>

</dd> </dl>

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>in payment or discharge of the costs, expenses and other obligations

incurred by the Memorial in the performance of its functions or the exercise of

its powers under this Act; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>in payment of any remuneration and allowances payable to any person under

this Act.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

</dd> </dl>

 </dl>

building, or any part of any such land or building;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>for regulating, controlling, restricting or prohibiting the making, using,

printing, publishing, exhibiting, selling or offering for sale of replicas,

photographs, representations or copies of historical material forming part of

the memorial collection; and</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>for prescribing penalties not exceeding a fine of $500 for offences

against the regulations.

</dd>

###  AUSTRALIAN WAR MEMORIAL ACT 1980- SECT 45  Rights of Board to vest in Memorial etc. 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Subject to this Act, upon the commencing date:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>any rights, property or assets that, immediately before that date were

vested in the Board are, by force of this subsection, vested in the Memorial;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>any historical material that, immediately before that date:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>was vested in the Commonwealth; and</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>formed part of the war relics of Australia within the meaning of the

repealed Act;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		is, by force of this subsection, vested in the Memorial;

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>the Memorial becomes, by force of this subsection, entitled to the custody

of material, being any other historical material or other goods or equipment

held, immediately before that date, in the custody of the Board, upon the terms

and conditions upon which that material was held by the Board; and</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>the Memorial becomes, by force of this subsection, liable to pay and

discharge any debts, liabilities or obligations of the Board that existed

immediately before that date.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>An arrangement or contract entered into by or on behalf of the Board as a

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the Memorial were substituted for the Board as a party to the arrangement

or contract; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>any reference in the arrangement or contract to the Board were (except in

relation to a matter that occurred before that date) a reference to the

Memorial.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>Where, immediately before the commencing date, proceedings to which the

Board was a party were pending in any court, the Memorial is, by force of this

subsection, substituted for the Board as a party to the proceedings and has the

same rights in the proceedings as the party for which it is substituted.

</dd> </dl>

</dd>
