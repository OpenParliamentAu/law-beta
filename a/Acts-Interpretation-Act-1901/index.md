###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901<part>Part I&#151;Preliminary </part>
**
###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 1  Short title [_see_ Note 1] 
<dl compact="">

		This Act may be cited as the _Acts Interpretation Act 1901_.

 </dl>
###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 2  Application of Act 
<dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Except so far as the contrary intention appears, this Act applies to all

Acts, including this Act.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>This Act shall bind the Crown. </dd> </dl>

###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901<part>Part II&#151;Commencement of Acts </part>
**
###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 3  Meaning of _commencement_  
<dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>In every Act, _commencement_, in relation to an Act or a

provision of an Act, means the time at which the Act or provision comes into

operation.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Where an Act, or any instrument (including any rules regulations or by-laws) made granted or issued under a power conferred by an Act, is expressed to come into operation on a particular day (whether the expression "come into operation" or "commence" is used), it shall come into operation immediately on the expiration of the last preceding day. </dd> </dl>

###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 5  Commencement of Acts 
<dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Every Act to which the Royal Assent has been given by the Governor-General

for and on behalf of the King on or before 31&#160;December 1937, shall be

deemed to have come into operation on the day on which that Act received the

Royal Assent, unless the contrary intention appears in the Act.</dd> <dt>(1A)</dt><dd>Every Act (other than an Act to alter the Constitution) to which the Royal Assent is given by the Governor-General for and on behalf of the King on or after 1 January 1938, shall come into operation on the twenty-eighth day after the day on which that Act receives the Royal Assent, unless the contrary intention appears in the Act.</dd> <dt>(1B)</dt><dd>Every Act to alter the Constitution to which the Royal Assent is given by the Governor-General for and on behalf of the King on or after 1 January 1938, shall come into operation on the day on which that Act receives the Royal Assent, unless the contrary intention appears in that Act.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Every Act reserved for the signification of the King's pleasure thereon shall come into operation on the day on which His Majesty's assent is proclaimed in the _Gazette_ by the Governor-General, unless the contrary intention appears in such Act. </dd> </dl>

###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 6  Evidence of date of assent or proclamation 
<dl compact="">

		The date appearing on the copy of an Act printed by the Government Printer,

and purporting to be the date on which the Governor-General assented thereto,

or made known the King's assent, shall be evidence that such date was the date

on which the Governor-General so assented or made known the King's assent, and

shall be judicially noticed.

###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901<part>Part III&#151;Repeal and expiration of Acts </part>
**
###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 7  Effect of repeal of Act 
<dl compact="">

		The repeal of an Act or part thereof by which a previous Act or part thereof

was repealed shall not have the effect of reviving such last-mentioned Act or

part thereof without express words.

 </dl>
###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 8  Effect of repeal 
<dl compact="">

		Where an Act repeals in the whole or in part a former Act, then unless the

contrary intention appears the repeal shall not:

 </dl>
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>revive anything not in force or existing at the time at which the repeal

takes effect; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>affect the previous operation of any Act so repealed, or anything duly

done or suffered under any Act so repealed; or</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>affect any right privilege obligation or liability acquired accrued or

incurred under any Act so repealed; or</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>affect any penalty forfeiture or punishment incurred in respect of any

offence committed against any Act so repealed; or</dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>affect any investigation legal proceeding or remedy in respect of any such

right privilege obligation liability penalty forfeiture or punishment as

aforesaid;

</dd>

</dl></dl>
###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 8A  Implied repeals etc. 
<dl compact="">

		A reference in section 7 or 8 to the repeal of an Act or of a part of an Act

includes a reference to:

 </dl>
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a repeal effected by implication;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the abrogation or limitation of the effect of the Act or part; and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>the exclusion of the application of the Act or part to any person,

subject-matter or circumstance.

</dd>

</dl></dl>
###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 8B  Effect of expiration of Act 
<dl compact="">

		Where an Act or a part of an Act expires, lapses or otherwise ceases to have

effect, sections 7 and 8 apply as if the Act or part had been repealed by

another Act.

 </dl>
###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 8C  References to part of an Act 
<dl compact="">

		A reference in section 7, 8, 8A or 8B to a part of an Act includes a

reference to any provision of, or words, figures, drawings or symbols in, an Act.

 </dl>
###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 9  Repealed Acts in force until substituted provisions operate 

Back to Top

<dl compact="">

		Where an Act repeals in the whole or in part a former Act and substitutes

provisions in lieu thereof, the repealed provisions shall remain in force until

the substituted provisions come into operation.

 </dl>
###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 10  References to amended or re-enacted Acts 
<dl compact="">

		Where an Act contains a reference to a short title that is or was provided by

law for the citation of another Act as originally enacted, or of another Act as

amended, then, except so far as the contrary intention appears:

 </dl>
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the reference shall be construed as a reference to that other Act as

originally enacted and as amended from time to time; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>where that other Act has been repealed and re-enacted, with or without

modifications, the reference shall be construed as including a reference to the

re-enacted Act as originally enacted and as amended from time to time and,

where, in connexion with that reference, particular provisions of the repealed

Act are referred to, being provisions to which provisions of the re-enacted Act

correspond, the reference to those particular provisions shall be construed as

including a reference to those corresponding provisions.

</dd>

</dl></dl>
###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 10A  References to amended or re-enacted laws of States and Territories 
<dl compact="">

		Where an Act contains a reference to a short title or other citation that is

or was provided by the law of a State or Territory for the citation of a law of

that State or Territory as originally enacted or made, or as amended, then,

except so far as the contrary intention appears:

 </dl>
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the reference shall be construed as a reference to that law as originally

enacted or made and as amended from time to time; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>where that law has been repealed and re-enacted or re-made, with or

without modifications, the reference shall be construed as including a

reference to the re-enacted or re-made law as originally enacted or made and as

amended from time to time and, where, in connection with that reference,

particular provisions of the repealed law are referred to, being provisions to

which provisions of the re-enacted or re-made law correspond, the reference to

those particular provisions shall be construed as including a reference to

those corresponding provisions.

</dd>

</dl></dl>
###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 11  Expiration of Act 
<dl compact="">

		The expiration of an Act shall not affect any civil proceeding previously

commenced under such Act, but every such proceeding may be continued and

everything in relation thereto be done in all respects as if the Act continued

in force.

 </dl>

###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901<part>Part IV&#151;General provisions </part>
**
###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 12  Every section a substantive enactment 
<dl compact="">

		Every section of an Act shall have effect as a substantive enactment without

introductory words.

 </dl>
###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 13  Headings, schedules, marginal notes, footnotes and endnotes 
<dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The headings of the Parts Divisions and Subdivisions into which any Act is

divided shall be deemed to be part of the Act.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Every schedule to an Act shall be deemed to form part thereof.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>No marginal note, footnote or endnote to an Act, and no heading to a section of an Act, shall be taken to be part of the Act. </dd> </dl>

Back to Top

###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 14  Acts may be altered etc. in same session 
<dl compact="">

		An Act may be altered amended or repealed in the same session of Parliament

in which it was passed.

 </dl>
###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 14A  Definitions inserted by amending Act 
<dl compact="">

		Where an amending Act inserts a definition in a provision of the Act being

amended, but does not specify the position in that provision where it is to be

inserted, it shall be deemed to be inserted in the appropriate alphabetical

position, determined on a letter-by-letter basis.

 </dl>
###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 14B  Commencement of paragraphs etc. in amending Act 
<dl compact="">

		Where:

 </dl>
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>an Act makes an amendment of another Act; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the amendment is in the form of:

</dd>

</dl></dl>
<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>a paragraph of a provision of the amending Act;</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>an item (whether or not so described) in a Schedule to the amending Act;

or</dd>

<dt>(iii)</dt><dd>a paragraph of such an item;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>
###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 15  Amending Act to be construed with amended Act 
<dl compact="">

		Every Act amending another Act shall, unless the contrary intention appears,

be construed with such other Act and as part thereof.

 </dl>
###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 15A  Construction of Acts to be subject to Constitution 
<dl compact="">

		Every Act shall be read and construed subject to the Constitution, and so as

not to exceed the legislative power of the Commonwealth, to the intent that

where any enactment thereof would, but for this section, have been construed as

being in excess of that power, it shall nevertheless be a valid enactment to

the extent to which it is not in excess of that power.

 </dl>
###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 15AA  Regard to be had to purpose or object of Act 
<dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>In the interpretation of a provision of an Act, a construction that would

promote the purpose or object underlying the Act (whether that purpose or

object is expressly stated in the Act or not) shall be preferred to a

construction that would not promote that purpose or object.

</dd> </dl>
###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 15AB  Use of extrinsic material in the interpretation of an Act 
<dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Subject to subsection (3), in the interpretation of a provision of an Act,

if any material not forming part of the Act is capable of assisting in the

ascertainment of the meaning of the provision, consideration may be given to

that material:

</dd> </dl>
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>to confirm that the meaning of the provision is the ordinary meaning

conveyed by the text of the provision taking into account its context in the

Act and the purpose or object underlying the Act; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>to determine the meaning of the provision when:

</dd>

</dl></dl>

Back to Top

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>the provision is ambiguous or obscure; or</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>the ordinary meaning conveyed by the text of the provision taking into

account its context in the Act and the purpose or object underlying the Act

leads to a result that is manifestly absurd or is unreasonable.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>
<dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>Without limiting the generality of subsection (1), the material that may

be considered in accordance with that subsection in the interpretation of a

provision of an Act includes:

</dd> </dl>
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>all matters not forming part of the Act that are set out in the document

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>any relevant report of a Royal Commission, Law Reform Commission,

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>any relevant report of a committee of the Parliament or of either House of

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>any treaty or other international agreement that is referred to in the

Act;</dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>any explanatory memorandum relating to the Bill containing the provision,

the provision was enacted;</dd>

<dt>(f)</dt><dd>the speech made to a House of the Parliament by a Minister on the occasion

of the moving by that Minister of a motion that the Bill containing the

provision be read a second time in that House;</dd>

<dt>(g)</dt><dd>any document (whether or not a document to which a preceding paragraph

applies) that is declared by the Act to be a relevant document for the purposes

of this section; and</dd>

<dt>(h)</dt><dd>any relevant material in the Journals of the Senate, in the Votes and

Proceedings of the House of Representatives or in any official record of

debates in the Parliament or either House of the Parliament.

</dd>

</dl></dl>
<dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>In determining whether consideration should be given to any material in

such material, regard shall be had, in addition to any other relevant matters,

to:

</dd> </dl>
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the desirability of persons being able to rely on the ordinary meaning

conveyed by the text of the provision taking into account its context in the

Act and the purpose or object underlying the Act; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the need to avoid prolonging legal or other proceedings without

compensating advantage.

</dd>

</dl></dl>

</dd>

</dl></dl>

 </dl>

</dd>

###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 15C  Jurisdiction of courts 
<dl compact="">

		Where a provision of an Act, whether expressly or by implication, authorizes

a civil or criminal proceeding to be instituted in a particular court in

relation to a matter:

 </dl>
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>that provision shall be deemed to vest that court with jurisdiction in

that matter;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>except so far as the contrary intention appears, the jurisdiction so

vested is not limited by any limits to which any other jurisdiction of the

court may be subject; and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>in the case of a court of a Territory, that provision shall be construed

as providing that the jurisdiction is vested so far only as the Constitution

permits.

</dd>

</dl></dl>

###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901<part>Part V&#151;Words and references in Acts </part>
**
###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 16  References to the Sovereign 
<dl compact="">

		In any Act references to the Sovereign reigning at the time of the passing of

such Act, or to the Crown, shall unless the contrary intention appears be

construed as references to the Sovereign for the time being.

 </dl>
###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 16A  References to the Governor-General 
<dl compact="">

		Where, in an Act, the Governor-General is referred to, the reference shall,

unless the contrary intention appears, be deemed to include:

 </dl>
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the person for the time being administering the Government of the

Commonwealth; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>where the reference occurs in or in relation to a provision conferring on

the Governor-General a power or function which the Governor-General or the

person administering the Government of the Commonwealth has for the time being

assigned to a person as his deputy, that last-mentioned person in his capacity

as deputy;

</dd>

</dl></dl>
###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 16B  References to the Governor of a State 
<dl compact="">

		Where, in an Act, the Governor of a State is referred to, the reference

shall, unless the contrary intention appears, be deemed to include the Governor

for the time being of the State or any other person who is, for the time being,

the chief executive officer or administrator of the government of the State.

 </dl>
###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 16C  References to Stipendiary Magistrate and Magistrate 
<dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Where, in an Act, reference is made to a Stipendiary Magistrate, the

reference shall be read as including a reference to any Magistrate in respect

of whose office an annual salary is payable.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Where, in an Act passed after the date of commencement of this section, reference is made to a Magistrate, the reference shall, unless the contrary intention appears, be read as a reference to: </dd> </dl>
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a Chief, Police, Stipendiary, Resident or Special Magistrate; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>any other Magistrate in respect of whose office an annual salary is

payable.

</dd>

</dl></dl>
<dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>Unless the contrary intention appears, a reference in an Act to a

_Magistrate_ does not include a reference to a Federal Magistrate.

</dd> </dl>
###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 17  Constitutional and official definitions [_see_ Note 2] 
<dl compact="">

		In any Act, unless the contrary intention appears:

 </dl>
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>_Australia_ or _the Commonwealth_ means the

Commonwealth of Australia and, when used in a geographical sense, includes the

Territory of Christmas Island and the Territory of Cocos (Keeling) Islands, but

does not include any other external Territory;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>_The Constitution_ shall mean the Constitution of the

Commonwealth;</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>_The Constitution Act_ shall mean _The Commonwealth of Australia Constitution Act_;</dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>_The Parliament_ shall mean the Parliament of the

Commonwealth;</dd>

<dt>(g)</dt><dd>_The Executive Council_ shall mean the Federal Executive

Council;</dd>

<dt>(h)</dt><dd>_Minister of State_ or _Minister_ shall mean one

of the King's Ministers of State for the Commonwealth;</dd>

<dt>(j)</dt><dd>_Proclamation_ shall mean Proclamation by the Governor-General

published in the _Gazette_;</dd>

<dt>(k)</dt><dd>_Consolidated Revenue Fund_ means the Consolidated Revenue

Fund referred to in section 81 of the Constitution;</dd>

<dt>(ka)</dt><dd>_Loan Fund_ means the Loan Fund established by section 19 of

the _Financial Management and Accountability Act 1997_;</dd>

<dt>(kb)</dt><dd>_Reserved Money Fund_ means the Reserved Money Fund

established by section 20 of the _Financial Management and Accountability Act 1997_;</dd>

<dt>(l)</dt><dd>_The seat of Government_ shall mean the seat of Government of

the Commonwealth;</dd>

<dt>(m)</dt><dd>_Gazette_ means the _Commonwealth of Australia Gazette_,

and includes the _Australian Government Gazette_ published during the

period commencing on 1 July 1973 and ending immediately before the commencement

of subsection 5(2) of the _Acts Interpretation Amendment Act 1976_;</dd>

<dt>(n)</dt><dd>_The Government Printer_ shall include any person printing for

the Government of the Commonwealth;</dd>

<dt>(o)</dt><dd>_State_ shall mean a State of the Commonwealth;</dd>

<dt>(p)</dt><dd>_Territory_, _Territory of the Commonwealth_,

_Territory under the authority of the Commonwealth_ or

_Territory of Australia_ means a Territory referred to in section

122 of the Constitution, and includes a Territory administered by the

Commonwealth under a Trusteeship Agreement;</dd>

###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901<part>Part VIII&#151;Distance and time </part>
**</center>

**

###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 35  Measurement of distance 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		In the measurement of any distance for the purposes of any Act, that distance

shall, unless the contrary intention appears, be measured in a straight line on

a horizontal plane.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 36  Reckoning of time 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Where in an Act any period of time, dating from a given day, act, or

event, is prescribed or allowed for any purpose, the time shall, unless the

contrary intention appears, be reckoned exclusive of such day or of the day of

such act or event.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Where the last day of any period prescribed or allowed by an Act for the doing of anything falls on a Saturday, on a Sunday or on a day which is a public holiday or a bank holiday in the place in which the thing is to be or may be done, the thing may be done on the first day following which is not a Saturday, a Sunday or a public holiday or bank holiday in that place. </dd> </dl></dl>

**

###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 37  Expressions of time 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		Where in an Act any reference to time occurs, such time shall, unless it is

otherwise specifically stated, be deemed in each State or part of the

Commonwealth to mean the standard or legal time in that State or part of the

Commonwealth.

 </dl></dl>

###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 38  Reference to Acts 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

the word "Act" alone.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>An Act passed by the Parliament of the United Kingdom may be referred to by the term "Imperial Act".</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>An Act passed by the Parliament of a State may be referred to by the term "State Act". </dd> </dl></dl>

**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Governor-General assents thereto or makes known the King's assent thereto.

 </dl></dl>

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>any State Act may be cited by a reference to the State by the Parliament

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 46  Construction of rules, regulations and by-laws 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Where an Act confers upon any authority power to make, grant or issue any

instrument (including rules, regulations or by-laws), then:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>unless the contrary intention appears, expressions used in any instrument

so made, granted or issued shall have the same meanings as in the Act

conferring the power, and this Act shall apply to any instrument so made,

granted or issued as if it were an Act and as if each such rule, regulation or

by-law were a section of an Act; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>any instrument so made, granted or issued shall be read and construed

subject to the Act under which it was made, granted or issued, and so as not to

exceed the power of that authority, to the intent that where any such

instrument would, but for this section, have been construed as being in excess

of the power conferred upon that authority, it shall nevertheless be a valid

instrument to the extent to which it is not in excess of that power.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>Where an Act confers upon an authority power to make an instrument

(including rules, regulations or by-laws) or a resolution:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>specifying, declaring or prescribing a matter or thing; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>doing anything in relation to a matter or thing;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

then, in exercising the power, the authority may identify the matter or thing by referring to a class or classes of matters or things.

###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 46A  Disallowable instruments 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Where a provision (in this subsection called the _enabling provision_) of a law confers power to make an instrument (however

purposes of this section, then, except so far as the law otherwise provides:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>sections 48, 48A, 48B, 49 and 50 apply in relation to the instrument as if:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>references to regulations were references to the instrument;</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>references to a regulation were references to a provision of the

instrument;</dd>

<dt>(iii)</dt><dd>references to repeal were references to revocation;</dd>

<dt>(iiia)</dt><dd>references in subsection 48(7) to another regulation included

references to a provision of another instrument made under the enabling

provision; and</dd>

<dt>(iv)</dt><dd>where the enabling provision is a provision of regulations&#151;references to

an Act were references to regulations;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>section 49A applies in relation to the instrument as if:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>the instrument were regulations under an Act; and</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>the reference in paragraph (1)(a) to regulations included a reference to

other instruments made under the enabling provision;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>the instrument shall not be taken to be a statutory rule within the

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>for the purposes of the application of subsection 5(3B) of that Act under

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>A reference in subsection (1) to a law is a reference to an Act or to

###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 47  Construction of resolutions 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		Where any resolution is or has been passed by either House of the Parliament

in purported pursuance of any Act, then, unless the contrary intention appears,

the resolution shall be read and construed subject to the Constitution and to

the Act under which it purports to have been passed, to the intent that where

the resolution would, but for this section, have been construed as being in

excess of authority, it shall nevertheless be a valid resolution to the extent

to which it is not in excess of authority.

 </dl></dl>

###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 48  Regulations [_see_ Note 4] 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Where an Act confers power to make regulations, then, unless the contrary

intention appears, all regulations made accordingly:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>shall be notified in the _Gazette_;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>shall, subject to this section, take effect from:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>a specified date;</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>a specified time on a specified date;</dd>

<dt>(iii)</dt><dd>the date, or date and time, of commencement of a specified Act or a

specified provision of an Act; or</dd>

<dt>(iv)</dt><dd>in any other case&#151;the date of notification; and

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>shall be laid before each House of the Parliament within 15&#160;sitting

days of that House after the making of the regulations.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>A regulation, or a provision of regulations, has no effect if, apart from

this subsection, it would take effect before the date of notification and as a

result:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the rights of a person (other than the Commonwealth or an authority of the

Commonwealth) as at the date of notification would be affected so as to

disadvantage that person; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>liabilities would be imposed on a person (other than the Commonwealth or

an authority of the Commonwealth) in respect of anything done or omitted to be

done before the date of notification.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>If any regulations are not laid before each House of the Parliament in

accordance with the provisions of subsection (1), they cease to have effect.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>If either House of the Parliament, in pursuance of a motion of which notice has been given within 15 sitting days after any regulations have been laid before that House, passes a resolution disallowing any of those regulations, any regulation so disallowed thereupon ceases to have effect.</dd> <dt>(5)</dt><dd>If, at the expiration of 15 sitting days after notice of a motion to disallow any regulation has been given in a House of the Parliament, being notice given within 15 sitting days after the regulation has been laid before that House: </dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the notice has not been withdrawn and the motion has not been called on;

or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the motion has been called on, moved and seconded and has not been

withdrawn or otherwise disposed of;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

the regulation specified in the motion shall thereupon be deemed to have been disallowed.

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(5A)</dt><dd>If, before the expiration of 15 sitting days after notice of a motion to

disallow any regulation has been given in a House of the Parliament:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the House of Representatives is dissolved or expires, or the Parliament is

prorogued; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>at the time of the dissolution, expiry or prorogation, as the case may be:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>the notice has not been withdrawn and the motion has not been called on;

or</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>the motion has been called on, moved and seconded and has not been

withdrawn or otherwise disposed of;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

the regulation shall, for the purposes of subsections (4) and (5), be deemed to have been laid before that first-mentioned House on the first sitting day of that first-mentioned House after the dissolution, expiry or prorogation, as the case may be.

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(6)</dt><dd>Where a regulation is disallowed, or is deemed to have been disallowed,

under this section or ceases to have effect by virtue of the operation of

subsection (3), the disallowance of the regulation or the operation of

subsection (3) in relation to the regulation, as the case may be, has the same

effect as a repeal of the regulation.</dd> <dt>(7)</dt><dd>Where: </dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a regulation (in this subsection referred to as the _relevant regulation_) is disallowed, or is deemed to have been disallowed, under

this section or ceases to have effect by virtue of the operation of subsection

(3); and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the relevant regulation repealed, in whole or in part, another regulation

that was in force immediately before the relevant regulation came into

operation;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

the disallowance of the relevant regulation or the operation of subsection (3) in relation to the relevant regulation, as the case may be, has the effect of reviving that other regulation from and including the date of the disallowance or the date on which the relevant regulation ceased to have effect by virtue of that operation of subsection (3), as the case may be, as if the relevant regulation had not been made.

###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 48A  Regulations not to be re-made while required to be tabled 
**

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

original regulation shall be made during the period defined by subsection (2)

regulation the same in substance as the original regulation.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The period referred to in subsection (1) is the period starting on the day on which the original regulation was made and ending at the end of 7 days after: </dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>if the original regulation has been laid, in accordance with paragraph

different days&#151;the later of those days; or</dd>

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>A regulation made in contravention of this section has no effect.

</dd> </dl></dl>

###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 49A  Prescribing matters by reference to other instruments

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Where an Act authorizes or requires provision to be made for or in

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>In this section, _regulations_ means regulations or rules

</dd> </dl></dl>

###  ACTS INTERPRETATION ACT 1901- SECT 50  Effect of repeal of regulations

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

 </dl></dl>
