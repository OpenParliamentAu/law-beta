<sect>    An Act to establish the Australian National Maritime Museum<lf> </lf></sect>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN NATIONAL MARITIME MUSEUM ACT 1990 - SECT 1 Short title </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   1\. This Act may be cited as the Australian National Maritime Museum Act 1990.*1* SEE NOTES TO FIRST ARTICLE OF THIS CHAPTER . </sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN NATIONAL MARITIME MUSEUM ACT 1990 - SECT 2 Commencement </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   2.*1* This Act commences:<lf>   (a) if this Act receives the Royal Assent before 1 July 1989 - on 1 July 1989; or<lf>   (b) if this Act receives the Royal Assent on or after 1 July 1989 - one month after it receives the Royal Assent. SEE NOTES TO FIRST ARTICLE OF THIS CHAPTER . </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN NATIONAL MARITIME MUSEUM ACT 1990 - SECT 3 Interpretation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   3\. (1) In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:<lf>   "acquire" means acquire by means of purchase, lease, loan or otherwise;<lf>   "appointed member" means a member other than the Director or the naval member;<lf>   "Australia" includes all external Territories;<lf>   "Australian marine environment" includes the environment of:<lf>   (a) the sea above the continental shelf adjacent to the coast of Australia and the bed of that sea; and<lf>   (b) the waters and beds of the lakes, rivers and other internal waters of Australia;<lf>   "Australian maritime history" includes:<lf>   (a) the history, including biological and geological history, of the Australian marine environment;<lf>   (b) the history of the interaction of human beings with the Australian marine environment;<lf>   (c) the history of sea travel by Australians, and by persons to and from Australia, whether for the purpose of migration, trade, exploration, defence or otherwise; and<lf>   (d) the history of research into, and the recovery of materials related to, any of the matters referred to in paragraphs (a), (b) and (c);<lf>   "Chairperson" means the Chairperson of the Council;<lf>   "continental shelf" has the same meaning as in theSeas and Submerged Lands Act 1973;<lf>   "Council" means the Council of the Australian National Maritime Museum established by section 11;<lf>   "Director" means the Director of the Museum;<lf>   "Fund" means the Australian National Maritime Museum Fund established by section 44;<lf>   "maritime historical material" means material (whether in written form or in any other form) relating to Australian maritime history;<lf>   "member" means a member of the Council;<lf>   "money of the Museum" includes money standing to the credit of the Fund;<lf>   "Museum" means the Australian National Maritime Museum established by section 5;<lf>   "national maritime collection" means the national collection of maritime historical material referred to in subsection 9 (1);<lf>   "naval member" means the member appointed under subsection 17 (2A) by the Chief of Naval Staff;<lf>   "vessel" means any kind of vessel or other craft that is, or has been, used in navigation by water, whether above or below the surface and however propelled or moved. 

  (2) Where, during a period, a place was a Territory referred to in section 122 of the Constitution (including a Territory administered by Australia under a Trusteeship Agreement), then, for the purposes of the definitions of "Australian marine environment" and "Australian maritime history" in subsection (1), a reference in those definitions to Australia includes a reference to the place during the period when it was such a Territory.

### <name>AUSTRALIAN NATIONAL MARITIME MUSEUM ACT 1990 - PART 2  PART 2 - THE AUSTRALIAN NATIONAL MARITIME MUSEUM </name>

**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN NATIONAL MARITIME MUSEUM ACT 1990 - SECT 5 Establishment of Museum </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   5\. (1) There is established by this section a museum by the name of the Australian National Maritime Museum. 

  (2) The Museum:<lf>   (a) is a body corporate;<lf>   (b) shall have a seal; and<lf>   (c) may sue and be sued. <p>  (3) The seal of the Museum shall be kept in such custody as the Council directs and shall not be used except as authorised by the Council. </p><p>  (4) All courts, judges and persons acting judicially shall take judicial notice of the imprint of the seal of the Museum appearing on a document and shall presume the document was duly sealed. </p></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN NATIONAL MARITIME MUSEUM ACT 1990 - SECT 6 Functions of Museum </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   6\. The functions of the Museum are:<lf>   (a) to exhibit, or to make available for exhibition by others, in Australia or elsewhere, material included in the national maritime collection or maritime historical material that is otherwise in the possession of the Museum;<lf>   (b) to co-operate with other institutions (whether public or private) in the exhibiting, or in the making available for exhibition, of such material;<lf>   (c) to develop, preserve and maintain the national maritime collection in accordance with section 9;<lf>   (d) to disseminate information relating to Australian maritime history and information relating to the Museum and its functions;<lf>   (e) to conduct, arrange for and assist research into matters relating to Australian maritime history; and<lf>   (f) to develop sponsorship, marketing and other commercial activities relating to the Museum's functions. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN NATIONAL MARITIME MUSEUM ACT 1990 - SECT 7 Powers of Museum </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   7\. (1) Subject to this Act, the Museum has power to do all things necessary or convenient to be done for or in connection with the performance of its functions and, in particular, has power:<lf>   (a) to purchase, commission the creation of or take on hire, deposit or loan, maritime historical material;<lf>   (b) to lend or hire out or otherwise deal with maritime historical material;<lf>   (c) to recover, or to arrange for or assist in the recovery of, maritime historical material from the Australian marine environment and from other areas;<lf>   (d) to dispose of, in accordance with section 10, material included in the national maritime collection and to dispose of maritime historical material that is otherwise in the possession of the Museum;<lf>   (e) from time to time as the occasion requires, to exhibit in Australia or elsewhere, material, whether in written form or in any other form and whether relating to Australia or to a foreign country;<lf>   (f) to accept gifts, devises, bequests or assignments made to the Museum, whether on trust or otherwise, and whether unconditionally or subject to a condition and, if a gift, devise, bequest or assignment is accepted by the Museum on trust or subject to a condition, to act as trustee or to comply with the condition, as the case may be;<lf>   (g) to acquire and operate vessels, whether in Australian waters or otherwise and whether or not the vessels are maritime historical material;<lf>   (h) to collect, and make available (whether in writing or in any other form and whether by sale or otherwise), information relating to Australian maritime history;<lf>   (j) to make available information relating to the Museum and its functions;<lf>   (k) to make available (whether by sale or otherwise) reproductions, replicas or other representations (whether in writing or in any other form) of maritime historical material;<lf>   (m) to arrange for the manufacture and distribution of (whether by sale or otherwise), any article or thing bearing a mark, symbol or writing that is associated with the Museum;<lf>   (n) to enter into contracts;<lf>   (o) to acquire, hold and dispose of real or personal property;<lf>   (p) to erect buildings and structures and carry out works;<lf>   (q) to take on leases of land or buildings and to grant leases and sub-leases of land or buildings;<lf>   (r) to fix charges for entry onto any land, or into any building, owned by, or under the control of the Museum, being charges that:<lf>     (i) are in addition to the charges fixed by the<lf> regulations; and<lf>     (ii) relate to special exhibitions or other special events;<lf>   (s) to purchase or take on hire, deposit or loan, and to dispose of or otherwise deal with, furnishings, equipment and other goods;<lf>   (t) to raise money for the purposes of the Museum by appropriate means, having regard to the proper performance of the functions of the Museum;<lf>   (u) to charge such fees and impose such charges (in addition to the charges fixed by regulations) as are reasonable in respect of services rendered by the Museum;<lf>   (w) to act on behalf of the Commonwealth or of an authority of the Commonwealth in the administration of a trust relating to maritime historical material or related matters; and<lf>   (y) to appoint agents and attorneys and act as an agent for other persons. 

  (2) The Museum may exercise its powers either alone or jointly with another person or other persons.

### <name>AUSTRALIAN NATIONAL MARITIME MUSEUM ACT 1990 - SECT 8 Land etc. for Museum </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   8\. (1) The Minister may make available to the Museum for the purposes of the Museum:<lf>   (a) such areas of land (including any buildings, structures or other improvements on those areas); and<lf>   (b) such areas of water;<lf> owned or held under lease by, or otherwise under the control of, the Commonwealth as are specified by the Minister by notice in the Gazette. 

  (2) The Minister may:<lf>   (a) where the Council so requests, transfer or cause to be transferred to the Museum maritime historical material owned by the Commonwealth; and<lf>   (b) transfer or cause to be transferred to the Museum such other goods or equipment owned by the Commonwealth as the Minister considers would be of use to the Museum. <p>  (3) Where the Minister causes an asset to be transferred to the Museum under subsection (2), the Minister may, by writing signed by him or her, declare that the transfer shall be taken to have had effect from a specified day, not being a day earlier than the day on which this Act commences, and, where the Minister makes such a declaration, it has effect accordingly. </p><p>  (4) Where an asset of the Commonwealth is transferred to the Museum under subsection (2), the Museum becomes liable to pay and discharge all debts, liabilities and obligations of the Commonwealth (if any) that existed immediately before the transfer in respect of that asset. </p><p>  (5) The Minister may, by writing signed by him or her, declare that a specified agreement or a specified instrument:<lf>   (a) to which the Commonwealth or the Commonwealth Government is a party; and<lf>   (b) that immediately before the commencement of this Act related to a maritime museum;<lf> has effect, after the commencement of this Act, as if:<lf>   (c) the Museum were substituted for the Commonwealth or the Commonwealth Government, as the case may be, as a party to the agreement or instrument; and<lf>   (d) any reference in the agreement or instrument to the Commonwealth or the Commonwealth Government were (except in relation to matters that occurred before the commencement of this Act) a reference to the Museum;<lf> and, where the Minister makes such a declaration, it has effect accordingly. <p>  (6) The Minister may, by writing signed by him or her, delegate, to the person holding or performing the duties of the office of Secretary to the Department, all or any of the Minister's powers under this section. </p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf>

**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN NATIONAL MARITIME MUSEUM ACT 1990 - SECT 9 National maritime collection </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   9\. (1) The Museum shall develop a national collection of maritime historical material. 

  (2) The national maritime collection shall consist of all maritime historical material in the sole ownership of the Museum that the Council has determined in accordance with subsection (3) should be included in the national maritime collection. 

  (3) The Council shall, from time to time, determine which of the maritime historical material that comes into the sole ownership of the Museum should be included in the national maritime collection. 

  (4) Any determination made by the Council for the purposes of subsection (3) shall be based upon criteria, and made in accordance with guidelines, approved by the Minister. 

  (5) The Museum shall not sell or otherwise dispose of material included in the national maritime collection except in accordance with section 10\. 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN NATIONAL MARITIME MUSEUM ACT 1990 - SECT 10 Disposal of material in national maritime collection </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   10\. (1) Subject to subsections (2) and (3), where the Council is satisfied that material included in the national maritime collection:<lf>   (a) is unfit for the collection; or<lf>   (b) is not required as part of the collection;<lf> the Council may determine that the material be disposed of by sale, exchange, gift or destruction. 

  (2) The Council shall not determine that material included in the national maritime collection be disposed of until the Council has obtained and considered reports from 2 independent experts setting out the views of those experts on the importance of the material to the national maritime collection. 

  (3) The Council shall not determine that material included in the national maritime collection be disposed of by destruction unless the Council is satisfied that the material cannot be disposed of by sale, exchange or gift. 

  (4) Where:<lf>   (a) the Council has determined, in accordance with this section, that material included in the national maritime collection should be disposed of; and<lf>   (b) if the value of the material, as determined in accordance with regulations made for the purposes of this paragraph, exceeds $20,000, the Minister has approved of its disposal;<lf> the Museum may dispose of the material accordingly. <p>  (5) The Museum must, in each report prepared under section 63M of the Audit Act 1901 (as that section applies by virtue of subsection 48 (1)), include particulars of any disposals of material during the financial year to which the report relates. </p></lf></lf></lf>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN NATIONAL MARITIME MUSEUM ACT 1990 - SECT 12 Functions of Council </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   12\. The functions of the Council are to ensure the proper and efficient performance of the functions of the Museum and, subject to section 14, to determine the policy of the Museum with respect to any matter. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN NATIONAL MARITIME MUSEUM ACT 1990 - SECT 13 Council may give directions to Director </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   13\. (1) The Council may give directions to the Director regarding the management of the affairs of the Museum. 

  (2) The Director shall comply with any such directions. 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN NATIONAL MARITIME MUSEUM ACT 1990 - SECT 14 Directions to Council </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   14\. (1) The Minister may, by notice in writing to the Chairperson, give directions to the Council with respect to the performance of the functions, or the exercise of the powers, of the Museum. 

  (2) The Council shall comply with any such directions. 

  (3) The Minister shall cause a copy of each direction to be laid before each House of the Parliament within 6 sitting days of that House after the direction is given. 

  (4) A direction that is not laid before each House of the Parliament in accordance with subsection (3) ceases to have effect. 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN NATIONAL MARITIME MUSEUM ACT 1990 - SECT 15 Committees of Council </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   15\. (1) The Council may constitute such committees as it thinks necessary for the purposes of this Act. 

  (2) A committee may be constituted wholly by members of the Council or partly by one or more members of the Council and partly by one or more other persons. 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN NATIONAL MARITIME MUSEUM ACT 1990 - SECT 16 Delegation by Council </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   16\. (1) The Council may, by resolution, either generally, or as otherwise provided by the resolution, delegate all or any of its powers under this Act to the Director, to any other member or to a member of staff of the Museum. 

  (2) A delegate is, in the exercise of a power so delegated, subject to the directions of the Council. 

  (3) A delegation of a power under this section:<lf>   (a) may be revoked by resolution of the Council (whether or not constituted by the persons constituting the Council at the time the power was delegated); and<lf>   (b) continues in force notwithstanding a change in the membership of the Council. </lf></lf>
</sect>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN NATIONAL MARITIME MUSEUM ACT 1990 - DIVISION 2  Division 2 - Membership and meetings of Council </name>

**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN NATIONAL MARITIME MUSEUM ACT 1990 - SECT 17 Membership of Council </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   17\. (1) The Council shall consist of the following members:<lf>   (a) the Chairperson;<lf>   (b) the Director;<lf>   (ba) an officer of the Royal Australian Navy;<lf>   (c) not fewer than 6 nor more than 9 other members. 

  (2) The members referred to in paragraphs (1) (a) and (c) shall be appointed by the Governor-General as part-time members. 

  (2A) The member referred to in paragraph (1) (ba) is to be appointed by the Chief of Naval Staff as a part-time member. 

  (3) An appointed member shall be a person having knowledge and experience with respect to matters relating to Australian maritime history or with respect to any other area of knowledge relevant to the affairs of the Museum. 

  (5) An appointed member holds office for such period, not exceeding 3 years, as is specified in the instrument of appointment of the member. 

  (5A) The naval member holds office at the pleasure of the Chief of Naval Staff. 

  (6) The performance of the functions, or the exercise of the powers, of the Council is not affected merely because:<lf>   (a) there is a vacancy in the office of Chairperson or Director; or<lf>   (ab) there is no member holding office under paragraph (1) (ba); or<lf>   (b) the number of persons appointed under paragraph (1) (c) falls below 6 for not longer than 3 months. </lf></lf></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN NATIONAL MARITIME MUSEUM ACT 1990 - SECT 18 Acting members </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   18\. (1) The Minister may appoint a person who is an appointed member to act as Chairperson:<lf>   (a) during a vacancy in the office of Chairperson (whether or not an appointment has previously been made to the office); or<lf>   (b) during any period, or during all periods, when the Chairperson is absent from duty or from Australia or is, for any other reason, unable to perform the functions of the office of Chairperson. 

  (2) The Minister may appoint a person who is not a member to act as a member referred to in paragraph 17 (1) (c):<lf>   (a) during a vacancy in the office of the member (whether or not an appointment has previously been made to the office); or<lf>   (b) during any period, or during all periods, when the member is absent from duty or from Australia or is, for any other reason (including the reason that the member is acting as Chairperson), unable to perform the duties of the office of member. <p>  (3) A person appointed under this section to act during a vacancy shall not continue to act for more than 12 months. </p><p>  (4) Anything done by or in relation to a person purporting to act under this section is not invalid on the ground that:<lf>   (a) the occasion for the person's appointment had not arisen;<lf>   (b) there is a defect or irregularity in connection with the person's appointment;<lf>   (c) the person's appointment had ceased to have effect; or<lf>   (d) the occasion for the person to act had not arisen or had ceased. </lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf>
</lf></lf></sect>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN NATIONAL MARITIME MUSEUM ACT 1990 - SECT 19 Leave of absence </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   19\. The Council may, in accordance with guidelines approved by the Minister, grant to an appointed member, or the naval member, leave of absence from a meeting of the Council upon such terms and conditions as to remuneration or otherwise as the Council determines. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN NATIONAL MARITIME MUSEUM ACT 1990 - SECT 20 Resignation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   20\. (1) An appointed member may resign the office of appointed member by writing signed by the appointed member and delivered to the Governor-General. 

  (2) The naval member may resign the office of naval member by writing signed by the naval member and delivered to the Chief of Naval Staff. 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN NATIONAL MARITIME MUSEUM ACT 1990 - SECT 21 Termination of appointment </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   21\. (1) The Governor-General may terminate the appointment of an appointed member by reason of the misbehaviour or physical or mental incapacity of that member. 

  (2) If an appointed member:<lf>   (a) becomes bankrupt, applies to take the benefit of any law for the relief of bankrupt or insolvent debtors, compounds with creditors or makes an assignment of remuneration for their benefit;<lf>   (b) is absent, except on leave granted by the Council in accordance with section 19, from 3 consecutive meetings of the Council otherwise than on business of the Council undertaken with the approval of the Council; or<lf>   (c) contravenes section 22 without reasonable excuse;<lf> the Governor-General shall terminate the appointment of that member. </lf></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN NATIONAL MARITIME MUSEUM ACT 1990 - SECT 22 Disclosure of interests </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   22\. (1) A member who has a direct or indirect pecuniary interest in a matter being considered or about to be considered by the Council shall, as soon as possible after the relevant facts have come to the member's knowledge, disclose the nature of the interest at a meeting of the Council. 

  (2) A disclosure under subsection (1) shall be recorded in the minutes of the meeting of the Council and the member shall not unless the Minister or the Council otherwise determines:<lf>   (a) be present during any deliberation of the Council with respect to that matter; or<lf>   (b) take part in any decision of the Council with respect to that matter. <p>  (3) For the purpose of the Council making such a determination in relation to a member who has made a disclosure, a member who has a direct or indirect pecuniary interest in the matter to which the disclosure relates shall not:<lf>   (a) be present during any deliberation of the Council for the purpose of making the determination; or<lf>   (b) take part in making the determination. </lf></lf></p></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN NATIONAL MARITIME MUSEUM ACT 1990 - SECT 23 Meetings of Council </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   23\. (1) The Chairperson:<lf>   (a) shall convene such meetings of the Council as are necessary for the efficient conduct of its functions; and<lf>   (b) shall, on receipt of a written request signed by not fewer than 4 members, convene a meeting of the Council. 

  (2) The Minister may at any time convene a meeting of the Council. 

  (3) The Chairperson shall preside at all meetings of the Council at which he or she is present. 

  (4) In the event of the absence of the Chairperson from a meeting of the Council, the members present shall elect one of their number to preside at that meeting. 

  (5) At a meeting of the Council, a quorum is constituted by a majority of the members. 

  (6) Where, because of subsection 22 (2), a member is not present at a meeting of the Council during a deliberation of the Council with respect to a matter, but there would be a quorum if that member were present, the remaining members present constitute a quorum for the purpose of any deliberation or decision of the Council at the meeting with respect to that matter. 

  (7) Subject to subsection (8), all questions arising at a meeting of the Council shall be decided by a majority of the votes of the members present and voting, including the member presiding. 

  (8) In the event of an equality of votes on a resolution proposed at a meeting of the Council, the resolution shall be taken not to be passed but, if the same resolution is proposed at the first meeting of the Council held after the date of that first-mentioned meeting and there is again an equality of votes, the member presiding has a casting vote on the resolution. 

  (9) The Council may regulate the conduct of proceedings at its meetings as it thinks fit. 

  (10) The Council shall keep minutes of proceedings at its meetings and shall forward a copy of those minutes to the Minister. 

  (11) If the Council so determines, a member of the Council may participate in, and form part of a quorum at, a meeting of the Council by means of any of the following methods of communication:<lf>   (a) telephone;<lf>   (b) close circuit television;<lf>   (c) any other method of communication determined by the Council. <p>  (12) A determination of the Council under subsection (11) may be made in respect of a particular meeting, or in respect of all meetings, of the Council. </p><p>  (13) A member of the Council who participates in a meeting as provided by subsection (11) shall be taken for the purposes of this Part to be present at the meeting. </p><p>  (14) If the Council so determines, a resolution shall be taken to have been passed at a meeting of the Council if, without meeting, a majority of the members who would, if present at a meeting of the Council and entitled to vote on the resolution at that meeting, have constituted a quorum of the Council indicate agreement with the resolution in accordance with the method determined by the Council. </p></lf></lf></lf>
