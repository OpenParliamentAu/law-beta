## SECT

### <name>ATOMIC ENERGY ACT 1953 - SECT 5 Interpretation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   5.(1) In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:<lf>   'atomic energy' means any form of energy released in the course of nuclear fission, nuclear fusion or other nuclear transmutation;<lf>   'Australia' includes the Territories;<lf>   'Commission' means the Australian Atomic Energy Commission that was constituted under this Act before the enactment of the Australian Nuclear Science and Technology Organisation Act 1987;<lf>   'minerals' includes all substances obtained or obtainable from the earth by underground or surface working;<lf>   'prescribed substance' means:<lf>    (a) uranium, thorium, an element having an atomic number greater than 92 or<lf> any other substance declared by the regulations to be capable of being used for the production of atomic energy or for research into matters connected with atomic energy; and<lf>    (b) any derivative or compound of a substance to which paragraph (a)<lf> applies;<lf>   'Ranger Project Area' means the land described in Schedule 2 to the Aboriginal Land Rights (Northern Territory) Act 1976;<lf>   (4) A reference in this Act to persons engaged in a joint venture shall be construed as a reference to persons carrying on or proposing to carry on any operations together, whether or not in partnership. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>ATOMIC ENERGY ACT 1953 - SECT 6 Act to bind Crown </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   6\. This Act binds the Crown in right of the Commonwealth, of each of the States and of the Northern Territory but nothing in this Act renders the Crown liable to be prosecuted for an offence. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>ATOMIC ENERGY ACT 1953 - SECT 7 Extension to external Territories </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   7\. This Act extends to every external Territory. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>ATOMIC ENERGY ACT 1953 - SECT 34 Exercise of powers </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   34\. The powers conferred by this Act may be exercised only:<lf>    (a) for purposes related to trade or commerce with other countries;<lf>    (b) for purposes related to trade or commerce among the States, between a<lf> State and a Territory or between 2 Territories;<lf>    (c) in relation to substances situated in or recovered from, or things done<lf> or proposed to be done in or in connection with, the Ranger Project Area;<lf>    (d) in relation to substances situated in or recovered from, or things done<lf> or proposed to be done in or in connection with, a Territory; or<lf>    (e) for other purposes of the Commonwealth not being purposes related to the<lf> defence of the Commonwealth. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>

### <name>ATOMIC ENERGY ACT 1953 - PART II  PART II-TITLE TO AND INFORMATION CONCERNING PRESCRIBED SUBSTANCES </name>

**<b>

### <name>ATOMIC ENERGY ACT 1953 - SECT 35 Title of Crown to prescribed substances in Territories of the Commonwealth </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   35.(1) This section applies to substances which, on or after the commencement of this Act, are prescribed substances existing in their natural condition, or in a deposit of waste material obtained from an underground or surface working, on or below the surface of land in a Territory, whether alienated from the Crown or not and, if alienated, whether alienated before or after the commencement of this Act.<lf>   (2) A substance to which this section applies which:<lf>    (a) is a prescribed substance at the commencement of this Act; and<lf>    (b) was not the property of the Commonwealth immediately before 11 September<lf> 1946 (being the date of commencement of the Atomic Energy (Control of Materials) Act 1946);<lf> is declared to have become the property of the Commonwealth on that date.<lf>   (3) A substance to which this section applies which:<lf>    (a) becomes a prescribed substance after the commencement of this Act; and<lf>    (b) is not, immediately before the date on which it becomes a prescribed<lf> substance, the property of the Commonwealth;<lf> becomes, by force of this Act, the property of the Commonwealth on that date.<lf>   (4) The title of the Commonwealth to any substance to which this section applies is subject to any rights granted after 10 September, 1946 by or under the law of a Territory, with express reference to that substance, but to no other rights. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>ATOMIC ENERGY ACT 1953 - SECT 36 Notification of discovery of prescribed substance </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   36.(1) A person who, whether before or after the commencement of this Act, has discovered that a substance that is or becomes a prescribed substance, or minerals containing a substance that is or becomes a prescribed substance, occurs or occur at any place in Australia shall report that discovery by notice in writing to the Minister within one month after:<lf>    (a) the commencement of this Act;<lf>    (b) the date of the making of the discovery; or<lf>    (c) the date on which the substance becomes a<lf> prescribed substance;<lf> whichever is the latest.<lf>   (1A) A person who, without reasonable excuse, fails to comply with subsection (1) is guilty of an offence punishable on conviction by a fine not exceeding:<lf>    (a) in the case of a natural person-$2,000; or<lf>    (b) in the case of a body corporate-$10,000.<lf>   (2) Subsection (1) does not require a person to report a discovery that he has reported to a Minister before the commencement of this Act. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>ATOMIC ENERGY ACT 1953 - SECT 37 Power to obtain information </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   37.(1) The Minister may, by notice in writing served on a person, require that person to furnish in writing, in the manner specified in the notice and within the time or times and in respect of the period or periods (if any) so specified, a statement, return or returns containing such particulars as are specified in the notice relating to:<lf>    (a) prescribed substances in his possession or under his control;<lf>    (b) minerals of a kind specified in the notice in his possession or under<lf> his control or present on or under land owned or occupied by him, being minerals from which, in the opinion of the Minister, a prescribed substance can be obtained; or<lf>    (c) work carried out by him or on his behalf or under his direction in<lf> connexion with the production or use of a prescribed substance or of minerals from which, in the opinion of the Minister, a prescribed substance can be obtained.<lf>   (2) A person who refuses or fails, without reasonable excuse, to comply with a notice served on the person under subsection (1) is guilty of an offence punishable on conviction by a fine not exceeding:<lf>    (a) in the case of a natural person-$2,000; or<lf>    (b) in the case of a body corporate-$10,000.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>

### <name>ATOMIC ENERGY ACT 1953 - SECT 41 Authority to mine prescribed substances on behalf of, or in association with, the Commonwealth </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   41.(1) Where it appears to the Minister that a prescribed substance, or minerals from which, in the opinion of the Minister, a prescribed substance can be obtained, is or are present on or under the whole or a part of an area of land in the Ranger Project Area, either in a natural state or in a deposit of waste material obtained from an underground or surface working, the Minister may, by writing under his hand, authorize a person, or 2 or more persons engaged in a joint venture, to carry on, on behalf of or in association with the Commonwealth, operations in accordance with this section on that land.<lf>   (2) Subject to any conditions or restrictions specified in the authority, the person so authorized in relation to any land may:<lf>    (a) enter upon that land, with such workmen and other persons as he thinks<lf> fit, and bring on to that land such machinery, vehicles and other things as he thinks fit;<lf>    (b) take possession of the whole or a part of that land;<lf>    (c) carry on, upon or under that land, operations for discovering prescribed<lf> substances, and for mining, recovering, treating and processing prescribed substances and such other minerals as it is necessary or convenient to mine or recover in order to obtain prescribed substances;<lf>    (d) for the purposes of the operations referred to in paragraph (c):<lf>          (i) erect or install buildings (including residential buildings),<lf> structures and machinery on that land;<lf>          (ii) cut and construct water races, drains, dams, tramways and roads<lf> on that land;<lf>          (iii) bore or sink for water, and pump, raise or use water, on that<lf> land; and<lf>          (iv) do other work on that land;<lf>    (e) demolish or remove buildings, structures and machinery erected or<lf> installed on that land;<lf>    (ea) remove from that land persons who enter upon, or are on, that land<lf> otherwise than:<lf>          (i) with the consent of the person so authorized; or<lf>          (ii) in pursuance of a right or power conferred by law;<lf>    (f) pass over, or authorize persons and things to pass or be carried over,<lf> land giving access to that land; and<lf>    (g) do all such other things as are necessary or convenient for the<lf> effectual exercise of the powers specified in the preceding paragraphs of this subsection.<lf>   (2AA) Operations carried on on the Ranger Project Area by the person or persons specified in an authority under this section (being an authority, whether granted before or after the commencement of this subsection, that authorizes, or purports to authorize, that person, or those persons as joint venturers, to carry on, on behalf of the Commonwealth, operations in accordance with this section on the Ranger Project Area subject to conditions and restrictions specified in the authority) shall, if carried on as provided by the authority and in accordance with the Ranger Project agreement, be deemed, for the purposes of this Act, to be carried on on behalf of the Commonwealth and to be authorized by the authority.<lf>   (2AB) The reference in subsection (2AA) to the Ranger Project agreement shall be construed as a reference to the agreement known as the Ranger Uranium Project Government Agreement that was made on 9 January 1979 between the Commonwealth, Peko-Wallsend Operations Ltd., Electrolytic Zinc Company of Australasia Limited and the Commission or, if that agreement is amended, that agreement as amended.<lf>   (2A) An authority under this section continues in force for the period for which the authority is conferred unless, before the expiration of that period, the authority:<lf>    (a) terminates or is terminated in accordance with a condition or<lf> restriction to which the authority is subject; or<lf>    (b) is revoked under section 41A or 41C.<lf>   (3) All prescribed substances and minerals mined or otherwise recovered in pursuance of an authority under this section that are not otherwise the property of the Commonwealth are, by force of this section, vested in the Commonwealth.<lf>   (4) Except as provided by the regulations, this section shall not be construed as intended to exclude or limit the operation of any provision of a law of a State or Territory that is capable of operating concurrently with this section. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>

### <name>ATOMIC ENERGY ACT 1953 - SECT 41A Revocation and variation of authorities under section 41 </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   41A.(1) Subject to sections 41B and 41C, an authority under section 41 shall not be varied or revoked otherwise than under and in accordance with this section.<lf>   (2) Subject to subsection (3), where the person on whom an authority has been conferred under section 41 applies, or, in a case where an authority has been conferred under section 41 on 2 or more persons engaged in a joint venture, those persons apply, in writing to the Minister for the revocation of the authority, the Minister may, in his discretion, by instrument in writing under his hand, revoke the authority.<lf>   (3) The Minister shall not, under subsection (2), revoke an authority unless he is satisfied that the conditions or restrictions to which the authority is subject, being conditions or restrictions
