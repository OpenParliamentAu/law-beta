### <name>AIR FORCE ACT 1923-1973 - SECT. 1\. Short title. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>                            AIR  FORCE  ACT  1923-1973<lf> An Act to provide for the Establishment, Organization and Government of the Royal Australian Air Force.<lf> Short title amended; No. 32, 1918, s. 2.<lf>   1\. This Act may be cited as the Air Force Act 1923-1973.*<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AIR FORCE ACT 1923-1973 - SECT. 2\. Definitions. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Substituted by No. 94, 1964, s. 3.<lf>   2\. In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears-<lf> <lf>   ''the Air Force Act'' means the Imperial Act called the Air Force Act;<lf> <lf>   ''the Defence Act'' means the Defence Act 1903-1964;<lf> <lf>   ''the Naval Defence Act'' means the Naval Defence Act 1910-1964;<lf> <lf>   ''this Act'' includes all regulations made or in force under the Air Force Act 1923-1964;<lf> <lf>   ''time of defence emergency'', ''time of war'' and ''war'' have the same meanings respectively as in the Defence Act.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AIR FORCE ACT 1923-1973 - SECT. 3\. Application of Defence Act. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Substituted by No. 94, 1964, s. 3\. Sub-section (1) amended by No. 50, 1965, s. 3.<lf>   3\. (1) Subject to this Act, Part I, sections twelve and thirteen of Part II, sections thirty, fifty A, fifty B, fifty-one and fifty-eight of Part III, Part IV and Parts VI to XI (inclusive) of the Defence Act apply to and in relation to the Air Force and the members of that Force.<lf> 

  (2) In the application of those Parts and sections to and in relation to the Air Force and the members of that Force, any reference to the Minister shall be read as a reference to the Minister administering this Act.<lf> </lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AIR FORCE ACT 1923-1973 - SECT. 4\. Extension of Act to Territories. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Substituted by No. 94, 1964, s. 3; amended by No. 216, 1973, s. 3.<lf>   4\. This Act extends to every Territory.<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AIR FORCE ACT 1923-1973 - SECT. 4A. Air Force. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Inserted by No. 94, 1964, s. 3.<lf>   4A. The Air Force of the Commonwealth consists of three parts, namely, the Permanent Air Force, the Air Force Emergency Force and the Citizen Air Force.<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AIR FORCE ACT 1923-1973 - SECT. 4B. Permanent Air Force. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Inserted by No. 94, 1964, s. 3.<lf>   4B. The Permanent Air Force consists of officers appointed to that Force or transferred to that Force from the Air Force Emergency Force or the Citizen Air Force and of airmen enlisted in that Force.<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AIR FORCE ACT 1923-1973 - SECT. 4C. Air Force Emergency Force. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Inserted by No. 94, 1964, s. 3.<lf>   4C. The Air Force Emergency Force consists of airmen enlisted in that Force and of such officers as are appointed to that Force or transferred to that Force from the Permanent Air Force or the Citizen Air Force.<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AIR FORCE ACT 1923-1973 - SECT. 4D. Citizen Air Force. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Inserted by No. 94, 1964, s. 3.<lf>   4D. (1) The Citizen Air Force consists of the Active Citizen Air Force and the Air Force Reserve.<lf> 

  (2) The Active Citizen Air Force consists of officers appointed to, and airmen enlisted in, that Force and of such officers as are transferred to that Force from the Permanent Air Force, the Air Force Emergency Force or the Air Force Reserve.<lf> <p>  (3) The Air Force Reserve consists of officers appointed to, and airmen enlisted in, that Reserve and of such officers as are transferred to that Reserve from the Permanent Air Force, the Air Force Emergency Force or the Active Citizen Air Force.<lf> </lf></p></lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AIR FORCE ACT 1923-1973 - SECT. 4E. Voluntary entry. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Substituted by No. 50, 1965, s. 4.<lf>   4E. Except as provided by Part IV of the Defence Act 1903-1965, the Air Force shall be kept up by the appointment to that Force, or the enlistment in that Force, of persons who volunteer and are accepted for service in that Force.<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AIR FORCE ACT 1923-1973 - SECT. 4F. Territorial limits of service. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Inserted by No. 94, 1964, s. 3.<lf>   4F. Members of the Air Force may be required to render air-force service on land or sea or in the air, and either within or beyond the territorial limits of Australia.<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AIR FORCE ACT 1923-1973 - SECT. 4G. Service of the Permanent Air Force. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Inserted by No. 94, 1964, s. 3.<lf>   4G. (1) Members of the Permanent Air Force are bound to render continuous full time air-force service for the respective terms for which-<lf> <lf>   (a)  in the case of officers-they hold their appointments in that Force; or<lf> <lf>   (b)  in the case of airmen-they enlisted or re-engaged to serve in that Force, <lf> unless their services are sooner lawfully terminated.<lf> 

  (2) If the term of appointment or the term of engagement of a member of the Permanent Air Force expires during a time for which any part of the Air Force Emergency Force is called out for continuous service, a time of defence emergency or a time of war, the term of his appointment, or of his engagement, as the case may be, in the Permanent Air Force shall be deemed to be extended until the end of that time or, if more than one of those times successively occur, until the end of the last occurring of those times.<lf> </lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AIR FORCE ACT 1923-1973 - SECT. 4H. Service of the Air Force Emergency Force. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Inserted by No. 94, 1964, s. 3.<lf>   4H. (1) Except as provided by the succeeding provisions of this section, members of the Air Force Emergency Force are not bound to serve continuously but are bound to render air-force service for such periods as are fixed by or in accordance with the regulations.<lf> 

  (2) Where the Governor-General considers it desirable to do so, he may, by notice in the Gazette, call out the Air Force Emergency Force, or any specified part of that Force, for continuous service.<lf> <p>  (3) Subject to the succeeding provisions of this section, while the Air Force Emergency Force, or a part of that Force, is called out for continuous service in pursuance of the last preceding sub-section, a member of that Force, or of that part of that Force, as the case may be, is bound to render continuous full time air-force service for such period or periods as the prescribed authority directs.<lf> <p>  (4) Where a member of the Air Force Emergency Force has completed a period of twelve consecutive months' full time air-force service, he shall be released from employment on that form of service with all convenient speed but, until he is so released, he remains bound, notwithstanding anything in the last preceding sub-section, to render that form of service.<lf> <p>  (5) Where a member of the Air Force Emergency Force has, whether in accordance with the last preceding sub-section or otherwise, been released from employment on continuous full time air-force service upon completion of a period of that form of service, then, except in time of war or in time of defence emergency, he is not liable to be again employed on continuous full time air-force service until the expiration of a period equal to the first-mentioned period.<lf> <p>  (6) A member of the Air Force Emergency Force may, at any time, voluntarily undertake to render continuous full time air-force service for a period specified by him and, if that undertaking is accepted, he is bound to render that form of service for that specified period, or for such period or periods within that specified period, as the prescribed authority directs.<lf> <p>  (7) In time of war or in time of defence emergency, members of the Air Force Emergency Force are bound to render continuous full time air-force service for such period or periods during that time as the prescribed authority directs.<lf> <p>  (8) A direction by the prescribed authority under this section may be given with respect to different parts, or members within specified classes of members, of the Air Force Emergency Force.<lf> </lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AIR FORCE ACT 1923-1973 - SECT. 4J. Service of the Citizen Air Force. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Inserted by No. 94, 1964, s. 3.<lf>   4J. (1) Subject to this section, members of the Citizen Air Force are not bound to serve continuously, but are bound to render air-force service for such periods as are fixed by or in accordance with the regulations.<lf> 

  (2) The regulations may make provision for different periods of air-force service with respect to different parts, or members within specified classes of members, of that Force.<lf> <p>  (3) A member of the Citizen Air Force may, at any time, voluntarily undertake to render continuous full time air-force service for a period specified by him and, if that undertaking is accepted, he is bound to render that form of service for that specified period, or for such period or periods within that specified period, as the prescribed authority directs.<lf> <p>  (4) Nothing in this section affects the liability of a member of the Citizen Air Force to be employed on continuous full time air-force service while he is a member of a part of that Force called out for such service under section fifty A of the Defence Act.<lf> </lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AIR FORCE ACT 1923-1973 - SECT. 5\. Application of Air Force Act. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Added by No. 74, 1939, s. 6.<lf> Sub-section (1) amended by No. 73, 1956, s. 3.<lf>   5\. (1) The Air Force Act in force on the date on which the Air Force Act 1939 came into operation shall, subject to this Act and to such modifications, adaptations and exceptions (if any) as are prescribed, apply in relation to the Air Force and the members thereof.<lf> Omitted by No. 73, 1956, s. 3; added by No. 94, 1964, s. 4\. 

  (2) Where by this Act it is provided that provisions of the Air Force Act or of any other law apply or may be applied subject to such modifications as are provided by the regulations, the regulations may make provisions in addition to or in substitution for any of the provisions that may be so modified.*<lf> </lf>
</lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AIR FORCE ACT 1923-1973 - SECT. 6\. Attachment, &c., of members of the Air Force to Naval and Military Forces. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Added by No. 74, 1939, s. 6.<lf>   6\. Members of the Air Force may, under such conditions (if any) as are prescribed, be attached or loaned to or seconded for service with, the Naval or Military Forces.<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AIR FORCE ACT 1923-1973 - SECT. 7\. Members of the Air Force serving on ship of Australian Navy. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Added by No. 74, 1939, s. 6; amended by No. 216, 1973, s. 3.<lf>   7\. A member of the Air Force who is borne on the books of any vessel of the Naval Forces of Australia or any vessel of the Royal Navy, or of the Naval Forces of any part of the King's dominions transferred to the Naval Forces of Australia, shall, in such manner and subject to such modifications (if any) as are prescribed, be subject to the Naval Defence Act as if he were a member of the Naval Forces:<lf> <lf>   Provided that-<lf> <lf>   (a)  this section shall not prevent the application of this Act to any person dealing with or having any relations with any such member, or to any such member if found on shore as a deserter or absentee without leave;<lf> <lf>   (b)  if any such member is employed on land the senior naval officer present may, if it seems to him expedient, order that he shall during that employment be subject to this Act, and while that order is in force he shall be subject to this Act accordingly; and<lf> <lf>   (c)  if any such member commits an offence for which he is not amenable to a Naval court-martial but for which he can be punished under this Act, he may be tried and punished for the offence under this Act.<lf> Section 8 repealed by No. 80, 1950, s. 3.<lf>                          *   *   *   *   *   *   *   *<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>

### <name>AIR FORCE ACT 1923-1973 - SECT. 9\. Regulations. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Added by No. 74, 1939, s. 6.<lf>   9\. The Governor-General may make regulations,* not inconsistent with this Act, prescribing all matters which are required or permitted to be prescribed, or which are necessary or convenient to be prescribed for securing the discipline and good government of the Air Force and the members thereof, whether within or beyond the limits of the Commonwealth, or for carrying out or giving effect to this Act.<lf> ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ -- <lf> </lf></lf></lf></sect>
