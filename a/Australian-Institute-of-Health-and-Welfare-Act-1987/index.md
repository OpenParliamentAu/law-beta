### <name>AUSTRALIAN INSTITUTE OF HEALTH AND WELFARE ACT 1987 - SECT 3 Interpretation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   3\. (1) In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:<lf>   "appoint" includes re-appoint;<lf>   "Chairperson" means the Chairperson of the Institute;<lf>   "Director" means the Director of the Institute;<lf>   "Ethics Committee" means the Health Ethics Committee of the Australian Institute of Health and Welfare;<lf>   "health-related information and statistics" means information and statistics collected and produced from data relevant to health or health services;<lf>   "Institute" means the Australian Institute of Health and Welfare;<lf>   "member" means a member of the Institute;<lf>   "production" means compilation, analysis and dissemination;<lf>   "State Health Minister" means:<lf>   (a) the Minister of the Crown for a State;<lf>   (b) the Minister of the Australian Capital Territory; or<lf>   (c) the Minister of the Northern Territory;<lf> who is responsible, or principally responsible, for the administration of matters relating to health in the State, the Australian Capital Territory or the Northern Territory, as the case may be;<lf>   "State Housing Department" means the Department of State of a State or Territory that deals with matters relating to housing in the State or Territory;<lf>   "State Housing Minister" means:<lf>   (a) the Minister of the Crown for a State; or<lf>   (b) the Minister of the Australian Capital Territory; or<lf>   (c) the Minister of the Northern Territory;<lf> who is responsible, or principally responsible, for the administration of matters relating to housing in the State or Territory, as the case may be;<lf>   "State Welfare Minister" means:<lf>   (a) the Minister of the Crown for a State; or<lf>   (b) the Minister of the Australian Capital Territory; or<lf>   (c) the Minister of the Northern Territory;<lf> who is responsible, or principally responsible, for the administration of matters relating to welfare in the State or Territory, as the case may be;<lf>   "trust money" means money received or held by the Institute on trust;<lf>   "trust property" means property received or held by the Institute on trust;<lf>   "welfare-related information and statistics" means information and statistics collected and produced from data relevant to the provision of welfare services;<lf>   "welfare services" includes:<lf>   (a) aged care services; and<lf>   (b) child care services (including services designed to encourage or support participation by parents in educational courses, training and the labour force); and<lf>   (c) services for people with disabilities; and<lf>   (d) housing assistance (including programs designed to provide access to secure housing in the long term and programs to provide access to crisis accommodation in the short term); and<lf>   (e) child welfare services (including, in particular, child protection and substitute care services); and<lf>   (f) other community services. 

  (2) A reference in this Act to the Chairperson, the Director or a member, in relation to a time when a person is acting in the office of Chairperson, Director, or a member, includes a reference to that person. 

  (3) The Chairperson may be referred to as the Chairman or Chairwoman, as the case requires.

**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN INSTITUTE OF HEALTH AND WELFARE ACT 1987 - SECT 4 Establishment of Institute </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   4\. (1) There is hereby established a body to be known as the Australian Institute of Health and Welfare. 

  (2) The Institute:<lf>   (a) is a body corporate with perpetual succession;<lf>   (b) shall have a common seal; and<lf>   (c) may sue and be sued in its corporate name. <p>  (3) All courts, judges and persons acting judicially shall take judicial notice of the imprint of the common seal of the Institute affixed to a document and shall presume that it was duly affixed. </p></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN INSTITUTE OF HEALTH AND WELFARE ACT 1987 - SECT 5 Functions of the Institute (Institute to have health-related and welfare-related functions) </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   5\. (1AA) The functions of the Institute are:<lf>   (a) the health-related functions conferred by subsection (1); and<lf>   (b) the welfare-related functions conferred by subsection (1A). 

(Health-related functions)<lf>   (1) The Institute's health-related functions are:<lf>   (a) to collect, with the agreement of the Australian Bureau of Statistics and, if necessary, with the Bureau's assistance, health-related information and statistics, whether by itself or in association with other bodies or persons;<lf>   (b) to produce health-related information and statistics, whether by itself or in association with other bodies or persons;<lf>   (c) to co-ordinate the collection and production of health-related information and statistics by other bodies or persons;<lf>   (d) to provide assistance, including financial assistance, for the collection and production of health-related information and statistics by other bodies or persons;<lf>   (e) to develop methods and undertake studies designed to assess the provision, use, cost and effectiveness of health services and health technologies;<lf>   (f) to conduct and promote research into the health of the people of Australia and their health services;<lf>   (g) to develop, in consultation with the Australian Bureau of Statistics, specialised statistical standards and classifications relevant to health and health services, and advise the Bureau on the data to be used by it for the purposes of health-related statistics;<lf>   (h) subject to section 29, to enable researchers to have access to health-related information and statistics held by the Institute or by bodies or persons with whom contracts or arrangements have been entered into by the Institute;<lf>   (j) to publish methodological and substantive reports on work carried out by or in association with the Institute under this subsection;<lf>   (k) to make recommendations to the Minister on the prevention and treatment of diseases and the improvement and promotion of the health and health awareness of the people of Australia; and<lf>   (m) to do anything incidental to any of the foregoing. <p>(Welfare-related functions)<lf>   (1A) The Institute's welfare-related functions are:<lf>   (a) to collect, with the agreement of the Australian Bureau of Statistics, and, if necessary, with the Bureau's assistance, welfare-related information and statistics (whether by itself or in association with other bodies or persons); and<lf>   (b) to produce welfare-related information and statistics (whether by itself or in association with other bodies or persons); and<lf>   (c) to co-ordinate the collection and production of welfare-related information and statistics by other bodies or persons; and<lf>   (d) to provide assistance (including financial assistance) for the collection and production of welfare-related information and statistics by other bodies or persons; and<lf>   (e) to develop, in consultation with the Australian Bureau of Statistics, specialised statistical standards and classifications relevant to welfare services; and<lf>   (f) subject to section 29, to enable researchers to have access to welfare-related information and statistics held by the Institute or by bodies or persons with whom contracts or arrangements have been entered into by the Institute; and<lf>   (g) to publish methodological and substantive reports on work carried out by or in association with the Institute under this subsection; and<lf>   (h) to do anything incidental to the functions conferred by paragraphs (a) to (g). <p>(Functions of Australian Bureau of Statistics not limited by this section)<lf>   (3) This section is not intended to limit the functions of the Australian Bureau of Statistics. </lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN INSTITUTE OF HEALTH AND WELFARE ACT 1987 - SECT 6 Powers of Institute </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   6\. The Institute has power to do all things necessary or convenient to be done for or in connection with the performance of its functions and, in particular, has power:<lf>   (a)  to enter into contracts or arrangements, including contracts or arrangements with bodies or persons to perform functions on behalf of the Institute;<lf>   (b) to acquire, hold and dispose of real or personal property;<lf>   (c) to occupy, use and control any land or building owned or held under lease by the Commonwealth and made available for the purposes of the Institute;<lf>   (d) to appoint agents and attorneys and act as an agent for other persons;<lf>   (e) to accept gifts, grants, devises and bequests made to the Institute, whether on trust or otherwise, and to act as trustee of money or other property vested in the Institute on trust;<lf>   (f) subject to section 29, to:<lf>     (i) release data to other bodies or persons; and<lf>     (ii) publish the results of any of its work; and<lf>   (g) to do anything incidental to any of its powers. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN INSTITUTE OF HEALTH AND WELFARE ACT 1987 - SECT 7 Directions by Minister </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   7\. (1) The Minister may, by notice in writing delivered to the Chairperson, give a direction to the Institute with respect to the performance of its functions or the exercise of its powers. 

  (1A) The Minister must consult the Chairperson before giving any direction to the Institute. 

  (1B) The Minister must consult each State Health Minister before giving the direction if the direction relates to the Institute's health-related functions. 

  (1C) The Minister must consult each State Welfare Minister before giving the direction if the direction:<lf>   (a) relates to the Institute's welfare-related functions; and<lf>   (b) does not concern housing matters. <p>  (1D) The Minister must consult each State Housing Minister before giving the direction if the direction:<lf>   (a) relates to the Institute's welfare-related functions; and<lf>   (b) concerns housing matters. <p>  (2) The Institute shall comply with any direction given under subsection (1). </p></lf></lf></p></lf></lf>
</sect>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN INSTITUTE OF HEALTH AND WELFARE ACT 1987 - DIVISION 2  Division 2 - Constitution and Meetings of Institute </name>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN INSTITUTE OF HEALTH AND WELFARE ACT 1987 - SECT 8 Constitution of Institute </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   8\. (1) Subject to subsection (2), the Institute shall consist of the following members:<lf>   (a) the Chairperson;<lf>   (b) the Director;<lf>   (c) a member nominated by the Australian Health Ministers' Advisory Council;<lf>   (ca) a member nominated by the Standing Committee of Social Welfare Administrators;<lf>   (cb) a representative of the State Housing Departments nominated in the manner determined by the Minister;<lf>   (d) the Australian Statistician;<lf>   (e) the Secretary to the Department;<lf>   (f) a person:<lf>     (i) who has knowledge of the needs of consumers of<lf> health services; and<lf>     (ii) whose nomination has been recommended by a<lf> body that is referred to in the Schedule and that represents consumers of health services; and<lf>     (iii) who has been nominated by the Minister;<lf>   (fa) a person:<lf>     (i) who has knowledge of the needs of consumers of<lf> welfare services; and<lf>     (ii) whose nomination has been recommended by a<lf> body that is referred to in the Schedule and that represents consumers of welfare services; and<lf>     (iii) who has been nominated by the Minister;<lf>   (fb) a person:<lf>     (i) who has knowledge of the needs of consumers of<lf> housing assistance services; and<lf>     (ii) whose nomination has been recommended by a<lf> body that is referred to in the Schedule and that represents consumers of housing assistance services; and<lf>     (iii) who has been nominated by the Minister;<lf>   (fc) a person:<lf>     (i) who has expertise in research into public health<lf> issues; and<lf>     (ii) whose nomination has been recommended by a<lf> body that is referred to in the Schedule and that is a peak body promoting research into public health issues; and<lf>     (iii) who has been nominated by the Minister;<lf>   (g) 3 other members nominated by the Minister;<lf>   (h) a member of the staff of the Institute elected by that staff. 

  (1A) A recommendation for the purposes of paragraph (1) (f), (fa), (fb) or (fc):<lf>   (a) may be made by one or more bodies; and<lf>   (b) may contain one or more names. <p>  (2) If the person referred to in paragraph (1) (d) or (e) is not available to serve as a member of the Institute, that person shall nominate a person to be a member of the Institute in lieu of himself or herself. </p><p>  (3) The performance of the functions, or the exercise of the powers, of the Institute is not affected by reason only of:<lf>   (a) a vacancy in the office of a member referred to in paragraph (1) (a), (b), (f), (fa), (fb), (fc) or (h);<lf>   (b) the number of members referred to in paragraph (g) falling below 3 for a period of not more than 6 months;<lf>   (ba) a vacancy of not more than 6 months duration in the office of a member referred to in paragraph (1) (c), (ca) or (cb);<lf>   (c) a vacancy in the office of the member referred to in paragraph (1) (d) or (e) or the member (if any) nominated in lieu of that member under subsection (2). <p>  (4) The following subsections have effect in relation to a member other than a member referred to in paragraph (1) (b), (d) or (e). </p><p>  (5) Subject to this section, a member shall be appointed by the Governor-General. </p><p>  (5A) Subject to this Act, a member referred to in paragraph (1) (a), (c), (ca), (cb), (f), (fa), (fb), (fc) or (g) may be appointed on a full-time or a part-time basis and holds office for such period, not exceeding 3 years, as is specified in the instrument of appointment. </p><p>  (5B) Subject to this Act, a member elected under paragraph (1) (h) holds office on a part-time basis for a period of one year commencing on:<lf>   (a) the day on which the poll for the election of the member is held; or<lf>   (b) if that day occurs before the expiration of the term of office of the person whose place the member fills - the day after the expiration of that term. <p>  (6) A person who has attained the age of 65 years shall not hold office as a member on a full-time basis. </p><p>  (7) A member holds office on such terms and conditions (if any) in respect of matters not provided for by this Act as are determined by the Governor-General. </p><p>  (8) The appointment of a member is not invalid because of a defect or irregularity in connection with the member's nomination or appointment. </p></lf></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN INSTITUTE OF HEALTH AND WELFARE ACT 1987 - SECT 9 Acting members </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   9\. (1) The Minister may appoint a person to act in the office of Chairperson, of Director, or of member (other than the Chairperson or Director):<lf>   (a) during a vacancy in the office, whether or not an appointment has previously been made to the office; or<lf>   (b) during any period, or during all periods, when the holder of the office is absent from duty or from Australia or is, for any other reason, unable to perform the functions of the office;<lf> but a person appointed to act during a vacancy shall not continue so to act for more than 12 months. 

  (2) A person may resign appointment under this section by instrument in writing delivered to the Minister. 

  (3) An appointment may be expressed to have effect only in such circumstances as are specified in the instrument of appointment. 

  (4) The Minister may:<lf>   (a) determine the terms and conditions of appointment, including remuneration and allowances, if any, of a person acting under subsection (1); and<lf>   (b) terminate such an appointment at any time. <p>  (5) Where a person is acting in an office and the office becomes vacant while that person is so acting, then, subject to subsection (3), the person may continue so to act until the Minister otherwise directs, the vacancy is filled or a period of 12 months from the date on which the vacancy occurred expires, whichever first happens. </p><p>  (6) While a person is acting in an office, the person has and may exercise all the powers, and shall perform all the functions and duties, of the holder of the office. </p><p>  (7) Anything done by or in relation to a person purporting to act under this section is not invalid by reason only that:<lf>   (a) the occasion for the appointment of the person had not arisen;<lf>   (b) there was a defect or irregularity in or in connection with the appointment;<lf>   (c) the appointment had ceased to have effect; or<lf>   (d) the occasion for the person to act had not arisen or had ceased. </lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN INSTITUTE OF HEALTH AND WELFARE ACT 1987 - SECT 10 Remuneration and allowances </name>

  (2) A member shall be paid such allowances as are prescribed. 

  (3) This section has effect subject to the Remuneration Tribunal Act 1973\. 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN INSTITUTE OF HEALTH AND WELFARE ACT 1987 - SECT 11 Leave of absence </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   11\. (1) Subject to section 87E of the Public Service Act 1922, a full-time member has such recreation leave entitlements as are determined by the Remuneration Tribunal. 

  (2) The Minister may:<lf>   (a) grant a full-time member leave of absence, other than recreation leave, on such terms and conditions as to remuneration or otherwise as the Minister determines; and<lf>   (b) grant a part-time member leave of absence on such terms and conditions as to remuneration or otherwise as the Minister determines. </lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN INSTITUTE OF HEALTH AND WELFARE ACT 1987 - SECT 12 Resignation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   12\. A member may resign by instrument in writing delivered to the Governor-General. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN INSTITUTE OF HEALTH AND WELFARE ACT 1987 - SECT 13 Termination of appointment </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   13\. (1) The Governor-General may terminate the appointment of a member because of misbehaviour or physical or mental incapacity. 

  (2) If a member:<lf>   (a) becomes bankrupt, applies to take the benefit of any law for the relief of bankrupt or insolvent debtors, compounds with creditors or assigns remuneration for their benefit;<lf>   (b) without reasonable excuse, contravenes section 14;<lf>   (c) being a full-time member who is paid remuneration under this Part:<lf>     (i) engages in paid employment outside his or her duties without<lf> the consent of the Minister; or<lf>     (ii) is absent from duty, without leave of absence for 14<lf> consecutive days or for 28 days in any period of 12 months; or<lf>   (d) being a part-time member, is absent, without leave by the Minister, from 3 consecutive meetings of the Institute;<lf> the Governor-General may terminate the appointment of the member. <p>  (3) Where:<lf>   (a) a member has been appointed under paragraph 8 (1) (c), (ca) or (cb) or subsection 8 (2) on the nomination of a body or person referred to in that paragraph or subsection, as the case may be, and the body or person notifies the Minister in writing that the nomination is withdrawn; or<lf>   (b) a member has been appointed under paragraph 8 (1) (g) on the nomination of the Minister and the Minister withdraws his or her nomination of the member; or<lf>   (c) a member has been elected under paragraph 8 (1) (h) and the member ceases to be a member of the staff of the Institute;<lf> the Governor-General shall terminate the appointment of the member. </lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN INSTITUTE OF HEALTH AND WELFARE ACT 1987 - SECT 14 Disclosure of interests </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   14\. (1) A member who has a direct or indirect interest, pecuniary or otherwise, in a matter being considered or about to be considered by the Institute, being an interest that would conflict with the proper performance of the member's functions in relation to the consideration of the matter, shall, as soon as practicable after the relevant facts have come to the knowledge of the member, disclose the nature of the interest at a meeting of the Institute. 

  (2) A disclosure under subsection (1) shall be recorded in the minutes of the meeting. 

  (3) This section does not apply to an interest of a member referred to in paragraph 8 (1) (c), (ca), (cb) or (h) or a member nominated under subsection 8 (2), being an interest that the member has by reason only of having been nominated by a body or person referred to in that paragraph or subsection. 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN INSTITUTE OF HEALTH AND WELFARE ACT 1987 - SECT 15 Meetings </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   15\. (1) Subject to this section, meetings of the Institute shall be held at such times and places as the Institute determines. 

  (2) The Institute shall meet at least once every 4 months. 

  (3) The Chairperson:<lf>   (a) may at any time convene a meeting; and<lf>   (b) shall convene a meeting on receipt of a written request signed by not fewer than 3 members. <p>  (4) The Minister may convene such meetings as the Minister considers necessary. </p><p>  (5) At a meeting:<lf>   (a) if the Chairperson is present, the Chairperson shall preside;<lf>   (b) if the Chairperson is absent, the members present shall appoint one of their number to preside;<lf>   (c) a majority of the members for the time being constitute a quorum;<lf>   (d) all questions shall be decided by a majority of the votes of the members present and voting; and<lf>   (e) the member presiding has a deliberative vote and, if necessary, also has a casting vote. <p>  (6) The Institute shall keep minutes of its proceedings. </p><p>  (7) The Institute shall regulate the procedure of its meetings as it thinks fit. </p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf>

  (12) Subsections (9) and (10) have effect subject to the Remuneration Tribunal Act 1973 . 

  (13) Section 14 applies in relation to a committee as if:<lf>   (a) references in that section to a member were references to a member of the committee; and<lf>   (b) references in that section to the Institute were references to the committee. </lf></lf>
