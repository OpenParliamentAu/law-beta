###  AUSTRALIA ACT 1986- SECT 1  Termination of power of Parliament of United Kingdom to legislate for Australia 
<dl compact="">

		No Act of the Parliament of the United Kingdom passed after the commencement

of this Act shall extend, or be deemed to extend, to the Commonwealth, to a

State or to a Territory as part of the law of the Commonwealth, of the State or

of the Territory.

 </dl>
###  AUSTRALIA ACT 1986- SECT 2  Legislative powers of Parliaments of States 
<dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>It is hereby declared and enacted that the legislative powers of the

Parliament of each State include full power to make laws for the peace, order

and good government of that State that have extra-territorial operation.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>It is hereby further declared and enacted that the legislative powers of the Parliament of each State include all legislative powers that the Parliament of the United Kingdom might have exercised before the commencement of this Act for the peace, order and good government of that State but nothing in this subsection confers on a State any capacity that the State did not have immediately before the commencement of this Act to engage in relations with countries outside Australia. </dd> </dl>
###  AUSTRALIA ACT 1986- SECT 3  Termination of restrictions on legislative powers of Parliaments of States 
<dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Act of the Parliament of the United Kingdom known as the Colonial Laws

Validity Act 1865 shall not apply to any law made after the commencement of

this Act by the Parliament of a State.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>No law and no provision of any law made after the commencement of this Act by the Parliament of a State shall be void or inoperative on the ground that it is repugnant to the law of England, or to the provisions of any existing or future Act of the Parliament of the United Kingdom, or to any order, rule or regulation made under any such Act, and the powers of the Parliament of a State shall include the power to repeal or amend any such Act, order, rule or regulation in so far as it is part of the law of the State. </dd> </dl>
###  AUSTRALIA ACT 1986- SECT 4  Powers of State Parliaments in relation to merchant shipping 
<dl compact="">

		Sections&#160;735 and 736 of the Act of the Parliament of the United Kingdom

known as the Merchant Shipping Act 1894, in so far as they are part of the law

of a State, are hereby repealed.

###  AUSTRALIA ACT 1986- SECT 6  Manner and form of making certain State laws 
<dl compact="">

		Notwithstanding sections&#160;2 and 3(2) above, a law made after the

commencement of this Act by the Parliament of a State respecting the

constitution, powers or procedure of the Parliament of the State shall be of no

force or effect unless it is made in such manner and form as may from time to

time be required by a law made by that Parliament, whether made before or after

the commencement of this Act.

 </dl>
###  AUSTRALIA ACT 1986- SECT 7  Powers and functions of Her Majesty and Governors in respect of States 
<dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Her Majesty's representative in each State shall be the Governor.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Subject to subsections&#160;(3) and (4) below, all powers and functions of Her Majesty in respect of a State are exercisable only by the Governor of the State.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>Subsection&#160;(2) above does not apply in relation to the power to appoint, and the power to terminate the appointment of, the Governor of a State.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>While Her Majesty is personally present in a State, Her Majesty is not precluded from exercising any of Her powers and functions in respect of the State that are the subject of subsection&#160;(2) above.</dd> <dt>(5)</dt><dd>The advice to Her Majesty in relation to the exercise of the powers and functions of Her Majesty in respect of a State shall be tendered by the Premier of the State. </dd> </dl>
###  AUSTRALIA ACT 1986- SECT 8  State laws not subject to disallowance or suspension of operation 
<dl compact="">

		An Act of the Parliament of a State that has been assented to by the Governor

of the State shall not, after the commencement of this Act, be subject to

disallowance by Her Majesty, nor shall its operation be suspended pending the

signification of Her Majesty's pleasure thereon.

 </dl>
###  AUSTRALIA ACT 1986- SECT 9  State laws not subject to withholding of assent or reservation 
<dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>No law or instrument shall be of any force or effect in so far as it

purports to require the Governor of a State to withhold assent from any Bill

for an Act of the State that has been passed in such manner and form as may

from time to time be required by a law made by the Parliament of the State.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>No law or instrument shall be of any force or effect in so far as it purports to require the reservation of any Bill for an Act of a State for the signification of Her Majesty's pleasure thereon. </dd> </dl>
###  AUSTRALIA ACT 1986- SECT 10  Termination of responsibility of United Kingdom Government in relation to State matters 
<dl compact="">

		After the commencement of this Act Her Majesty's Government in the United

Kingdom shall have no responsibility for the government of any State.

 </dl>

###  AUSTRALIA ACT 1986- SECT 15  Method of repeal or amendment of this Act or Statute of Westminster 
<dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>This Act or the Statute of Westminster 1931, as amended and in force from

time to time, in so far as it is part of the law of the Commonwealth, of a

State or of a Territory, may be repealed or amended by an Act of the Parliament

of the Commonwealth passed at the request or with the concurrence of the

Parliaments of all the States and, subject to subsection&#160;(3) below, only

in that manner.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>For the purposes of subsection&#160;(1) above, an Act of the Parliament of the Commonwealth that is repugnant to this Act or the Statute of Westminster 1931, as amended and in force from time to time, or to any provision of this Act or of that Statute as so amended and in force, shall, to the extent of the repugnancy, be deemed an Act to repeal or amend the Act, Statute or provision to which it is repugnant.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>Nothing in subsection&#160;(1) above limits or prevents the exercise by the Parliament of the Commonwealth of any powers that may be conferred upon that Parliament by any alteration to the Constitution of the Commonwealth made in accordance with section&#160;128 of the Constitution of the Commonwealth after the commencement of this Act. </dd> </dl>
###  AUSTRALIA ACT 1986- SECT 16  Interpretation 
<dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:

</dd> </dl>
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_appeal_ includes a petition of appeal, and a complaint in the

nature of an appeal. _appeal to Her Majesty in Council_ includes any appeal to Her Majesty. _Australian court_ means a court of a State or any other court of Australia or of a Territory other than the High Court. _court_ includes a judge, judicial officer or other person acting judicially. _decision_ includes determination, judgment, decree, order or sentence. _Governor_, in relation to a State, includes any person for the time being administering the government of the State. _State_ means a State of the Commonwealth and includes a new State. _the Commonwealth of Australia Constitution Act_ means the Act of the Parliament of the United Kingdom known as the Commonwealth of Australia Constitution Act. _the Constitution of the Commonwealth_ means the Constitution of the Commonwealth set forth in section&#160;9 of the Commonwealth of Australia Constitution Act, being that Constitution as altered and in force from time to time. _the Statute of Westminster 1931_ means the Act of the Parliament of the United Kingdom known as the Statute of Westminster 1931\.  </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>The expression _a law made by that Parliament_ in

section&#160;6 above and the expression _a law made by the Parliament_ in section&#160;9 above include, in relation to the State of

Western Australia, the Constitution Act 1889 of that State.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>A reference in this Act to the Parliament of a State includes, in relation to the State of New South Wales, a reference to the legislature of that State as constituted from time to time in accordance with the Constitution Act, 1902, or any other Act of that State, whether or not, in relation to any particular legislative act, the consent of the Legislative Council of that State is necessary. </dd> </dl></dl>
