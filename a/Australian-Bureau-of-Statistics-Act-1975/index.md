### <name>AUSTRALIAN BUREAU OF STATISTICS ACT 1975 - LONG TITLE </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>      An Act to establish an Australian Bureau of Statistics and<lf>                       for related Purposes<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN BUREAU OF STATISTICS ACT 1975 - PART I  PART I-PRELIMINARY </name>
</b>** 
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN BUREAU OF STATISTICS ACT 1975 - SECT 1 Short title </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   1\. This Act may be cited as the Australian Bureau of Statistics Act 1975.*1* SEE NOTES TO FIRST ARTICLE OF THIS CHAPTER . </sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN BUREAU OF STATISTICS ACT 1975 - SECT 2 Commencement </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   2\. This Act shall come into operation on a date to be fixed by Proclamation.*1* SEE NOTES TO FIRST ARTICLE OF THIS CHAPTER . </sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN BUREAU OF STATISTICS ACT 1975 - SECT 3 Interpretation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   3\. (1) In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:<lf>   'Bureau' means the Australian Bureau of Statistics established by subsection 5 (1);<lf>   'Chairperson' means the Chairperson of the Council holding office under subsection 19 (2);<lf>   'Council' means the Australian Statistics Advisory Council established by section 17;<lf>   'member' means a member of the Council;<lf>   'Statistician' means the Australian Statistician referred to in subsection 5 (2).<lf>   (2) The Chairperson may be referred to as the Chairman or Chairwoman, as the case requires. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN BUREAU OF STATISTICS ACT 1975 - SECT 4 Australian Statistician to have functions etc. of Commonwealth Statistician </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   4\. (2) After the commencement of this Act:<lf>   (a) the Australian Statistician shall:<lf>     (i) have the functions, powers and duties expressed by any Act, or by<lf> any regulations or other instrument made under any Act, to be conferred or imposed on the Commonwealth Statistician; and<lf>     (ii) have the functions and powers expressed by a law of a Territory, or by any regulations or other instrument made under such a law, to be conferred on the Commonwealth Statistician; and<lf>   (b) a reference in any Act, in any law of a Territory, or in an instrument<lf> under any Act or law of a Territory, to the Commonwealth Statistician shall have effect as if the office of Australian Statistician were a continuation of the office of the Commonwealth Statistician. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN BUREAU OF STATISTICS ACT 1975 - PART II  PART II-AUSTRALIAN BUREAU OF STATISTICS AND AUSTRALIAN STATISTICIAN </name>
</b>** 
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN BUREAU OF STATISTICS ACT 1975 - SECT 5 Establishment of Bureau and office of Statistician </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   5\. (1) There is hereby established a Bureau to be known as the Australian Bureau of Statistics.<lf>   (2) There shall be an Australian Statistician.<lf>   (3) The Bureau shall consist of the Statistician and the staff referred to in subsection 16 (1).<lf>   (4) The Statistician shall control the operations of the Bureau and shall have such other functions, powers and duties as are conferred or imposed upon the Statistician by or under any Act and such other functions and powers as are conferred upon the Statistician by or under any law of a Territory. </lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN BUREAU OF STATISTICS ACT 1975 - SECT 6 Functions of Bureau </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   6\. (1) The functions of the Bureau are as follows:<lf>   (a) to constitute the central statistical authority for the Australian<lf> Government and, by arrangements with the Governments of the States, provide statistical services for those Governments;<lf>   (b) to collect, compile, analyse and disseminate statistics and related<lf> information;<lf>   (c) to ensure co-ordination of the operations of official bodies in the<lf> collection, compilation and dissemination of statistics and related information, with particular regard to:<lf>     (i) the avoidance of duplication in the collection by official bodies<lf> of information for statistical purposes;<lf>     (ii) the attainment of compatibility between, and the integration of,<lf> statistics compiled by official bodies; and<lf>     (iii) the maximum possible utilization, for statistical purposes, of<lf> information, and means of collection of information, available to official bodies;<lf>   (d) to formulate, and ensure compliance with, standards for the carrying out<lf> by official bodies of operations for statistical purposes;<lf>   (e) to provide advice and assistance to official bodies in relation to<lf> statistics; and<lf>   (f) to provide liaison between Australia, on the one hand, and other<lf> countries and international organizations, on the other hand, in relation to statistical matters.<lf>   (2) For the purpose of the performance of its functions and for the purpose of co-ordinating statistical activities and securing the observance of statistical standards, the Bureau may collaborate with bodies, being Departments and authorities of the States, the Administrations and authorities of the external Territories and local governing bodies, in the collection, compilation, analysis and dissemination of statistics, including statistics obtained from the records of those bodies.<lf>   (3) Subject to subsection (4), each new proposal for the collection of information for statistical purposes by the Bureau shall be laid before both Houses of the Parliament before its implementation, unless the proposal is for the collection of information on a voluntary basis.<lf>   (4) Where, in relation to a proposal to which subsection (3) is applicable, being a proposal for the collection of information relating to businesses, the Minister considers it necessary to commence implementation of the proposal at a time when it is not practicable to comply with subsection (3) the Minister may authorize the implementation of the proposal without compliance with that subsection but in such a case particulars of the nature of the information to which the authorization relates shall be laid before each House of the Parliament within 5 sitting days of that House after the giving of the authorization.<lf>   (5) For the purposes of this section:<lf>   (a) a reference to statistical purposes shall be read as including purposes<lf> in connexion with the collection, compilation, analysis and dissemination of statistics; and<lf>   (b) a reference to an official body shall be read as a reference to:<lf>     (i) a Department of the Australian Public Service;<lf>     (ii) the holder of an office established for a public purpose by or<lf> under an Act or a law of an internal Territory; or<lf>     (iii) a body corporate, or other body, established for a public<lf> purpose by or under an Act or a law of an internal Territory other than such a body corporate, or other body, that is declared by the regulations not to be an official body for the purposes of this Act. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN BUREAU OF STATISTICS ACT 1975 - SECT 7 Appointment and tenure of office of Statistician </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   7\. (1) The Statistician shall be appointed by the Governor-General and, subject to this Act, holds office for such period, not exceeding 7 years, as is specified in the instrument of his or her appointment but is eligible for re-appointment.<lf>   (2) A person who has attained the age of 65 years shall not be appointed or re-appointed as the Statistician and a person shall not be appointed or re-appointed as the Statistician for a period that extends beyond the date on which the person will attain the age of 65 years.<lf>   (3) The Statistician holds office on such terms and conditions (if any) in respect of matters not provided for by this Act as are determined by the Governor-General. </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN BUREAU OF STATISTICS ACT 1975 - SECT 8 Statistician not to undertake any other work </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   8\. The Statistician shall not engage in paid employment outside the duties of his or her office except with the approval of the Minister. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN BUREAU OF STATISTICS ACT 1975 - SECT 9 Remuneration and allowances </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   9\. (1) The Statistician shall be paid such remuneration as is determined by the Remuneration Tribunal, but, if no determination of that remuneration by the Tribunal is in operation, the Statistician shall be paid such remuneration as is prescribed.<lf>   (2) The Statistician shall be paid such allowances as are prescribed.<lf>   (3) This section has effect subject to the Remuneration Tribunal Act 1973\. </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN BUREAU OF STATISTICS ACT 1975 - SECT 10 Leave of absence </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   10\. The Minister may grant leave of absence to the Statistician upon such terms and conditions as to remuneration or otherwise as the Minister determines. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN BUREAU OF STATISTICS ACT 1975 - SECT 11 Resignation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   11\. The Statistician may resign his or her office by writing signed by the Statistician and delivered to the Governor-General. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN BUREAU OF STATISTICS ACT 1975 - SECT 12 Removal from office </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   12\. (1) The Governor-General may remove the Statistician from office on an address praying for his or her removal on the ground of misbehaviour or incapacity being presented to the Governor-General by each House of the Parliament in the same session of the Parliament.<lf>   (2) The Governor-General may suspend the Statistician from office on the ground of misbehaviour or incapacity.<lf>   (3) Where the Governor-General suspends the Statistician from office, the Minister shall cause a statement of the ground of the suspension to be laid before each House of the Parliament within 7 sitting days of that House after the suspension.<lf>   (4) Where such a statement has been laid before a House of the Parliament, that House may, within 15 sitting days of that House after the day on which the statement has been laid before it, by resolution, declare that the Statistician should be removed from office and, if each House so passes such a resolution, the Governor-General shall remove the Statistician from office.<lf>   (5) If, at the expiration of 15 sitting days of a House of the Parliament after the day on which the statement has been laid before that House, that House has not passed such a resolution, the suspension terminates.<lf>   (6) The suspension of the Statistician from office under this section does not affect any entitlement of the Statistician to be paid remuneration and allowances.<lf>   (7) If the Statistician becomes bankrupt, applies to take the benefit of any law for the relief of bankrupt or insolvent debtors, compounds with his or her creditors or makes an assignment of his or her remuneration for their benefit, the Governor-General shall remove the Statistician from office.<lf>   (8) The Governor-General may, with the consent of the Statistician, retire the Statistician from office on the ground of incapacity.<lf>   (9) The Statistician shall not be removed or suspended from office except as provided by this section. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN BUREAU OF STATISTICS ACT 1975 - SECT 15 Acting appointments </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   15\. (1) The Governor-General may appoint a person to act in the office of Statistician during any period, or during all periods, when the person holding the office is absent from duty or from Australia or is, for any reason, unable to perform the duties of his or her office or during a vacancy in that office, but a person so appointed to act during a vacancy shall not continue so to act for more than 12 months.<lf>   (2) The Governor-General may:<lf>   (a) determine the terms and conditions of appointment of a person appointed<lf> under this section; and<lf>   (b) at any time terminate such an appointment.<lf>   (3) Where a person is acting in the office of Statistician in pursuance of an appointment under this section otherwise than during a vacancy in that office and the office becomes vacant while that person is so acting, that person may continue to act in the office until the Governor-General otherwise directs, the vacancy is filled or a period of 12 months from the date on which the vacancy occurred expires, whichever first happens.<lf>   (4) Sections 10 and 11 apply in relation to a person appointed under this section in like manner as they apply in relation to the Statistician.<lf>   (5) While a person is acting in the office of Statistician in pursuance of an appointment under this section, the person has all the functions, powers and duties of the Statistician under this Act or any other law.<lf>   (6) The validity of anything done by a person appointed under this section shall not be called in question on the ground that the occasion for the appointment had not arisen or that the appointment had ceased to have effect. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN BUREAU OF STATISTICS ACT 1975 - SECT 16 Staff </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   16\. (1) Subject to subsection (2), the staff required for the purposes of the Bureau shall be persons appointed or employed under, or whose services are made available in accordance with arrangements made under, the Public Service Act 1922.<lf>   (2) Subject to subsection (2A), the Statistician may engage persons, including persons referred to in subsection (1), to assist in carrying out the functions of the Bureau.<lf>   (2A) Persons referred to in subsection (1) may be engaged under subsection (2) only in relation to:<lf>   (a) the taking of the Census mentioned in the Census and Statistics Act<lf> 1905; and<lf>   (b) the collection of other statistics and related information.<lf>   (3) The terms and conditions of employment of persons engaged as mentioned in subsection (2) (including persons employed at the commencement of this Act) shall be such as are, with the approval of the Public Service Board, determined by the Statistician.<lf>   (4) The Statistician has all the powers of, or exercisable by, a Secretary under the Public Service Act 1922 so far as those powers relate to the branch of the Australian Public Service comprising the staff referred to in subsection (1) as if that branch were a separate Department of the Australian Public Service.<lf>   (5) For the purposes of section 26 of the Public Service Act 1922, the Statistician shall be deemed to be a Secretary. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN BUREAU OF STATISTICS ACT 1975 - PART III  PART III-AUSTRALIAN STATISTICS ADVISORY COUNCIL </name>
</b>** 
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN BUREAU OF STATISTICS ACT 1975 - SECT 17 Establishment of Council </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   17\. There is hereby established a Council to be known as the Australian Statistics Advisory Council. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN BUREAU OF STATISTICS ACT 1975 - SECT 18 Functions of Council </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   18\. (1) The functions of the Council are to advise the Minister and the Statistician in relation to:<lf>   (a) the improvement, extension and co-ordination of statistical services<lf> provided for public purposes in Australia;<lf>   (b) annual and longer term priorities and programs of work that should be<lf> adopted in relation to major aspects of the provision of those statistical services; and<lf>   (c) any other matters relating generally to those statistical services.<lf>   (2) Either the Minister or the Statistician, or both of them, may refer matters of the kind referred to in subsection (1) to the Council for the purpose of seeking the advice of the Council in relation to those matters. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN BUREAU OF STATISTICS ACT 1975 - SECT 19 Membership of Council </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   19.*2*  (1) The Council shall consist of:<lf>   (a) the Chairperson;<lf>   (b) the Statistician; and<lf>   (c) such other members, being not less than 10 and not more than 22 in<lf> number, as the Minister determines.<lf>   (2) The Chairperson and the members referred to in paragraph (1) (c) shall be appointed by the Minister as part-time members, and shall hold office, subject to this Act:<lf>   (a) in the case of the Chairperson-for a period of 5 years; and<lf>   (b) in the case of each other member-for such period, not exceeding 3 years,<lf> as is specified in his or her instrument of appointment;<lf> but are eligible for re-appointment.<lf>   (3) If the Premier of a State or the Chief Minister of the Northern Territory nominates a person for appointment to the Council, the Minister shall appoint that person as one of the members referred to in paragraph (1) (c) unless the Council already includes a member appointed on the nomination of the Premier of that State or the Chief Minister of the Northern Territory, as the case may be. *2*S. 19 (3)-The Australian Bureau of Statistics Act 1975 was modified by regulation 2 and Schedule 1 of the A.C.T. **<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN BUREAU OF STATISTICS ACT 1975 - SECT 20 Resignation and removal of members </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   20.(1) A member may resign his or her office by writing signed by the member and delivered to the Minister.<lf>   (2) The Minister may remove a member from office for misbehaviour or physical or mental incapacity. </lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN BUREAU OF STATISTICS ACT 1975 - SECT 21 Remuneration of members </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   21\. (1) A member of the Council shall be paid such remuneration as is determined by the Remuneration Tribunal, but, if no determination of that remuneration by the Tribunal is in operation, the member shall be paid such remuneration as is prescribed.<lf>   (2) A member of the Council shall be paid such allowances as are prescribed.<lf>   (3) This section has effect subject to the Remuneration Tribunal Act 1973\. </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN BUREAU OF STATISTICS ACT 1975 - SECT 22 Meetings </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   22\. (1) The Council shall hold such meetings as are necessary for the performance of its functions, and shall meet at least once in every calendar year.<lf>   (2) The meetings of the Council may be convened by the Chairperson or by the Minister.<lf>   (3) At a meeting of the Council, a quorum is constituted by one-third of the members for the time being holding office.<lf>   (4) The Chairperson shall preside at all meetings of the Council at which the Chairperson is present.<lf>   (5) If the Chairperson is not present at a meeting of the Council, the members present shall elect one of their number to preside at the meeting.<lf>   (6) Questions arising at a meeting of the Council shall be determined by a majority of the votes of the members present and voting.<lf>   (7) The member presiding at a meeting of the Council has a deliberative vote and, in the event of an equality of votes, also has a casting vote. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN BUREAU OF STATISTICS ACT 1975 - PART IV  PART IV-MISCELLANEOUS </name>
</b>** 
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN BUREAU OF STATISTICS ACT 1975 - SECT 24 Annual reports </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   24\. (1) The Statistician shall, as soon as practicable after 30 June in each year, prepare and submit to the Minister, for presentation to the Parliament, a report on the operations of the Bureau during the period of 12 months that ended on that date.<lf>   (2) The Council shall, as soon as practicable after 30 June in each year, prepare and submit to the Minister, for presentation to the Parliament, a report relating to matters connected with the operation of this Act.<lf>   (3) The Minister shall cause each of the reports referred to in subsections (1) and (2) to be laid before each House of the Parliament within 15 sitting days of that House after its receipt by the Minister. </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN BUREAU OF STATISTICS ACT 1975 - SECT 25 Regulations </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   25\. The Governor-General may make regulations, not inconsistent with this Act, prescribing all matters required or permitted by this Act to be prescribed, or necessary or convenient to be prescribed for carrying out or giving effect to this Act. </sect>
