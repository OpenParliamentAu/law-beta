### <name>ANTARCTIC MARINE LIVING RESOURCES CONSERVATION ACT 1981 - LONG TITLE </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>        An Act relating to the conservation of marine living<lf>        resources of the Antarctic and its surrounding seas<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>ANTARCTIC MARINE LIVING RESOURCES CONSERVATION ACT 1981 - PREAMBLE </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   WHEREAS a Convention entitled "Convention on the Conservation of Antarctic Marine Living Resources" (being the Convention a copy of the English text of which is set out in the Schedule) was opened for signature at Canberra on 1 August 1980: 

  AND WHEREAS Australia is a signatory State to, and the Depositary under, the Convention: 

  AND WHEREAS it is desirable to make provision for giving effect to the Convention and to conservation measures by which Australia accepts in accordance with Article IX of the Convention to be bound, and to make other provision relating to the conservation of marine living resources of the Antarctic and the seas surrounding the Antarctic: 

  BE IT THEREFORE ENACTED by the Queen, and the Senate and the House of Representatives of the Commonwealth of Australia, as follows: 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>ANTARCTIC MARINE LIVING RESOURCES CONSERVATION ACT 1981 - SECT 1 Short title </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   1\. This Act may be cited as the Antarctic Marine Living Resources Conservation Act 1981.*1* SEE NOTES TO FIRST ARTICLE OF THIS CHAPTER . </sect>
**<b>

### <name>ANTARCTIC MARINE LIVING RESOURCES CONSERVATION ACT 1981 - SECT 2 Commencement </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   2.*1* (1) Sections 1, 2 and 3 shall come into operation on the day on which this Act receives the Royal Assent. 

  (2) The remaining provisions of this Act shall come into operation on a date to be fixed by Proclamation. 

  (3) The date fixed under subsection (2) shall not be a date earlier than the date on which the Convention enters into force for Australia. SEE NOTES TO FIRST ARTICLE OF THIS CHAPTER . 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>ANTARCTIC MARINE LIVING RESOURCES CONSERVATION ACT 1981 - SECT 3 Interpretation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   3\. (1) In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:<lf>   "another Contracting Party" means a Contracting Party to the Convention other than Australia;<lf>   "Australia", when used in a geographical sense, includes the external Territories;<lf>   "Australian fishing zone" has the same meaning as in the Fisheries Management Act 1991;<lf>   "Australian national" means:<lf>   (a) an Australian citizen; or<lf>   (b) a body corporate established by or under a law of the Commonwealth, of a State or of a Territory;<lf>   "Australian vessel" means:<lf>   (a) a vessel that is registered in Australia;<lf>   (b) a vessel that is under the control or in the possession of any one or more of the following:<lf>     (i) the Commonwealth (including an arm of the Defence Force)<lf> or a State or Territory;<lf>     (ii) a corporation established for a public purpose by or<lf> under a law of the Commonwealth or of a State or Territory;<lf>     (iii) a company or other body corporate incorporated under a<lf> law in force in a State or Territory, being a company or other body corporate in which the Commonwealth has a controlling interest; or<lf>   (c) a vessel (other than a vessel referred to in paragraph (a) or (b)) that has Australian nationality;<lf>   "authority of another Contracting Party" means a permit, licence or other authority issued or given by another Contracting Party under a law of that other Contracting Party or (whether under a law or otherwise) for purposes of, and in accordance with, the Convention, or an arrangement made by another Contracting Party for purposes of, and in accordance with, the Convention;<lf>   "conservation measures" means measures for the conservation of Antarctic marine living resources that are adopted from time to time by the Commission for the Conservation of Antarctic Marine Living Resources under Article IX of the Convention and by which Australia accepts in accordance with that Article to be bound;<lf>   "contravention", in relation to this Act, a provision of this Act, or a condition of a permit, includes a failure to comply with this Act, that provision or that condition;<lf>   "Convention" means the Convention entitled "Convention on the Conservation of Antarctic Marine Living Resources"that was opened for signature at Canberra on 1 August 1980, a copy of the English text of which is set out in the Schedule;<lf>   "Convention area" means the area south of the Antarctic Convergence;<lf>   "foreign person" means a person other than an Australian national;<lf>   "foreign vessel" means a vessel other than an Australian vessel;<lf>   "harvest", in relation to marine organisms, includes:<lf>   (a) take, collect, catch, capture, kill, injure, interfere with, cut up, dismember, clean, sort, coat, pack, process, preserve, store, carry or transport, for any purpose; or<lf>   (b) search for with the object of finding and taking for any purpose;<lf>   "inspector" means:<lf>   (a) a person appointed as an inspector under paragraph 13 (1) (a); or<lf>   (b) a person referred to in subsection 13 (2);<lf>   "interfere with", in relation to marine organisms, includes harass, chase, herd, tag, mark or brand;<lf>   "law of the Territory" means a law in force in the Territory other than an Act or instrument (not being an Ordinance or an instrument made under an Ordinance) made under an Act;<lf>   "marine organism" means a marine organism in the Convention area that belongs to any species of living organisms that is found in the Convention area;<lf>   "master", in relation to a vessel, means the person having command or charge of the vessel;<lf>   "permit" means a permit in force under this Act;<lf>   "special inspector" means a person appointed as a special inspector under paragraph 13 (1) (b);<lf>   "the Territory" means the Australian Antarctic Territory;<lf>   "this Act" includes the regulations;<lf>   "vessel" includes an air-cushion vehicle or other similar craft. 

  (2) Where a provision of this Act requires a notice to be served on a person by the Minister, the notice may be served on that person personally, by post or in any other prescribed manner. 

  (3) A reference in this Act to an offence against this or any other Act, to an offence against regulations made under any Act or to an offence against a provision of this Act shall be read as including a reference to an offence created by section 6, 7 or 7A of the Crimes Act 1914 in relation to this Act or that other Act or those regulations, as the case may be. 

  (4) A reference to an inspector in a provision of this Act that confers a function or power or imposes a duty upon inspectors shall be read as including a reference to any special inspector in respect of whom that function, power or duty is specified in pursuance of subsection 13 (4). 

  (5) A reference in this Act to a member of the Australian Federal Police or to a member of a police force shall be read as including a reference to a special member of the Australian Federal Police. 

  (6) Except in so far as the contrary intention appears, an expression that is used in this Act and in the Convention (whether or not a particular meaning is assigned to it by the Convention) has, in this Act, the same meaning as in the Convention.

### <name>ANTARCTIC MARINE LIVING RESOURCES CONSERVATION ACT 1981 - SECT 4 Act to bind Crown </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   4\. (1) This Act binds the Crown in right of the Commonwealth, of each of the States, of the Northern Territory and of Norfolk Island. 

  (2) Nothing in this Act renders the Commonwealth or a State or Territory liable to be prosecuted for an offence. 

  (3) Subsection (2) does not affect any liability of the master of a vessel of which the Commonwealth or a State or Territory is the owner to be prosecuted for an offence. 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>ANTARCTIC MARINE LIVING RESOURCES CONSERVATION ACT 1981 - SECT 5 Application of Act </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   5\. (1) Subject to this section, this Act applies both within and outside Australia and extends to every external Territory. 

  (2) Subject to subsection 4 (1) of the Antarctic Treaty Act 1960 and to subsection (3) of this section:<lf>   (a) to the extent that a provision of this Act has effect in and in relation to any waters or place beyond the outer limits of the Australian fishing zone, that provision applies only in relation to Australian nationals and Australian vessels and the members of the crew (including masters) of Australian vessels; and<lf>   (b) to the extent that a provision of this Act has effect in and in relation to Australia or the Australian fishing zone, that provision applies in relation to all persons, including foreign persons, and to all vessels, including foreign vessels. <p>  (3) This Act has effect subject to:<lf>   (a) the obligations of Australia under international law, including obligations under any international agreement binding on Australia; and<lf>   (b) any law of the Commonwealth giving effect to such an agreement. </lf></lf></p></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>ANTARCTIC MARINE LIVING RESOURCES CONSERVATION ACT 1981 - SECT 6 Application of laws of the Territory </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   6\. A law of the Territory does not have effect to the extent (if any) to which it is inconsistent with a provision of the regulations, but such a law shall not be taken for the purposes of this subsection to be inconsistent with such a provision to the extent that it is capable of operating concurrently with that provision. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>ANTARCTIC MARINE LIVING RESOURCES CONSERVATION ACT 1981 - SECT 7 Certain persons not guilty of offences against this Act </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   7\. A person is not guilty of an offence against this Act by reason of any act or thing done by him or her to the extent that the doing of that act or thing is authorized by:<lf>   (a) a permit, licence or other authority in force, at the time when that act or thing is done, under a law of the Commonwealth other than this Act; or<lf>   (b) an authority of another Contracting Party in force at the time when that act or thing is done. </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>ANTARCTIC MARINE LIVING RESOURCES CONSERVATION ACT 1981 - SECT 8 Harvesting, and research with respect to, marine organisms prohibited </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   8\. (1) Subject to section 7 and subsection (2) of this section, where, otherwise than in accordance with a permit, a person in the Convention area harvests, or carries out research with respect to, any marine organisms, the person is guilty of an offence against this subsection punishable, upon conviction:<lf>   (a) if the offender is a natural person - by a fine not exceeding $50,000; or<lf>   (b) if the offender is a body corporate - by a fine not exceeding $100,000\. 

  (2) It is a defence to a prosecution for an offence against subsection (1) if it is proved that:<lf>   (a) the act in question was reasonably necessary to avoid injury to any person or damage to any vessel; or<lf>   (b) where paragraph (a) does not apply and the act in question was done by the holder of a permit - that act was unavoidable in the course of taking action in accordance with the permit or was reasonably necessary to avoid damage to equipment used in taking action in accordance with the permit. </lf></lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>ANTARCTIC MARINE LIVING RESOURCES CONSERVATION ACT 1981 - SECT 9 Permits </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   9\. (1) Subject to this section, upon application made to the Minister in accordance with the prescribed form or, if no form is prescribed, a form approved by him or her, the Minister may, in his or her discretion, grant to a person a permit in writing authorizing the person, in, or in a specified part of, the Convention area, to do either or both of the following:<lf>   (a) harvest marine organisms of a specified kind or kinds;<lf>   (b) carry out research with respect to marine organisms of a specified kind or kinds. 

  (2) A permit shall not be granted under subsection (1) authorizing a person to do an act, or an act included in a class of acts, that is prescribed for the purposes of this subsection. 

  (3) The Minister shall, in and in relation to the exercise of his or her powers under this section, have due regard to the objective and principles of the Convention. 

  (4) A permit granted under this section:<lf>   (a) is subject to such conditions (if any) as are specified in the permit or as are imposed in pursuance of subsection (5) in respect of the permit;<lf>   (b) comes into force on a date specified in the permit or, if no date is so specified, on the date on which it is granted; and<lf>   (c) remains in force for such period as is specified in the permit. <p>  (5) At any time while a permit is in force the Minister may, upon his or her own motion or upon application made in accordance with subsection (6), by notice in writing served on the holder of the permit, vary or revoke a condition to which the permit is subject or impose a further condition in respect of the permit. </p><p>  (6) The holder of a permit may make application in writing to the Minister for the variation or revocation of a condition to which the permit is subject or for the imposition of a further condition in respect of the permit. </p><p>  (7) The Minister shall cause to be kept, in such manner and at such place or places as he or she directs, a register of permits in force from time to time, showing the purpose for which the permit was granted and the conditions (if any) to which each permit is from time to time subject and such other matters relating to each permit as the Minister thinks fit. </p><p>  (8) Such fees as are prescribed are payable in respect of grants of permits. </p><p>  (9) In this section, "grant" includes grant by way of renewal. </p></lf></lf></lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>ANTARCTIC MARINE LIVING RESOURCES CONSERVATION ACT 1981 - SECT 10 Contravening conditions of permits </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   10\. Where a person contravenes a condition of a permit that is applicable to him or her, the person is guilty of an offence punishable, upon conviction:<lf>   (a) if the offender is a natural person - by a fine not exceeding $5,000; or<lf>   (b) if the offender is a body corporate - by a fine not exceeding $10,000\. </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>ANTARCTIC MARINE LIVING RESOURCES CONSERVATION ACT 1981 - SECT 11 Suspension of permits </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   11\. (1) The Minister may, in his or her discretion, by notice given by any means the Minister thinks appropriate to the holder of a permit:<lf>   (a) subject to subsection (2), suspend the permit for a period not exceeding 90 days if he or she suspects on reasonable grounds that circumstances exist by reason of which he or she would be empowered to cancel the permit; or<lf>   (b) revoke the suspension of the permit at any time. 

  (2) Where proceedings for an offence in relation to a permit are commenced during the period of suspension of the permit, the suspension may be continued until the proceedings (including any appeal) are completed. 

  (3) During the period of suspension of a permit, the permit has no force or effect, but the period of currency of the permit continues to run. 
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>ANTARCTIC MARINE LIVING RESOURCES CONSERVATION ACT 1981 - SECT 12 Cancellation of permits </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   12\. The Minister may, in his or her discretion, by notice in writing served on the holder of a permit, cancel the permit if:<lf>   (a) he or she is satisfied that there has been a contravention of a condition to which the permit is subject;<lf>   (b) he or she is satisfied that the holder of the permit has contravened a provision of this Act;<lf>   (c) he or she is satisfied that the act or acts authorized by the permit may adversely affect a population of any species of marine organisms; or<lf>   (d) the holder of the permit has been convicted of an offence against the Fisheries Act 1952, the Continental Shelf (Living Natural Resources) Act 1968, the Antarctic Treaty (Environment Protection) Act 1980, the Whale Protection Act 1980, the Fisheries Management Act 1991 or regulations made under any of those Acts. </lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>ANTARCTIC MARINE LIVING RESOURCES CONSERVATION ACT 1981 - SECT 13 Inspectors and special inspectors </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   13\. (1) The Minister may, by instrument signed by him or her:<lf>   (a) appoint a person as an inspector; or<lf>   (b) appoint a person, upon the nomination of the Commission for the Conservation of Antarctic Marine Living Resources, as a special inspector;<lf> for the purposes of this Act. 

  (2) By force of this subsection, every member of the Australian Federal Police or of the police force of a Territory is an inspector. 

  (3) The Minister may, in his or her discretion:<lf>   (a) determine terms and conditions of appointment, including remuneration and allowances, of a person appointed under subsection (1); and<lf>   (b) at any time terminate such an appointment. <p>  (4) A person appointed as a special inspector under paragraph (1) (b) has, during the continuance of his or her appointment, such of the functions, powers and duties of inspectors as are specified in the instrument of his or her appointment. </p></lf></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>ANTARCTIC MARINE LIVING RESOURCES CONSERVATION ACT 1981 - SECT 14 Identity cards </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   14\. (1) The Minister shall cause to be issued to each inspector, other than a member of a police force, and to each special inspector, an identity card in a form approved by the Minister containing a photograph of the holder. 

  (2) Where a person to whom an identity card has been issued under subsection (1) ceases to be an inspector or special inspector, as the case may be, he or she shall forthwith return the identity card to the Minister. 

  (3) A person who contravenes subsection (2) is guilty of an offence punishable, upon conviction, by a fine not exceeding $100\. 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>ANTARCTIC MARINE LIVING RESOURCES CONSERVATION ACT 1981 - SECT 15 Arrest without warrant </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   15\. (1) An inspector may, without warrant, arrest any person if he or she believes on reasonable grounds:<lf>   (a) that the person has committed or is committing an offence against this Act; and<lf>   (b) that proceedings against the person by summons would not be effective. 

  (2) Where an inspector (other than a member of a police force who is in uniform) arrests a person under subsection (1), he or she shall:<lf>   (a) if the inspector is a member of a police force - produce, for inspection by that person, written evidence of the fact that he or she is a member of a police force; or<lf>   (b) in any other case - produce his or her identity card for inspection by that person. <p>  (3) Where a person is arrested under subsection (1), an inspector shall forthwith bring the person, or cause him or her to be brought, before a Justice of the Peace or other proper authority to be dealt with in accordance with law. </p><p>  (4) Nothing in this section prevents the arrest of a person under any other law. </p></lf></lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>ANTARCTIC MARINE LIVING RESOURCES CONSERVATION ACT 1981 - SECT 16 General powers of inspectors </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   16\. (1) Subject to this section, an inspector may search a vessel if he or she believes on reasonable grounds that there is in, on or attached to that vessel:<lf>   (a) a marine organism in respect of which an offence against this Act has been committed; or<lf>   (b) anything that may afford evidence as to the commission of an offence against this Act;<lf> and for that purpose stop or detain that vessel. 

  (2) An inspector shall not search:<lf>   (a) a vessel belonging to an arm of the Defence Force; or<lf>   (b) a foreign vessel of war;<lf> unless permission to do so is given by the person for the time being having command of the vessel and, if that person so requires, personnel of an arm of the Defence Force or of the foreign vessel, as the case may be, are present to supervise the search. <p>  (3) An inspector may:<lf>   (a) require any person whom he or she finds committing or whom he or she suspects on reasonable grounds of having committed an offence against this Act to state his or her full name and usual place of residence; and<lf>   (b) require any person whom he or she finds doing or whom he suspects on reasonable grounds of having done an act in respect of which the person is required to hold a permit to produce such a permit or evidence of the existence and contents of such a permit. <p>  (4) Where an inspector (other than a member of a police force who is in uniform) stops, or proposes to search or detain, a vessel, he or she shall:<lf>   (a) if he or she is a member of a police force - produce, for inspection by the master of the vessel, written evidence of the fact that he or she is a member of a police force; or<lf>   (b) in any other case - produce his or her identity card for inspection by that person;<lf> and, if he or she fails to do so, he or she is not authorized to search or detain that vessel. <p>  (5) Where an inspector (other than a member of a police force who is in uniform) makes a requirement of a person in pursuance of subsection (3), he or she shall:<lf>   (a) if he or she is a member of a police force - produce, for inspection by that person, written evidence of the fact that he or she is a member of a police force; or<lf>   (b) in any other case - produce his or her identity card for inspection by that person;<lf> and, if he or she fails to do so, that person is not obliged to comply with the requirement. <p>  (6) A person who, without reasonable excuse, fails to comply with a requirement made of him or her by an inspector under this section is guilty of an offence punishable, upon conviction, by a fine not exceeding $1,000\. </p><p>  (7) For the purposes of paragraph (3) (b), "permit" includes an authority of another Contracting Party that is in force. </p></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf></p></lf></lf></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>ANTARCTIC MARINE LIVING RESOURCES CONSERVATION ACT 1981 - SECT 17 Seizure and forfeiture </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   17\. (1) Where a court convicts a person of an offence against this Act, the court may order the forfeiture to the Commonwealth of any vessel or article used or otherwise involved in the commission of the offence. 

  (2) An inspector may seize any vessel or article that he or she believes on reasonable grounds has been used or otherwise involved in the commission of an offence against this Act and may retain it until the expiration of a period of 60 days after the seizure, or, if proceedings for an offence against this Act in the commission of which it may have been used or otherwise involved are instituted within that period, until the proceedings are terminated. 

  (3) The Minister may authorize a vessel or article seized under subsection (2) or anything in, on or attached to such a vessel to be released to its owner, or to the person from whose possession it was seized, either unconditionally or on such conditions as he or she thinks fit, including conditions as to the giving of security for payment of its value if it is forfeited. 

  (4) A vessel or article forfeited under this section may be sold or otherwise disposed of as the Minister thinks fit. 

  (5) An inspector may seize any marine organisms in respect of which he or she believes on reasonable grounds that an offence against this Act has been committed. 

  (6) The Minister may cause any marine organisms seized under subsection (5) to be retained or disposed of and, if an offence against this Act was not committed in respect of them, any person who has suffered loss or damage by reason of the seizure is entitled to reasonable compensation. 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>ANTARCTIC MARINE LIVING RESOURCES CONSERVATION ACT 1981 - SECT 18 Prosecution of offences against subsection 8 (1) </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   18\. (1) Subject ot subsection (2), an offence against subsection 8 (1) is an indictable offence. 

  (2) Notwithstanding that an offence against subsection 8 (1) is an indictable offence, a court of summary jurisdiction may hear and determine proceedings in respect of such an offence if the court is satisfied that it is proper to do so and the defendant and the prosecutor consent. 

  (3) Where, in accordance with subsection (2), a court of summary jurisdiction convicts a person of an offence against subsection 8 (1), the penalty that the court may impose is:<lf>   (a) if the offender is a natural person - a fine not exceeding $5,000; or<lf>   (b) if the offender is a body corporate - a fine not exceeding $10,000\. </lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>ANTARCTIC MARINE LIVING RESOURCES CONSERVATION ACT 1981 - SECT 19 Evidentiary certificates </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   19\. (1) The Minister or a person authorized in writing by him or her to give certificates under this section may give a certificate:<lf>   (a) that, at a time specified in the certificate, a vessel specified in the certificate was, or was not, an Australian vessel;<lf>   (b) that an area specified in the certificate is in the Convention area, or is in a part of the Convention area specified in the certificate;<lf>   (c) that, at a time specified in the certificate, an area specified in the certificate was part of the Australian fishing zone;<lf>   (d) that, at a time specified in the certificate, a person specified in the certificate was, or was not, the holder of a permit authorizing the person, in the Convention area, or in a part of the Convention area specified in the certificate, to harvest marine organisms of a kind or kinds specified in the certificate; or<lf>   (e) that, at a time specified in the certificate, a person specified in the certificate was, or was not, the holder of a permit authorizing the person, in the Convention area, or in a part of the Convention area specified in the certificate, to carry out research with respect to marine organisms of a kind or kinds specified in the certificate. 

  (2) A person giving a certificate under paragraph (1) (d) or (e) that a person was, at a time specified in the certificate, the holder of a permit may, in the certificate, certify that conditions specified in the certificate were the conditions to which the permit was subject at the time so specified.

of Antarctic<lf> Seals;<lf> BEARING in mind the concern regarding the conservation of<lf> Antarctic marine living resources expressed by the Consultative<lf> Parties at the Ninth Consultative Meeting This Convention applies to the Antarctic marine living<lf> resources of the area south of 60 degrees South latitude and to<lf> the Antarctic marine living resources of the area between that<lf> latitude and the Antarctic Convergence which form part of the<lf> Antarctic marine ecosystem.<lf>   2\. Antarctic marine living resources means the populations of<lf> fin fish, molluscs, crustaceans and all other species of living<lf> organisms, including birds, found south of the Antarctic<lf> Convergence.<lf>   3\. The Antarctic marine ecosystem means the complex of<lf> relationships of Antarctic marine living resources with each<lf> other and with their physical environment.<lf>   4\.
