## SECT
<sect>           An Act to establish a Film, Television and Radio School<lf> </lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - PART I  PART I - PRELIMINARY </name>
</b>** 
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 1 Short title </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   1\. This Act may be cited as the Australian Film, Television and Radio School Act 1973.*1* SEE NOTES TO FIRST ARTICLE OF THIS CHAPTER . </sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 2 Commencement </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   2\. This Act shall come into operation on the day on which it receives the Royal Assent.*1* SEE NOTES TO FIRST ARTICLE OF THIS CHAPTER . </sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 3 Interpretation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   3\. In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:<lf>   "Acting Director" means a person appointed under section 31 to act as the Director;<lf>   "appointed" includes re-appointed, and "appointment" has a corresponding meaning;<lf>   "broadcast" means to transmit by way of television or radio, and "broadcasting" has a corresponding meaning;<lf>   "Council" means the Council of the Australian Film, Television and Radio School established by section 7;<lf>   "Director" means the Director of the School appointed under section 24;<lf>   "ex-officio member" means a member, being the Director or an Acting Director;<lf>   "member" means a member of the Council;<lf>   "member of the staff of the School" means, except in section 21, a person who is employed on a full-time basis under section 34, being a person who has been so employed, or whose term of employment is, for a period of 12 months or more;<lf>   "program" means:<lf>   (a) an aggregate of images or sounds, or of images and sounds, embodied in any material; or<lf>   (b) an aggregate of images or sounds, or of images and sounds, that is, or is intended to be, broadcast without first having been embodied in any material;<lf>   "School" means the Australian Film, Television and Radio School established by section 4\. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - PART II  PART II - THE AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL </name>
</b>** 
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 4 Establishment of School </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   4.(1) There is hereby established a school to be known as the Australian Film, Television and Radio School. 

<lf>   (2) The School:<lf>   (a) is a body corporate;<lf>   (b) shall have a seal; and<lf>   (c) may sue and be sued. <p><lf>   (3) The design of the seal of the School shall be as determined by the Council. <p><lf>   (4) The seal of the School shall be kept in such custody as the Council directs and shall not be used except as authorized by the Council. <p><lf>   (5) All courts, judges and persons acting judicially shall take judicial notice of the imprint of the seal of the School appearing on a document and shall presume that the document was duly sealed. </lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 5 Functions of School </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   5.(1) The functions of the School are:<lf>   (a) to provide advanced education and training by way of the development of the knowledge and skills required in connexion with the production of programs;<lf>   (b) to conduct and encourage research in connexion with the production of programs;<lf>   (c) to conduct such seminars and courses of education or training for persons engaged, or to be engaged, directly or indirectly, in connexion with the production of programs as are approved by the Council;<lf>   (d) to co-operate and make arrangements with other institutions and persons for purposes in connexion with the production of programs or the provision of education or training of the kind referred to in paragraph (a);<lf>   (e) for purposes in connexion with the production of programs or the provision of education or training of the kind referred to in paragraph (a), to provide facilities for, and to offer the services of the staff of the School to, such other institutions or persons as are approved by the Council;<lf>   (f) to make grants of financial assistance to persons to enable or assist those persons to receive education or undergo training of the kind referred to in paragraph (a);<lf>   (g) to award such degrees, diplomas and certificates in relation to the passing of examinations or otherwise in relation to the education and training provided by the School as are provided for by the regulations; and<lf>   (h) to do anything incidental or conducive to the performance of the foregoing functions. 

<lf>   (2) The School shall exercise its functions with a view to enabling and encouraging the production of programs of a high degree of creativeness and of high technical and artistic standards. </lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 6 Powers of School </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   6\. Subject to this Act, the School has power to do all things that are necessary or convenient to be done for or in connexion with the performance of its functions and, in particular, has power:<lf>   (a) to enter into contracts;<lf>   (b) to erect buildings;<lf>   (c) to occupy, use and control any land or building owned or held under lease by the Commonwealth and made available for the purposes of the School;<lf>   (d) to acquire, hold and dispose of real or personal property; and<lf>   (e) to accept gifts, devises and bequests made to the School, whether on trust or otherwise, and to act as trustee of moneys or other property vested in the School upon trust. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - PART III  PART III - THE COUNCIL OF THE SCHOOL </name>
</b>** 
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 7 The Council </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   7.(1) The School shall be governed by a council to be known as the Council of the Australian Film, Television and Radio School. 

<lf>   (2) All acts and things done in the name of, or on behalf of, the School by the Council or with the authority of the Council shall be deemed to have been done by the School. </lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 8 Constitution of Council </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   8.(1) The Council shall consist of:<lf>   (a) the Director;<lf>   (b) a staff member elected by the staff members in the manner prescribed;<lf>   (c) a student of the School elected by the students of the School in the manner prescribed;<lf>   (d) 3 members appointed by the Governor-General; and<lf>   (e) 3 members of Convocation appointed by the Council. 

<lf>   (4) If an appointment of an Acting Director is in force under section 31, the Acting Director may attend meetings of the Council and, for the purposes of those meetings, shall be deemed to be a member of the Council. <p><lf>   (5) An act or decision of the Council is not invalid by reason only of a vacancy or vacancies in the membership of the Council. <p><lf>   (6) The appointment or election of a member is not invalid by reason only of a defect or irregularity in connexion with the appointment or election. <p><lf>   (7) The term of office of a member shall be as provided by this Act, but, subject to section 11, a member is eligible for re-appointment or re-election. <p><lf>   (8) Subject to this Act:<lf>   (a) a member appointed under paragraph (1) (d) holds office for such period, not exceeding 3 years, as is specified in the instrument of appointment; and<lf>   (b) a member appointed under paragraph (1) (e) holds office for a period of 3 years. <p><lf>   (9) In this section:<lf>   "staff member" means a person who:<lf>   (a) is employed on a full-time or part-time basis under section 34; and<lf>   (b) has been so employed, or whose term of employment is, for at least 12 months. </lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 9 Members elected by members of staff of School </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   9.(1) Except as otherwise provided by this section, the member elected under paragraph 8 (1) (b) holds office, subject to this Act, for a period of one year and the term of office of the member shall not commence before the expiration of the term of office of the member whose place the first-mentioned member fills. 

<lf>   (2) If the member has ceased to hold office before the expiration of his or her term of office, a member shall, if the Minister so directs, be elected under paragraph 8 (1) (b) to hold office from the date of his or her election until the expiration of that term. <p><lf>   (3) A member elected under paragraph 8 (1) (b) ceases to hold office if he or she ceases to be a member of the staff of the School. </lf></p></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 10 Members elected by students </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   10.(1) In this section, "student member" means the member referred to in paragraph 8 (1) (c). 

<lf>   (2) Except as otherwise provided by this section, the student member holds office, subject to this Act, for the period of one year and the term of office of a student member shall not commence before the expiration of the term of office of the member whose place he or she fills. <p><lf>   (3) If the student member has ceased to hold office before the expiration of his or her term of office, a student member shall, if the Minister so directs, be elected under paragraph 8 (1) (c) to hold office from the date of his or her election until the expiration of that term. <p><lf>   (4) A student member ceases to be a member if the Council determines that he or she has ceased to be a student of the School. </lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 11 Eligibility for appointment as member </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   11.(1) Subject to this section, where a person has been elected or appointed as a member on two successive occasions, the person is not eligible to be again elected or appointed as a member. 

<lf>   (2) Where a person has been elected or appointed as a member on two successive occasions and a period of not less than one year has elapsed since the expiration of the second term of office of the member, the member is eligible to be again elected or appointed as a member. </lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 12 Delegation by Council </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   12.(1) Subject to any direction of the Minister, the Council may, by resolution, either generally or as otherwise provided by the resolution, delegate to a member of the Council, a committee of the Council or a member of the staff of the School any of its powers under this Act, other than this power of delegation. 

<lf>   (2) A power so delegated, when exercised by the delegate, shall, for the purposes of this Act, be deemed to have been exercised by the Council. <p><lf>   (3) A delegation of a power under this section:<lf>   (a) may be revoked by resolution of the Council (whether or not constituted by the persons constituting the Council at the time the power was delegated);<lf>   (b) does not prevent the exercise of the power by the Council; and<lf>   (c) continues in force notwithstanding a change in the membership of the Council. <p><lf>   (4) Section 34A of the Acts Interpretation Act 1901 applies in relation to a delegation under this section as if the Council were a person. <p><lf>   (5) A certificate signed by the Chairman of the Council stating any matter with respect to a delegation of a power under this section is prima facie evidence of that matter. <p><lf>   (6) A document purporting to be a certificate mentioned in subsection (5) shall, unless the contrary is established, be deemed to be such a certificate and to have been duly given. </lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 13 Chairman and Deputy Chairman of Council </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   13.(1) Subject to subsection (3), the Governor-General shall appoint a member of the Council to be the Chairman of the Council. 

<lf>   (2) Subject to subsection (3), the Council shall appoint a member of the Council to be the Deputy Chairman of the Council. <p><lf>   (3) The Governor-General shall not appoint as Chairman, and the Council shall not appoint as Deputy Chairman, an ex-officio member or a member referred to in paragraph 8 (1) (b) or (c). <p><lf>   (4) The member appointed as Chairman or Deputy Chairman holds office as Chairman or Deputy Chairman until the expiration of the member's term of office as a member that is current at the time of the member's appointment, but ceases to be the Chairman or Deputy Chairman if he or she ceases to be a member, and may  resign from office as Chairman or Deputy Chairman by writing signed by the member and delivered:<lf>   (a) in the case of the Chairman - to the Governor-General; and<lf>   (b) in the case of the Deputy Chairman - to the Chairman. <p><lf>   (5) A person is eligible to be re-appointed as the Chairman or Deputy Chairman. </lf></p></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 14 Resignation of members </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   14\. A member, other than an ex-officio member, may resign from office by writing signed by the member and delivered to the Governor-General. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 15 Dismissal of members </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   15\. The Governor-General may remove a member, other than an ex-officio member, from office on the ground of misbehaviour or physical or mental incapacity. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 16 Fees and allowances </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   16.(1) A member other than the Director shall be paid such fees as are determined by the Remuneration Tribunal. 

<lf>   (2) A member other than the Director shall be paid such allowances as are prescribed. <p><lf>   (3) This section has effect subject to the Remuneration Tribunal Act 1973\. </lf></p></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 17 Leave of absence </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   17.(1) Subject to section 87E of the Public Service Act 1922, a full-time member has such recreation leave entitlements as are determined by the Remuneration Tribunal. 

<lf>   (2) The Council may grant a full-time member leave of absence, other than recreation leave, on such terms and conditions as to remuneration or otherwise as the Council determines. <p><lf>   (3) The Council may grant a part-time member leave to be absent from a meeting or meetings of the Council. </lf></p></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 18 Vacation of office </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   18\. If a member, other than an ex-officio member:<lf>   (a) becomes bankrupt, applies to take the benefit of any law for the relief of bankrupt or insolvent debtors, compounds with creditors or makes an assignment of remuneration for their benefit;<lf>   (b) is absent without leave of the Council from 3 consecutive meetings of the Council; or<lf>   (c) without reasonable excuse, fails to comply with the member's obligations under section 19;<lf>  the Governor-General shall remove the member from office. </lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 19 Disclosure of pecuniary interests </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   19.(1) A member who has a direct or indirect pecuniary interest in a matter being considered or about to be considered by the Council shall, as soon as possible after the relevant facts have come to the member's knowledge, disclose the nature of the interest at a meeting of the Council. 

<lf>   (2) A disclosure under subsection (1) shall be recorded in the minutes of the meeting of the Council and the member shall not:<lf>   (a) be present during any deliberation of the Council with respect to that matter; or<lf>   (b) take part in any decision of the Council with respect to that matter. </lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 20 Meetings of Council </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   20.(1) The Council shall hold such meetings as are necessary for the performance of its functions. 

<lf>   (2) The Chairman, or if for any reason the Chairman is not available, the Deputy Chairman:<lf>   (a) may convene meetings of the Council, and shall comply with any resolution of the Council with respect to the convening of meetings; and<lf>   (b) shall, on receipt of a written request signed by not less than 2 members, convene a meeting of the Council. <p><lf>   (3) If neither the Chairman nor the Deputy Chairman is available, the Director may convene a meeting of the Council. <p><lf>   (4) The Chairman shall preside at all meetings of the Council at which he or she is present. <p><lf>   (5) At a meeting of the Council at which the Chairman is not present the Deputy Chairman shall preside. <p><lf>   (6) At a meeting of the Council at which neither the Chairman nor the Deputy Chairman is present, the members present shall appoint one of their number to preside. <p><lf>   (7) The quorum at a meeting of the Council is:<lf>   (a) before the first members have been elected under paragraphs 8 (1) (b) and (c) - 5 members; and<lf>   (b) after the first members have been so elected - 5 members. <p><lf>   (8) Questions arising at a meeting of the Council shall be determined by a majority of the votes of the members present. <p><lf>   (9) The member presiding at a meeting of the Council has a deliberative vote, and, in the event of an equality of votes, also has a casting vote. </lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - PART IV  PART IV - CONVOCATION </name>
</b>** 
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 21 Constitution of Convocation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   21.(1) Convocation shall consist of:<lf>   (a) all members and past members of the Council;<lf>   (b) all persons who were, at any time, members of the Interim Council;<lf>   (c) all members and past members of the staff of the School;<lf>   (d) all persons who have been awarded degrees or diplomas by the School; and<lf>   (e) such persons as the Council admits as members of Convocation. 

<lf>   (1A)  A reference in subsection (1) to a member of the staff of the school is a reference to a member of the academic or technical staff of the School engaged in, or directly or indirectly associated with, teaching at the School. <p><lf>   (2) The Council shall not admit a person as a member of Convocation under paragraph (1) (e) unless the Council considers the person to be suitably qualified to be so admitted by virtue of the person's knowledge or experience in connexion with films, television or radio. <p><lf>   (3) In paragraph (1) (b), "Interim Council" means the Council established on 14th November, 1969, to investigate and report on the form and location of a national film and television training school. </lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 22 Roll of members </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   22.(1) Until Convocation is constituted, the Council shall cause to be kept a roll of all persons who are entitled to become members of Convocation. 

<lf>   (2) When the number of persons so enrolled amounts to 100, the Council shall report the fact to the Governor-General. <p><lf>   (3) The report referred to in subsection (2) shall be published in the Gazette, and Convocation shall be deemed to be constituted on the day of that publication. <p><lf>   (4) After Convocation has been constituted, the Council shall cause to be kept a roll of all members of Convocation. </lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 23 Meetings of Convocation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   23\. Meetings of Convocation may be convened by the Chairman of the Council. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - PART V  PART V - THE DIRECTOR </name>
</b>** 
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 24 Director of School </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   24.(1) There shall be a Director of the School, who shall be appointed by the Governor-General on the recommendation of the Council. 

<lf>   (2) The Director holds office on such terms and conditions (if any) in respect of matters not provided for by this Act as are determined by the Governor-General. </lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 25 Tenure of office </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   25\. The Director shall be appointed for a period not exceeding 7 years, but is eligible for re-appointment. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 26 Functions of Director </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   26\. Subject to the general direction of the Council, the Director shall manage the affairs of the School. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 27 Remuneration and allowances of Director </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   27.(1) The Director shall be paid such remuneration as is determined by the Remuneration Tribunal. 

<lf>   (2) The Director shall be paid such allowances as are prescribed. <p><lf>   (3) This section has effect subject to the Remuneration Tribunal Act 1973\. </lf></p></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 28 Resignation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   28\. The Director may resign from office by writing signed by the Director and delivered to the Governor-General. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 29 Vacation of office of Director </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   29.(1) If the Director:<lf>   (a) becomes bankrupt, applies to take the benefit of any law for the relief of bankrupt or insolvent debtors, compounds with creditors or makes an assignment of remuneration for their benefit;<lf>   (b) is absent from duty, except on leave of absence, for 14 consecutive days or for 28 days in any 12 months; or<lf>   (c) without reasonable excuse, fails to comply with the Director's obligations under section 19;<lf> the Governor-General shall remove the Director from office. 

<lf>   (2) The Governor-General may remove the Director from office for misbehaviour or physical or mental incapacity. </lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 30 Director not to undertake any other work </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   30.(1) The Director shall not engage in paid employment outside the duties of the Director's office except with the approval of the Council. 

<lf>   (2) The Council shall not give an approval for the purposes of subsection (1) unless it is satisfied that the paid employment will not interfere with the performance of the duties of the Director under this Act. </lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 31 Acting Director </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   31.(1) The Governor-General may appoint a person to act as the Director:<lf>   (a) during a vacancy in the office of Director, whether or not an appointment has previously been made to the office; or<lf>   (b) during any period, or during all periods, when the Director is, or is about to be, absent from duty or from Australia or, for any reason, unable to perform the functions of the Director's office. 

<lf>   (2) The Governor-General may:<lf>   (a) subject to this Act, on the recommendation of the Council, determine the terms and conditions of appointment of an Acting Director; and<lf>   (b) at any time terminate such an appointment. <p><lf>   (3) Where a person is acting as the Director in accordance with paragraph (1) (b) and the office of Director becomes vacant while that person is so acting, that person may continue so to act until the Governor-General otherwise directs or the vacancy is filled. <p><lf>   (4) The appointment of an Acting Director ceases to have effect if the Acting Director resigns the appointment by writing signed by the Acting Director and delivered to the Governor-General. <p><lf>   (5) While the appointment of an Acting Director remains in force, the Acting Director has, and may exercise, all the powers and shall perform all the functions of the Director. <p><lf>   (7) Anything done by or in relation to a person purporting to act as the Director under subsection (1) is not invalid on the ground that:<lf>   (a) the occasion for the person's appointment had not arisen;<lf>   (b) there was a defect or irregularity in connection with the person's appointment;<lf>   (c) the person's appointment had ceased to have effect; or<lf>   (d) the occasion for the person to act had not arisen or had ceased. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 32 Eligibility of Director and Acting Director </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   32.(1) A person who has attained the age of 70 years shall not be appointed as the Director or as an Acting Director. 

<lf>   (2) A person shall not be appointed as the Director or as an Acting Director for a period that extends beyond the date on which the person will attain the age of 70 years. </lf>
</sect>

</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   36.(1) There are payable to the School such moneys as are appropriated by the Parliament for the purposes of the School. 

<lf>   (2) The Minister for Finance may give directions as to the amounts in which, and the times at which, moneys referred to in subsection (1) are to be paid to the School. </lf>

</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   37.(1) The Council shall prepare estimates, in such form as the Minister directs, of expenditure of the School, out of moneys to be paid to the School under section 36, for each financial year and, if the Minister so directs, for any other period specified by the Minister, and the Council shall submit estimates so prepared to the Minister not later than such date as the Minister directs. 

<lf>   (2) Moneys paid to the School under section 36 shall not be expended by the School otherwise than in accordance with estimates of expenditure approved by the Minister. </lf>

</b>**

**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 39 Application of moneys </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   39.(1) The moneys of the School shall be applied only:<lf>   (a) in payment or discharge of the costs and expenses of the School under this Act; and<lf>   (b) in payment of any remuneration (including fees) and allowances payable under this Act. 

<lf>   (2) Notwithstanding anything contained in this Act, where a gift or bequest made to the School is subject to conditions in relation to the purposes for which it is to be applied, the moneys paid to the School as a result of the gift or bequest, and any income derived from the investment of those moneys, may be applied for those purposes and subject to those conditions but not otherwise. </lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>AUSTRALIAN FILM, TELEVISION AND RADIO SCHOOL ACT 1973 - SECT 40 Power to purchase and dispose of assets </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   40.(1) The School shall not, without the approval of the Minister:<lf>   (a) acquire any property, right or privilege for a consideration exceeding in amount or value $250,000 or, if a higher amount is prescribed, that higher amount;<lf>   (b) dispose of any property, right or privilege where the amount or value of the consideration for the disposal, or the value of the property, right or privilege, exceeds $250,000 or, if a higher amount is prescribed, that higher amount;<lf>   (c) enter into a contract for the construction of a building for the School, being a contract under which the School is to pay an amount exceeding $50,000 or, if a higher amount is prescribed, that higher amount; or<lf>   (d) enter into a lease of land for a period exceeding 10 years. 

<lf>   (2) The taking on lease or the purchase of land or buildings required for the purposes of the School shall be effected under the Lands Acquisition Act 1955\. </lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>

</b>** 

## SECT
