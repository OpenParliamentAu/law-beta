###  AUSTRALIAN TRADE COMMISSION ACT 1985- SECT 1  Short title [_see_ Note 1] 
This Act may be cited as the _Australian Trade Commission Act 1985_.

###  AUSTRALIAN TRADE COMMISSION ACT 1985- SECT 7  Australian Trade Commission 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>There is established by this Act a Commission by the name of the

Australian Trade Commission.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Commission: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>is a body corporate;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>shall have a seal;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>may acquire, hold and dispose of real and personal property; and</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>may sue and be sued.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Note:	The _Commonwealth Authorities and Companies Act 1997_

applies to the Commission. That Act deals with matters relating to Commonwealth

authorities, including reporting and accountability, banking and investment,

and conduct of officers.

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>The seal of the Commission shall be kept in such custody as the Board

directs and shall not be used except as authorised by the Board.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>All courts, judges and persons acting judicially shall take judicial notice of the imprint of the seal of the Commission appearing on a document and shall presume that the document was duly sealed. </dd> </dl>

**

###  AUSTRALIAN TRADE COMMISSION ACT 1985- SECT 8  Functions of Commission 
**

 <dl compact="">

		The functions of the Commission are:

 </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>to facilitate and encourage trade between Australia and foreign countries

(in this section referred to as _Australian export trade_) by:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>representing the trading and commercial interests of Australia in foreign

countries;</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>assisting, directly or indirectly, Australian organisations in trade

negotiations;</dd>

<dt>(iii)</dt><dd>promoting, or participating in or co-ordinating projects to promote,

Australian export trade;</dd>

<dt>(iv)</dt><dd>obtaining, and making available to Australian organisations, information

relating to current or future opportunities for Australian export trade,

including opportunities for involvement in overseas development projects;</dd>

<dt>(v)</dt><dd>supporting and facilitating investment in foreign countries, and

facilitating investment in Australia, where that investment is likely to

enhance opportunities for Australian export trade;</dd>

<dt>(vi)</dt><dd>carrying out, or assisting other persons to carry out, or participating

with other persons in carrying out, in whole or in part, overseas development

projects, in circumstances where that course of action will benefit Australian

organisations;</dd>

<dt>(viii)</dt><dd>administering the _Export Market Development Grants Act 1997_;</dd>

<dt>(ix)</dt><dd>developing and administering schemes to provide assistance in the

development of markets in foreign countries; and</dd>

<dt>(x)</dt><dd>facilitating access by persons to Departments of State of the Commonwealth

or of a State and to instrumentalities established by or under a law of the

Commonwealth or of a State where that access is likely to enhance opportunities

for Australian export trade;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>to do any other act or thing required by this Act or by any other Act to

be done by the Commission; and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>to act, outside Australia, as agent for Departments of State of the

Commonwealth or for instrumentalities established by or under a law of the

Commonwealth.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

**

###  AUSTRALIAN TRADE COMMISSION ACT 1985- SECT 9  Duties 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Commission shall perform its functions in such a manner as will best

assist in the development of trade between Australia and foreign countries.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>In performing its functions, the Commission shall: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>comply with any directions given to it under section&#160;10; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>have regard to:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>the desirability of improving and extending the range and accessibility of

advice, assistance and financial support available (whether through the

Commission or otherwise) to persons involved, or likely to be involved, either

directly or indirectly, in trade between Australia and foreign countries;</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>the need to provide its services as efficiently and economically as

possible; and</dd>

<dt>(iii)</dt><dd>Australia's obligations under international agreements.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

**

###  AUSTRALIAN TRADE COMMISSION ACT 1985- SECT 10  Power of Minister to give directions 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Minister may give to the Commission, in writing, such directions with

respect to the performance of its functions, and the exercise of its powers,

under this Act, as appear to the Minister to be necessary.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Nothing in subsection&#160;(1) shall be construed: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>as requiring the approval of the Minister to the entry by the Commission

into a particular contract or other agreement or arrangement under this Act or

to the giving by the Commission of any particular guarantee under this Act;

or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>as empowering the Minister to determine that the Commission shall or shall

not enter into any particular contract or other agreement or arrangement or

shall or shall not give any particular guarantee;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

but the Commission shall not enter into a particular contract or other agreement or arrangement, or give a particular guarantee, contrary to a direction by the Minister under this section.

<dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>Nothing in subsection&#160;(1) shall be construed as empowering the

Minister to determine that the Commission should deal in a particular manner

with a particular person, or with a particular claim or application for a grant

or other benefit, under the _Export Market Development Grants Act 1997_ or

under a scheme approved by the Minister under section&#160;30 of this Act.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>A direction of the Minister under this section shall not operate so as to affect prejudicially an application under the _Export Market Development Grants Act 1997_ in relation to a grant year (within the meaning of that Act) that commenced before the day on which the direction was given.</dd> <dt>(5)</dt><dd>This section does not affect the operation of any other provision of this Act or of any other Act that confers a power upon the Minister to give directions to the Commission. </dd> </dl>

###  AUSTRALIAN TRADE COMMISSION ACT 1985- SECT 12  Membership of Board 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Board shall consist of not fewer than 10 nor more than 12 members,

namely:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a Chairperson;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>a Deputy Chairperson;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>the Managing Director;</dd>

<dt>(ca)</dt><dd>the Managing Director of EFIC;</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>2 government members; and</dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>such number of other members, being not fewer than 4 nor more than 6, as

the Minister determines, in writing, to be appropriate.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(1A)</dt><dd>Nothing in subsection&#160;(1) prevents the appointment of the Managing

Director as Chairperson.</dd> <dt>(1B)</dt><dd>The Managing Director, and the Deputy Managing Director, of EFIC may not be appointed as Chairperson or Deputy Chairperson.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>A member of the Board, other than the Managing Director and the Managing Director of EFIC, shall be appointed by the Minister with effect from such day as the Minister specifies in the instrument of appointment of that member.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>Appointed members shall hold office as part-time members.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>An appointed member, other than a government member, holds office, subject to this Act, for such period, not exceeding 5 years, as the Minister specifies in the instrument of appointment of the member, but is eligible for re-appointment.</dd> <dt>(5)</dt><dd>A government member holds office, subject to this Act, during the Minister's pleasure.</dd> <dt>(9)</dt><dd>Appointed members hold office on such terms and conditions (if any) in respect of matters not provided for by this Act as are determined, in writing, by the Minister.</dd> <dt>(10)</dt><dd>The exercise of a power or the performance of a function of the Board is not affected by reason of there being a vacancy or vacancies in the membership of the Board or by reason of the number of appointed members referred to in paragraph&#160;(1)(e) falling below 4 for not longer than 6 months. </dd> </dl>

###  AUSTRALIAN TRADE COMMISSION ACT 1985- SECT 13  Functions of Board in relation to Commission 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Subject to section&#160;10, the Board shall determine the policy to be

followed in the conduct of the affairs of the Commission under this Act.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Without derogating, by implication, from the responsibility of the Managing Director for the day-to-day conduct of the affairs of the Commission under this Act, those affairs shall be conducted by the Board.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>All acts and things done in the name of, or on behalf of, the Commission by, or with the authority of, the Board or the Managing Director, for the purposes of this Act, of the _Export Market Development Grants Act 1997_ or of any other Act that confers functions on the Commission, shall be deemed to have been done by the Commission.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>A decision made by the Board in the exercise of its powers under this Act is not invalidated, and shall not be called in question, on the ground that the Board has limited the exercise of its discretion in a particular case by reference to any general policy determined by it in the performance of its functions. </dd> </dl>

###  AUSTRALIAN TRADE COMMISSION ACT 1985- SECT 16  Acting Chairperson and acting Deputy Chairperson 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Minister may appoint a person (other than the Managing Director, or

the Deputy Managing Director, of EFIC) to act as Chairperson:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>during a vacancy in the office of Chairperson, whether or not an

appointment has previously been made to the office; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>during any period, or during all periods, when the Chairperson is absent

from duty or from Australia or is, for any other reason, unable to perform the

functions of his or her office;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

but a person appointed to act during a vacancy shall not continue so to act for more than 12 months.

<dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Minister may appoint a person (other than the Managing Director, or

the Deputy Managing Director, of EFIC) to act as Deputy Chairperson:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>during a vacancy in the office of Deputy Chairperson, whether or not an

appointment has previously been made to the office; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>during any period, or during all periods, when the Deputy Chairperson is

absent from duty or from Australia or is, for any other reason (including the

reason that the Deputy Chairperson is Acting as Chairperson) unable to perform

the functions of his or her office;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

but a person appointed to act during a vacancy shall not continue so to act for more than 12 months.

<dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>An appointment of a person under subsection&#160;(1) or (2) may be

expressed to have effect only in such circumstances as are specified in the

instrument of appointment.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>The Minister may: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>determine the terms and conditions of appointment of a person acting as

Chairperson or Deputy Chairperson, including, in the case of a person other

than the Managing Director, terms and conditions relating to remuneration and

allowances; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>at any time terminate such an appointment.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(5)</dt><dd>Where a person is acting as Chairperson in accordance with

paragraph&#160;(1)(b) or as Deputy Chairperson in accordance with

paragraph&#160;(2)(b) and the office in which the person is acting becomes

vacant while that person is so acting, that person may continue so to act until

the Minister otherwise directs, the vacancy is filled or a period of 12 months

from the date on which the vacancy occurred expires, whichever first happens.</dd> <dt>(6)</dt><dd>The appointment of a person to act as Chairperson or as Deputy Chairperson ceases to have effect if the person resigns the appointment by writing signed by the person and delivered to the Minister.</dd> <dt>(7)</dt><dd>While a person is acting as Chairperson, the person may exercise all the powers, and shall perform all the functions, of the Chairperson.</dd> <dt>(8)</dt><dd>While a person is acting as Deputy Chairperson, the person may exercise all the powers, and shall perform all the functions, of the Deputy Chairperson.</dd> <dt>(9)</dt><dd>The validity of anything done by, or in relation to, a person purporting to act under subsection&#160;(1) or (2) shall not be called in question on the ground that the occasion for the appointment of the person had not arisen, that there was a defect or irregularity in or in connection with the appointment, that the appointment had ceased to have effect or that the occasion for the person to act had not arisen or had ceased. </dd> </dl>
