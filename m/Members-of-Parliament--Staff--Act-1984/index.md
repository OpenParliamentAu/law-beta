### <name>MEMBERS OF PARLIAMENT (STAFF) ACT 1984 - LONG TITLE </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>       An Act to provide for the employment of consultants and<lf>       staff by Ministers, certain office-holders, Senators and<lf>           Members of the House of Representatives, and for<lf>                         related purposes<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>MEMBERS OF PARLIAMENT (STAFF) ACT 1984 - PART I  PART I - PRELIMINARY </name>
</b>** 
**<b>

### <name>MEMBERS OF PARLIAMENT (STAFF) ACT 1984 - SECT 1 Short title </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   1\. This Act may be cited as the Members of Parliament (Staff) Act 1984.*1* SEE NOTES TO FIRST ARTICLE OF THIS CHAPTER . </sect>
**<b>

### <name>MEMBERS OF PARLIAMENT (STAFF) ACT 1984 - SECT 2 Commencement </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   2.*1* (1) Part I shall come into operation on the day on which this Act receives the Royal Assent. 

<lf>   (2) The remaining provisions of this Act shall come into operation on such day as is, or such respective days as are, fixed by Proclamation. SEE NOTES TO FIRST ARTICLE OF THIS CHAPTER . </lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>MEMBERS OF PARLIAMENT (STAFF) ACT 1984 - SECT 3 Interpretation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   3\. In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:<lf>   "Agency" means the Merit Protection and Review Agency;<lf>   "Board" means the Public Service Board;<lf>   "office-holder" means:<lf>   (a) a person who holds a relevant office;<lf>   (aa) a person, not being a Senator or Member of the House of Representatives, who held the office of Prime Minister; or<lf>   (b) a person in respect of whom a determination by the Prime Minister under section 12 is in force;<lf>   "officer" has the same meaning as that expression has in the Public Service Act;<lf>   "pensioner" means:<lf>   (aa) a person to whom an invalidity pension, within the meaning of the Superannuation Act 1990 is payable; or<lf>   (a) a person to whom an invalidity pension within the meaning of the Superannuation Act 1976 is payable; or<lf>   (b) a person to whom a pension under the Superannuation Act 1922 is payable, being a person who was retired on the ground of invalidity or of physical or mental incapacity to perform the person's duties;<lf>   "Public Service Act" means the Public Service Act 1922;<lf>   "relevant office" means:<lf>   (a) an office of Minister;<lf>   (b) the office of Leader of the Opposition in the Senate;<lf>   (c) the office of Leader of the Opposition in the House of Representatives;<lf>   (d) the office of Deputy Leader of the Opposition in the Senate;<lf>   (e) the office of Deputy Leader of the Opposition in the House of Representatives; or<lf>   (f) an office of leader or deputy leader, in a House of the Parliament, of a recognized political party (other than an office that is held by a person who also holds an office referred to in any of the preceding paragraphs of this definition);<lf>   "relevant public servant" means a person who is an employee within the meaning of the Public Service Act;<lf>   "relevant Secretary", in relation to a consultant appointed, or proposed to be appointed, by a Minister, means the Secretary of the Department administered by the Minister or, if there is more than one Department administered by the Minister, whichever Secretary the Minister considers appropriate. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>MEMBERS OF PARLIAMENT (STAFF) ACT 1984 - PART II  PART II - MINISTERIAL CONSULTANTS </name>
</b>** 
**<b>

### <name>MEMBERS OF PARLIAMENT (STAFF) ACT 1984 - SECT 4 Ministers may engage consultants </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   4\. (1) A Minister may, with the approval of the Prime Minister, on behalf of the Commonwealth, engage, under an agreement in writing, a natural person, body corporate or partnership as a consultant. 

<lf>   (2) An agreement for the engagement by a Minister of a consultant under subsection (1) shall contain one or more of the following provisions:<lf>   (a) a provision to the effect that the consultant is engaged to perform, under the supervision of the Minister, such tasks as are specified in the agreement;<lf>   (b) a provision to the effect that the consultant is engaged to perform, under the supervision of the Minister, such tasks as are from time to time specified by the Minister;<lf>   (c) a provision to the effect that the consultant is engaged to perform, under the supervision of the relevant Secretary, such tasks as are specified in the agreement;<lf>   (d) a provision to the effect that the consultant is engaged to perform, under the supervision of the relevant Secretary, such tasks as are from time to time specified by the Secretary with the Minister's consent. <p><lf>   (3) Subject to subsection (4), a Minister who engages a consultant under subsection (1) may determine which of the provisions referred to in subsection (2) is or are to be included in the agreement under which the consultant is engaged. <p><lf>   (4) In an agreement for the engagement by a Minister of a consultant under this Part, a task shall not be specified by the Minister, for the purposes of a provision of the kind referred to in paragraph (2) (c), without the agreement of the relevant Secretary. </lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>MEMBERS OF PARLIAMENT (STAFF) ACT 1984 - SECT 5 Terms and conditions of engagement </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   5\. (1) The terms and conditions of an engagement by a Minister of a natural person under this Part, other than terms and conditions relating to any matter in relation to which:<lf>   (a) a determination is to be made by the Minister under section 4 or 8; or<lf>    (b) express provision is made by section 9;<lf> shall be determined by the Board. 

<lf>   (2) The terms and conditions of an engagement by a Minister of a body corporate or a partnership under this Part, other than terms and conditions relating to any matter in relation to which:<lf>   (a) a determination is to be made by the Minister under section 4; or<lf>   (b) express provision is made by section 9;<lf> shall be determined by the Board. <p><lf>   (3) The terms and conditions of an engagement under this Part that are determined by the Board under subsection (1) or (2) shall be specified in the agreement relating to the arrangement. <p><lf>   (4) Without limiting the generality of subsection (1) or (2), an agreement for the engagement by a Minister of a consultant under this Part shall make provision for the payment to the consultant of an amount by way of compensation for the early termination of the engagement if the engagement is terminated by subsection 9 (1) or by the Minister under subsection 9 (2) before the expiration of the period specified in the agreement as the term of the engagement. <p><lf>   (5) An agreement under section 4 may be varied in a manner provided for in the agreement. </lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>MEMBERS OF PARLIAMENT (STAFF) ACT 1984 - SECT 6 Natural persons may be engaged under contracts of employment or contracts for services </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   6\. An agreement for the engagement of a natural person as a consultant under this Part shall specify whether the agreement is to be taken to be a contract of employment or a contract for services. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>MEMBERS OF PARLIAMENT (STAFF) ACT 1984 - SECT 7 Engagement of natural persons under contracts of employment </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   7\. (1) In this section, "relevant agreement" means an agreement under section 4 that is to be taken to be a contract of employment. 

<lf>   (2) The engagement of a natural person as a consultant under a relevant agreement may be on a full-time or part-time basis. <p><lf>   (3) Subject to section 8, a term or condition of the engagement of a natural person as a consultant under a relevant agreement may be specified in the agreement by reference to the entitlement that the person would have:<lf>   (a) if a specified Act applied in relation to the person; or<lf>   (b) if a specified Act applied in relation to the person with such modifications as are set out in the agreement. </lf></lf></lf></p></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>MEMBERS OF PARLIAMENT (STAFF) ACT 1984 - SECT 8 Superannuation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   8\. (1) In this section:<lf>   "relevant agreement" means an agreement under section 4 that is to be taken to be a contract of employment;<lf>   "relevant person" means a natural person other than:<lf>   (a) a person who, immediately before being engaged under this Part, was an eligible employee within the meaning of the Superannuation Act 1976; or<lf>   (ab) a person who, immediately before being engaged under this Part, was a member of the superannuation scheme established by deed under the Superannuation Act 1990; or<lf>   (b) a pensioner. 

<lf>   (2) A relevant agreement for the engagement by a Minister of a relevant person as a consultant may contain provisions determined by the Minister in relation to the provision to the person of superannuation benefits or benefits in the nature of superannuation benefits. <p><lf>   (3) Without limiting the generality of subsection (2), a relevant agreement for the engagement of a relevant person as a consultant may provide:<lf>   (a) that, if the person was engaged before 1 July 1990, the Superannuation Act 1976 applies in relation to the person as if the person were a temporary employee within the meaning of that Act;<lf> and, where a relevant agreement so provides, the Superannuation Act 1976 applies in relation to the person in accordance with the agreement. <p><lf>   (3A) Without limiting subsection (2), a relevant agreement for the engagement of a relevant person after 30 June 1990 as a consultant may provide that the Superannuation Act 1976 applies in relation to the person as if the person were a temporary employee within the meaning of section 13 of that Act if:<lf>   (a) immediately before the person was so engaged, deferred benefits were applicable to him or her under that Act or the Superannuation Act 1922; or<lf>   (b) the person became, by virtue of his or her being so engaged, a re-employed former contributor with preserved rights within the meaning of the Superannuation Act 1976\. <p><lf>   (3B) Where a relevant agreement so provides, the Superannuation Act 1976 applies in relation to the person in accordance with the agreement. <p><lf>   (3C) Without limiting subsection (2), a relevant agreement for the engagement of a relevant person as a consultant may provide that the Superannuation Act 1990 applies in relation to the person as if the person were a temporary employee within the meaning of that Act if the relevant person:<lf>   (a) is engaged after 30 June 1990; and<lf>   (b) is not a person to whom paragraph (3A) (a) or (b) applies. <p><lf>   (3D) Where a relevant agreement so provides, the Superannuation Act 1990 applies in relation to the person in accordance with the agreement. <p><lf>   (4) Notwithstanding anything in the Superannuation Act 1976 or any other Act, the Superannuation Act 1976:<lf>   (a) does not apply in relation to a relevant person engaged as a consultant under this Part unless the application of that Act is expressly provided for, in accordance with subsection (3) or (3A), in the agreement for the engagement of the person; and<lf>   (b) does not apply in relation to such a person except as provided by subsection (3) or (3B). <p><lf>   (4A) The Superannuation Act 1990 applies in relation to a relevant person if the person makes a declaration and election under subsection 244 (1) of the Superannuation Act 1976\. <p><lf>   (4B) If the relevant person revokes, under section 247 of the Superannuation Act 1976, the declaration and election made under subsection 244 (1) of that Act, subsection (4) applies in relation to the person as if he or she had not made the declaration and election. <p><lf>   (4C) Subject to subsection (4A), in spite of anything in the Superannuation Act 1990 or any other Act, the Superannuation Act 1990:<lf>   (a) applies in relation to a relevant person engaged as a consultant under this Part only if the application of that Act is expressly provided for in the agreement for the engagement of the person; and<lf>   (b) applies in relation to such a person only as provided by subsection (3D). <p><lf>   (5) A Minister is not empowered to determine provisions for the purposes of subsection (2) except in accordance with arrangements approved by the Minister for Finance. </lf></p></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></p></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>MEMBERS OF PARLIAMENT (STAFF) ACT 1984 - SECT 9 Termination of engagement </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   9\. (1) The engagement of a consultant under this Part terminates if the Minister by whom the consultant was engaged:<lf>   (a) dies;<lf>   (b) ceases to hold office as a Minister; or<lf>   (c) ceases to administer the Department administered by the Minister at the time when the consultant was engaged. 

<lf>   (2) A Minister may at any time, by notice in writing given to a consultant engaged by the Minister under this Part, terminate the engagement of the consultant. <p><lf>   (3) A consultant engaged by a Minister under this Part may at any time, by notice in writing given to the Minister, terminate the engagement. <p><lf>   (4) The engagement of a consultant that is not terminated:<lf>   (a) by subsection (1);<lf>   (b) by the Minister under subsection (2); or<lf>   (c) by the consultant under subsection (3);<lf> terminates at the expiration of such period not exceeding 3 years as is specified, in the agreement under which the consultant was engaged, as the term of the engagement. <p><lf>   (5) Where the engagement of a person under this Part is terminated by subsection (1), the Prime Minister may by writing direct that the engagement of the person shall be deemed:<lf>   (a) not to have been so terminated; and<lf>   (b) to have continued, or to continue, until a specified date;<lf> and, where the Prime Minister so directs, the engagement of the person under this Part shall for all purposes be deemed to have continued, or to continue, until that date. </lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>MEMBERS OF PARLIAMENT (STAFF) ACT 1984 - SECT 10 Rights of officers engaged as consultants </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   10\. For the purposes of the Public Service Act, an officer who is engaged under this Part under an agreement that is to be taken to be a contract of employment shall, while the engagement continues, be deemed to be employed as the holder of a Commonwealth office within the meaning of Part IV of that Act. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>MEMBERS OF PARLIAMENT (STAFF) ACT 1984 - SECT 11 Rights of relevant public servants engaged as consultants </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   11\. (1) For the purposes of the Public Service Act, a relevant public servant who is engaged under this Part shall, subject to subsection (2), while the engagement continues, be deemed to be on leave without pay but the service of the relevant public servant pursuant to that engagement, while the relevant public servant is so deemed to be on leave, shall be taken into account as if it were service as a relevant public servant. 

<lf>   (2) A relevant public servant who was selected for employment under the Public Service Act, or whose employment under that Act was authorized, on the condition that the employment should not continue after the expiration of a specified period or on the condition that the employment should not continue after the completion of specified work shall, for the purposes of that Act, be deemed to cease to be a relevant public servant at the expiration of that period or the completion of that work, as the case may be. </lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>MEMBERS OF PARLIAMENT (STAFF) ACT 1984 - PART III  PART III - STAFF OF OFFICE-HOLDERS </name>
</b>** 
**<b>

### <name>MEMBERS OF PARLIAMENT (STAFF) ACT 1984 - SECT 12 Determinations by Prime Minister </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   12\. The Prime Minister may, by writing, determine that, having regard to the Parliamentary duties of a Senator or a Member of the House of Representatives, the Senator or Member ought to be empowered to employ staff under this Part. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>MEMBERS OF PARLIAMENT (STAFF) ACT 1984 - SECT 13 Office-holders may employ staff </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   13\. (1) An office-holder may, on behalf of the Commonwealth, employ, under an agreement in writing, a person as a member of the office-holder's staff. 

<lf>   (2) The power conferred on an office-holder by subsection (1) is not exercisable otherwise than in accordance with arrangements approved by the Prime Minister, and the exercise of that power is subject to such conditions as are determined by the Prime Minister. </lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>MEMBERS OF PARLIAMENT (STAFF) ACT 1984 - SECT 14 Terms and conditions of employment </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   14\. (1) Subject to subsection (2) and to any applicable determination made under subsection (3), the terms and conditions of employment of a person employed by an office-holder under this Part, other than terms and conditions (in this section referred to as "prescribed terms and conditions") relating to any matter in relation to which:<lf>   (a) a determination is to be made by the office-holder under section 15; or<lf>   (b) express provision is made by section 16;<lf> are the same as the terms and conditions applicable in relation to an officer performing the duties of an office in the Australian Public Service having a classification specified in the agreement for the employment of the person. 

<lf>   (2) Notwithstanding anything in subsection (1) or in the Commonwealth Employees (Redeployment and Retirement) Act 1979, that Act does not apply in relation to the employment of a person under this Part. <p><lf>   (3) The Prime Minister may by writing determine that the terms and conditions of employment of a person employed under this Part (other than prescribed terms and conditions) are varied (whether by the variation of particular terms and conditions or by the inclusion of new terms and conditions, not being prescribed terms and conditions) as specified in the determination. <p><lf>   (4) A determination under subsection (3) may be expressed to apply in relation to all persons employed under this Part, in relation to all persons included in a class of persons so employed or in relation to a specified person so employed. </lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>MEMBERS OF PARLIAMENT (STAFF) ACT 1984 - SECT 15 Superannuation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   15\. (1) In this section:<lf>   "relevant person" means a person other than:<lf>   (a) a person who, immediately before being employed under this Part, was an eligible employee within the meaning of the Superannuation Act 1976;or<lf>   (ab) a person who, immediately before being employed under this Part, was a member of the superannuation scheme established by deed under the Superannuation Act 1990; or<lf>   (b) a pensioner. 

<lf>   (2) An agreement under section 13 for the employment by an office- holder of a relevant person as a member of the office-holder's staff may contain provisions determined by the office-holder in relation to the provision to the person of superannuation benefits or benefits in the nature of superannuation benefits. <p><lf>   (3) Without limiting the generality of subsection (2), an agreement under section 13 for the employment of a relevant person may provide:<lf>   (a) that, if the person was employed before 1 July 1990, the Superannuation Act 1976 applies in relation to the person as if the person were a temporary employee within the meaning of that Act;<lf> and, where an agreement so provides, the Superannuation Act 1976 applies in relation to the person in accordance with the agreement. <p><lf>   (3A) Without limiting subsection (2), an agreement, under section 13, for the employment of a relevant person after 30 June 1990 may provide that the Superannuation Act 1976 applies in relation to the person as if the person were a temporary employee within the meaning of section 11 of that Act if:<lf>   (a) immediately before the person was so employed, deferred benefits were applicable to him or her under that Act or the Superannuation Act 1922; ### <name>MEMBERS OF PARLIAMENT (STAFF) ACT 1984 - SECT 16 Termination of employment </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   16\. (1) The employment of a person under this Part terminates if the office-holder by whom the person was employed dies. 

<lf>   (2) The employment of a person under this Part terminates if:<lf>   (a) in the case of a person employed by a person who holds a relevant office - the last-mentioned person ceases to hold the relevant office;<lf>   (b) in the case of a person employed by a Senator in respect of whom a determination by the Prime Minister under section 12 is in force - the Senator ceases to be a Senator or the determination is revoked; or<lf>   (c) in the case of a person employed by a Member of the House of Representatives in respect of whom a determination by the Prime Minister under section 12 is in force - the Member ceases to be a Member of the House of Representatives or the determination is revoked. <p><lf>   (2A) For the purposes of paragraph (2) (b), a Senator shall be deemed not to have ceased to be a Senator while he or she continues to be entitled to the Parliamentary allowance that was payable to him or her as a Senator. <p><lf>   (2B) For the purposes of paragraph (2) (c), a Member of the House of Representatives shall be deemed not to have ceased to be such a Member while he or she continues to be entitled to the Parliamentary allowance that was payable to him or her as such a Member. <p><lf>   (3) An office-holder may at any time, by notice in writing given to a person employed by the office-holder under this Part, terminate the person's employment. <p><lf>   (4) A person employed by an office-holder under this Part may at any time, by notice in writing given to the office-holder, terminate the person's employment. <p><lf>   (5) Where the employment of a person under this Part is terminated by subsection (1) or (2), the Prime Minister may by writing direct that the employment of the person shall be deemed:<lf>   (a) not to have been so terminated; and<lf>   (b) to have continued, or to continue, until a specified date;<lf> and, where the Prime Minister so directs, the employment of the person under this Part shall for all purposes be deemed to have continued, or to continue, until that date. </lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf>

### <name>MEMBERS OF PARLIAMENT (STAFF) ACT 1984 - SECT 17 Rights of officers employed by office-holders </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   17\. (1) For the purposes of the Public Service Act, an officer who is employed under this Part shall, while the employment continues, be deemed to be employed as the holder of a Commonwealth office within the meaning of Part IV of that Act. 

<lf>   (2) An officer employed under this Part may, by notice in writing given to the Agency:<lf>   (a) before the termination of the employment under section 16; or<lf>   (b) before the expiration of the period of 30 days, or such further period as the Agency allows, after the termination of the employment under section 16;<lf> make an application to the Agency for the making of a determination under this section. <p><lf>   (3) The Agency shall refer an application made to it under subsection (2) to a Re-integration Assessment Committee. <p><lf>   (4) The Re-integration Assessment Committee to which an application under subsection (2) is referred shall inquire into the application and shall, having regard to:<lf>   (a) the office in the Australian Public Service held by the officer before being employed under this Part;<lf>   (b) the duration of the officer's employment under this Part;<lf>   (c) the nature of the duties performed by the officer while employed under this Part; and<lf>   (d) any other matter that in the opinion of the Committee is relevant;<lf> determine the classification (being a classification that is the same as, or higher than, the classification of the officer as an unattached officer at the time of the determination) that the officer is to have as an unattached officer in the Australian Public Service and, unless there is only one rate of salary applicable in respect of the classification so determined, the rate of salary at which the officer is to be paid. <p><lf>   (5) A determination under subsection (4) in relation to an officer shall be reduced to writing and copies of the determination shall be furnished to the Board and to the officer. <p><lf>   (6) A determination under subsection (4) in relation to an officer takes effect, or shall be deemed to have taken effect, upon the termination under section 16 of the employment of the officer under this Part. </lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf>

<lf>   (2) A relevant public servant who was selected for employment under the Public Service Act, or whose employment under that Act was authorized, on the condition that the employment should not continue after the expiration of a specified period or on the condition that the employment should not continue after the completion of specified work shall, for the purposes of that Act, be deemed to cease to be a relevant public servant at the expiration of that period or the completion of that work, as the case may be. </lf>
