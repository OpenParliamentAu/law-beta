**

###  METEOROLOGY ACT 1955 
**


**

###  Compilation Information 
**







**

###  Long Title 
**
###  METEOROLOGY ACT 1955- SECT 1  Short title [_see_ Note 1] 
This Act may be cited as the _Meteorology Act 1955_.

 
###  METEOROLOGY ACT 1955- SECT 3  Interpretation  
In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:

 
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_the Bureau_ means the Commonwealth Bureau of Meteorology

established by this Act.

 </dl></dl>

_the Director_ means the Director of Meteorology. 

**

###  METEOROLOGY ACT 1955- SECT 4  Extension to Territories  
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Subject to subsection&#160;(2), this Act extends to all the Territories.

</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Minister may, by notice published in the _Gazette_, declare that this Act shall, on a date specified in the notice, cease to extend to a Territory specified in the notice. </dd> </dl>

**

###  METEOROLOGY ACT 1955- SECT 5  The Commonwealth Bureau of Meteorology  
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>For the purposes of this Act, there shall be a Commonwealth Bureau of

Meteorology and a Director of Meteorology. </dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Bureau shall be under the charge of the Director, who shall, subject to the directions of the Minister, have the general administration of this Act. </dd> </dl>

**

###  METEOROLOGY ACT 1955- SECT 6  Functions of the Bureau  
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The functions of the Bureau are:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the taking and recording of meteorological observations and other

observations required for the purposes of meteorology; </dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the forecasting of weather and of the state of the atmosphere;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>the issue of warnings of gales, storms and other weather conditions likely

to endanger life or property, including weather conditions likely to give rise

to floods or bush fires; </dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>the supply of meteorological information; </dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>the publication of meteorological reports and bulletins; </dd>

<dt>(f)</dt><dd>the promotion of the use of meteorological information; </dd>

<dt>(g)</dt><dd>the promotion of the advancement of meteorological science, by means of

meteorological research and investigation or otherwise;</dd>

<dt>(h)</dt><dd>the furnishing of advice on meteorological matters; and </dd>

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>co-operation with the authority administering the meteorological service

of any other country (including a Territory specified under subsection 4(2)) in

relation to any of the matters specified in the preceding paragraphs of this

subsection.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Bureau shall perform its functions under this Act in the public

interest generally and in particular:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>for the purposes of the Defence Force; </dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>for the purposes of navigation and shipping and of civil aviation; and </dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>for the purpose of assisting persons and authorities engaged in primary

production, industry, trade and commerce.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

**

###  METEOROLOGY ACT 1955- SECT 7  Powers of the Director 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Director has such powers as are necessary to enable the Bureau to

perform its functions under the last preceding section, and, in particular, may:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>establish meteorological offices and observing stations; </dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>arrange with any Department, authority or person to take and record

meteorological observations and transmit meteorological reports and

information; </dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>arrange means of communication for the transmission and reception of

meteorological reports and information; and </dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>arrange for the training of persons in meteorology.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Departments and authorities with which, and the persons with whom,

arrangements may be made under the last preceding subsection include

Departments and authorities of a State or Territory and persons in the service

of such a State or Territory of such a Department or authority.

</dd> </dl>

**

###  METEOROLOGY ACT 1955- SECT 8  Charges  
**

 <dl compact="">

		The Director may, subject to any directions of the Minister, make charges for

forecasts, information, advice, publications and other matter supplied in

pursuance of this Act.

 </dl>

**

###  METEOROLOGY ACT 1955- SECT 9  Regulations  
**

 <dl compact="">

		The Governor-General may make regulations, not inconsistent with this Act,

prescribing all matters which by this Act are required or permitted to be

prescribed, or which are necessary or convenient to be prescribed for carrying

out or giving effect to this Act.

 </dl>
