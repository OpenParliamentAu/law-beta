### <name>INTERNATIONAL AIR SERVICES COMMISSION ACT 1992 - LONG TITLE </name>

## SECT
<sect>            An Act to establish the International Air Services<lf>                   Commission, and for related purposes<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>INTERNATIONAL AIR SERVICES COMMISSION ACT 1992 - PART 1  PART 1 - PRELIMINARY </name>
</b>** 
**<b>

### <name>INTERNATIONAL AIR SERVICES COMMISSION ACT 1992 - SECT 1 Short title </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   1\. This Act may be cited as the International Air Services Commission Act 1992.*1* SEE NOTES TO FIRST ARTICLE OF THIS CHAPTER . </sect>
**<b>

### <name>INTERNATIONAL AIR SERVICES COMMISSION ACT 1992 - SECT 2 Commencement </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   2\. This Act commences on 1 July 1992\. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>INTERNATIONAL AIR SERVICES COMMISSION ACT 1992 - SECT 3 Object of Act </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   3\. The object of this Act is to enhance international air services by fostering:<lf>   (a) greater economic efficiency in the airline industry, and increased competition between Australian carriers; and<lf>   (b) increased responsiveness by airlines to the needs of consumers, including an increased range of choices and benefits; and<lf>   (c) Australian tourism and trade; and<lf>   (d) the maintenance of Australian carriers capable of competing effectively with airlines of foreign countries. </lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>INTERNATIONAL AIR SERVICES COMMISSION ACT 1992 - SECT 4 Interpretation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   4\. (1) In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:<lf>   "another country" includes any region:<lf>   (a) that is part of a foreign country; or<lf>   (b) that is under the protection of a foreign country; or<lf>   (c) for whose international relations a foreign country is responsible;<lf>   "Australian carrier", in relation to particular capacity, means a person who:<lf>   (a) conducts, or proposes to conduct, an international airline service to and from Australia; and<lf>   (b) under the bilateral arrangement to which the capacity relates, may be permitted to carry passengers or freight, or both passengers and freight, under that arrangement as an airline designated, nominated or otherwise similarly authorised by Australia;<lf>   "available capacity" has the meaning given in subsection 5 (1);<lf>   "bilateral arrangement" means an agreement or arrangement between:<lf>   (a) Australia, or an entity or organisation nominated or otherwise similarly authorised by Australia to enter into the agreement or arrangement; and<lf>   (b) another country;<lf> under which the carriage by air of passengers or freight, or both passengers and freight, between Australia and the other country is permitted;<lf>   "capacity" means an amount (however calculated or described) of space for the carriage of passengers or freight, or both passengers and freight, that persons designated, nominated or otherwise similarly authorised by Australia are permitted under a bilateral arrangement to carry by air;<lf>   "Chairperson" means the Chairperson of the Commission;<lf>   "Commission" means the International Air Services Commission established by section 6;<lf>   "determination" means a determination made by the Commission under section 7 or 8 concerning the allocation of capacity;<lf>   "interim determination" means a determination that includes a statement, under paragraph 15 (2) (b), 19 (1) (b) or 54 (4) (a), to the effect that it is an interim determination;<lf>   "member" means a member of the Commission;<lf>   "new capacity" has the meaning given in subsection 5(3);<lf>   "operational decision", in relation to particular capacity, means a decision (including the granting of any licence or the giving of any approval) under the Air Navigation Act 1920, or the regulations made under that Act, that must be made if an Australian carrier is to be permitted to operate an international airline service using that capacity;<lf>   "reduce", in relation to capacity, includes reduce to nil;<lf>   "renewal" means renewal of a determination under section 8;<lf>   "review" means review of a determination under section 10;<lf>   "shelf capacity" has the meaning given in subsection 5(4);<lf>   "transfer application" means an application, by an Australian carrier to whom a determination allocates capacity, for one or both of the following:<lf>   (a) a variation of the determination in a way that allocates, or has the effect of allocating, that capacity to another Australian carrier;<lf>   (b) a variation of the determination that varies, or has the effect of varying, one or more conditions of a kind referred to in paragraph 15 (2) (d), (e) or (f).<lf>   "wholly-owned subsidiary" has the same meaning as in the Corporations Law; 

<lf>   (2) A reference in this Act to an allocation of capacity is a reference to a finding, included in a determination, that a specified Australian carrier should be permitted to use that capacity. <p><lf>   (3) A reference in this Act to the benefit to the public in relation to an allocation of capacity is a reference to the benefit to the public that would occur if the Australian carrier to whom the capacity is allocated were permitted to use that capacity. <p><lf>   (4) A reference in this Act to use of capacity is a reference to the operation of an international air service to provide the carriage of passengers or freight, or both passengers and freight, to which the capacity relates. <p><lf>   (5) For the purposes of this Act, capacity is taken to be fully used if the capacity is used, by the Australian carrier to whom a determination allocates the capacity, to operate the maximum number of flights permitted by the determination in using that capacity. </lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf>

### <name>INTERNATIONAL AIR SERVICES COMMISSION ACT 1992 - SECT 5 Available capacity, new capacity and shelf capacity </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   5\. (1) Subject to subsection (2) of this section and subsection 9 (2A), capacity is taken to be available capacity for the purposes of this Act if operational decisions are not in force in relation to that capacity. 

<lf>   (2) If the Commission makes a determination, the capacity to which it relates is taken not to be available capacity during the period beginning when the determination is made and ending when the Secretary advises the Commission under section 50 in relation to the capacity. <p><lf>   (3) For the purposes of this Act, capacity is taken to be new capacity if it is available capacity in relation to which the Commission has not previously:<lf>   (a) made a determination concerning its allocation; or<lf>   (b) considered making, but decided not to make, such a determination; or<lf>   (c) invited applications concerning its allocation and caused  to be recorded in the minutes of a meeting of the Commission a statement to the effect that no applications were received in response to the invitation or that any applications so received were withdrawn. <p><lf>   (4) For the purposes of this Act, capacity is taken to be shelf capacity if it is available capacity that is not new capacity. </lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf>
</sect>

### <name>INTERNATIONAL AIR SERVICES COMMISSION ACT 1992 - PART 2  PART 2 - KEY PROVISIONS </name>

**<b>

### <name>INTERNATIONAL AIR SERVICES COMMISSION ACT 1992 - SECT 6 The International Air Services Commission </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   6\. (1) The International Air Services Commission is established. 

<lf>   (2) The functions of the Commission are:<lf>   (a) to make determinations under sections 7 and 8; and<lf>   (b) to conduct reviews of those determinations; and<lf>   (c) to provide advice to the Minister about any matter referred to the Commission by the Minister concerning international air operations. <p><lf>   (2A) To remove any doubt, it is declared that the Commission may, at the same time, consider, and make decisions on, more than one matter in relation to particular capacity, for example, the renewal of a determination that allocated capacity and the allocation of new capacity. <p><lf>   (3) In performing its functions, the Commission must:<lf>   (a) comply with any applicable policy statements made by the Minister under section 11; and<lf>   (b) have regard to Australia's international obligations concerning the operation of international air services. <p><lf>   (4) The Commission has the power to do everything necessary or convenient to be done for or in connection with the performance of its functions.<lf> Note: Part 4 is about the Commission's procedure. Part 5 is about the membership of the Commission. </lf></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>INTERNATIONAL AIR SERVICES COMMISSION ACT 1992 - SECT 7 Determinations allocating capacity </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   7\. (1) The Commission may make a determination allocating available capacity. 

<lf>   (2) The determination:<lf>   (a) must not allocate available capacity unless the Commission is satisfied that the allocation would be of benefit to the public; and<lf>   (b) if more than one application was made under Division 1 of Part 3 relating to the allocation - must make the allocation that the Commission is satisfied, having regard to the applications made, would be of the greatest benefit to the public. <p><lf>   (3) In assessing the benefit to the public of an allocation of capacity, the Commission must apply the criteria set out for that purpose in the policy statements made by the Minister under section 11.<lf> Note: Division 1 of Part 3 is about making determinations. Part 4 is about the Commission's procedure. </lf></lf></p></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>INTERNATIONAL AIR SERVICES COMMISSION ACT 1992 - SECT 8 Renewal of determinations </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   8\. (1) The Commission may, at any time while a determination is in force, make a fresh determination allocating the capacity to which the original determination relates. 

<lf>   (2) The fresh determination:<lf>   (a) must make the same allocation of capacity as the original determination unless:<lf>     (i) the Commission is satisfied that that allocation is no<lf> longer of benefit to the public; or<lf>     (ii) the original determination is an interim determination;<lf> and<lf>   (b) comes into force immediately after the end of the period during which the original determination was in force. <p><lf>   (3) If the fresh determination does not make the same allocation of capacity as the original determination, it must not make a different allocation of capacity unless the Commission is satisfied that that allocation would be of benefit to the public. <p><lf>   (4) In assessing the benefit to the public of an allocation of capacity, the Commission must apply the criteria set out for that purpose in any policy statements made by the Minister under section 11.<lf> Note: Division 2 of Part 3 is about renewing determinations. Part 4 is about the Commission's procedure. </lf></lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>INTERNATIONAL AIR SERVICES COMMISSION ACT 1992 - SECT 9 Effect of determinations on the making of operational decisions </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   9\. (1) The Secretary must not make an operational decision in relation to capacity that is inconsistent with a determination relating to the allocation of that capacity. 

<lf>   (2) The Secretary must not make an operational decision in relation to capacity that comes into existence on or after 1 July 1992 unless:<lf>   (a) a determination is in force relating to the allocation of that capacity; or<lf>   (b) the capacity relates to a non-scheduled flight within the meaning of the Air Navigation Act 1920; or<lf>   (c) the decision is made in other circumstances prescribed by the regulations. <p><lf>   (2A) An operational decision made in relation to capacity as mentioned in paragraph (2) (c) is not taken to be an operational decision for the purposes of subsection 5 (1). <p><lf>   (3) Nothing in this Act obliges the Secretary to make an operational decision implementing a determination.<lf> Note: Section 50 imposes notification requirements on the Secretary. </lf></lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>INTERNATIONAL AIR SERVICES COMMISSION ACT 1992 - SECT 10 Review of determinations </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   10\. (1) The Commission may, at any time, conduct a review of a determination if it believes that there may be grounds for varying, suspending or revoking the determination. 

<lf>   (2) The Commission must conduct a review of a determination if an Australian carrier to whom the determination allocates capacity applies to the Commission under section 21 for the determination to be varied.<lf> Note: Division 3 of Part 3 is about reviewing determinations. Part 4 is about the Commission's procedure. </lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>INTERNATIONAL AIR SERVICES COMMISSION ACT 1992 - SECT 11 Policy statements </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   11\. (1) The Minister may, by instrument in writing, make policy statements about the way in which the Commission is to perform its functions. 

<lf>   (2) In particular, the policy statements may set out:<lf>   (a) the criteria to be applied by the Commission in assessing the benefit to the public of allocations of capacity; and<lf>   (b) how the Commission is to fix the periods during which determinations are to be in force; and<lf>   (c) matters relating to the Commission's consideration of whether determinations should be interim determinations; and<lf>   (d) matters relating to the inclusion of conditions in determinations and the variation or revocation of such conditions. <p><lf>   (3) Without limiting the criteria that, under paragraph (2) (a), may be set out in the policy statements, the policy statements may set out:<lf>   (a) criteria that relate to the matters referred to in section 3; and<lf>   (b) criteria that apply in relation to particular circumstances, including where:<lf>     (i) capacity is not limited under bilateral arrangements; or<lf>     (ii) only one application has been made for the allocation of<lf> capacity; or<lf>     (iii) no submissions are received opposing allocation of<lf> capacity to an applicant; or<lf>     (iv) an application for the allocation of capacity is opposed<lf> on particular grounds. <p><lf>   (4) A policy statement:<lf>   (a) must not deal with the making of a particular determination or decision by the Commission; and<lf>   (b) is invalid to the extent that it purports to deal with such a matter. <p><lf>   (5) The policy statements are disallowable instruments for the purposes of section 46A of the Acts Interpretation Act 1901\. </lf></p></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>

### <name>INTERNATIONAL AIR SERVICES COMMISSION ACT 1992 - DIVISION 1  Division 1 - Determinations allocating capacity </name>

**<b>

### <name>INTERNATIONAL AIR SERVICES COMMISSION ACT 1992 - SECT 12 Applications relating to new capacity </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   12\. (1) Before making a determination allocating new capacity, the Commission must, by notice, invite:<lf>   (a) applications for a determination allocating the capacity; and<lf>   (b) submissions about the allocation of the capacity.<lf> Note: Section 52 is about publication of notices. 

<lf>   (2) Without limiting the matters that may be included in the notice, the notice must:<lf>   (a) set out the matters that the regulations require to be set out in the notice; and<lf>   (b) specify the period within which applications are required to be made; and<lf>   (c) may include guidance as to:<lf>     (i) any matters that the Commission considers to be<lf> particularly important in making its determination in relation to that new capacity; and<lf>     (ii) the weight that the Commission is likely to give to such<lf> matters. <p><lf>   (3) Any person may apply to the Commission for a determination allocating the capacity. </lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>INTERNATIONAL AIR SERVICES COMMISSION ACT 1992 - SECT 13 Applications relating to shelf capacity </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   13\. (1) The Commission must not make a determination allocating shelf capacity unless a person has applied to the Commission for a determination allocating the capacity. 

<lf>   (2) On receiving the application, the Commission must, by notice, invite:<lf>   (a) other applications for a determination allocating the capacity; and<lf>   (b) submissions about the allocation of the capacity.<lf> Note: Section 52 is about publication of notices. <p><lf>   (3) Without limiting the matters that may be included in the notice, the notice must:<lf>   (a) set out the matters that the regulations require to be set out in the notice; and<lf>   (b) specify the period within which other applications are required to be made. <p><lf>   (4) Any other person may apply to the Commission for a determination allocating the capacity. </lf></p></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>INTERNATIONAL AIR SERVICES COMMISSION ACT 1992 - SECT 14 Requirements of applications </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   14\. (1) An application under this Division:<lf>   (a) must be in writing; and<lf>   (b) must be delivered at the address specified in the regulations as the Commission's address for receiving applications; and<lf>   (c) in the case of an application under subsection 12 (3) or 13 (4) - must be so delivered within the period referred to in paragraph 12 (2) (b) or 13 (3) (b), as the case requires; and<lf>   (d) must comply with the requirements of form and content referred to in subsection (2). 

<lf>   (2) The regulations may impose requirements as to the form and content of such applications. <p><lf>   (3) The Commission may reject an application without considering it further if it does not comply with subsection (1). </lf></p></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>INTERNATIONAL AIR SERVICES COMMISSION ACT 1992 - SECT 15 Content of determinations </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   15\. (1) A determination may include such terms and conditions as the Commission thinks fit. 

<lf>   (2) Without limiting subsection (1), the determination:<lf>   (a) must specify the period, under subsection (3), during which the determination is to be in force; and<lf>   (b) may include a statement to the effect that the determination is an interim determination; and<lf>   (c) must include a condition that the capacity be fully used, except so far as:<lf>     (i) the determination provides otherwise in relation to a<lf> specified period commencing when the determination comes into force; or<lf>     (ii) the regulations otherwise permit; and<lf>   (d) must include a condition that, except to the extent permitted by the condition referred to in paragraphs (e) and (ea), the available capacity in question is only to be used by the one or more Australian carriers to whom the capacity is allocated; and<lf>   (e) must include a condition stating the extent (if any) to which any such carrier may use that capacity by providing international air services jointly with another Australian carrier or any other person; and<lf>   (ea) may include a condition that, to the extent that any of  the capacity is allocated to a particular Australian carrier, it may be used  in whole or in part by any one or more of the following:<lf>     (i) the carrier;<lf>     (ii) a wholly-owned subsidiary of the carrier;<lf>     (iii) if the carrier is a wholly-owned subsidiary of another<lf> Australian carrier - that other carrier; and<lf>   (f) must include a condition stating the extent to which changes in the ownership or control of any such carrier are permitted while the determination is in force. <p><lf>   (2A) If a determination includes a condition of a kind mentioned in paragraph (2) (ea), the determination may include conditions that are applicable to all, or some only, of the persons who are permitted to use the capacity concerned. <p><lf>   (2B) If:<lf>   (a) a carrier is a wholly-owned subsidiary of another Australian carrier; and<lf>   (b) that other carrier would, apart from any condition included under this subsection in a determination, be permitted by a condition referred to  in paragraph (2) (ea) to use any capacity allocated to the subsidiary;<lf> the determination must include a condition stating the extent to which changes in the ownership or control of that other carrier are permitted if that other carrier is to continue to be permitted to use that capacity while the determination is in force. <p><lf>   (3) The period referred to in paragraph (2) (a) is:<lf>   (a) if the policy statements made by the Minister under section 11 set out how the period is to be fixed - the period as so fixed; or<lf>   (b) in any other case:<lf>     (i) if the determination is an interim determination - 3 years;<lf> or<lf>     (ii) if the determination is not an interim determination - 5<lf> years. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>INTERNATIONAL AIR SERVICES COMMISSION ACT 1992 - SECT 16 Notification of determinations </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   16\. As soon as practicable after the making of a determination, the Commission must:<lf>   (a) make copies of the determination and its reasons for the determination available to the public; and<lf>   (b) publish, in such manner as it thinks appropriate, a notice stating:<lf>     (i) that the determination has been made; and<lf>     (ii) where copies of the determination and reasons may be<lf> obtained. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>

### <name>INTERNATIONAL AIR SERVICES COMMISSION ACT 1992 - DIVISION 2  Division 2 - Renewal of determinations </name>
</b>** Note: This Division is about renewing determinations under section 8\. 
**<b>

### <name>INTERNATIONAL AIR SERVICES COMMISSION ACT 1992 - SECT 17 Consideration of renewals </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   17\. (1) The Commission must start its consideration of the renewal of a determination (other than an interim determination) at least 12 months before the end of the period during which the determination is in force. 

<lf>   (2) Before starting its consideration, the Commission must, by notice, invite submissions about the renewal.<lf> Note: Section 52 is about publication of notices. <p><lf>   (3) Any person may make submissions to the Commission about the renewal. </lf></p></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>INTERNATIONAL AIR SERVICES COMMISSION ACT 1992 - SECT 18 Renewal of interim determinations </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   18\. (1) The Commission must start its consideration of the renewal of an interim determination as soon as practicable after the determination is made. 

<lf>   (2) Sections 12 and 14 apply in relation to the renewal of an interim determination in the same way they apply in relation to the making of a determination under section 7 allocating available capacity. <p><lf>   (3) If no applications are made under subsection 12 (3) (as it applies by virtue of subsection (2) of this section) in relation to the interim determination, the Commission must issue a notice stating that:<lf>   (a) no such applications were made; and<lf>   (b) the determination is no longer to be treated as an interim determination.<lf> Note: Section 52 is about publication of notices. <p><lf>   (4) If such a notice is issued, this Act (including paragraph 15 (3) (b) or 19 (2) (b), as the case requires) has effect in relation to the determination as if it is no longer an interim determination. </lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>INTERNATIONAL AIR SERVICES COMMISSION ACT 1992 - SECT 19 Content of determinations </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   19\. (1) A determination under section 8:<lf>   (a) must specify the period, under subsection (2), during which the determination is to be in force; and<lf>   (b) may include a statement to the effect that the determination is an interim determination; and<lf>   (c) subject to subsection (3), must include the same terms and conditions as the original determination to which it relates.

### <name>INTERNATIONAL AIR SERVICES COMMISSION ACT 1992 - SECT 27A Meetings of Commission </name>

</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   28\. In performing its functions, the Commission:<lf>   (a) must act with as little formality as possible; and<lf>   (b) must act as quickly as is appropriate given the requirements of this Part and the need properly to consider a matter before it; and<lf>   (c) may decide a matter before it without holding a hearing; and<lf>   (d) is not bound by the rules of evidence; and<lf>   (e) may inform itself on anything relevant to a matter before it in any way it thinks fit; and<lf>   (f) may receive information or submissions orally or by written statements; and<lf>   (g) may, in respect of a matter before it, consult such persons as it thinks fit. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
