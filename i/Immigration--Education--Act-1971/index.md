**

###  IMMIGRATION (EDUCATION) ACT 1971 
**








**

###  Long Title 
**

###  IMMIGRATION (EDUCATION) ACT 1971- SECT 1  Short title [_see_ Note 1] 
<dl compact="">

		This Act may be cited as the _Immigration (Education) Act 1971_.

 </dl>
###  IMMIGRATION (EDUCATION) ACT 1971- SECT 2  Commencement [_see_ Note 1] 
<dl compact="">

		This Act shall come into operation on the day on which it receives the Royal

Assent.

 </dl>
###  IMMIGRATION (EDUCATION) ACT 1971- SECT 3  Interpretation  
<dl compact="">

		In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:

 </dl>
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_approved course_ means an English course or a citizenship course

provided under section 4\. _approved English course_ means an English course provided in accordance with section 4 or 4B. _capital equipment of an educational nature_ includes tape recording and playing equipment and portable class-rooms, but does not include any other building. _citizenship course_ means a course of instruction designed to impart an understanding of ways of life in Australia and of the rights and duties of an Australian citizen. _English course_ means a course of instruction in the English language. _functional English_ has the same meaning as in the Migration Act. _independent school_ means a school or institution in Australia, other than a school or institution conducted by the Government of the Commonwealth, a State or a Territory, at which primary education or secondary education is, or both are, provided, but does not include a school conducted for the profit, direct or indirect, of an individual or individuals. _Migration Act_ means the _Migration Act 1958_. _permanent entry permit_ has the same meaning as in the Migration Act as in force immediately before the commencement of section 3 of the _Migration Reform Act 1992_. _permanent visa_ has the same meaning as in the Migration Act. _school authority_, in relation to an independent school, means the person or body conducting the school. _teaching and learning materials_ includes printed materials, audio materials, visual aids and audiovisual aids. _temporary visa_ has the same meaning as in the Migration Act.  </dl></dl>

###  IMMIGRATION (EDUCATION) ACT 1971- SECT 4  English courses and citizenship courses 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		The Minister may arrange for English courses and citizenship courses to be

provided:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>outside Australia for persons intending to migrate to Australia; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>in Australia for persons who:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>hold a permanent visa; or</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>hold a temporary visa of a class specified by the Minister by notice

published in the _Gazette_; or</dd>

<dt>(iii)</dt><dd>previously held a permanent entry permit or a permanent visa and have

become Australian citizens; or</dd>

<dt>(iv)</dt><dd>are under 18 and have at least one parent who has held or holds a

permanent entry permit or a permanent visa; or</dd>

<dt>(v)</dt><dd>are citizens of New Zealand who hold a special category visa; and

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>in the Territory of Cocos (Keeling) Islands or in the Territory of

Christmas Island for persons in the Territory concerned who:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>hold a permanent visa; or</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>hold a temporary visa of a class specified by the Minister by notice

published in the _Gazette_; or</dd>

<dt>(iii)</dt><dd>previously held a permanent entry permit or a permanent visa and have

become Australian citizens; or</dd>

<dt>(iv)</dt><dd>are under 18 and have at least one parent who has previously held or

holds a permanent entry permit or a permanent visa.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

###  IMMIGRATION (EDUCATION) ACT 1971- SECT 4A  Fees for English courses

exceeding the applicable fee limit per year per student, in respect of English

courses provided in accordance with section 4.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Regulations for the purposes of subsection (1) must: </dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>exempt from any fees prescribed under that subsection persons who, at the

time of enrolment in the English course, are:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>provide that fees are payable on enrolment in the English course.

###  IMMIGRATION (EDUCATION) ACT 1971- SECT 4B  English courses  
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		The Minister is to arrange for English courses to be provided for persons who:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>have made an application for a permanent visa; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>were at least 18 at the time the permanent visa came into force; and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>do not have functional English at the time of making the application;

and</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>have paid, or are exempt from paying, visa application charge under

section 45A of the Migration Act; and</dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>are not excluded from being provided with English tuition by the

regulations.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

**

###  IMMIGRATION (EDUCATION) ACT 1971- SECT 4C  Obligation to provide English tuition  
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		Subject to section 4D, the Commonwealth is obliged to provide, or arrange the

provision of, 510 hours of tuition in an approved English course to a person if

the person:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>has made an application for a permanent visa; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>was at least 18 at the time the permanent visa came into force; and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>does not have functional English at the time of making the application;

and</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>has paid, or is exempt from paying, visa application charge under section

45A of the Migration Act; and</dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>is not excluded from being provided with English tuition by the

regulations; and</dd>

<dt>(f)</dt><dd>has not previously been entitled under this Act to 510 hours of tuition in

an approved English course.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

**

###  IMMIGRATION (EDUCATION) ACT 1971- SECT 4D  Cessation of obligation to provide English tuition 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Subject to this section, the obligation of the Commonwealth to a person

under section 4C ceases:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>if the provider of an approved English course determines, in writing, in

accordance with procedures approved in writing by the Secretary, that the

person has functional English; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>if the person fails to register with the provider of an approved English

course within the period of 3 months starting on the visa commencement date;

or</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>if the person fails to commence an approved English course within the

period of 12 months starting on the visa commencement date; or</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>at the end of the period of 36 months starting on the visa commencement

date.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>If:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the obligation to a person would, under subsection (1), or under this

subsection, cease at a particular date; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the Secretary is satisfied that it would be unreasonable for the

obligation to so cease;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

the Secretary may, in writing, determine that the obligation is not to cease, or is to cease only at a later date.

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>In determining whether it is unreasonable for an obligation to cease, the

Secretary may only have regard to:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the prevention of the person from undertaking the whole or a part of an

approved English course by the action or inaction of a person who provides

approved English courses; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>any other matters that are prescribed.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(4)</dt><dd>In this section:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_visa commencement date_ for a person means:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>if the person was in Australia on the day on which the relevant visa came

into force&#151;that day; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>if the person was not in Australia on the day on which the relevant visa

came into force&#151;the day that the person first enters Australia after that day.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

###  IMMIGRATION (EDUCATION) ACT 1971- SECT 5  Provision of teaching and learning materials 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		The Minister may arrange for the purchase or production of, and the

distribution of, teaching and learning materials:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>for use in approved courses; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>for use in schools (whether or not in an approved course) for:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>teaching the English language; or</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>imparting an understanding of ways of life in Australia and of the rights

and duties of an Australian citizen;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		to children referred to in subparagraph 4(b)(iv) or (c)(iv).

</dl></dl></dl>

###  IMMIGRATION (EDUCATION) ACT 1971- SECT 7  Training courses for teachers 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Minister may arrange for the provision of training courses for

teachers who are engaged, or intend to engage, in giving approved courses.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Where a teacher attending a training course referred to in subsection (1) is paid salary by his or her employer for the whole or a part of the period of his or her attendance, the Minister may authorize the payment to the employer of the whole or a part of the cost of the salary so paid.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>In this section: </dd> </dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_employer_ includes the Government of a State or Territory. _salary_ includes an allowance in the nature of a travelling allowance.  </dl></dl>

###  IMMIGRATION (EDUCATION) ACT 1971- SECT 8  Research projects 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		The Minister may arrange for the conduct of research projects designed to

improve the form or content of approved courses.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  IMMIGRATION (EDUCATION) ACT 1971- SECT 9  Payments under arrangements etc. 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>An arrangement under this Act may make provision for, or in relation to,

the making of payments by the Commonwealth to another party to the arrangement

in connexion with matters to which the arrangement relates.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Without limiting the generality of subsection (1), an arrangement under section 4 for the provision of approved courses by the Government of a State or Territory or by the school authority of an independent school may make provision for, or in relation to, the payment by the Commonwealth of the whole or a part of: </dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the cost of salaries and fees of teaching staff engaged in the giving of

the courses; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>administrative costs (including the salaries of administrative staff)

incurred in connexion with the courses; and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>the cost of capital equipment of an educational nature to be used in the

courses; and</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>the cost of teaching and learning materials (other than materials provided

under section 5) to be used in the courses; and</dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>the cost of student support services.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>An arrangement under section 4 for the provision of approved courses by

the Government of a State or Territory or by the school authority of an

independent school may make provision for the Commonwealth to supply, whether

as a gift or otherwise, to that Government or school authority capital

equipment of an educational nature to be used in the courses.

</dd> </dl></dl>

###  IMMIGRATION (EDUCATION) ACT 1971- SECT 10  Delegation 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Minister may, by instrument in writing, delegate to a person appointed

or engaged under the _Public Service Act 1999_, either generally or

otherwise as provided in the instrument of delegation, all or any of his powers

or functions under this Act, except this power of delegation.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>A power or function so delegated may be exercised or performed by the delegate in accordance with the instrument of delegation.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>A delegation under this section is revocable at will and does not prevent the exercise of a power or the performance of a function by the Minister. </dd> </dl></dl>

**

###  IMMIGRATION (EDUCATION) ACT 1971- SECT 10A  Delegation 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		The Secretary may, by signed instrument, delegate any or all of his or her

powers under this Act to an officer of the Department.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  IMMIGRATION (EDUCATION) ACT 1971- SECT 11  Expenditure to be made from appropriations 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		Expenditure by the Commonwealth for the purposes of this Act shall be made

out of moneys appropriated by the Parliament for those purposes.

 </dl></dl>

###  IMMIGRATION (EDUCATION) ACT 1971- SECT 12  Annual reports by the Minister 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		The Minister shall, as soon as practicable after 30th June in each year,

cause to be laid before each House of the Parliament a report on the operation

of this Act during the year ended on that date, including particulars of

expenditure by the Commonwealth for the purposes of this Act during that year.

 </dl></dl>

###  IMMIGRATION (EDUCATION) ACT 1971- SECT 13  Regulations 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		The Governor-General may make regulations, not inconsistent with this Act,

prescribing all matters required or permitted by this Act to be prescribed or

necessary or convenient to be prescribed for carrying out or giving effect to

this Act.

 </dl></dl>
