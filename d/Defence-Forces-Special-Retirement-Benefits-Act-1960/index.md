### <name>DEFENCE FORCES SPECIAL RETIREMENT BENEFITS ACT 1960 - LONG TITLE </name>

## SECT
<sect>       An Act to provide special Retirement Benefits for certain Members of the<lf> Australian Regular Army<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>DEFENCE FORCES SPECIAL RETIREMENT BENEFITS ACT 1960 - SECT 1 Short title </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   1\. This Act may be cited as the Defence Forces Special Retirement Benefits Act 1960.*1* 

SEE NOTES TO FIRST ARTICLE OF THIS CHAPTER . 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>DEFENCE FORCES SPECIAL RETIREMENT BENEFITS ACT 1960 - SECT 2 Commencement </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   2\. This Act shall come into operation on a date to be fixed by Proclamation.*1* 

SEE NOTES TO FIRST ARTICLE OF THIS CHAPTER . 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>DEFENCE FORCES SPECIAL RETIREMENT BENEFITS ACT 1960 - SECT 3 This Act to be construed with Defence Forces Retirement Benefits Act 1948 </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   3\. (1) This Act and the Defence Forces Retirement Benefits Act 1948-1959 shall be read and construed together as one Act. 

  (2) Without in any way limiting the generality of the last preceding sub-section -<lf>   (a) in this Act, unless the contrary intention appears, expressions defined by the Defence Forces Retirement Benefits Act 1948-1959 have the same respective meanings as they have in that Act;<lf>   (b) a reference in this Act to the category of a person to whom this Act applies shall be read as a reference to the category of members within which that person was, immediately before his retirement, included for the purposes of the Defence Forces Retirement Benefits Act 1948-1959 and a number specified in a column headed "Category" in a Schedule to this Act shall be read as a reference to the category of members in relation to which that number is specified in the First Schedule to the Defence Forces Retirement Benefits Act 1948-1959; and<lf>   (c) the provisions of the Defence Forces Retirement Benefits Act 1948-1959 and of the Defence Forces Retirement Benefits Act 1959<lf>       apply, so far as they are applicable and are not inconsistent with this<lf>       Act, to and in relation to persons to whom this Act applies and to and<lf>       in relation to gratuities and pensions under this Act, and, for the<lf>       purposes<lf>       of those provisions as so applying, a gratuity or pension under<lf>       this Act shall be deemed to be a gratuity or pension under the Defence<lf>       Forces Retirement Benefits Act 1948-1959 and a person in receipt of a<lf>       pension under this Act shall be deemed to be a pensioner under the<lf>       Defence Forces Retirement Benefits Act 1948-1959.<lf> <p></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>DEFENCE FORCES SPECIAL RETIREMENT BENEFITS ACT 1960 - SECT 4 Persons to whom this Act applies </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   4\. (1) In this section -<lf> "the prescribed period" means -<lf>     (a) in relation to a person who is a member of the Royal Australian<lf>     Army Ordnance Corps on the proclaimed date - the period of<lf>     nine months commencing on that date; and<lf>     (b) in any other case - the period of three months commencing on<lf>     that date;<lf> "the proclaimed date" means the date of commencement of this Act. 

  (2) This Act applies to a person -<lf>   (a) who, on the proclaimed date -<lf>     (i) is a male member of the Australian Regular Army;<lf>     (ii) is a contributor to the Fund; and<lf>     (iii) is serving under an engagement, being an engagement for a<lf>     term that does not expire during the prescribed period;<lf>   (b) who is not, at any time during the prescribed period, an officer;<lf>   (c) the fifty-fifth anniversary of whose birthday has not occurred before the proclaimed date and does not occur during the prescribed period;<lf>   (d) who is declared by the Minister of State for the Army on the proclaimed date, by instrument in writing, to be a member who is to be discharged from the Australian Regular Army during the prescribed period by reason of a reduction in the establishment of his regiment or corps;<lf>   (e) who is discharged from the Australian Regular Army during the prescribed period for that reason; and<lf>   (f) who does not become a member on the day following the date of his discharge. <p></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>DEFENCE FORCES SPECIAL RETIREMENT BENEFITS ACT 1960 - SECT 5 Service for pension </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   5\. (1) For the purposes of sections six and seven of this Act, "service for pension", in relation to a person, includes -<lf>   (a) service before attaining the age of twenty years under the engagement under which he was serving immediately before his retirement or, if he has had successive and continuous engagements, under the first of those engagements, other than service under an initial engagement of less than six years; and<lf>   (b) service in respect of which, by virtue of section thirty-six of the Defence Forces Retirement Benefits Act 1948-1955 or of that Act as amended from time to time, he was not permitted or required to contribute to the Fund. 

  (2) Where, by virtue of an election made by a person to whom this Act applies under section twenty-six of the Defence Forces Retirement Benefits Act 1948 or of that Act as amended from time to time, a period of service, or part of a period of service, of the person was required to be taken into account for the purposes of pension under the Defence Forces Retirement Benefits Act 1948-1959, that period or that part of that period, as the case may be, shall be taken into account for the purposes of pension under this Act, but shall not be taken into account for any other purpose of this Act. 
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>DEFENCE FORCES SPECIAL RETIREMENT BENEFITS ACT 1960 - SECT 6 Persons discharged with less than six years' service for pension </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   6\. A person to whom this Act applies who, on retirement, has not completed six years' service for pension is entitled, in addition to a refund under section sixty of the Defence Forces Retirement Benefits Act 1948-1959 of the amount of the contributions paid by him to the Fund, to a gratuity of an amount calculated at the rate of Twenty pounds for each year of service for pension completed by him on retirement. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>DEFENCE FORCES SPECIAL RETIREMENT BENEFITS ACT 1960 - SECT 7 Persons discharged with not less than six years', but less than fifteen years', service for pension </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   7\. (1) A person to whom this Act applies who, on retirement, has completed not less than six years' service for pension and is not entitled to a pension under section nine or ten of this Act -<lf>   (a) shall, for the purposes of sub-section (1) of section forty-two of the Defence Forces Retirement Benefits Act 1948-1959, be deemed to have retired after completion of his engagement or, if he has had successive and continuous engagements, after completion of the last of those engagements; and<lf>   (b) is entitled, in addition to a refund under section sixty of the Defence Forces Retirement Benefits Act 1948-1959 of the amount of the contributions paid by him to the Fund and a gratuity under section forty-two of that Act, to a gratuity of an amount calculated by multiplying the amount applicable to him under the next succeeding subsection by a number equal to the number of years of service for pension completed by him on retirement. 

    (2) For the purposes of paragraph (b) of the last preceding sub-section, the amount applicable to a person is the amount ascertained by multiplying by fourteen the sum of -<lf>       (a) the amount of the daily rate of active pay applicable to him on the<lf>       date<lf>       of his retirement having regard to his rank, branch and group on that<lf>       date and to the period of his service in that rank; and<lf>       (b) the amount of Ten shillings and sixpence.<lf> <p></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>DEFENCE FORCES SPECIAL RETIREMENT BENEFITS ACT 1960 - SECT 8 Special provisions applicable to gratuities </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   8\. (1) Section sixty-six of the Defence Forces Retirement Benefits Act 1959 does not apply to a person to whom this Act applies. 

    (2) Section sixty-eight of the Defence Forces Retirement Benefits Act 1959<lf> does not apply to or in relation to a gratuity to which a person is entitled under section six of this Act or under paragraph (b) of sub-section (1) of section seven of this Act. <p>    (3) Where a person entitled to a gratuity under section six of this Act, or<lf> under paragraph (b) of sub-section (1) of section seven of this Act, makes an election under sub-section (1) of section seventy-four B of the Defence Forces Retirement Benefits Act 1948-1959, paragraph (c) of sub-section (2) of that last-mentioned section does not apply in relation to the payment of that gratuity to him. <p></p></lf></p></lf>
</sect>

### <name>DEFENCE FORCES SPECIAL RETIREMENT BENEFITS ACT 1960 - SECT 12 Commonwealth contributions in respect of pensions </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   12\. (1) In this section, "special contribution" means -<lf>   (a) in relation to a person to whom section nine of this Act applies who has, for the purposes of that section, paid a contribution to the Fund - that contribution;<lf>   (b) in relation to a person to whom section ten of this Act applies, being a person not contributing on retirement for additional benefits as defined in sub-section (2) of that section, who has, for the purposes of that section, paid a contribution to the Fund - that contribution; and<lf>   (c) in relation to a person to whom section ten of this Act applies, being a person contributing on retirement for additional benefits as defined in sub-section (2) of that section, who has, for the purposes of that section, paid a contribution to the Fund that is in excess of the normal additional pension contribution applicable to him under that section - that contribution to the extent of the excess. 

  (2) In the application of section sixty-one of the Defence Forces Retirement Benefits Act 1959 in respect of a payment of pension from the Fund to or in respect of a person to whom this Act applies, such part of any special contribution paid by the person to the Fund as the Board, on the advice of the Commonwealth Actuary, determines shall be deemed to be a contribution of a kind referred to in paragraph (a) of that section.
