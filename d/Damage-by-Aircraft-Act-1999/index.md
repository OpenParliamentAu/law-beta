###  Long Title 
**
###  DAMAGE BY AIRCRAFT ACT 1999- SECT 1  Short title [_see_ Note 1] 
<dl compact="">

		This Act may be cited as the _Damage by Aircraft Act 1999_.

 </dl>
###  DAMAGE BY AIRCRAFT ACT 1999- SECT 2  Commencement [_see_ Note 1] 
<dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Subject to this section, this Act commences on a day to be fixed by

Proclamation.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The day fixed under subsection (1) must not be a day that occurs before the day on which Australia's denunciation of the Convention on Damage caused by Foreign Aircraft to third Parties on the Surface takes effect. </dd> </dl>
###  DAMAGE BY AIRCRAFT ACT 1999- SECT 3  Object of Act 
<dl compact="">

		The main object of this Act is to facilitate the recovery of damages for

certain injury, loss, damage or destruction caused by aircraft, or by people,

animals or things that are dropped, or that fall, from aircraft that are in

flight.

 </dl>
###  DAMAGE BY AIRCRAFT ACT 1999- SECT 4  Definitions 
<dl compact="">

		In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:

 </dl>
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_aircraft_ has the same meaning as in the _Civil Aviation Act 1988_, but does not include model aircraft. _Australian territory_ has the same meaning as in the _Air Navigation Act 1920_. _Commonwealth aircraft_ means an aircraft, other than a Defence Force aircraft, that is:  </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>in the possession or under the control of the Commonwealth or an authority

of the Commonwealth; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>being used wholly or principally for a purpose of the Commonwealth.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_Defence Force aircraft_ means aircraft of any part of the Defence

Force, including any aircraft commanded by a member of that Force in the course

of duties as such a member. _in flight_ has the meaning given by section 5\. _operator_ has the meaning given by section 6\.  </dl></dl>

**

###  DAMAGE BY AIRCRAFT ACT 1999- SECT 5  Meaning of _in flight_ 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>For the purposes of this Act, an aircraft that is lighter than air is

taken to be _in flight_ from the moment when it becomes detached

from the earth's surface until the moment when it becomes again attached to the

earth's surface.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>For the purposes of this Act, a power-driven aircraft that is heavier than air is taken to be _in flight_ from the moment when power is applied for the purpose of take-off until the moment when its landing run ends.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>For the purposes of this Act, an aircraft that is heavier than air but is not power-driven is taken to be _in flight_ from the moment when it becomes airborne (whether or not it is then attached to any other aircraft or machine) until the moment when its landing run ends. </dd> </dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

**

###  DAMAGE BY AIRCRAFT ACT 1999- SECT 6  Meaning of _operator_ 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Subject to subsection (2), if a person uses an aircraft, the person is,

for the purposes of this Act, taken to be the operator of the aircraft.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>If a person authorises the use of an aircraft but retains control of its navigation, then, for the purposes of this Act: </dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the person who is authorised to use the aircraft is not taken to be the

operator of the aircraft; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the person who retains control of its navigation is taken to be the

operator of the aircraft.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

**

###  DAMAGE BY AIRCRAFT ACT 1999- SECT 7  Use of an aircraft by employees 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		If an employee of a person (the _employer_) uses an aircraft in

the course of his or her employment (whether or not the employee is authorised

to do so), then, for the purposes of this Act:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the employee is not taken to use the aircraft; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the employer is taken to use the aircraft.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

**

###  DAMAGE BY AIRCRAFT ACT 1999- SECT 8  Act binds the Crown 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		This Act binds the Crown in each of its capacities.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  DAMAGE BY AIRCRAFT ACT 1999- SECT 9  Application of Act 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>This Act extends to each external Territory.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>This Act does not apply in relation to a Defence Force aircraft.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>This Act applies to acts, omissions, matters and things within Australian territory.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>Subject to subsection (2), this Act applies in relation to the following: </dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>Commonwealth aircraft;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>aircraft owned by a foreign corporation or a trading or financial

corporation (within the meaning of paragraph 51(xx) of the Constitution);</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>aircraft (including foreign aircraft) engaged in:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>international air navigation; or</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>air navigation in relation to trade and commerce with other countries and

among the States; or</dd>

<dt>(iii)</dt><dd>air navigation conducted by a foreign corporation or a trading or

financial corporation (within the meaning of paragraph 51(xx) of the

Constitution); or</dd>

<dt>(iv)</dt><dd>air navigation to or from, or within, the Territories; or</dd>

<dt>(v)</dt><dd>landing at, or taking off from, a place acquired by the Commonwealth for

public purposes.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

**

###  DAMAGE BY AIRCRAFT ACT 1999- SECT 10  Liability for injury, loss etc. 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>This section applies if a person or property on, in or under land or water

suffers personal injury, loss of life, material loss, damage or destruction

caused by:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>an impact with an aircraft that is in flight, or that was in flight

immediately before the impact happened; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>an impact with part of an aircraft that was damaged or destroyed while in

flight; or</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>an impact with a person, animal or thing that dropped or fell from an

aircraft in flight; or</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>something that is a result of an impact of a kind mentioned in paragraph

(a), (b) or (c).

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>If this section applies, the following people are jointly and severally

liable in respect of the injury, loss, damage or destruction:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the operator of the aircraft immediately before the impact happened;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the owner of the aircraft immediately before the impact happened;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>if the operator of the aircraft immediately before the impact happened was

authorised to use the aircraft but did not have the exclusive right to use it

for a period of more than 14 consecutive days&#151;the person who so authorised the

use of the aircraft;</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>if the operator of the aircraft immediately before the impact happened was

using the aircraft without the authority of the person entitled to control its

navigation&#151;the person entitled to control the navigation of the aircraft.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2A)</dt><dd>Subsection&#160;(2) does not apply to a person if, immediately before the

impact happened:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the person was the owner of the aircraft; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the person did not have an active role in the operation of the aircraft;

and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>either:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>there was a lease or other arrangement in force (whether or not with the

owner) under which another person had the exclusive right to use the aircraft;

or</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>another person had the exclusive right to use the aircraft and there was

an agreement in force under which the owner provided financial accommodation in

connection with the aircraft.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>Paragraph (2)(d) does not apply if the person entitled to control the

navigation of the aircraft had taken all reasonable steps to prevent the

unauthorised use of the aircraft.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>If: </dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>an injury, loss, damage or destruction of the kind mentioned in subsection

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>is a result of a collision or interference between 2 or more aircraft in

flight; or</dd></dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>2 or more aircraft jointly cause any such injury, loss, damage or

destruction;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

this section applies in relation to each of the aircraft. 

**

###  DAMAGE BY AIRCRAFT ACT 1999- SECT 11  Recovery of damages without proof of intention, negligence etc. 
**

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		Damages in respect of an injury, loss, damage or destruction of the kind to

which section 10 applies are recoverable in an action in a court of competent

jurisdiction in Australian territory against all or any of the persons who are

jointly and severally liable under that section in respect of the injury, loss,

damage or destruction without proof of intention, negligence or other cause of

action, as if the injury, loss, damage or destruction had been caused by the

wilful act, negligence or default of the defendant or defendants.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  DAMAGE BY AIRCRAFT ACT 1999- SECT 12  Regulations 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		The Governor-General may make regulations prescribing all matters:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>required or permitted by this Act to be prescribed; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>necessary or convenient to be prescribed for carrying out or giving effect

to this Act.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>
