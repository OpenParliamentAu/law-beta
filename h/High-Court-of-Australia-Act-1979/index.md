###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 1  Short title [_see_ Note 1] 
This Act may be cited as the _High Court of Australia Act 1979_.

 
###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 2  Commencement [_see_ Note 1] 
This Act shall come into operation on a date to be fixed by Proclamation.

 
###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 3  Repeal 
The following Acts are repealed:

 
High Court Procedure Act 1903

High Court Procedure Amendment Act 1903

High Court Procedure Act 1915

High Court Procedure Act 1921

High Court Procedure Act 1925

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 5  The High Court 
**

 <dl compact="">

		The High Court is a superior court of record and consists of the Chief

Justice and six other Justices appointed by the Governor- General by commission.

 </dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 6  Consultation with State Attorneys-General on appointment of Justices 
**

<dl compact="">

		Where there is a vacancy in an office of Justice, the Attorney- General

shall, before an appointment is made to the vacant office, consult with the

Attorneys-General of the States in relation to the appointment.

 </dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 7  Qualification of Justices 
**

 <dl compact="">

		A person shall not be appointed as a Justice unless:

 </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>he or she is or has been a Judge of a court created by the Parliament or

of a court of a State or Territory; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>he or she has been enrolled as a barrister or solicitor, as a barrister

and solicitor, or as a legal practitioner, of the High Court or of the Supreme

Court of a State or Territory for not less than 5 years.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 8  Seniority 
**

 <dl compact="">

		The Justices other than the Chief Justice have seniority according to the

dates of their commissions or, where the commissions of two or more Justices

bear the same date, according to the precedence assigned to them by their

commissions.

 </dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 9  Acting Chief Justice 
**

 <dl compact="">

		Whenever:

 </dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the Chief Justice is absent from Australia or is unable or unavailable to

perform the duties of his or her office; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>there is a vacancy in the office of Chief Justice;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

the next senior Justice who is in Australia and is able and willing to do so shall perform the duties, and may exercise the powers, of the Chief Justice, and shall, during any period of absence of the Chief Justice from Australia or during a vacancy in the office of Chief Justice, be designated Acting Chief Justice. 

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 10  Justices not to hold other office 
**

 <dl compact="">

		A Justice is not capable of accepting or holding any other office of profit

within Australia.

 </dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 11  Oath or affirmation of allegiance and of office 
**

 <dl compact="">

		A person who is appointed as a Justice after the commencement of this Act

shall, before proceeding to discharge the duties of his or her office, take an

oath or make an affirmation in accordance with the form in the Schedule.

 </dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 12  Salary and allowances 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Chief Justice and the other Justices shall receive salary and annual

allowances at such respective rates as are fixed from time to time by the

Parliament.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The salary and annual allowance of a Justice accrue from day to day and are payable monthly.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>The Chief Justice and the other Justices shall receive such respective allowances in respect of travelling expenses as are provided by the Parliament. </dd> </dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 13   Appropriation 
**

 <dl compact="">

		The salaries, annual allowances and other allowances referred to in section

12 are payable out of the Consolidated Revenue Fund, which, to the necessary

extent, is appropriated accordingly.

 </dl>

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979<division>Division 2&#151;Seat of the High Court </division> 
**</center>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 14  Seat [_see_ Note 2] 
**

 <dl compact="">

		On and after a date to be fixed by Proclamation, the seat of the High Court

shall be at the seat of Government in the Australian Capital Territory.

 </dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 15  Places of sittings 
**

 <dl compact="">

		Sittings of the High Court shall be held from time to time as required at the

seat of the Court but the Court may sit at other places within Australia and

the external Territories.

 </dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 16  Judgment may be pronounced at any sitting 
**

 <dl compact="">

		The High Court may pronounce judgment at a sitting of the Court held at a

place other than the place at which the relevant proceeding was held by the

Court.

 </dl>

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 17  Administration of the Court 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The High Court shall administer its own affairs subject to, and in

accordance with, this Act.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Court has power for the purposes of the Court to do all things that are necessary or convenient to be done for or in connection with the administration of its affairs and, without limiting the generality of the foregoing, has power: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>to enter into contracts;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>to acquire, hold and dispose of real and personal property;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>to take on hire, to exchange, and to accept on deposit or loan, library

material, and also furnishings, equipment and goods needed for the purposes of

the Court;</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>to control and manage any land or building occupied by the Court and any

adjacent land or building that is declared by Proclamation to be part of the

precincts of the Court;</dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>to accept gifts, devises and bequests made to the Court upon trust and act

as trustee of moneys or other property vested in the Court upon trust; and</dd>

<dt>(f)</dt><dd>to do such other things as it is authorized by this Act to do.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>Any real property, and any personal property (other than money), held by

the Court shall be deemed to be the property of the Commonwealth.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>For the purposes of the _Lands Acquisition Act 1989_, the Court shall be deemed to be an authority incorporated by a law of the Commonwealth.</dd> <dt>(5)</dt><dd>The Court may appoint committees consisting of Justices, or of Justices and other persons, for the purpose of advising the Court in relation to: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the exercise of the powers of the Court under this Act; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the making of Rules of Court.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 18  Chief Executive and Principal Registrar of the Court 
**

 <dl compact="">

		There shall be a Chief Executive and Principal Registrar of the High Court,

who shall be appointed by the Governor-General upon the nomination of the Court.

 </dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 19  Functions and powers of the Chief Executive and Principal Registrar 
**

<dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Chief Executive and Principal Registrar has the function of acting on

behalf of, and assisting, the Justices in the administration of the affairs of

the High Court under section 17 and has such other functions as are conferred

on him or her by this Act or are assigned to him or her by the Court.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Chief Executive and Principal Registrar has power to do all things that are necessary or convenient to be done for or in connection with the performance of his or her functions and, in particular, has power, on behalf of the Court, to give directions in writing for the purpose of regulating the conduct of persons on any land or in any building referred to in paragraph 17(2)(d).</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>Where directions are given by the Chief Executive and Principal Registrar under subsection (2): </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a copy of the directions shall be published in the _Gazette_; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the directions have effect on and from the date of publication.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(4)</dt><dd>The provisions of section 48 (except paragraphs (1)(a) and (b) and

subsection (2)) and sections 48A, 48B and 49 of the _Acts Interpretation Act 1901_ apply, by force of this section, to directions given under subsection

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>of this section in like manner as those provisions apply to regulations.</dd> <dt>(5)</dt><dd>Nothing in the provisions applied by subsection (4) affects the operation of a direction given under subsection (2) at any time before the direction becomes void, or is disallowed, in accordance with those provisions.</dd> <dt>(6)</dt><dd>A person who contravenes or fails to comply with a direction in force under subsection (2) is guilty of an offence. </dd> </dd></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

Penalty:	$100.

<dl compact="">

<dt>(6A)</dt><dd>Subsection&#160;(6) is an offence of strict liability.

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Note:	For _strict liability_, see section&#160;6.1 of

the _Criminal Code_.

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(7)</dt><dd>In the performance of his or her functions and the exercise of his or her

powers the Chief Executive and Principal Registrar shall comply with any

directions given to him or her by the Court.</dd> <dt>(8)</dt><dd>Nothing in this section affects the power of the Court to punish contempts of the Court. </dd> </dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 20  Terms and conditions of appointment 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Chief Executive and Principal Registrar holds office for such period,

not exceeding 5 years, as is specified in the instrument of his or her

appointment, but is eligible for re-appointment.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>The Chief Executive and Principal Registrar holds office on such terms and conditions (if any) in respect of matters not provided for by this Act as are determined by the Court. </dd> </dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 21  Remuneration and allowances 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Chief Executive and Principal Registrar shall be paid such

remuneration as is determined by the Remuneration Tribunal, but, if no

determination of that remuneration by the Tribunal is in operation, he or she

shall be paid such remuneration as is prescribed by Rules of Court.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Chief Executive and Principal Registrar shall be paid such allowances as are prescribed by Rules of Court.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>This section has effect subject to the _Remuneration Tribunal Act 1973_.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>Subsection 7(9) of the _Remuneration Tribunal Act 1973_ has effect in relation to the remuneration and allowances payable to the Chief Executive and Principal Registrar, as if the High Court were a public statutory corporation and the Chief Executive and Principal Registrar held an office in the service of that corporation. </dd> </dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 22  Leave of absence 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Chief Executive and Principal Registrar has such recreation leave

entitlements as are determined by the Remuneration Tribunal.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The High Court may grant the Chief Executive and Principal Registrar leave of absence, other than recreation leave, on such terms and conditions as to remuneration or otherwise as the Court determines. </dd> </dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 23  Resignation 
**

 <dl compact="">

		The Chief Executive and Principal Registrar may resign his or her office by

writing signed by him or her and delivered to the Governor-General.

 </dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 24  Termination of appointment 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Governor-General may terminate the appointment of the Chief Executive

and Principal Registrar by reason of the misbehaviour or the physical or mental

incapacity of the Chief Executive and Principal Registrar.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>If the Chief Executive and Principal Registrar: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>becomes bankrupt, applies to take the benefit of any law for the relief of

bankrupt or insolvent debtors, compounds with his or her creditors or makes an

assignment of his or her remuneration for their benefit;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>is absent from duty, except with leave of absence, for 14&#160;consecutive

days or for 28 days in any 12 months; or</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>without the approval of the Court, engages in paid employment outside the

duties of his or her office;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

the Governor-General shall terminate the appointment of the Chief Executive and Principal Registrar.

<dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>The powers of the Governor-General under subsection (1) shall be exercised

only in accordance with advice that is consistent with recommendations by the

Court.

</dd> </dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 25  Acting Chief Executive and Principal Registrar 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The High Court may appoint a person to act as Chief Executive and

Principal Registrar:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>during a vacancy in the office of Chief Executive and Principal Registrar,

whether or not an appointment has previously been made to the office; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>during any period, or during all periods, when the Chief Executive and

Principal Registrar is absent from duty or from Australia or is, for any other

reason, unable to perform the duties of his or her office;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

but a person appointed to act during a vacancy shall not continue so to act for more than 12 months.

<dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>An appointment of a person under subsection (1) may be expressed to have

effect only in such circumstances as are specified in the instrument of his or

her appointment.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>The Court may: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>determine the terms and conditions of appointment, including remuneration

and allowances, of a person acting as Chief Executive and Principal Registrar;

and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>terminate such an appointment at any time.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(4)</dt><dd>Where a person is acting as Chief Executive and Principal Registrar in

accordance with paragraph (1)(b) and the office of Chief Executive and

Principal Registrar becomes vacant while that person is so acting, then,

subject to subsection (2), that person may continue so to act until the Court

otherwise directs, the vacancy is filled or a period of 12 months from the date

on which the vacancy occurred expires, whichever first happens.</dd> <dt>(5)</dt><dd>The appointment of a person to act as Chief Executive and Principal Registrar ceases to have effect if he or she resigns his or her office by writing signed by him or her and delivered to the Chief Justice.</dd> <dt>(6)</dt><dd>While a person is acting as Chief Executive and Principal Registrar, he or she has and may exercise all the powers of, and shall perform all the functions of, the Chief Executive and Principal Registrar under this Act or any other law.</dd> <dt>(7)</dt><dd>The validity of anything done by a person purporting to act under subsection (1) shall not be called in question on the ground that the occasion for his or her appointment had not arisen, that there is a defect or irregularity in or in connection with his or her appointment, that the appointment had ceased to have effect or that the occasion for him or her to act had not arisen or had passed. </dd> </dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 26  Officers and employees 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Chief Executive and Principal Registrar is to appoint such other

officers of the High Court as the Court considers necessary.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>The Chief Executive and Principal Registrar may engage such employees as the Court considers necessary for the purposes of the Court.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>The terms and conditions of employment (including terms and conditions as to remuneration and allowances) of officers or employees appointed or engaged in accordance with this section are such as are determined by the Court.</dd> <dt>(5)</dt><dd>An appointment under subsection (1) is not a public office for the purposes of the _Remuneration Tribunal Act 1973_.</dd> <dt>(6)</dt><dd>The Chief Executive and Principal Registrar may, on behalf of the Court, arrange with an Agency Head within the meaning of the _Public Service Act 1999_, or with an authority of the Commonwealth, for the services of officers or employees of the Agency or of the authority, as the case may be, to be made available for the purposes of the Court.</dd> <dt>(7)</dt><dd>The Senior Registrar and Deputy Registrars are to perform such duties in respect of proceedings in the Court as are assigned to them by Rules of Court, by an order of the Court or by the Chief Executive and Principal Registrar. </dd> </dl>

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 27  Marshal and Deputy Marshals 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Marshal:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>is charged with the service and execution of all writs, summonses, orders,

warrants, precepts, process and commands of the Court that are directed to him

or her; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>shall take, receive and detain all persons who are committed to his or her

custody by the Court, and shall discharge all such persons when directed by the

Court or otherwise required by law.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>A Deputy Marshal may, subject to any directions of the Marshal, exercise

any power or perform any function of the Marshal.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>The Marshal or a Deputy Marshal may authorize such persons as he or she thinks fit to assist him or her in the exercise of any power or performance of any function by him or her, and any act done by a person so appointed shall be deemed to be done by and under the authority of the Marshal or Deputy Marshal, as the case may be.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>If the Marshal or a Deputy Marshal is a party to a proceeding in the Court, all writs, summonses, orders, warrants, precepts, process and commands in the proceeding that should, in the ordinary course, be directed to the Marshal or Deputy Marshal shall be directed to such disinterested person as the Court appoints, and the person so appointed may execute and return them.</dd> <dt>(5)</dt><dd>The Minister may arrange with the appropriate Minister of a State or of the Northern Territory for an officer or officers of that State or Territory to perform the functions of a Deputy Marshal in that State or Territory.</dd> <dt>(6)</dt><dd>A copy of an arrangement made under subsection (5) shall be published in the _Gazette_. </dd> </dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 28  Certain officers may administer oaths and affirmations 
**

 <dl compact="">

		The Chief Executive and Principal Registrar, the Senior Registrar, the Deputy

Registrars and any other persons authorized by Rules of Court may administer

oaths and affirmations for the purposes of any proceedings in the Court.

 </dl>

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 30  Registry 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>There shall be a Registry of the High Court, which shall be at the seat of

the Court.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Registry shall be under the control of the Chief Executive and Principal Registrar.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>There shall be an office of the Registry at the seat of the Court, at the capital city of each State, at Darwin in the Northern Territory and at such other places as the Court deems necessary.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>The Minister may arrange with the appropriate Minister of a State or of the Northern Territory for an officer or officers of that State or Territory to perform on behalf of the Court at any office in that State or Territory of the Registry of the Court all or any of the functions referred to in subsection (6).</dd> <dt>(5)</dt><dd>The Chief Justice may arrange with the Chief Justice of the Federal Court of Australia for an officer or officers of the Federal Court of Australia to perform on behalf of the High Court at an office or offices of the Registry of the High Court referred to in the arrangement all or any of the functions referred to in subsection&#160;(6).</dd> <dt>(6)</dt><dd>The functions to which an arrangement under subsection (4) or (5) may relate are: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the receipt of documents to be lodged with or filed in the High Court;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the signing and issuing of writs, commissions and process;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>the administration of oaths and affirmations for the purposes of any

proceedings in the High Court; and</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>such other functions as are permitted by Rules of Court to be performed in

pursuance of such an arrangement.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(7)</dt><dd>Documents received at any office of the Registry for lodgment with or

filing in the Court shall be deemed to be received at the Registry.</dd> <dt>(8)</dt><dd>A copy of an arrangement made under subsection (4) or (5) shall be published in the _Gazette._</dd> (9)	Where an office of the Registry is established at a place not specifically mentioned in subsection (3), a notice stating that an office of the Registry has been established at that place shall be published in the Gazette.  </dl>

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 31  Transfer of proceedings 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Subject to section 80 of the Constitution, the High Court may, at any

stage of a proceeding, direct that the proceeding or a part of the proceeding

be held or continued at a place specified in the order, subject to such

conditions (if any) as the Court imposes.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Rules of Court may make provision for the transmission of documents between offices of the Registry of the Court, including transmission by telegraph, telex or any similar means of communication. </dd> </dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 32  Seals 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The High Court shall have a seal on which are inscribed the words "The

Seal of the High Court of Australia".</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>A duplicate of the seal of the Court shall be kept at each office of the Registry of the Court.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>Such other seals as are required for the business of the Court shall be kept and used at each office of the Registry and shall be in such form and kept in such custody as the Chief Justice directs.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>A document, a copy of a document or an exemplification purporting to be sealed with the seal of the High Court or a duplicate of that seal, or with a seal referred to in subsection (3), is receivable in evidence without further proof of the seal. </dd> </dl>

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 34  Date of process 
**

 <dl compact="">

		All writs, commissions and process issued from the High Court shall be dated

as of the day on which they are issued.

 </dl>

**</center>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 35  Moneys appropriated for the Court 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>There are payable to the High Court such moneys as are appropriated by the

Parliament for the purposes of the Court.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Minister for Finance may give directions as to the amounts in&#160;which, and the times at which, moneys referred to in subsection&#160;(1) are to be paid to the Court. </dd> </dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 36  Estimates 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The High Court shall prepare estimates, in such form as the Minister

approves, of receipts and expenditure in respect of the administration of the

affairs of the Court under section 17 for each financial year and, if so

requested by the Minister, for any other period, and shall submit estimates so

prepared to the Minister not later than such date as the Minister specifies.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Moneys paid to the Court under section 35 shall not be expended otherwise than in accordance with the estimates of expenditure approved by the Minister. </dd> </dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 37  Application of moneys appropriated for the Court 
**

 <dl compact="">

		Moneys paid to the High Court under section 35 shall be applied only:

 </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>in payment or discharge of the costs and expenses of the administration of

the affairs of the Court under section 17; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>in payment of any remuneration and allowances payable under this Act to

any person other than a Justice.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 38  Trust moneys 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>A separate account or separate accounts shall be kept in the books of the

High Court in respect of moneys held by the Court upon trust.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Any moneys or other property held by the Court upon trust shall be dealt with in accordance with the powers and duties of the Court as trustee. </dd> </dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 39  Other moneys 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Any moneys held by the Court other than:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>moneys held by the Court upon trust; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>moneys paid to the Court under section 35;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

may be expended for the purposes of the Court.

<dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>Moneys to which subsection (1) applies that are not immediately required

to be expended may be invested:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>on deposit with an ADI;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>in securities of the Commonwealth; or</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>in any other manner approved by the Treasurer.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 40  Limitation on contracts 
**

 <dl compact="">

		The High Court shall not, except with the approval of the Minister, enter

into a contract under which the Court is to pay or to receive an amount

exceeding $250,000 or, if a higher amount is prescribed, that higher amount.

 </dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 41  Accounts with ADIs 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Chief Executive and Principal Registrar may, on behalf of and in the

name of the High Court, open and maintain an account or accounts with an ADI or

ADIs and shall maintain at all times at least one such account.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Chief Executive and Principal Registrar shall pay all moneys received by the Court into an account referred to in this section. </dd> </dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 42  Proper accounts to be kept 
**

 <dl compact="">

		The High Court shall cause to be kept proper accounts and records of the

transactions and affairs relating to the administration of the affairs of the

Court under section 17 and shall do all things necessary to ensure that all

payments out of the moneys held by the Court are correctly made and properly

authorized and that adequate control is maintained over the assets held by, or

in the custody of, the Court and over the incurring of liabilities by the Court.

 </dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 43  Audit 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Auditor-General shall inspect and audit the accounts and records of

financial transactions relating to the administration of the affairs of the

High Court under section 17 and the records relating to assets held by, or in

the custody of, the Court and shall forthwith draw the attention of the

Minister to any irregularity disclosed by the inspection and audit that, in the

opinion of the Auditor-General, is of sufficient importance to justify his or

her so doing.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Auditor-General may, at his or her discretion, dispense with all or any part of the detailed inspection and audit of any accounts or records referred to in subsection (1).</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>The Auditor-General shall, at least once in each financial year, report to the Minister the results of the inspection and audit carried out under subsection (1).</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>The Auditor-General or a person authorized by him or her is entitled at all reasonable times to full and free access to all accounts and records maintained by the Court relating directly or indirectly to the receipt or payment of moneys, or to the acquisition, receipt, custody or disposal of assets, by the Court.</dd> <dt>(5)</dt><dd>The Auditor-General or a person authorized by him or her may make copies of, or take extracts from, any such accounts and records.</dd> <dt>(6)</dt><dd>The Auditor-General or a person authorized by him or her may require any person to furnish him or her with such information in the possession of the person or to which the person has access as the Auditor-General or authorized person considers necessary for the purposes of the functions of the Auditor-General under this Act, and the person shall comply with the requirement.</dd> <dt>(7)</dt><dd>A person who contravenes subsection (6) is guilty of an offence and is punishable, upon conviction, by a fine not exceeding $200.</dd> <dt>(8)</dt><dd>Subsection&#160;(7) is an offence of strict liability. </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Note:	For _strict liability_, see section&#160;6.1 of

the _Criminal Code_.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 44  Exemption from taxation 
**

 <dl compact="">

		The High Court is not subject to taxation under any law of the Commonwealth

or of a State or Territory.

 </dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 45  Proceedings in respect of administration of the Court 
**

 <dl compact="">

		Any judicial or other proceedings relating to matters arising out of&#160;the

administration of the affairs of the High Court under section&#160;17,

including any proceedings relating to matters arising out of the performance of

the functions or the exercise of the powers of the Chief Executive and

Principal Registrar under this Act, may be instituted by or against the

Commonwealth, as the case requires.

 </dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 46  Exercise of powers of the Court in administrative matters 
**

<dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Subject to this section, the powers of the High Court under this Act may

be exercised by the Justices or by a majority of them.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Court may, either generally or as otherwise provided by the instrument of delegation, by writing signed by the Justices or a majority of them, delegate all or any of the powers of the Court under this Act (except this power of delegation) to any one or more of the Justices.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>A power so delegated, when exercised by the delegate or delegates, shall, for the purposes of this Act, be deemed to have been exercised by the Court.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>A delegation under this section does not prevent the exercise of a power by the Court.</dd> <dt>(5)</dt><dd>A decision of the Court, or of a delegate or delegates of the Court, in exercise of a power under this Act may be made known by the Chief Justice, who may give any directions or take any other action necessary to give effect to the decision. </dd> </dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 47  Annual reports and financial statements 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The High Court shall, as soon as practicable after 30 June in each year,

prepare and submit to the Minister a report relating to the administration of

the affairs of the Court under section 17 during the year that ended on that 30

June, together with financial statements in respect of that year in such form

as the Minister for Finance approves.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Before submitting financial statements to the Minister under subsection (1), the Court shall submit them to the Auditor-General, who shall report to the Minister: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>whether, in his or her opinion, the statements are based on proper

accounts and records;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>whether the statements are in agreement with the accounts and records;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>whether, in his or her opinion, the receipt, expenditure and investment of

moneys, and the acquisition and disposal of assets, by the Court during the

year have been in accordance with this Act; and</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>as to such other matters arising out of the statements as the

Auditor-General considers should be reported to the Minister.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>The Minister shall cause a copy of the report and financial statements of

the Court together with a copy of the report of the Auditor-General to be laid

before each House of the Parliament within 15 sitting days of that House after

their receipt by the Minister.

</dd> </dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 48  Rules of Court 
**

 <dl compact="">

		The power of the Justices or of a majority of them to make Rules of Court

under section 86 of the _Judiciary Act 1903_ extends to making any Rules

of Court required or permitted by this Act to be made or necessary or

convenient to be made for carrying into effect the provisions of this Act.

 </dl>

**

###  HIGH COURT OF AUSTRALIA ACT 1979- SECT 49  Regulations 
**

 <dl compact="">

		The Governor-General may make regulations prescribing matters:

 </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>required or permitted by this Act to be prescribed; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>necessary or convenient to be prescribed for carrying out or giving effect

to this Act.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>
