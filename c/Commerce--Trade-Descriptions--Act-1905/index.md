### <name>COMMERCE (TRADE DESCRIPTIONS) ACT 1905-1973 - SECT. 1\. Short title. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>                       COMMERCE  (TRADE  DESCRIPTIONS)  ACT<lf> <lf>                                    1905-1973<lf> <lf>                An Act relating to Commerce with other Countries.<lf> Heading substituted by No. 216, 1973, s. 3.<lf>                               PART  I-PRELIMINARY<lf> Short title and commencement.<lf> Short title amended; No. 32, 1918, s. 2.<lf>   1\. This Act may be cited as the Commerce (Trade Descriptions) Act 1905-1973 and shall commence on a day to be fixed by proclamation not being earlier than six months after the passing of this Act.*<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>

### <name>COMMERCE (TRADE DESCRIPTIONS) ACT 1905-1973 - SECT. 2\. Incorporation. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   2\. This Act shall be incorporated and read as one with the Customs Act 1901.<lf> </lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>COMMERCE (TRADE DESCRIPTIONS) ACT 1905-1973 - SECT. 3\. Definitions. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Amended by No. 15, 1930, s. 2; No. 13, 1933, ss. 2 and 3; No. 80, 1950, s. 3; and No. 216, 1973, s. 3.<lf>   3\. In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears-<lf> <lf>   ''Officer'' means an officer of Customs, and, in relation to exports, also includes an officer of the Department of Primary Industry, or any employee of the Department of Primary Industry, or any officer of a State engaged in the performance or execution for the Government of the Commonwealth of any work connected with the inspection or examination of goods in pursuance of this Act. <lf> <lf>   ''The Secretary'' means the Secretary to the Department of Primary Industry.<lf> <lf>   ''Trade description,'' in relation to any goods, means any description, statement, indication, or suggestion, direct or indirect-<lf> <lf>      (a)  as to the nature, number, quantity, quality, purity, class, grade, measure, gauge, size, or weight of the goods; or<lf> <lf>      (b)  as to the country or place in or at which the goods were made or produced; or<lf> <lf>      (c)  as to the manufacturer or producer of the goods or the person by whom they were selected, packed, or in any way prepared for the market; or<lf> <lf>      (d)  as to the mode of manufacturing, producing, selecting, packing, or otherwise preparing the goods; or<lf> <lf>      (e)  as to the material or ingredients of which the goods are composed, or from which they are derived; or<lf> <lf>      (f)  as to the goods being the subject of an existing patent, privilege, or copyright,<lf> <lf>     and includes a Customs entry relating to goods; and any mark which according to the custom of the trade or common repute is commonly taken to be an indication of any of the above matters shall be deemed to be a trade description within the meaning of this Act.<lf> <lf>   ''False trade description'' means a trade description which, by reason of anything contained therein or omitted therefrom, is false or likely to mislead in a material respect as regards the goods to which it is applied, and includes every alteration of a trade description, whether by way of addition, effacement, or otherwise, which makes the description false or likely to mislead in a material respect.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>COMMERCE (TRADE DESCRIPTIONS) ACT 1905-1973 - SECT. 4\. Application of trade description. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Sub-section (1) amended by No. 15, 1930, s. 3.<lf>   4\. (1) A false trade description shall be deemed to be applied to goods if-<lf> <lf>   (a)  it is applied to the goods themselves; or<lf> <lf>   (b)  it is applied to any covering, label, reel, or thing used in connexion with the goods; or<lf> <lf>   (c)  it is used in any manner likely to lead to the belief that it describes or designates the goods.<lf> Inserted by No. 15, 1930, s. 3\. 

  (1A) A trade description which is required by the regulations to be applied to any goods shall be deemed to be applied to the goods if-<lf> <lf>   (a)  it is applied to the goods themselves; or<lf> <lf>   (b)  it is used in relation to the goods in the manner prescribed.<lf> <p>  (2) ''Covering'' includes any stopper, glass, bottle, vessel, box, capsule, case, frame, or wrapper; and ''label'' includes any band or ticket.<lf> </lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>COMMERCE (TRADE DESCRIPTIONS) ACT 1905-1973 - SECT. 5\. Inspection of imports and exports. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>                  PART  II-INSPECTION  OF  IMPORTS  AND EXPORTS<lf> Heading substituted by No. 216, 1973, s. 3.<lf> Sub-section (1) amended by No. 15, 1930, s. 4.<lf>   5\. (1) An officer may inspect and examine all prescribed goods which are imported, or which are entered for export or brought for export to any wharf or place or are in course of manufacture or preparation for export.<lf> 

  (2) The officer may where practicable take samples of any goods inspected by him pursuant to this section, and the samples so taken shall be dealt with as prescribed.<lf> <p>  (3) For the purposes of this section an officer may enter any ship, wharf or place, and may open any packages, and may do all things necessary to enable him to carry out his powers and duties under this section.<lf> </lf></p></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>COMMERCE (TRADE DESCRIPTIONS) ACT 1905-1973 - SECT. 6\. Notice of intention to export. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Amended by No. 15, 1930, s. 5; No. 13, 1933, s. 2; No. 80, 1950, s. 3; No. 93, 1966, s. 3; and No. 216, 1973, s. 3.<lf>   6\. Every person who intends to export any goods of a kind or class required under this Act to be inspected or examined by an officer, shall, if required to do so by regulation, before the goods are shipped, give notice, in accordance with the regulations, to the Customs, or to the Department of Primary Industry, of his intention to export the goods and of the place where the goods may be inspected.<lf> <lf>   Penalty: Forty dollars.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>COMMERCE (TRADE DESCRIPTIONS) ACT 1905-1973 - SECT. 7\. Prohibition of imports not bearing prescribed trade description. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>                                PART  III-IMPORTS<lf> Heading substituted by No. 216, 1973, s 3.<lf> Sub-section (1) amended by No. 15, 1930, s. 6.<lf>   7\. (1) The regulations may prohibit the importation or introduction into Australia of any goods unless there is applied to them a trade description of such character, relating to such matters, and applied in such manner, as is prescribed.<lf> Amended by No. 216, 1973, s. 3\. 

  (2) All goods imported in contravention of any regulation under this section may be detained by the Collector and may by direction of the Minister be seized as forfeited to the Crown.<lf> <p>  (3) Subject to the regulations, the Comptroller-General, or on appeal from him the Minister, may in any case, and if in his opinion the contravention has not occurred either knowingly or negligently shall permit any goods which are liable to be or have been seized as forfeited under this section to be delivered to the owner or importer upon security being given to the satisfaction of the Comptroller-General that the prescribed trade description will be applied to the goods or that they will be forthwith exported.<lf> <p>  (4) No regulations under this section shall take effect until after the expiration of not less than three months from notification in the Gazette.<lf> </lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>COMMERCE (TRADE DESCRIPTIONS) ACT 1905-1973 - SECT. 8\. Imported goods found in Australia without prescribed trade description. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Amended by No. 15, 1930, s. 7.<lf>   8\. All imported goods to which a trade description is by the regulations required to be applied, and which are found in Australia without the prescribed trade description, shall until the contrary is proved be deemed, subject to the regulations, to have been imported in contravention of the regulations.<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>COMMERCE (TRADE DESCRIPTIONS) ACT 1905-1973 - SECT. 9\. Importation of falsely marked goods. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Substituted by No. 216, 1973, s. 3.<lf>   9\. (1) A person shall not import any goods to which a false trade description is applied.<lf> <lf>   Penalty: Two hundred dollars.<lf> 

  (2) In a prosecution for an offence against sub-section (1) it is a defence if the defendant proves that he did not knowingly import the goods in contravention of that sub-section.<lf> </lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>COMMERCE (TRADE DESCRIPTIONS) ACT 1905-1973 - SECT. 9A. Imported goods found in Australia with false trade description. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Inserted by No. 15, 1930, s. 8.<lf>   9A. All imported goods found in Australia which bear a false trade description shall, until the contrary is proved, be deemed to have been imported in contravention of this Act.<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>COMMERCE (TRADE DESCRIPTIONS) ACT 1905-1973 - SECT. 10\. Forfeiture of falsely marked goods. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Amended by No. 216, 1973, s. 3.<lf>   10\. All goods to which any false trade description is applied are hereby prohibited to be imported, and shall if imported be forfeited to the Crown.<lf> <lf>   Provided that the Comptroller-General, or on appeal from him the Minister, may, if he is satisfied that any goods which have been seized as forfeited under this section were not knowingly imported in contravention of this Act, permit the importer to correct the false trade description, and may, when the correction has been made to his satisfaction, order the release of the goods, subject to the payment by the importer to the Customs of the expenses of the seizure, and thereupon the forfeiture shall be remitted.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>COMMERCE (TRADE DESCRIPTIONS) ACT 1905-1973 - SECT. 11\. Heading substituted by No. 216, 1973, s. 3\. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>                                 PART  IV-EXPORTS<lf> Prohibition of exports not bearing the prescribed trade description.<lf> Sub-section (1) amended by No. 15, 1930, s. 9.<lf>   11\. (1) The regulations may prohibit the exportation of any goods, unless there is applied to them a trade description of such character, relating to such matters, and applied in such manner, as is prescribed.<lf> Amended by No. 15, 1930, s. 9; No. 13, 1933, s. 2; No. 80, 1950, s. 3; and No. 216, 1973, s. 3\. 

  (2) All such goods to which the prescribed trade description is not applied, which are exported or entered for export or put on board any ship or boat for export or brought to any wharf or place for export, may be detained by the Collector or the Secretary, and may by direction of the Minister of State for Primary Industry be seized as forfeited to the Crown.<lf> Amended by No. 15, 1930, s. 9; No. 13, 1933, s. 2; No. 80, 1950, s. 3; and No. 216, 1973, s. 3\. <p>  (3) Subject to the regulations the Secretary, or on appeal from him the Minister of State for Primary Industry, may in any case, and if in his opinion the contravention has not occurred either knowingly or negligently shall permit any goods which are liable to be or have been seized as forfeited under this section to be delivered to the owner or exporter, upon security being given to the satisfaction of the Secretary that the goods shall not be exported in contravention of the regulations.<lf> </lf></p></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>COMMERCE (TRADE DESCRIPTIONS) ACT 1905-1973 - SECT. 12\. Penalty for applying false trade description to exports. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Amended by No. 93, 1966, s. 3.<lf>   12\. No person shall-<lf> <lf>   (a)  knowingly apply any false trade description to any goods intended or entered for export or put on any ship or boat for export, or brought to any wharf or place for the purpose of export; or<lf> <lf>   (b)  knowingly export or enter for export or put on any ship or boat for export any goods to which a false trade description is applied.<lf> <lf>   Penalty: Two hundred dollars.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>COMMERCE (TRADE DESCRIPTIONS) ACT 1905-1973 - SECT. 13\. Exportation of falsely marked goods. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Amended by No. 15, 1930, s. 10; No. 13, 1933, s. 2; No. 80, 1950, s. 3; and No. 216, 1973, s. 3.<lf>   13\. All goods to which any false trade description is applied are hereby prohibited to be exported, and shall, if exported or entered for export or put on any ship or boat for export, or brought to any wharf or place for the purpose of export, be forfeited to the Crown.<lf> <lf>   Provided that the Secretary, or on appeal from him the Minister of State for Primary Industry, may, if he is satisfied that the owner of any goods, which have been seized as forfeited under this section did not knowingly act in contravention of this Act, permit the correction of the false trade description, and may, when the correction has been made to his satisfaction, order the release of the goods, subject to the payment by the exporter of the expenses of the seizure, and thereupon the forfeiture shall be remitted.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>COMMERCE (TRADE DESCRIPTIONS) ACT 1905-1973 - SECT. 14\. Marking of goods for export. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   14\. Any goods intended for export which have been inspected in pursuance of this Act may in manner prescribed be marked with the prescribed trade description.<lf> Section 15 repealed by No. 15, 1930, s. 11.<lf>                          *   *   *   *   *   *   *   *<lf> </lf></lf></lf></sect>
