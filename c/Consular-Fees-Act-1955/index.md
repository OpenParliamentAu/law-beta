###  CONSULAR FEES ACT 1955- SECT 1  Short title [_see_ Note 1] 
<dl compact="">

		This Act may be cited as the _Consular Fees Act 1955_.

 </dl>
###  CONSULAR FEES ACT 1955- SECT 2  Interpretation 
<dl compact="">

		In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:

 </dl>
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_Australian Consular Officer_ means a person appointed to hold or

act in any of the following offices of the Commonwealth in a country or place

outside Australia:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>Consul-General;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>Consul;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>Vice-Consul;</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>Trade Representative; and</dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>Consular Agent.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_Australian Diplomatic Officer_ means a person appointed to hold or

act in any of the following offices of the Commonwealth in a country or place

outside Australia:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>Ambassador;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>High Commissioner;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>Minister;</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>Head of a Mission;</dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>Commissioner;</dd>

<dt>(f)</dt><dd>Charg&eacute; d'Affaires; and</dd>

<dt>(g)</dt><dd>Counsellor, Secretary or Attach&eacute; at an Embassy, High Commissioner's

office, Legation or other post.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_consular act_ means an act specified in the regulations as being a

consular act for the purposes of this Act.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  CONSULAR FEES ACT 1955- SECT 3  Fees may be prescribed for consular acts 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		The regulations may provide for the imposition of fees, to be collected on

behalf of the Commonwealth, for the performance, whether in Australia or

elsewhere, of consular acts by:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>an Australian Diplomatic Officer or an Australian Consular Officer; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the person holding or acting in the office of Secretary to the Department

or an officer of the Department acting with the authority of the Secretary;

or</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>an employee of the Commonwealth authorised, in writing, by the Secretary

to the Department; or</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>an employee of the Australian Trade Commission authorised, in writing, by

the Secretary to the Department.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

**

###  CONSULAR FEES ACT 1955- SECT 4  List of fees to be displayed 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		An Australian Diplomatic Officer or an Australian Consular Officer who

performs consular acts shall cause to be displayed in his office, in a position

where it may readily be inspected by persons seeking the performance of

consular acts, a list of the consular acts for which fees are chargeable under

this Act, and of other acts that may be performed by him for which fees are

chargeable under any other Act, showing the respective fees chargeable.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  CONSULAR FEES ACT 1955- SECT 5  Saving of fees under other Acts 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		A fee under this Act is not chargeable in respect of an act for which a fee

is chargeable under another Act (whether passed before or after the

commencement of this Act).

 </dl></dl>

**

###  CONSULAR FEES ACT 1955- SECT 6  Regulations 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		The Governor-General may make regulations, not inconsistent with this Act,

prescribing all matters required or permitted by this Act to be prescribed, or

necessary or convenient to be prescribed for carrying out or giving effect to

this Act, and, in particular, providing that the prescribed fees are not

payable in such cases as are prescribed

 </dl></dl>
