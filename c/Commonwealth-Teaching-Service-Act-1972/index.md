</b>**

(1) In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears-<lf> <lf>   ''advanced education institution '' means an institution in Australia that, under an approval of the Commissioner, is to be treated as an advanced education institution for the purposes of Part IV;<lf> <lf>   ''Commonwealth school '' means a school conducted by the Commonwealth, by an authority of the Commonwealth or by the Administration of a Territory and includes a school conducted in Papua New Guinea by a prescribed institution;<lf> <lf>   '' full-time student '' means a student included in a class of students that, under an approval of the Commissioner, is to be treated as a class of students engaged in full-time study for the purposes of Part IV;<lf> <lf>   '' member of the Service '' means a person appointed as an officer, or engaged as a temporary employee, in accordance with Part III;<lf> <lf>   '' part-time student '' means a student other than a full-time student;<lf> <lf>   '' scholarship '' means a Commonwealth Teaching Service Scholarship provided for by Part IV;<lf> <lf>   '' teaching duties '' includes-<lf> <lf>      (a)  the administration of a school; and<lf> <lf>      (b)  other duties connected with the teaching or other services provided at a school, being duties for the performance of which experience as a teacher is an advantage;<lf> <lf>   '' the Commissioner '' means the Commonwealth Teaching Service Commissioner;<lf> <lf>   '' the Service '' means the Commonwealth Teaching Service;<lf> <lf>   '' University '' means a University in Australia, and includes-<lf> <lf>      (a) a University College in Australia; or<lf> <lf>      (b) an institution that, under an approval of the Commissioner, is to be treated as a University for the purposes of Part IV.<lf> Added by No. 98, 1973, s. 4\. 

  (2) In this Act, a reference to teaching duties in a school includes-<lf> <lf>   (a) the inspection or supervision of persons engaged in teaching duties; and<lf> <lf>   (b)  other duties connected with the teaching or other services provided by an authority conducting two or more schools, being duties for the performance of which experience as a teacher is an advantage.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>

</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>           PART  II-THE  COMMONWEALTH  TEACHING  SERVICE  COMMISSIONER<lf>   6\. There shall be a Commonwealth Teaching Service Commissioner, who shall be appointed by the Governor-General.<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>COMMONWEALTH TEACHING SERVICE ACT 1972 - SECT. 7\. Tenure of office. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   7\. (1) Subject to this Act, the Commissioner holds office for such period, not exceeding seven years, as is specified in the instrument of his appointment and on such terms and conditions as the Governor-General determines, but is eligible for re-appointment.<lf> 

  (2) A person who has attained the age of sixty-five years shall not be appointed or re-appointed as the Commissioner, and a person shall not be appointed or re-appointed as the Commissioner for a period that extends beyond the date on which he will attain the age of sixty-five years.<lf> </lf>
</lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>COMMONWEALTH TEACHING SERVICE ACT 1972 - SECT. 8\. Remuneration and allowances. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Sub-section (1) amended by No. 98, 1973, s. 8.<lf>   8\. (1) The Commissioner shall be paid salary at such rate as the Parliament fixes.*<lf> 

  (2) The Commissioner shall be paid such allowances (other than annual allowances) as are prescribed.<lf> </lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>COMMONWEALTH TEACHING SERVICE ACT 1972 - SECT. 9\. Leave of absence. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   9\. The Minister may grant leave of absence to the Commissioner upon such terms and conditions as to remuneration or otherwise as the Minister determines.<lf> </lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>COMMONWEALTH TEACHING SERVICE ACT 1972 - SECT. 10\. Resignation. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   10\. The Commissioner may resign his office by writing under his hand delivered to the Governor-General but the resignation does not have effect until it is accepted by the Governor-General.<lf> </lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>COMMONWEALTH TEACHING SERVICE ACT 1972 - SECT. 11\. Commissioner not to undertake other work. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   11\. (1) The Commissioner shall not engage in paid employment outside the duties of his office except with the approval of the Minister.<lf> 

  (2) The Minister shall not give an approval for the purposes of the last preceding sub-section unless he is satisfied that the paid employment will not interfere with the performance of the duties of the Commissioner under this Act. <lf> </lf>
</lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>COMMONWEALTH TEACHING SERVICE ACT 1972 - SECT. 12\. Termination of employment. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   12\. (1) The Governor-General may retire the Commissioner on the ground of invalidity.<lf> 

  (2) The Governor-General shall terminate the appointment of the Commissioner if the Commissioner-<lf> <lf>   (a)  absents himself from duty for fourteen consecutive days, or for twenty-eight days in any twelve months, except on leave granted by the Minister; <lf> <lf>   (b)  is guilty of misbehaviour; or<lf> <lf>   (c)  becomes bankrupt, applies to take the benefit of any law for the relief of bankrupt or insolvent debtors, compounds with his creditors or makes an assignment of his remuneration for their benefit.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>COMMONWEALTH TEACHING SERVICE ACT 1972 - SECT. 13\. Application of Superannuation Act. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   13\. For the purposes of sub-sections (3A) and (4) of section 4 of the Superannuation Act 1922-1971, the Commissioner shall be deemed to be required, by the terms of his appointment, to give the whole of his time to the duties of his office.<lf> </lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>COMMONWEALTH TEACHING SERVICE ACT 1972 - SECT. 14\. Oath or affirmation. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Amended by No. 98, 1973, s. 8.<lf>   14\. The Commissioner shall not perform the duties of his office unless he has made and subscribed, before a justice of the peace or a commissioner for taking affidavits, an oath or affirmation in accordance with the form of oath or affirmation in the First Schedule.<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>COMMONWEALTH TEACHING SERVICE ACT 1972 - SECT. 15\. Acting appointments. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   15\. (1) Where the person holding the office of Commonwealth Teaching Service Commissioner is, or is expected to be, absent from duty or there is a vacancy in that office, the Governor-General may appoint a person to act in that office during the absence or until the filling of the vacancy, as the case may be, but a person so appointed by reason of a vacancy in the office of Commissioner shall not act in that office for a continuous period of more than six months.<lf> 

  (2) Where a person has been appointed under this section to act in the office of Commissioner otherwise than during a vacancy in that office and the person who holds that office ceases to hold office during the period of appointment, the appointment shall be deemed to continue until it is terminated by the Governor-General or until the expiraton of six months from the date on which the Commissioner ceases to hold office, whichever first happens.<lf> <p>  (3) The Governor-General may at any time terminate an appointment under this section.<lf> Amended by No. 98, 1973, s. 8\. <p>  (4) Sections 9, 10 and 14 apply in relation to a person appointed under this section to act in the office of Commissioner in like manner as they apply in relation to the Commissioner.<lf> <p>  (5) Subject to this section, a person appointed under this section to act in the office of Commissioner holds office on such terms and conditions as the Governor-General determines.<lf> <p>  (6) A person appointed under this section to act in the office of Commissioner has all the functions, powers and duties of the Commissioner under this Act.<lf> </lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</lf></sect>

### <name>COMMONWEALTH TEACHING SERVICE ACT 1972 - SECT. 16\. Function, duties and powers of Commissioner. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   16\. (1) The function of the Commissioner is to make persons available for the performance of teaching duties in Commonwealth schools and other schools in accordance with this section.<lf> Amended by No. 216, 1973, s. 3\. 

  (2) The Commissioner shall make available to each authority conducting a Commonwealth school or Commonwealth schools in the Northern Territory, for the performance of teaching duties in that school or those schools, persons in such number, and having such qualifications, as will, in the opinion of the authority, ensure the efficient operation of that school or those schools.<lf> Amended by No. 216, 1973, s. 3\. <p>  (3) The Commissioner may, and, if so directed by the Minister, shall-<lf> <lf>   (a)  make a person or persons available to an authority conducting a Commonwealth school or Commonwealth schools in a Territory other than the Northern Territory for the performance of teaching duties in that school or those schools; and<lf> <lf>   (b)  make a person or persons available to the Government of a country other than Australia or to an authority of such a Government for the performance of teaching duties in a school or schools conducted in that country by that Government or authority.<lf> <p>  (4) Where a State or an authority of a State has made, or proposes to make, a person or persons available to an authority or authorities conducting a Commonwealth school or Commonwealth schools for the performance of teaching duties in that school or those schools, the Commissioner may, and, if so directed by the Minister, shall, make a person or persons available to that State or to that authority of that State for the performance of teaching duties in a school or schools conducted by that State or by that authority of that State.<lf> <p>  (5) Before making a person or persons available in accordance with the preceding provisions of this section to an authority or authorities conducting a school or schools in a Territory, the Commissioner shall consult that authority or those authorities as to the persons required for the efficient operation of the school or schools.<lf> <p>  (6) The Commissioner shall not direct an officer to perform duties in a place outside Australia and the Territories unless the officer consents to perform duties in that place.<lf> <p>  (7) Where the Commissioner makes a person available to an authority that conducts two or more schools, the authority may make use of the services of the person in whichever of those schools the authority thinks fit.<lf> <p>  (8) The Commissioner has power to do all things necessary or convenient to be done for or in connexion with the performance of his function under this section.<lf> <p>  (9) In this section, a reference to the authority conducting a school or schools shall-<lf> <lf>   (a)  in relation to a school or schools conducted by the Commonwealth-be read as a reference to the Department of State responsible for the conduct of the school or schools; and<lf> <lf>   (b)  in relation to a school or schools conducted by the Administration of a Territory or conducted in a Territory by a prescribed institution-be read as a reference to the Administration of that Territory.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf>
</lf></sect>

### <name>COMMONWEALTH TEACHING SERVICE ACT 1972 - SECT. 17\. Delegation. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   17\. (1) The Commissioner may, by instrument in writing under his hand, delegate to an officer of, or a person employed by, the Commonwealth, an authority of the Commonwealth or the Administration of a Territory, either generally or otherwise as provided by the instrument of delegation, all or any of his powers under this Act except this power of delegation.<lf> 

  (2) A power so delegated may be exercised by the delegate in accordance with the instrument of delegation and this Act has effect in relation to the exercise of the power by the delegate as if a reference in this Act to the Commissioner were a reference to the delegate.<lf> <p>  (3) A delegation under this section is revocable in writing at will and does not prevent the exercise of a power by the Commissioner.<lf> </lf></p></lf>
</lf></sect>

### <name>COMMONWEALTH TEACHING SERVICE ACT 1972 - SECT. 20\. Officers. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>                        Division 2-Officers and Employees<lf>   20\. (1) The Commissioner shall, on behalf of the Commonwealth, appoint as officers such number of persons as he thinks necessary for the purposes of this Act.<lf> Amended by No. 98, 1973, s. 8\. 

  (2) Subject to the next succeeding sub-section, a person shall not be appointed as an officer unless-<lf> <lf>   (a)  he is a British subject;<lf> <lf>   (b)  he possesses such educational qualifications, and such other qualifications (if any), as are determined by the Commissioner;<lf> <lf>   (c)  the Commissioner is satisfied as to his health and physical fitness;<lf> <lf>   (d)  the Commissioner is satisfied that he is a fit and proper person to be an officer; and<lf> <lf>   (e)  he makes and subscribes, before the Commissioner, a person appointed by the Commissioner or a justice of the peace, an oath or affirmation of allegiance in accordance with the form of oath or affirmation in the Second Schedule.<lf> <p>  (3) The Commissioner may, with the approval of the Minister, appoint as an officer a person who is not a British subject, or is a British subject but is not a citizen of a country forming part of the Queen's dominions, and has not made and subscribed the oath or affirmation referred to in paragraph (e) of the last preceding sub-section but is otherwise eligible for appointment as an officer.<lf> <p>  (4) Subject to this Part, officers hold office on such terms and conditions as the Commissioner, with the approval of the Public Service Board, determines.<lf> <p>  (5) Officers shall perform duties as directed by the Commissioner.<lf> </lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>

### <name>COMMONWEALTH TEACHING SERVICE ACT 1972 - SECT. 21\. Recruitment of officers. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   21\. (1) The Commissioner may, from time to time, by advertisement in the daily newspapers or elsewhere, invite persons to apply for appointment as officers.<lf> 

  (2) The Commissioner shall specify in any such advertisement-<lf> <lf>   (a)  the classes of positions in respect of which applications for appointment are invited;<lf> <lf>   (b)  the salaries, or limits of salaries, that will be applicable upon appointment;<lf> <lf>   (c)  where applicable-<lf> <lf>       (i)  the age limits for appointment;<lf> <lf>      (ii)  the qualifications required to be possessed before appointment and the period, if any, within which the qualifications or any of them must have been obtained; and<lf> <lf>      (iii)  the date on which persons who obtained or obtain the required qualifications during a period specified for the purposes of the last preceding sub-paragraph will cease to be qualified for appointment by virtue of those qualifications;<lf> <lf>   (d)  the manner of ascertaining the order in which offers of appointment will be made;<lf> <lf>   (e)  the date by which applications for appointment are required to be received; and<lf> <lf>   (f)  such other matters, if any, as the Commissioner considers desirable.<lf> <p>  (3) Offers of appointment to qualified persons who have applied for appointment in pursuance of an advertisement under this section shall be made in the order ascertained in accordance with the terms of the advertisement.<lf> <p>  (4) Nothing in this section prevents the appointment as an officer of a qualified person who has applied for appointment otherwise than in pursuance of an advertisement under this section.<lf> </lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>COMMONWEALTH TEACHING SERVICE ACT 1972 - SECT. 22\. Appointments to be on probation. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   22\. (1) Unless the Commissioner, in a particular case, otherwise directs, the appointment of every officer shall be on probation for a period of six months commencing on the date on which the officer commences duties in pursuance of his appointment.<lf> 

  (2) A person appointed as an officer on probation remains a probationer until his appointment is confirmed or terminated in accordance with this section.<lf> <p>  (3) The Commissioner may, at any time during the period of six months, terminate the appointment.<lf> <p>  (4) As soon as practicable after the expiration of the period of six months, the Commissioner shall-<lf> <lf>   (a)  confirm the appointment;<lf> <lf>   (b)  terminate the appointment; or<lf> <lf>   (c)  direct that the probationer continue on probation for such further period (not being a period exceeding six months) as the Commissioner thinks fit.<lf> <p>  (5) Where the Commissioner directs that a probationer continue on probation for a further period, he may confirm or terminate the appointment of the probationer at any time during that further period and, if he has not confirmed or terminated the appointment before the expiration of that period, shall do so as soon as practicable after the expiration of that period.<lf> <p>  (6) Where the appointment of a probationer is terminated, the Commissioner shall notify the probationer in writing of the reasons for the termination.<lf> </lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>COMMONWEALTH TEACHING SERVICE ACT 1972 - SECT. 23\. Employees. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   23\. (1) The Commissioner may, on behalf of the Commonwealth, engage persons as temporary employees.<lf> Amended by No. 98, 1973, s. 8\. 

  (2) Subject to the next succeeding sub-section, a person shall not be engaged as a temporary employee unless he makes and subscribes, before the Commissioner, a person appointed by the Commissioner or a justice of the peace, an oath or affirmation of allegiance in accordance with the form of oath or affirmation in the Second Schedule.<lf> <p>  (3) The Commissioner may, with the approval of the Minister, engage as a temporary employee a person who is not a British subject, or is a British subject but is not a citizen of a country forming part of the Queen's dominions, and has not made and subscribed the oath or affirmation referred to in the last preceding sub-section.<lf> <p>  (4) Persons engaged as temporary employees shall be employed on such terms and conditions as the Commissioner, with the approval of the Public Service Board, determines.<lf> <p>  (5) Persons engaged as temporary employees shall perform duties as directed by the Commissioner.<lf> </lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</lf></sect>

### <name>COMMONWEALTH TEACHING SERVICE ACT 1972 - SECT. 26\. Reclassification. </name>
</b>**
