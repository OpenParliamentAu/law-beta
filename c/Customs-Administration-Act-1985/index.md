###  Long Title

###  CUSTOMS ADMINISTRATION ACT 1985- SECT 1  Short title [_see_ Note 1] 
This Act may be cited as the _Customs Administration Act 1985_.

 
###  CUSTOMS ADMINISTRATION ACT 1985- SECT 2  Commencement [_see_ Note 1] 
This Act shall come into operation on a day to be fixed by Proclamation.

 
###  CUSTOMS ADMINISTRATION ACT 1985- SECT 3  Interpretation 
In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:

 
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_appoint_ includes re-appoint. _CEO_ means the Chief Executive Officer of Customs. _law of customs_ means:  </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>this Act; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>any other Act of which the CEO has the general administration; or</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>if the CEO has the general administration of a particular provision or

provisions only of an Act&#151;that provision or those provisions; or</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>if the CEO has the general administration of an Act, or of a particular

provision or provisions of an Act, only in so far as that Act, or that

provision or those provisions, relates or relate to a particular matter or

matters&#151;that Act, or that provision or those provisions, in so far as that

Act, or that provision or those provisions, relates or relate to that matter or

those matters; or</dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>any instrument (including rules, regulations, by-laws or determinations)

under this Act, under an Act to which paragraph&#160;(b) applies, under a

provision to which paragraph&#160;(c) applies or under an Act or provision

referred to in paragraph&#160;(d) in so far as it relates to a matter so

referred to.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

###  CUSTOMS ADMINISTRATION ACT 1985- SECT 4  Australian Customs Service 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>There is hereby established an Australian Customs Service.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>There shall be a Chief Executive Officer of Customs, who shall, under the Minister, control the Australian Customs Service.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>The Australian Customs Service consists of the CEO and the staff referred to in section 15.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>Where a person employed in the Australian Customs Service, or a person not so employed who is authorized in writing by the CEO to perform a function or functions of a person employed in the Australian Customs Service, performs a function or exercises a power under a law of customs, the person is, in the performance of that function or the exercise of that power, subject to the directions of the CEO. </dd> </dl>

###  CUSTOMS ADMINISTRATION ACT 1985- SECT 4A  Minister may give directions to CEO 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Minister may give written directions to the CEO with respect to the

general policy to be pursued in relation to the administration of the

Australian Customs Service.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>If the Minister gives a direction under subsection (1), the Minister must cause a copy of the direction to be laid before each House of the Parliament within 15 sitting days of that House after giving the direction.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>The CEO must comply with all written directions given by the Minister under subsection (1). </dd> </dl>

**

###  CUSTOMS ADMINISTRATION ACT 1985- SECT 5  Appointment of CEO 
**

 <dl compact="">

		The CEO shall be appointed by the Governor-General.

 </dl>

**

###  CUSTOMS ADMINISTRATION ACT 1985- SECT 6  Tenure of office 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Subject to this Act, the CEO is to be appointed for a specified period of

not more than 5 years but is eligible for re-appointment.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>The CEO holds office on such terms and conditions not provided for by this Act or any other Act as the Governor-General determines. </dd> </dl>

**

###  CUSTOMS ADMINISTRATION ACT 1985- SECT 7  Employment outside official duties 
**

 <dl compact="">

		The CEO shall not, without the approval of the Minister, engage in paid

employment outside the duties of the office of CEO.

 </dl>

**

###  CUSTOMS ADMINISTRATION ACT 1985- SECT 8   Remuneration and allowances 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The CEO shall be paid such remuneration as is determined by the

Remuneration Tribunal.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The CEO shall be paid such allowances as are prescribed.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>This section has effect subject to the _Remuneration Tribunal Act 1973_. </dd> </dl>

**

###  CUSTOMS ADMINISTRATION ACT 1985- SECT 9  Leave of absence 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The CEO has such recreation leave entitlements as are determined by the

Remuneration Tribunal.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Minister may grant the CEO leave of absence, other than recreation leave, on such terms and conditions as to remuneration or otherwise as the Minister determines. </dd> </dl>

**

###  CUSTOMS ADMINISTRATION ACT 1985- SECT 10  Resignation 
**

 <dl compact="">

		The CEO may resign from office by writing signed by the CEO and delivered to

the Governor-General.

 </dl>

**

###  CUSTOMS ADMINISTRATION ACT 1985- SECT 11  Disclosure of financial interests 
**

 <dl compact="">

		The CEO shall give written notice to the Minister of all direct and indirect

pecuniary interests that the CEO has or acquires in any business in Australia

or elsewhere or in any body corporate carrying on such a business.

 </dl>

**

###  CUSTOMS ADMINISTRATION ACT 1985- SECT 12  Suspension and removal from office of CEO 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Governor-General may remove the CEO from office on an address praying

for the removal of the CEO on the ground of proved misbehaviour or physical or

mental incapacity being presented to the Governor-General by each House of the

Parliament in the same session of the Parliament.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Governor-General may suspend the CEO from office on the ground of misbehaviour or physical or mental incapacity.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>Where the Governor-General suspends the CEO, the Minister shall cause a statement of the grounds of the suspension to be laid before each House of the Parliament within 7 sitting days of that House after the suspension.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>If, at the expiration of 15 sitting days of a House of the Parliament after the day on which the statement was laid before that House, an address under subsection (1) has not been presented to the Governor-General by each House of the Parliament, the suspension terminates.</dd> <dt>(5)</dt><dd>The suspension of the CEO from office under this section does not affect any entitlement of the CEO to be paid remuneration and allowances.</dd> <dt>(6)</dt><dd>If: </dd> </dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the CEO becomes bankrupt, applies to take the benefit of any law for the

relief of bankrupt or insolvent debtors, compounds with his or her creditors or

makes an assignment of his or her remuneration for their benefit;</dd>

<dt>(aa)</dt><dd>the CEO fails, without reasonable excuse, to comply with a direction

given under section 4A;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the CEO fails, without reasonable excuse, to comply with section 11;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>the CEO engages, except with the approval of the Minister, in paid

employment outside the duties of the office of CEO; or</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>the CEO is absent from duty, except on leave of absence, for 14

consecutive days or 28 days in any 12 months;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

the Governor-General shall remove the CEO from office.

<dl compact="">

<dt>(7)</dt><dd>The Governor-General may, with the consent of the CEO, retire the CEO from

office on the ground of physical or mental incapacity.</dd> <dt>(8)</dt><dd>The CEO shall not be suspended, removed or retired from office except as provided by this section. </dd> </dl>

###  CUSTOMS ADMINISTRATION ACT 1985- SECT 13  Acting CEO 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Governor-General may appoint a person to act in the office of CEO:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>during a vacancy in that office; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>during any period, or during all periods, when the person holding that

office is absent from duty or from Australia or is, for any other reason,

unable to perform the functions of that office.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>An appointment of a person under subsection (1) may be expressed to have

effect only in such circumstances as are specified in the instrument of

appointment.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>A person appointed under subsection (1) to act during a vacancy shall not continue so to act for more than 12 months.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>Where a person is acting in the office of CEO in accordance with paragraph (1)(b) and the office becomes vacant while the person is so acting, then, subject to subsection (2), the person may continue so to act until the Governor-General otherwise directs, the vacancy is filled or a period of 12 months from the day on which the vacancy occurs expires, whichever first happens.</dd> <dt>(5)</dt><dd>While a person is acting in the office of CEO, the person has and may exercise all the powers, and shall perform all the functions, of the CEO under this Act or any other law.</dd> <dt>(6)</dt><dd>The Governor-General may: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>determine the terms and conditions of appointment, including remuneration

and allowances, of a person acting in the office of CEO; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>terminate such an appointment at any time.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(7)</dt><dd>A person appointed under subsection (1) may resign the appointment by

writing signed by the person and delivered to the Governor-General.</dd> <dt>(8)</dt><dd>The validity of anything done by or in relation to a person purporting to act in the office of CEO shall not be called in question on the ground that the occasion for the appointment had not arisen, that there was a defect or irregularity in or in connection with the appointment, that the appointment had ceased to have effect or that the occasion for the person to act had not arisen or had ceased. </dd> </dl>

###  CUSTOMS ADMINISTRATION ACT 1985- SECT 14  Delegation 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The CEO may, by signed instrument, delegate to an officer of Customs all

or any of the functions and powers of the CEO under a law of customs or any

other law of the Commonwealth.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>A delegation under this section may be made subject to a power of review and alteration by the CEO, within a period specified in the instrument of delegation, of acts done under the delegation.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>A function or power so delegated, when performed or exercised by the delegate, shall, for the purposes of the law of customs, or the other law of the Commonwealth, under which the function was established or the power conferred, be taken to have been performed or exercised by the CEO.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>Paragraph 34AB(c) of the _Acts Interpretation Act 1901_ does not apply to a delegation under subsection (1).</dd> <dt>(5)</dt><dd>In this section: </dd> </dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_officer of Customs_ means a person who is an Officer of Customs

for the purposes of the _Customs Act 1901_.

 </dl></dl>

 <dl compact="">

###  CUSTOMS ADMINISTRATION ACT 1985- SECT 16  Prohibition of disclosure of certain information  
**

 _Overview_

<dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>This section:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>prohibits the unauthorised recording and disclosure of certain information

held by the Australian Customs Service; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>provides for exceptions in relation to the prohibition; and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>makes particular provision in relation to the authorised disclosure of

personal information.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

_Persons to whom section applies_

<dl compact="">

<dt>(1AA)</dt><dd>This section applies to:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the CEO; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>a person performing duties in the Australian Customs Service as a person

employed or engaged by the Commonwealth, a Commonwealth agency, a State or a

State agency; and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>any of the following persons:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>a person engaged to provide goods or services to the Commonwealth through

the Australian Customs Service (whether or not under an agreement to which the

Commonwealth is a party);</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>if the person mentioned in subparagraph&#160;(i) is a body corporate&#151;a

director, an employee or an agent of the body corporate;</dd>

<dt>(iii)</dt><dd>if the person mentioned in subparagraph&#160;(i) is an individual&#151;an

employee or agent of the individual; and

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>a person to whom the CEO has delegated a power or function of the CEO

under a law of customs or any other law of the Commonwealth; and</dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>a person authorised by the CEO to exercise a power or function under a law

of customs or any other law of the Commonwealth.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

_Definitions_

<dl compact="">

<dt>(1A)</dt><dd>In this section:

</dd> </dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_Commonwealth agency_ means any instrumentality or agency of the

Crown in right of the Commonwealth and includes:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>any body corporate in which the Commonwealth holds a controlling interest; 

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

but does not include a Minister of the Crown in right of the 
<center>Back to Top</center>
 Commonwealth.

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_duties_, in relation to a person, means the duties of the person

in the capacity because of which he or she is a person to whom this section

with the Australian Customs Service.

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Example:	In the case of a person who is employed by a State

employee of that State agency.

 </dl></dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_international organisation_ means:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>an international organisation within the meaning of the _Diplomatic Privileges and Immunities Act 1967_; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>any other international organisation prescribed by the regulations for the

purposes of this paragraph.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_personal information_ has the same meaning as in the _Privacy Act 1988_. _principal officer_, in relation to a Commonwealth agency or a State agency, means:  </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>if the agency is constituted by one person&#151;that person; and</dd>

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_protected information_ means information that directly or

indirectly comes to the knowledge of, or into the possession of, a person while

_Prohibition against disclosure etc. of protected information_

<dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>A person who is or has been a person to whom this section applies must not

directly or indirectly:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>make a record of any protected information; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>disclose to any person any protected information;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

except:

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>as authorised by this section; or</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>as required or authorised by any other law; or</dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>in the course of performing the person's duties.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

Penalty:	Imprisonment for 2 years.

<dl compact="">

###  CUSTOMS ADMINISTRATION ACT 1985- SECT 16AA  Conduct by directors, employees and agents 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>This section applies for the purposes of a prosecution for:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>an offence created by a provision in this Act or the regulations; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>an offence created by section&#160;6 of the _Crimes Act 1914_, or

section&#160;11.1, 11.2, 11.4 or 11.5 of the _Criminal Code_, that relates

to an offence referred to in paragraph (a).

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>If it is necessary to prove the state of mind of a body corporate in

relation to particular conduct, it is enough to show that:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the conduct was engaged in by a director, employee or agent of the body

corporate within the scope of his or her actual or apparent authority; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the director, employee or agent had the state of mind.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>If:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>conduct is engaged in on behalf of a body corporate by a director,

employee or agent of the body corporate; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the conduct is within the scope of his or her actual or apparent authority;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

the conduct is taken, for the purposes of the prosecution of an offence against this Act, to have been engaged in also by the body corporate unless the body corporate establishes that it took reasonable precautions and exercised due diligence to avoid the conduct.

<dl compact="">

<dt>(4)</dt><dd>In this section:

</dd> </dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_director_, in relation to a body that:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>is incorporated for a public purpose by a law of the Commonwealth, of a

State or of a Territory; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>is constituted by one or more members;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

means the member, or any of the members, constituting the body.

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_engage in conduct_ includes fail or refuse to engage in conduct. _state of mind_, in relation to a person, includes:  </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the person's knowledge, intention, opinion, belief or purpose; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the person's reasons for the intention, opinion, belief or purpose.

</dd>
