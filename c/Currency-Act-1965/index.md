### <name>CURRENCY ACT 1965-1973 - SECT. 1\. Short title. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>                             CURRENCY  ACT  1965-1973<lf> <lf>              An Act relating to Currency, Coinage and Legal Tender.<lf> <lf>                               PART  I-PRELIMINARY<lf> Short title amended; No. 32, 1918, s. 2.<lf>   1\. This Act may be cited as the Currency Act 1965-1973.*<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>CURRENCY ACT 1965-1973 - SECT. 2\. Commencement. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   2.* (1) Part I and section 24 of this Act shall come into operation on the date on which this Act receives the Royal Assent.<lf> 

  (2) The remaining provisions of this Act shall come into operation on the fourteenth day of February, One thousand nine hundred and sixty-six.<lf> Section 3 repealed by No. 216, 1973, s. 3.<lf>                          *   *   *   *   *   *   *   *<lf> </lf></lf></lf>
</lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>CURRENCY ACT 1965-1973 - SECT. 4\. Definitions. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Amended by No. 216, 1973, s. 3.<lf>   4\. In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears-<lf> <lf>   ''Australia'' includes all the Territories to which this Act extends by virtue of the next succeeding section;<lf> <lf>   ''the repealed Acts'' means the Acts repealed by this Act;<lf> <lf>   ''the Schedule'' means the Schedule to this Act.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>CURRENCY ACT 1965-1973 - SECT. 5\. Extension to Territories. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Amended by No. 216, 1973, s. 3.<lf>   5\. This Act extends to all the external Territories except Papua New Guinea and the Territory of Christmas Island.<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>CURRENCY ACT 1965-1973 - SECT. 6\. Crown to be bound. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   6\. This Act binds the Crown in right of the Commonwealth or a State.<lf> </lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>CURRENCY ACT 1965-1973 - SECT. 7\. Repeal. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>                                PART  II-CURRENCY<lf>   7\. The following Acts are repealed:-<lf> <lf>   Coinage Act 1909;<lf> <lf>   Coinage Act 1936;<lf> <lf>   Coinage Act 1947.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>CURRENCY ACT 1965-1973 - SECT. 8\. Monetary unit and denominations of money. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   8\. (1) The monetary unit, or unit of currency, of Australia is the dollar.<lf> 

  (2) The denominations of money in the currency of Australia are the dollar and the cent.<lf> <p>  (3) A cent is one-hundredth part of a dollar.<lf> <p>  (4) The equivalent in the currency provided for by this Act of One sovereign or pound in the currency provided for by the repealed Acts is Two dollars, the like equivalent of One shilling is Ten cents and the like equivalent of One penny is five-sixths of a cent.<lf> </lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>CURRENCY ACT 1965-1973 - SECT. 9\. Transactions to be in Australian currency. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   9\. (1) Subject to this section, every sale, every bill of exchange or promissory note, every security for money, and every other contract, agreement, deed, instrument, transaction, dealing, matter or thing relating to money, or involving the payment of, or a liability to pay, money, that is made, executed, entered into or done, shall, unless it is made, executed, entered into or done according to the currency of some country other than Australia, be made, executed, entered into or done according to the currency of Australia provided for by this Act.<lf> 

  (2) Nothing in this section operates so as to invalidate a will or other testamentary instrument.<lf> </lf>
</lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>CURRENCY ACT 1965-1973 - SECT. 10\. References to previous currency to be construed as references to currency under this Act. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   10\. (1) Subject to this section-<lf> <lf>   (a)  a reference in a law of the Commonwealth;<lf> <lf>   (b)  a reference in a bill of exchange, promissory note, security for money, contract or agreement (whether the contract or agreement is in writing or not), deed or other instrument; and<lf> <lf>   (c)  a reference in any other manner (not being a reference in a law other than a law of the Commonwealth),<lf> to an amount of money in the currency provided for by the repealed Acts shall, unless the context is such that it would be inappropriate or the contrary intention appears, be construed as a reference to a corresponding amount of money in the currency provided for by this Act, calculated on the basis of the equivalents specified in sub-section (4) of section 8 of this Act.<lf> 

  (2) Where such a reference as is mentioned in the last preceding sub-section is to a percentage or other proportion expressed in terms of money, the reference shall be construed as a reference to an equivalent percentage or proportion expressed in terms of money in the currency provided for by this Act. <lf> <p>  (3) Where such a reference as is mentioned in sub-section (1) of this section (not being a reference in a law of the Commonwealth) is to the amount of a payment payable periodically (not being a payment for the remuneration of an employee, whether the remuneration is by way of salary, wages, commission or otherwise) and that amount is an amount of pounds, shillings and pence, pounds and pence, shillings and pence or pence, the corresponding amount in the currency provided for by this Act shall be calculated on the basis that an amount of pence specified in the first column of the table in sub-section (3) of the next succeeding section corresponds to the amount of cents specified in the second column of that table opposite to that amount of pence.<lf> Amended by No. 216, 1973, s. 3\. <p>  (4) For the purposes of this section-<lf> <lf>   ''law of the Commonwealth'' means-<lf> <lf>       (a)  an Act;<lf> <lf>       (b)  an instrument (including regulations or rules or an industrial award, order, determination or agreement or similar instrument) having effect by virtue of an Act;<lf> <lf>       (c)  an Ordinance of a Territory and any other law in force in a Territory;<lf> <lf>       (d)  an instrument (including regulations or rules or an industrial award, order, determination or agreement or similar instrument) having effect by virtue of such an Ordinance or law; and<lf> <lf>       (e)  an instrument having effect by virtue of any such regulations or rules as are mentioned in paragraph (b) or (d) of this definition;<lf> <lf>   ''Territory'' means a Territory in which this Act applies or to which this Act extends.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></p></lf>

### <name>CURRENCY ACT 1965-1973 - SECT. 12\. Forms. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   12\. Where a law of the Commonwealth as defined by sub-section (4) of section 10 of this Act, being a law passed or made before the fourteenth day of February, One thousand nine hundred and sixty-six, contains a form that refers to an amount of money in the currency provided for by the repealed Acts, or provides for an amount of money to be specified in that currency, a person using the form may fill it in in such a manner as to set forth the equivalent of that amount in the currency provided for by this Act.<lf> </lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>CURRENCY ACT 1965-1973 - SECT. 13\. Standard weight and composition of coins. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>                                PART  III-COINAGE<lf>   13\. The standard composition of the coins of the denominations specified in the Schedule is as specified in the Schedule and the standard weight of those coins is as prescribed.<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>CURRENCY ACT 1965-1973 - SECT. 14\. Treasurer may issue coins. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   14\. (1) The Treasurer may cause to be made and issued coins of the denominations of money specified in the Schedule.<lf> 

  (2) A coin to be so made and issued-<lf> <lf>   (a)  shall be of the standard composition specified in the Schedule in relation to that coin; and<lf> <lf>   (b)  shall be of the standard weight, and of the design and dimensions, prescribed in relation to that coin.<lf> Substituted by No. 4, 1969, s. 3\. <p>  (3) In the making of a coin, a remedy in respect of weight (that is to say, a variation from the standard weight applicable to the coin) is allowable as prescribed.<lf> Sub-section (4) omitted by No. 4, 1969, s. 3.<lf>                          *   *   *   *   *   *   *   *<lf> </lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>CURRENCY ACT 1965-1973 - SECT. 15\. Treasurer may issue coins under repealed Acts for limited time. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   15\. Notwithstanding the repeal effected by section 7 of this Act, the Treasurer may, until a date to be fixed by Proclamation as the date on and after which this section shall cease to be in force, continue to cause to be made and issued coins in accordance with the repealed Acts.<lf> </lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>CURRENCY ACT 1965-1973 - SECT. 16\. Legal tender. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>                              PART  IV-LEGAL  TENDER<lf>   16\. (1) A tender of payment of money is a legal tender if it is made in coins that are made and issued under this Act and are of current weight-<lf> <lf>   (a)  in the case of coins of the denomination of Five cents, Ten cents, Twenty cents or Fifty cents-for payment of an amount not exceeding Five dollars but for no greater amount; and<lf> <lf>   (b)  in the case of any other coins-for payment of an amount not exceeding Twenty cents but for no greater amount.<lf> 

  (2) For the purposes of the last preceding sub-section, a coin shall be deemed to be not of current weight if it has become diminished in weight by wear or otherwise so as to be of less weight than the weight prescribed as the least current weight of that coin.<lf> </lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>CURRENCY ACT 1965-1973 - SECT. 17\. Coins called in not legal tender. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   17\. The references to coins in sub-section (1) of the last preceding section do not include references to coins that have been called in in pursuance of this Act.<lf> </lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>CURRENCY ACT 1965-1973 - SECT. 18\. Transactions may be in previous currency during transition period. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>                         PART  V-TRANSITIONAL  PROVISIONS<lf>   18\. (1) Notwithstanding the provisions of Part II, a payment, a sale or a security for money, or any other contract, agreement, deed, instrument, transaction, dealing, matter or thing relating to money, or involving the payment of, or a liability to pay, money (but not including a bill of exchange or promissory note), that is made, executed, entered into or done may be made, executed, entered into or done according to the currency provided for by the repealed Acts.<lf> 

  (2) Section 10 of this Act does not, before the date fixed by Proclamation in pursuance of section 21 of this Act, apply in relation to-<lf> <lf>   (a)  a reference to an amount of money in the currency provided for by the repealed Acts in a security for money, contract, agreement, deed or other instrument executed or entered into in pursuance of the last preceding sub-section; or<lf> <lf>   (b)  a reference in any other manner to an amount of money in the currency provided for by the repealed Acts, being a reference made in pursuance of that sub-section.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>CURRENCY ACT 1965-1973 - SECT. 19\. Payments during and after transition period. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   19\. (1) For the purpose of making, in the currency provided for by this Act, a payment-<lf> <lf>   (a)  that is payable in pursuance of, under or by virtue of a sale or a security for money, or any other contract, agreement, deed, instrument, transaction, dealing, matter or thing made, executed, entered into or done in pursuance of sub-section (1) of the last preceding section; and<lf> <lf>   (b)  that-<lf> <lf>       (i)  is made by a bill of exchange or promissory note; or<lf> <lf>      (ii)  is not so made but was payable before the date fixed in pursuance of section 21 of this Act and is not made until after that date,<lf> the amount of money in the currency provided for by this Act that corresponds to an amount of money in the currency provided for by the repealed Acts is, subject to the next succeeding sub-section, an amount calculated on the basis of the equivalents specified in sub-section (4) of section 8 of this Act.<lf> 

  (2) Sub-section (3) of section 11 of this Act has effect for the purposes of the last preceding sub-section and so has effect as if the reference in that sub-section to ''the last preceding sub-section'' were a reference to the last preceding sub-section of this section.<lf> </lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>

## SECT

and are of current weight-<lf> <lf>   (a)  in the case of bronze coins-for payment of an amount not exceeding Twenty cents or Two shillings but for no greater amounts; and<lf> <lf>   (b)  in the case of any other coins-for payment of an amount not exceeding Five dollars or Two pounds ten shillings but for no greater amount.<lf> <p>  (4) A tender of payment of money is a legal tender if it is made partly in coins that are referred to in the repealed Acts and are of current weight and partly in coins that are made and issued under this Act and are of current weight-<lf> <lf>   (a)  in the case ### <name>CURRENCY ACT 1965-1973 - SECT. 21\. Duration of section 18\. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   21\. Upon a date to be fixed by Proclamation, sub-section (1) of section 18 of this Act shall be deemed to be repealed and section 8 of the Acts Interpretation Act 1901-1964 shall be deemed to apply as if that sub-section had been repealed by an Act other than this Act.<lf> </lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>CURRENCY ACT 1965-1973 - SECT. 22\. Prohibition of other than official coins. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>                              PART  VI-MISCELLANEOUS<lf>   22\. A person shall not make or issue a piece of gold, silver, copper, nickel, bronze or of any other material, whether metal or otherwise, of any value, other than a coin made or issued under the repealed Acts or under this Act or a British coin as defined by the repealed Acts, as a token for money or as purporting that the holder is entitled to demand any value denoted on it.<lf> <lf>   Penalty: One hundred dollars.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>CURRENCY ACT 1965-1973 - SECT. 23\. Coins may be called in. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   23\. (1) The Governor-General may, by Proclamation, call in any coins issued under this Act or the repealed Acts before a date specified in the Proclamation. <lf> 

  (2)  A Proclamation under the last preceding sub-section has effect from such date as is specified in the Proclamation for the purpose.<lf> </lf>
</lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>CURRENCY ACT 1965-1973 - SECT. 24\. Certain days to be non-business days for purposes of Bills of Exchange Act. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   24\. (1) Notwithstanding the provisions of sub-section (3) of section 98 of the Bills of Exchange Act 1909-1958, the tenth, eleventh and twelfth days of February, One thousand nine hundred and sixty-six, are non-business days for the purposes of that Act.<lf> 

  (2) Notwithstanding any provision of the Bills of Exchange Act 1909-1958, a cheque may be presented for payment by a banker (including the banker on whom that cheque is drawn) on any of the days referred to in the last preceding sub-section.<lf> </lf>
</lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>CURRENCY ACT 1965-1973 - SECT. 25\. Special provision with respect to certain cheques. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   25\. A cheque dated on or after the fourteenth day of February, One thousand nine hundred and sixty-six, shall be deemed to have been drawn before that date if there is written upon the cheque a certificate by a banker that the cheque was drawn before that date but the cheque shall not, by reason only of the certificate, be taken to be a stale cheque.<lf> </lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>CURRENCY ACT 1965-1973 - SECT. 26\. Regulations. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   26\. The Governor-General may make regulations, not inconsistent with this Act, prescribing all matters that are required or permitted by this Act to be prescribed or are necessary or convenient to be prescribed for carrying out or giving effect to this Act.<lf> <lf>                                   -----------<lf> </lf></lf></lf></sect>
