###  CRIMINOLOGY RESEARCH ACT 1971- SECT 1  Short title [_see_ Note 1]  
<dl compact="">

		This Act may be cited as the _Criminology Research Act 1971_.  

 </dl>
###  CRIMINOLOGY RESEARCH ACT 1971- SECT 2  Commencement [_see_ Note 1] 
<dl compact="">

		This Act shall come into operation on a date to be fixed by Proclamation.

 </dl>
###  CRIMINOLOGY RESEARCH ACT 1971- SECT 4  Interpretation 
<dl compact="">

		In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:

 </dl>
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_Acting Director_ means an Acting Director of the Institute

appointed under section 22\. _appoint_ includes re-appoint. _criminological research_ means research in connexion with:  </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the causes, correction and prevention of criminal behaviour; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>any related matter.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_the appropriate Minister_ means:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>in relation to a State, such Minister of the Crown of that State as is

prescribed, and includes any Minister of the Crown for the time being acting

for and on behalf of that Minister; and</dd>

<dt>(aa)</dt><dd>in relation to the Australian Capital Territory, such Minister of the

Australian Capital Territory as is prescribed, and includes any Minister of the

Australian Capital Territory for the time being acting for and on behalf of

that Minister; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>in relation to the Northern Territory, such Minister of the Northern

Territory as is prescribed, and includes any Minister of the Northern Territory

for the time being acting for and on behalf of that Minister.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_the Board_ means the Board of Management of the Institute

established by section 8\. _the Council_ means the Criminology Research Council established by section 34\. _the Director_ means the Director of the Institute appointed under section 15\. _the Fund_ means the Criminology Research Fund established by section 44\. _the Institute_ means the Australian Institute of Criminology established by section 5\.  </dl></dl>

###  CRIMINOLOGY RESEARCH ACT 1971- SECT 5  Establishment of Institute 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>There is hereby established an institute to be known as the Australian

Institute of Criminology.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Institute: </dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>is a body corporate; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>shall have a seal.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Note:	The _Commonwealth Authorities and Companies Act 1997_

applies to the Institute. That Act deals with matters relating to Commonwealth

authorities, including reporting and accountability, banking and investment,

and conduct of officers.

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>The seal of the Institute shall be kept in such custody as the Board

directs and shall not be used except as authorized by the Board.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>All courts, judges and persons acting judicially shall take judicial notice of the imprint of the seal of the Institute appearing on a document and shall presume that the document was duly sealed. </dd> </dl></dl>

**

###  CRIMINOLOGY RESEARCH ACT 1971- SECT 6  Functions of Institute 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		The functions of the Institute are:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>to conduct, or arrange for the conduct of, such criminological research as

is approved by the Board or is requested by the Attorney-General;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>to communicate to the Commonwealth, the States, the Australian Capital

Territory and the Northern Territory the results of research conducted by the

Institute;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>to conduct, or arrange for the conduct of, such seminars and courses of

training or instruction for persons engaged, or to be engaged, in

criminological research or in work related to the prevention or correction of

criminal behaviour as are approved by the Board or are requested by the

Attorney-General;</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>to advise the Council in relation to needs for, and programmes of,

criminological research;</dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>to provide secretarial and administrative services for the Council;</dd>

<dt>(f)</dt><dd>to give advice and assistance in relation to any research performed wholly

or partly with moneys provided out of the Fund;</dd>

<dt>(g)</dt><dd>to give advice in relation to the compilation of statistics relating to

crime;</dd>

<dt>(h)</dt><dd>to publish such material resulting from or connected with the performance

of its functions as is approved by the Board;</dd>

<dt>(ha)</dt><dd>to collect information and statistics (without detracting from, and in

the context of, the overall collecting and coordinating role of the Australian

Bureau of Statistics);</dd>

<dt>(hb)</dt><dd>to provide information and advice to Departments, agencies and

authorities of the Commonwealth, of the States, of the Australian Capital

Territory and of the Northern Territory dealing with the administration of

criminal justice;</dd>

<dt>(hc)</dt><dd>to collaborate, in and outside Australia, with governments, institutions

and authorities, and with bodies and persons, in relation to research, or the

training of persons, in or in connection with the administration of criminal

justice; and</dd>

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>to do anything incidental or conducive to the performance of any of the

foregoing functions.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

###  CRIMINOLOGY RESEARCH ACT 1971- SECT 6A  Power of Attorney-General to request Institute to perform certain functions 
**

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Attorney-General may request the Institute to:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>conduct, or arrange for the conduct of, criminological research into a

matter specified by the Attorney-General; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>to conduct seminars or courses of training or instruction in a matter

specified by the Attorney-General, being seminars or courses of training or

instruction for persons engaged, or to be engaged, in criminological research

or in work related to the prevention or correction of criminal behaviour.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>Where the Attorney-General so requests the Institute to conduct, or

arrange for the conduct of, criminological research, or to conduct seminars or

courses of training or instruction, the Attorney-General may also require the

Board to ensure that such priority is given to the conduct of that research, or

to the conduct of those seminars or courses of training or instruction, as the

Attorney-General specifies.

</dd> </dl></dl>

###  CRIMINOLOGY RESEARCH ACT 1971- SECT 7  Powers of Institute 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

be done for or in connexion with the performance of its functions and, in

particular, has power:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(aa)</dt><dd>to acquire, hold and dispose of real and personal property;</dd>

<dt>(ab)</dt><dd>to enter into contracts;</dd>

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>to erect buildings;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>to occupy, use and control any land or building owned or held under lease

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>to accept gifts, devises and bequests made to the Institute, whether on

the Institute upon trust.

</dd>

###  CRIMINOLOGY RESEARCH ACT 1971- SECT 14  Meetings of Board 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Chair of the Board or the deputy of the Chair may convene meetings of

the Board.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>If neither the Chair nor the deputy of the Chair is available, the Attorney-General may convene a meeting of the Board.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>The Chair or the deputy of the Chair shall preside at all meetings of the Board at which he or she is present.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>At a meeting of the Board at which neither the Chair nor the deputy of the Chair is present, the members present shall elect one of the members appointed by the Attorney-General to preside.</dd> <dt>(5)</dt><dd>For the purposes of a meeting of the Board, 2 of the members appointed by the Attorney-General and 2 of the members appointed by the Council constitute a quorum.</dd> <dt>(6)</dt><dd>A member of the Board may, by writing signed by the member, appoint another member or the deputy of another member as his or her proxy to attend and vote on his or her behalf at a meeting of the Board at which neither the first-mentioned member nor his or her deputy is present.</dd> <dt>(7)</dt><dd>At a meeting of the Board: </dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>all decisions shall be made by a majority of votes of the members voting,

whether personally or by proxy; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the member presiding has a deliberative vote and, in the event of an

equality of votes, also has a casting vote.

</dd>

**

###  CRIMINOLOGY RESEARCH ACT 1971- SECT 38  Resignation of members of Council and deputies of members 
**

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The member of the Council who is appointed to represent the Commonwealth,

Attorney-General.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>A member of the Council who is appointed to represent a State, or the deputy of that member, may resign from office by writing signed by the member or the deputy, as the case may be, and delivered to the appropriate Minister of the State.</dd> <dt>(2A)</dt><dd>The member of the Council who is appointed to represent the Australian Capital Territory, or the deputy of that member, may resign his or her office by writing signed by the member or deputy, as the case may be, and delivered to the appropriate Minister of the Australian Capital Territory.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>The member of the Council who is appointed to represent the Northern Territory, or the deputy of that member, may resign his or her office by writing signed by the member or deputy, as the case may be, and delivered to the appropriate Minister of the Northern Territory. </dd> </dl></dl>

###  CRIMINOLOGY RESEARCH ACT 1971- SECT 39  Meetings of Council 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Chair of the Council or the deputy of the Chair may convene meetings

of the Council.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>If neither the Chair nor the deputy of the Chair is available, the Attorney-General may convene a meeting of the Council.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>The Chair or the deputy of the Chair shall preside at all meetings of the Council at which he or she is present.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>At a meeting of the Council at which neither the Chair nor the deputy of the Chair is present, the members present shall elect one of their number to preside.</dd> <dt>(5)</dt><dd>For the purposes of a meeting of the Council, the member representing the Commonwealth and 4 other members constitute a quorum.</dd> <dt>(6)</dt><dd>A member of the Council may, by writing signed by the member, appoint another member or the deputy of another member as the proxy of the first-mentioned member to attend and vote on behalf of the first-mentioned member at a meeting of the Council at which neither the first-mentioned member nor the deputy of the first-mentioned member is present.</dd> <dt>(7)</dt><dd>At a meeting of the Council: </dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>all decisions shall be made by a majority of votes of the members voting,

###  CRIMINOLOGY RESEARCH ACT 1971- SECT 46  Moneys payable to Fund [_see_ Note 2]

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>any moneys paid by a State or the Northern Territory to the Commonwealth,

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>any other moneys derived by the Council as a result of its administration

of the Fund; and</dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>any gifts or bequests made to the Fund or to the Council.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

###  CRIMINOLOGY RESEARCH ACT 1971- SECT 47  Application of moneys in Fund 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Moneys in the Fund, other than moneys to which subsection (2) applies,

shall be applied only:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>in the making of payments for use for such purposes of, or related to,

criminological research (including the dissemination of information and advice,

and the publication of reports, periodicals, books and papers, in connexion

with criminological research and the results of criminological research) as are

determined by the Council; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>in the discharge of any obligations or liabilities of the Council arising

under this Act.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>Where a gift or bequest made to the Fund or to the Council is subject to

conditions in relation to the purposes for which it is to be applied, the

moneys paid into the Fund as a result of the gift or bequest, and any income

derived from the investment of those moneys, shall be applied only for those

purposes and subject to those conditions.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>Surplus money of the Fund may be invested, in the name of the Council, under section 18 of the _Commonwealth Authorities and Companies Act 1997_, unless subsection (2) applies to the money and the conditions referred to in that subsection specify the manner in which the money is to be or may be invested. In that case, the money may only be invested in accordance with those conditions. </dd> </dl></dl>
