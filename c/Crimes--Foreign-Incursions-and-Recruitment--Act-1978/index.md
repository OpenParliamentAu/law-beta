**<b>

### <name>CRIMES (FOREIGN INCURSIONS AND RECRUITMENT) ACT 1978 - LONG TITLE </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> An Act relating to Incursions into Foreign States and Recruitment for Service<lf>                  in Armed Forces in Foreign States<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>CRIMES (FOREIGN INCURSIONS AND RECRUITMENT) ACT 1978 - SECT 1 Short title </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   1\. This Act may be cited as the Crimes (Foreign Incursions and Recruitment) Act 1978.*1* SEE NOTES TO FIRST ARTICLE OF THIS CHAPTER . </sect>
**<b>

### <name>CRIMES (FOREIGN INCURSIONS AND RECRUITMENT) ACT 1978 - SECT 2 Commencement </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   2\. This Act shall come into operation on the day on which it receives the Royal Assent.*1* SEE NOTES TO FIRST ARTICLE OF THIS CHAPTER . </sect>
**<b>

### <name>CRIMES (FOREIGN INCURSIONS AND RECRUITMENT) ACT 1978 - SECT 3 Interpretation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   3.(1) In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:<lf>   'armed force'does not include an armed force forming part of the Defence Force of Australia;<lf>   'Australia'includes the external Territories;<lf>   'foreign State'means a place outside Australia that is:<lf>    (a) an independent sovereign state; or<lf>    (b) an area of land (whether or not it is self-governing) that is not part<lf> of an independent sovereign state;<lf>   'government', in relation to a foreign State or a part of a foreign State, means the authority exercising effective governmental control in that foreign State or that part of that foreign State;<lf>   'incite'includes urge, aid and encourage and also includes print or publish any writing that incites, urges, aids or encourages;<lf>   'recruit' includes procure, induce and incite.<lf>   (2) A reference in this Act to a part of a foreign State shall be read as a reference to a political subdivision of a foreign State. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>CRIMES (FOREIGN INCURSIONS AND RECRUITMENT) ACT 1978 - SECT 4 Extension of Act to Territories </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   4\. This Act extends to every Territory. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>CRIMES (FOREIGN INCURSIONS AND RECRUITMENT) ACT 1978 - SECT 5 Act not to apply to acts done for defence of Australia </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   5\. Nothing in this Act applies to any act done by a person acting in the course of the person's duty to the Commonwealth in relation to the defence of Australia. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>CRIMES (FOREIGN INCURSIONS AND RECRUITMENT) ACT 1978 - SECT 6 Incursions into foreign States for purpose of engaging in hostile activities </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   6.(1) A person shall not:<lf>    (a) enter a foreign State with intent to engage in a hostile activity in<lf> that foreign State; or<lf>    (b) engage in a hostile activity in a foreign State.<lf>   Penalty: Imprisonment for 14 years.<lf>   (2) A person shall not be taken to have committed an offence against this section unless:<lf>    (a) at the time of the doing of the act that is alleged to constitute the<lf> offence, the person:<lf>          (i) was an Australian citizen; or<lf>          (ii) not being an Australian citizen, was ordinarily resident in<lf> Australia; or<lf>    (b) the person was present in Australia at any time during the period of one<lf> year immediately preceding the doing of that act and, at any time when the person was so present, his or her presence was for a purpose connected with that act, or for purposes that included such a purpose.<lf>   (3) For the purposes of subsection (1), engaging in a hostile activity in a foreign State consists of doing an act for the purpose of achieving any one or more of the following objectives (whether or not such an objective is achieved):<lf>    (a) the overthrow by force or violence of the government of the foreign<lf> State or of a part of the foreign State;<lf>    (aa) engaging in armed hostilities in the foreign State;<lf>    (b) causing by force or violence the public in the foreign State to be in<lf> fear of suffering death or personal injury;<lf>    (c) causing the death of, or bodily injury to, a person who:<lf>          (i) is the head of state of the foreign State; or<lf>          (ii) holds, or performs any of the duties of, a public office of the<lf> foreign State or of a part of the foreign State; or<lf>    (d) unlawfully destroying or damaging any real or personal property<lf> belonging to the government of the foreign State or of a part of the foreign State.<lf>   (4) Nothing in this section applies to an act done by a person in the course of, and as part of, the person's service in any capacity in or with:<lf>    (a) the armed forces of the government of a foreign State; or<lf>    (b) any other armed force in respect of which a declaration by the Minister<lf> under subsection 9 (2) is in force. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>CRIMES (FOREIGN INCURSIONS AND RECRUITMENT) ACT 1978 - SECT 7 Preparations for incursions into foreign States for purpose of engaging in hostile activities </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   7.(1) A person shall not, whether within or outside Australia:<lf>    (a) do any act preparatory to the commission of an offence against section<lf> 6, whether by that person or by another person;<lf>    (b) accumulate, stockpile or otherwise keep arms, explosives, munitions,<lf> poisons or weapons for the purpose of the commission of an offence against section 6, whether by that person or by another person;<lf>    (c) train or drill or participate in training or drilling, or be present at<lf> a meeting or assembly of persons with intent to train or drill or to participate in training or drilling, any other person in the use of arms or explosives, or the practice of military exercises, movements or evolutions, for the purpose of preparing that other person to commit an offence against section 6;<lf>    (d) allow himself or herself to be trained or drilled, or be present at a<lf> meeting or assembly of persons with intent to allow himself or herself to be trained or drilled, in the use of arms or explosives, or the practice of military exercises, movements or evolutions, for the purpose of the commission of an offence against section 6;<lf>    (e) give money or goods to, or perform services for, any other person or any<lf> body or association of persons for the purpose of supporting or promoting the commission of an offence against section 6;<lf>    (f) receive or solicit money or goods, or the performance of services, for<lf> the purpose of supporting or promoting the commission of an offence against section 6;<lf>    (g) being the owner, lessee, occupier, agent or superintendent of any<lf> building, room, premises or place, knowingly permit a meeting or assembly of persons to be held in the building, room, premises or place for the purpose of committing, or supporting or promoting the commission of, an offence against paragraph (a), (b), (c), (d), (e) or (f); or<lf>    (h) being the owner, charterer, lessee, operator, agent or master of a<lf> vessel or the owner, charterer, lessee, operator or pilot in charge of an aircraft, knowingly permit the vessel or aircraft to be used for the purpose of committing, or supporting or promoting the commission of, an offence against paragraph (a), (b), (c), (d), (e) or (f).<lf>   (1A) A reference in subsection (1) to the commission of an offence against section 6 is a reference to the doing of an act that would constitute, or would but for subsection 6 (2) constitute, an offence against section 6.<lf>   (1B) A person shall not be taken to have committed an offence against this section merely because of doing an act by way of, or for the purposes of, the provision of aid of a humanitarian nature.<lf>   (2) A person shall not be taken to have committed an offence against this section in respect of the doing of an act outside Australia unless:<lf>    (a) at the time of the doing of that act, the person:<lf>          (i) was an Australian citizen; or<lf>          (ii) not being an Australian citizen, was ordinarily resident in<lf> Australia; or<lf>    (b) the person was present in Australia at any time during the period of one<lf> year immediately preceding the doing of that act and, at any time when the person was so present, his or her presence was for a purpose connected with that act, or for purposes that included such a purpose.<lf>   Penalty: Imprisonment for 10 years. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>CRIMES (FOREIGN INCURSIONS AND RECRUITMENT) ACT 1978 - SECT 8 Recruiting persons to join organizations engaged in hostile activities against foreign governments </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   8\. A person shall not, in Australia, recruit another person to become a member of, or to serve in any capacity with, a body or association of persons the objectives of which are or include any of the objectives referred to in subsection 6 (3).<lf>   Penalty: Imprisonment for 7 years. </lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>CRIMES (FOREIGN INCURSIONS AND RECRUITMENT) ACT 1978 - SECT 9 Recruiting persons to serve in or with an armed force in a foreign State </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   9.(1) A person shall not, in Australia:<lf>    (a) recruit another person to serve in any capacity in or with an armed<lf> force in a foreign State, whether the armed force forms part of the armed forces of the government of that foreign State or otherwise;<lf>    (b) publish an advertisement for the purpose of recruiting persons to serve<lf> in any capacity in or with such an armed force;<lf>    (c) publish an advertisement containing any information:<lf>          (i) relating to the place at which, or the manner in which, persons<lf> may make applications to serve, or obtain information relating to service, in any capacity in or with such an armed force; or<lf>          (ii) relating to the manner in which persons may travel to a foreign<lf> State for the purpose of serving in any capacity in or with such an armed force; or<lf>    (d) do any other act or thing for the purpose of facilitating or promoting<lf> the recruitment of persons to serve in any capacity in or with such an armed force.<lf>   Penalty:<lf>    (a) if the person is a natural person-$20,000 or imprisonment for 7 years,<lf> or both; or<lf>    (b) if the person is a body corporate-$100,000.<lf>   (2) If the Minister has, by instrument signed by the Minister and published in the Gazette, declared that it is in the interests of the defence or international relations of Australia to permit the recruitment in Australia, either generally or in particular circumstances or subject to specified conditions, of persons to serve in or with a specified armed force, or to serve in or with a specified armed force in a particular capacity, subsection (1) does not apply, or does not apply in those circumstances or where those conditions are complied with, as the case may be, to or in relation to recruitment to serve, or the publication of an advertisement containing information with respect to service, in or with that armed force, or in or with that armed force in that capacity, as the case may be.<lf>   (3) If a person recruits another person to enter into a commitment or engagement to serve in any capacity in or with an armed force, the first-mentioned person shall be taken, for the purposes of this section, to recruit that other person to serve in or with that armed force whether or not the commitment or engagement is legally enforceable and whether or not it constitutes a legal or formal enlistment in that force.<lf>   (4) The provisions of section 48 (except paragraphs (1) (a) and (b) and subsection (2)) and of sections 48A, 48B and 49 of the Acts Interpretation Act 1901 apply, by force of this section, to a declaration made under subsection (2) of this section in like manner as those provisions apply to regulations.<lf>   (5) For the purposes of this section, the publication of an item of news shall be deemed to constitute the publication of an advertisement if the publication was procured by the payment of, or by a promise to pay, money or by the provision of, or by a promise to provide, any other consideration. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>CRIMES (FOREIGN INCURSIONS AND RECRUITMENT) ACT 1978 - SECT 9A Mode of trial </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   9A.(1) Subject to subsection (2), a prosecution for an offence against this Act shall be on indictment.<lf>   (2) Where the law of a State or Territory makes provision for a person who pleads guilty to a charge in proceedings for the person's commitment for trial on indictment to be committed to a higher court and dealt with otherwise than on indictment, a person charged in that State or Territory with an offence against this Act may be dealt with in accordance with that law.<lf>   (3) A reference in this section to an offence against this Act includes a reference to an offence against:<lf>    (a) section 6 or 7 of the Crimes Act 1914; or<lf>    (b) subsection 86 (1) of that Act by virtue of paragraph (a) of that<lf> subsection;<lf> that relates to an offence against this Act. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>CRIMES (FOREIGN INCURSIONS AND RECRUITMENT) ACT 1978 - SECT 10 Consent of Attorney-General required for prosecutions </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   10\. (1) Proceedings for the commitment of a person for trial on indictment for an offence against this Act, or for the summary trial of a person for an offence against this Act, shall not be instituted except with the consent in writing of the Attorney-General.<lf>   (2) Notwithstanding that a consent has not been given in relation to the offence in accordance with subsection (1):<lf>    (a) a person may be charged with an offence against this Act;<lf>    (b) a person may be arrested for an offence against this Act, and a warrant<lf> for such an arrest may be issued and executed; and<lf>    (c) a person so charged may be remanded in custody or on bail;<lf> but no further step in proceedings referred to in subsection (1) shall be taken in relation to the offence until such a consent has been given.<lf>   (3) Nothing in subsection (2) prevents the discharge of the accused if proceedings are not continued within a reasonable time.<lf>   (4) A reference in this section to an offence against this Act includes a reference to an offence against:<lf>    (a) section 6, 7 or 7A of the Crimes Act 1914; or<lf>    (b) subsection 86 (1) of that Act by virtue of paragraph (a) of that<lf> subsection;<lf> that relates to an offence against this Act. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>CRIMES (FOREIGN INCURSIONS AND RECRUITMENT) ACT 1978 - SECT 11 Certificates of Ministers </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   11.(1) In a proceeding against a person for an offence against this Act in relation to a foreign State, a certificate by a Minister, stating that a place or an area specified in the certificate is or is in, or on a specified day or during a specified period was or was in, an independent sovereign state is conclusive evidence of the matters stated in the certificate.<lf>   (2) In a proceeding against a person for an offence against this Act, a certificate by a Minister stating that, if the person had done an act specified in the certificate, being an act alleged to constitute the offence, the person would not have been acting in the course of the person's duty to the Commonwealth in relation to the defence of Australia is prima facie evidence of the matters stated in the certificate.<lf>   (3) In a proceeding against a person for an offence against this Act in relation to the government of a foreign State or a part of a foreign State, a certificate by a Minister stating that an authority described in the certificate is, or on a specified day or during a specified period was, in effective governmental control in a specified foreign State, or in a specified part of a foreign State, is prima facie evidence of the matters stated in the certificate.<lf>   (4) A reference in this section to an offence against this Act includes a reference to an offence against:<lf>    (a) section 6, 7 or 7A of the Crimes Act 1914; or<lf>    (b) subsection 86 (1) of that Act by virtue of paragraph (a) of that<lf> subsection;<lf> that relates to an offence against this Act. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
