###  COMMONWEALTH BANK SALE ACT 1995- SECT 1  Short title [_see_ Note 1] 
<dl compact="">

		This Act may be cited as the _Commonwealth Bank Sale Act 1995_.

 </dl>
###  COMMONWEALTH BANK SALE ACT 1995- SECT 2  Commencement [_see_ Note 1] 
<dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Subject to this section, this Act commences on the day on which it

receives the Royal Assent.</dd> <tt>	</tt>(2)	Part 3, and all the items of the Schedule (except items 1, 12, 16, 17, 21, 22, 23, 26, 27, 31, 37 and 48), commence at the transfer time. <dt>(3)</dt><dd>Item 12 of the Schedule commences on a date to be fixed by Proclamation.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>Item 22 of the Schedule commences on the first anniversary of the day on which the transfer time occurs.</dd> <dt>(5)</dt><dd>Item 37 of the Schedule commences at the transfer time or at the beginning of 9 May 1996, whichever is later.</dd> <dt>(6)</dt><dd>Items 1 and 48 of the Schedule commence on the 10th anniversary of the day on which the transfer time occurs. </dd> </dl>
###  COMMONWEALTH BANK SALE ACT 1995- SECT 3  Interpretation 
<dl compact="">

		In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:

 </dl>
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_Bank body_ means:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the Commonwealth Bank, the Commonwealth Development Bank of Australia

Limited or the Commonwealth Bank Officers Superannuation Corporation; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>a body corporate that is a subsidiary (as defined in Division 6 of Part

1.2 of the Corporations Law) of the Commonwealth Bank.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_Commonwealth Bank_ means the Commonwealth Bank of

Australia. _transfer time_ means the time when the Commonwealth ceases to have the status of being the holder of shares in the Commonwealth Bank that carry more than 50% of the total voting rights attached to the voting shares in the Commonwealth Bank.  </dl></dl>

###  COMMONWEALTH BANK SALE ACT 1995- SECT 5  Interpretation 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Unless the contrary intention appears, expressions used in this Division

that are also used in the SRC Act have the same respective meanings as in that

Act.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>In this Division: </dd> </dl></dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_SRC Act_ means the _Safety, Rehabilitation and Compensation ActÂ 1988_.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  COMMONWEALTH BANK SALE ACT 1995- SECT 6  SRC Act continues to apply to Commonwealth Bank etc. for certain purposes 
**

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		If a Bank body ceases to be a Commonwealth authority for the purposes of the

SRC Act at the transfer time, the SRC Act continues to apply in relation to the

Bank body to the extent, and in the manner, set out in sections 7 to 11 of this

Act.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  COMMONWEALTH BANK SALE ACT 1995- SECT 7  SRC Act continues to apply for previous injuries etc. 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The SRC Act continues to apply after the transfer time in relation to:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>injuries suffered by employees of the Bank body before the transfer time;

and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>loss of, or damage to, property incurred by employees of the Bank body

before the transfer time;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

including employees who have stopped being employees of the Bank body before the transfer time.

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>For the purposes of subsection (1), the SRC Act continues to apply as if:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the Bank body continued to be a Commonwealth authority; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the chief executive officer (however described) of the Bank body continued

to be the principal officer of a Commonwealth authority.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

**

###  COMMONWEALTH BANK SALE ACT 1995- SECT 8  Payment of premiums 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>After the transfer time, Division 4A of Part VII of the SRC Act applies to

the Bank body as if:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the Bank body continued to be a Commonwealth authority; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the Bank body were not required to pay a premium under that Division in

respect of the part of a financial year that occurs after the transfer time;

and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>the following word and paragraph were added at the end of subsection

96H(1) of the SRC Act:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

	"; or 	(f)	in the case of an authority&#151;the authority is not required to pay a

premium under this Division in respect of part of a financial year.".

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>If an amount of premium payable by the Bank body in respect of a period

before the transfer time remains unpaid, the amount is a debt due to the

Commonwealth by the Bank body and is payable to Comcare.

</dd> </dl></dl>

**

###  COMMONWEALTH BANK SALE ACT 1995- SECT 9  Part VIIIA of the SRC Act 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		Part VIIIA of the SRC Act continues to apply to the Bank body after the

transfer time as if any licence granted to the Bank body under that Part were

revoked at the transfer time.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  COMMONWEALTH BANK SALE ACT 1995- SECT 10  Refund of licence fee 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>If the Bank body has paid a licence fee under section 107R of the SRC Act

in respect of the licence fee year in which the transfer time occurs, then the

Bank body is entitled to be paid a refund of the amount worked out using the

following formula:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<sub> ![A graphic exists here.](http://www.comlaw.gov.au/Details/C2004C00871/Html/53D45B75779633A6CA256F7100513C1F/$FILE/1202.gif)</sub>

where:

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_Number of refund days_ means the total number of days in

the period beginning at the transfer time and ending on the last day of the

licence fee year in which the transfer time occurs.

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>In this section:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_licence fee year_ means the year beginning on the date of

commencement of a licence granted under section 107J of the SRC Act and ending

on the day before the anniversary of that date.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  COMMONWEALTH BANK SALE ACT 1995- SECT 11  Amendment of declarations 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>If a notice was published in the _Gazette_ before the transfer time

declaring the Bank body to be a body corporate to which the SRC Act applies for

the purposes of the definition of _Commonwealth authority_ in subsection 4(1) of the SRC Act, then the notice has effect after the

transfer time as if it did not contain a reference to the Bank body.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Subsection (1) does not, by implication, prevent the amendment or revocation of the notice. </dd> </dl></dl>

**

###  COMMONWEALTH BANK SALE ACT 1995- SECT 12  Section 128A of the SRC Act 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		Section 128A of the SRC Act continues to apply to the Commonwealth Bank after

the transfer time as if the Commonwealth Bank continued to be a prescribed

Commonwealth authority for the purposes of that section.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  COMMONWEALTH BANK SALE ACT 1995- SECT 13  Notification of amount of salary etc. 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>This section applies to a Bank body that was a Commonwealth authority for

the purposes of the SRC Act immediately before the transfer time.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The chief executive officer (however described) of the Bank body must notify the Commission, within 28 days after the transfer time, of the amount of salary, wages or pay paid to employees of the Bank body in the period starting on 1 July in the financial year in which the transfer time occurred and ending at the transfer time. </dd> </dl></dl>

###  COMMONWEALTH BANK SALE ACT 1995- SECT 15  Deferred benefits 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>This section applies if, immediately before the transfer time, an employee

of a Bank body was a person to whom deferred benefits were applicable under

section 78 of the DFRDB Act.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>For the purposes of Division 3 of Part IX of the DFRDB Act, the employee is taken to continue in public employment, after the transfer time, so long as the employee continues to be employed by a Bank body.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>This section is subject to Division 3 of Part IX of the DFRDB Act. </dd> </dl></dl>

**

###  COMMONWEALTH BANK SALE ACT 1995- SECT 16  Periods of eligible employment 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		If, immediately before the transfer time, a particular period of employment

of a person by a Bank body was a period of eligible employment for the purposes

of Division 3 of Part IX of the DFRDB Act, that period of employment continues

to be a period of eligible employment for the purposes of that Division.

 </dl></dl>

###  COMMONWEALTH BANK SALE ACT 1995- SECT 17  Saving previous operation of section 109 of the Commonwealth Banks Act 
**

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		The repeal of section 109 of the _Commonwealth Banks Act 1959_ does not

affect:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the vesting of property in the Commonwealth Bank under that section before

the repeal; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the incurring of liabilities by the Commonwealth Bank under that section

before the repeal.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

**

###  COMMONWEALTH BANK SALE ACT 1995- SECT 18  Application of bad debt provisions of the Income Tax Assessment Act 
**

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>In applying Subdivision 165-C or 166-C of the _Income Tax Assessment Act 1997_ to decide whether a Bank body can deduct an amount for a bad debt in

the year of income in which the completion time occurred, or in a later year of

income, it must be assumed that the persons who beneficially owned the shares

in the Commonwealth Bank immediately after the completion time were also the

beneficial owners of the shares at all times before the completion time.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>In this section: </dd> </dl></dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_completion time_ means the time when the Commonwealth

ceases to be a shareholder in the Commonwealth Bank.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  COMMONWEALTH BANK SALE ACT 1995- SECT 19  Regulations 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Governor-General may make regulations prescribing matters:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>required or permitted by this Act to be prescribed; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>necessary or convenient to be prescribed for carrying out or giving effect

to this Act.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>In particular, regulations may be made in relation to matters of a

transitional or saving nature arising out of the sale of the Commonwealth's

shares in the Commonwealth Bank, or otherwise arising out of the enactment of

this Act.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>Subsection 48(2) of the _Acts Interpretation Act 1901_ does not apply to regulations that are made under this section and take effect at or after the transfer time. </dd> </dl></dl>

###  COMMONWEALTH BANK SALE ACT 1995- SECT 20  Exemption from certain State and Territory taxes 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>No tax is payable under a law of a State or Territory in respect of an

exempt matter or anything connected with an exempt matter.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>An authorised person may, by signed writing, certify that: </dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a specified matter or thing is an exempt matter; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>a specified thing was done in connection with a specified exempt matter.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>For all purposes and in all proceedings, a certificate under subsection

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>is evidence of the matter stated in the certificate.</dd></dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>A document that appears to be a certificate under subsection (2) is taken to be such a certificate and to have been properly given unless the contrary is established.</dd> <dt>(5)</dt><dd>In this section: </dd> </dl></dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_authorised person_ means:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the Minister; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the Secretary to the Department; or</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>a person who:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>is an SES employee, or acting SES employee, in the Department; and</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>is authorised in writing by the Secretary to the Department for the

purposes of this section.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_exempt matter_ means any of the following:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the sale by the Commonwealth of any of its shares in the Commonwealth Bank

(other than a sale of those shares to the Commonwealth Bank);</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>an agreement relating to a sale that is covered by paragraph&#160;(a);</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>the transfer of any of the Commonwealth's shares in the Commonwealth Bank

to a person other than the Commonwealth Bank;</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>the receipt of money by the Commonwealth in respect of a sale that is

covered by paragraph (a).

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_tax_ means stamp duty, or any other tax, fee, duty, levy or

charge, but does not include:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a fee payable under the Corporations Law of a State or Territory; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>a tax, fee, duty, levy or charge specified in a notice signed by the

Minister and published in the _Gazette_.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>
