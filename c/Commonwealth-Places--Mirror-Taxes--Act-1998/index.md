###  COMMONWEALTH PLACES (MIRROR TAXES) ACT 1998- SECT 1  Short title [_see_ Note 1] 
This Act may be cited as the _Commonwealth Places (Mirror Taxes) Act 1998_.

 
###  COMMONWEALTH PLACES (MIRROR TAXES) ACT 1998- SECT 2  Commencement [_see_ Note 1] 
This Act commences on the day on which it receives the Royal Assent.

 
###  COMMONWEALTH PLACES (MIRROR TAXES) ACT 1998- SECT 3  Definitions 
In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:

 
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_applied law_ means the provisions of a State taxing law that apply

in relation to a Commonwealth place in accordance with this Act. _authority_, in relation to a State, means any of the following:  </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the Governor, a Minister or a member of the Executive Council of the

State;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>a court of the State;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>a person who holds office as a member of a court of the State;</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>a body created by or under the law of the State;</dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>an officer or employee of the State, or of a body referred to in

paragraph&#160;(d).

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_Commonwealth place_ means a place referred to in paragraph 52(i)

of the Constitution, other than the seat of government. _corresponding applied law_, in relation to a State taxing law, means an applied law that corresponds to the State taxing law. _corresponding State taxing law_, in relation to an applied law, means the State taxing law to which the applied law corresponds. _excluded by paragraph 52(i) of the Constitution_ means inapplicable by reason only of the operation of section&#160;52 of the Constitution in relation to Commonwealth places. _in relation to_, when used in relation to a Commonwealth place, means in, or in relation to, the Commonwealth place. _modifications_ includes additions, omissions and substitutions.  _proceedings_ means any proceedings, whether civil or criminal and whether original or appellate. _scheduled law_, in relation to a State, means a law that is specified in Schedule&#160;1 in relation to the State, but does not include any part of such a law that is prescribed by the regulations for the purposes of this definition. _State law_, in relation to a State, means:  </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>any law in force in the State, whether written or unwritten; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>any instrument made or having effect under a law referred to in

paragraph&#160;(a);

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

but does not include a law of the Commonwealth, whether written or unwritten, or an instrument made or having effect under such a law.

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_State taxing law_, in relation to a State, means the following, as

in force from time to time:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a scheduled law of the State;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>a State law that imposes tax and is prescribed by the regulations for the

purposes of this paragraph;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>any other State law of the State, to the extent that it is relevant to the

operation of a law covered by paragraph&#160;(a) or (b).

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

###  COMMONWEALTH PLACES (MIRROR TAXES) ACT 1998- SECT 4  Scope of this Act 
**

 <dl compact="">

		This Act has effect only to the extent that it is an exercise of the

legislative powers of the Parliament under the following provisions of the

Constitution:

 </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>paragraph 52(i);</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>section&#160;73;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>paragraph 77(iii);</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>paragraph 51(xxxix), so far as it relates to paragraph 52(i),

section&#160;73 or paragraph 77(iii).

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

###  COMMONWEALTH PLACES (MIRROR TAXES) ACT 1998- SECT 5  This Act binds the Crown 
**

 <dl compact="">

		This Act binds the Crown in each of its capacities, but does not make the

Crown liable to be prosecuted for an offence.

 </dl>

###  COMMONWEALTH PLACES (MIRROR TAXES) ACT 1998- SECT 7  Operative date for applied laws 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>An applied law does not have effect in relation to an amount that would

(apart from this subsection) have become due for payment before 6&#160;October

1997 under the applied law.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>In determining whether an amount is or was payable under an applied law, it must be assumed that all obligations of any relevant person that arose before 6&#160;October 1997 have been fully and promptly complied with.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>An applied law does not have effect in relation to an amount that would (apart from this subsection) have become due for payment as stamp duty on an instrument made before 6&#160;October 1997.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>In determining whether an amount is or was payable under an applied law relating to stamp duty, it must be assumed that: </dd> </dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>all relevant instruments made before 6&#160;October 1997 were lodged for

assessment of stamp duty; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the stamp duty (if any) on those instruments was assessed and paid.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

###  COMMONWEALTH PLACES (MIRROR TAXES) ACT 1998- SECT 8  Modification of applied laws 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The regulations may prescribe modifications of any applied law.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Treasurer of a State may, by notice in writing, prescribe modifications of the applied laws of the State, other than modifications for the purpose of overcoming a difficulty that arises from the requirements of the Constitution.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>A notice under subsection&#160;(2) is a disallowable instrument for the purposes of section&#160;46A of the _Acts Interpretation Act 1901_.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>Modifications may be made under this section only to the extent that they are necessary or convenient: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>for the purpose of enabling the effective operation of an applied law as a

law of the Commonwealth; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>for the purpose of enabling an applied law to operate so that the combined

tax liability of a taxpayer under:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>the applied law; and</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>the corresponding State taxing law;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		is as nearly as possible the same as the taxpayer's liability would be under

the corresponding State taxing law alone if the Commonwealth places in the

State were not Commonwealth places.

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(5)</dt><dd>Modifications under this section:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>may be expressed to take effect from a date that is earlier than the date

on which the modifications are notified in the _Gazette_; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>may deal with the circumstances in which the modifications apply, and with

matters of a transitional or saving nature.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(6)</dt><dd>To the extent of any inconsistency, modifications under

subsection&#160;(1) prevail over modifications under subsection&#160;(2).

</dd> </dl>

###  COMMONWEALTH PLACES (MIRROR TAXES) ACT 1998- SECT 9  Arrangements with States 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Governor-General may make an arrangement with the Governor of a State

in relation to the exercise or performance of a power, duty or function (not

being a power, duty or function involving the exercise of judicial power) by an

authority of the State under the applied laws of the State.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Where such an arrangement is in force, the power, duty or function may or must, as the case may be, be exercised or performed accordingly.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>The Governor-General may arrange with the Governor of a State for the variation or revocation of an arrangement made under this section in relation to the State.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>An arrangement, variation or revocation under this section must be made by instrument in writing, a copy of which must be published in the _Gazette_. </dd> </dl>

###  COMMONWEALTH PLACES (MIRROR TAXES) ACT 1998- SECT 10  Jurisdiction of courts 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Within the limits of their several jurisdictions, the courts of a State

are invested with federal jurisdiction in all matters arising under an applied

law as having, or as having had, effect in relation to a Commonwealth place.

For this purpose, _limits_ means limits of any kind, whether as to

subject matter or otherwise, but does not include any limitation that exists by

reason of a place being a Commonwealth place.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The jurisdiction conferred by subsection&#160;(1) in relation to matters arising under an applied law is to be exercised in accordance with the applied law.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>Among other things, modifications under section&#160;8&#160;may modify an applied law so that the applied law, as modified, makes provision for and in relation to investing a court of a State with federal jurisdiction, whether within the limits of its jurisdiction or otherwise.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>The jurisdiction with which courts are invested by subsection&#160;(1) or by modifications under section&#160;8, is invested subject to the restriction in subsection&#160;(5) of this section, but not subject to any other conditions or restrictions.</dd> <dt>(5)</dt><dd>If a State law prohibits any appeal from such a court, then an appeal does not lie to the High Court from a decision of that court unless the High Court grants special leave to appeal.</dd> <dt>(6)</dt><dd>Nothing in this Act affects the operation of section&#160;38 of the _Judiciary Act 1903_.</dd> <dt>(7)</dt><dd>Unless the High Court gives special leave to appeal, an appeal does not lie to the High Court from a judgment, decree, order or sentence of: </dd> </dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a Justice of the High Court; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>a federal court other than the High Court; or</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>a court of a State or Territory;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

if any ground relied upon in support of the appeal involves a question as to the operation or interpretation of section&#160;52 of the Constitution in relation to a place (not being the seat of government).

###  COMMONWEALTH PLACES (MIRROR TAXES) ACT 1998- SECT 11  Procedure in proceedings under applied law 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Subject to this Act:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>any proceedings under an applied law (_core proceedings_) must

be instituted and conducted in the same manner as though they were proceedings

under the corresponding State taxing law; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>any other proceedings in relation to the core proceedings (including

declining to proceed further in a prosecution) must also be taken as though the

core proceedings were proceedings under the corresponding State taxing law.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>The trial on indictment of an offence against an applied law must be by

jury.

</dd> </dl>

**

###  COMMONWEALTH PLACES (MIRROR TAXES) ACT 1998- SECT 12  Continuation of proceedings where place found to be a Commonwealth place 
**

<dl compact="">

		If proceedings have been commenced under a State taxing law and the court is

satisfied that:

 </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the State taxing law is excluded by paragraph 52(i) of the Constitution;

and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>an applied law corresponds to the State taxing law;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

then the proceedings must be continued as though they had been commenced under the applied law. 

**

###  COMMONWEALTH PLACES (MIRROR TAXES) ACT 1998- SECT 13  Objection not allowable on ground of duplicate proceedings 
**

<dl compact="">

		In any proceedings under an applied law, an objection must not be allowed

merely on the ground that proceedings have been commenced, or are pending,

under the corresponding State taxing law.

 </dl>

**

###  COMMONWEALTH PLACES (MIRROR TAXES) ACT 1998- SECT 14  Proceedings on certain appeals 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>This section applies to an appeal from a judgment, decree, order or

sentence of a court of a State or Territory in proceedings under a State taxing

law, except where the appeal is an appeal to the High Court.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>If the court is satisfied that: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the State taxing law is excluded by paragraph 52(i) of the Constitution;

and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>an applied law corresponds to the State taxing law;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

then the court must deal with the appeal as though:

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>the proceedings in relation to which the appeal was brought had been

brought under the applied law; and</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>the judgment, decree, order or sentence had been given or made in

proceedings brought under the applied law.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

###  COMMONWEALTH PLACES (MIRROR TAXES) ACT 1998- SECT 15  Grant of pardon, remission etc. 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>If a person is convicted under an applied law of a State, then an

authority of the State may exercise or perform the same powers and functions in

relation to the convicted person as the authority would have been empowered to

exercise or perform under the State laws of the State if the offence had been

committed in the State but not in relation to a Commonwealth place.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Nothing in this section affects any power or function of the Governor-General. </dd> </dl>

**

###  COMMONWEALTH PLACES (MIRROR TAXES) ACT 1998- SECT 16  Certificates about ownership of land 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>In proceedings under an applied law (or purporting to be under an applied

law), a certificate in writing given by an authorised person about any of the

following matters relating to land is evidence of the matters stated in the

certificate:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the ownership of the land, or of an estate or interest in the land, on a

date or during a period specified in the certificate;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the existence and ownership of a right in respect of the land, on a date

or during a period specified in the certificate.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>A document that purports to be a certificate referred to in

subsection&#160;(1) is taken to be such a certificate, and to have been duly

given, unless the contrary is proved.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>In this section: </dd> </dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_authorised person_ means a person who is a delegate, in respect of

any power or function, under section&#160;139 of the _Lands Acquisition Act 1989_.

 </dl></dl>

###  COMMONWEALTH PLACES (MIRROR TAXES) ACT 1998- SECT 17  Extinguishment of causes of action 
**

 <dl compact="">

		If:

 </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>an act or omission gives, or gave, to a person a cause of action under an

applied law and also gives, or gave, to the person a cause of action under the

corresponding State taxing law; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the cause of action under the corresponding State taxing law has been

extinguished;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

then the cause of action under the applied law is also extinguished. 

**

###  COMMONWEALTH PLACES (MIRROR TAXES) ACT 1998- SECT 18  Validation of things purportedly done under a State taxing law 
**

<dl compact="">

		If:

 </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>something purports to have been done in relation to a Commonwealth place

under a State taxing law; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the State taxing law is excluded by paragraph 52(i) of the Constitution;

and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>an applied law corresponded to the State taxing law;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

then that thing is taken to have been done under the applied law.

###  COMMONWEALTH PLACES (MIRROR TAXES) ACT 1998- SECT 19  Instruments referring to State taxing laws 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>This section applies to an instrument or other writing that relates to an

act, matter or thing that has a connection with a Commonwealth place.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>In so far as: </dd> </dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the instrument or writing contains a reference to a State taxing law;

and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the State taxing law is excluded by paragraph 52(i) of the Constitution;

and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>an applied law corresponds to the State taxing law;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

the reference has effect as if it were a reference to the applied law.

###  COMMONWEALTH PLACES (MIRROR TAXES) ACT 1998- SECT 20  Other Commonwealth laws not to apply in relation to applied laws 
**

<dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Subject to this Act, no Commonwealth law (other than an applied law)

applies in relation to:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>any applied law; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>anything done under an applied law.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(1A)</dt><dd>To avoid doubt, Chapter&#160;2 of the _Criminal Code_ does not apply

in relation to, or in relation to anything done under, an applied law.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Subsection&#160;(1) has effect subject to any modifications prescribed by the regulations.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>To the extent that: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a Commonwealth law applies to a State taxing law, or to things done under

a State taxing law; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>there is a corresponding applied law;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

the Commonwealth law also applies to the applied law and to things done under the corresponding applied law.

<dl compact="">

<dt>(4)</dt><dd>If:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a law of the Commonwealth (other than an applied law) contains a reference

to a State taxing law; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>an applied law corresponds to the State taxing law;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

then the reference is taken to include a reference to the applied law.

###  COMMONWEALTH PLACES (MIRROR TAXES) ACT 1998- SECT 21  Commonwealth laws providing exemptions from Commonwealth taxes 
**

<dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>This section applies to a provision (the _exempting provision_) of  a Commonwealth law (other than an applied

law) that is expressed to exempt a person or body from taxes or charges, or

specified taxes or charges, under a law of the Commonwealth.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Unless it expressly provides otherwise, the exempting provision is not to be treated as applying to taxes or charges under an applied law. </dd> </dl>

###  COMMONWEALTH PLACES (MIRROR TAXES) ACT 1998- SECT 22  Saving provision for applied law where place ceases to be a Commonwealth place 
**

<dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>This section applies if an applied law ceases, or ceased, to have effect

in relation to a place at a particular time because the place ceases, or

ceased, to be a Commonwealth place at that time.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The following things are not affected: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the previous operation of the applied law before that time;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>any right, privilege, obligation or liability acquired, accrued or

incurred under the applied law;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>any penalty, forfeiture or punishment incurred in respect of an offence

against the applied law;</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>any investigation, legal proceeding or remedy in respect of any right,

privilege, obligation, liability, penalty, forfeiture or punishment referred to

in paragraph&#160;(b) or (c).

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>Any penalty, forfeiture or punishment referred to in paragraph&#160;(2)(c)

may be imposed as if the applied law had not ceased to have effect.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>An investigation, legal proceeding or remedy referred to in paragraph&#160;(2)(d) may be instituted, continued or enforced as if the applied law had not ceased to have effect.</dd> <dt>(5)</dt><dd>This section is not intended to affect the operation of a State law. </dd> </dl>

###  COMMONWEALTH PLACES (MIRROR TAXES) ACT 1998- SECT 23  Money paid or received under applied law 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Notwithstanding anything in the terms of an applied law, there must be

credited to the Consolidated Revenue Fund all amounts received under an applied

law that are required by section&#160;81 of the Constitution to be so credited.

</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Commonwealth is liable to pay to a State amounts equal to amounts that are credited to the Consolidated Revenue Fund as mentioned in subsection&#160;(1) in relation to an applied law of the State.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>Amounts payable by the Commonwealth under subsection&#160;(2) are to be reduced by amounts paid by the Commonwealth under any applied law of the State concerned. For this purpose, _amounts paid by the Commonwealth_ does not include amounts paid by way of tax.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>The Consolidated Revenue Fund is appropriated for the purpose of: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>payments under this section; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>payments by the Commonwealth under an applied law.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(5)</dt><dd>The _Financial Management and Accountability Act 1997_ does not apply

to amounts received under an applied law.

</dd> </dl>
