### <name>Long Title </name>

## SECT
<sect>                         COMMONWEALTH BANKS ACT 1959-1973<lf> An Act to establish a Commonwealth Banking Corporation and to make provision for the conduct of the business of the Commonwealth Trading Bank of Australia, the Commonwealth Savings Bank of Australia and the Commonwealth Development Bank of Australia.<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name><part><longtitle>COMMONWEALTH BANKS ACT 1959</longtitle>- PART 1</part> </name>
</b>**                                PART I-PRELIMINARY<lf> </lf>
**<b>

### <name><longtitle>COMMONWEALTH BANKS ACT 1959</longtitle>- SECT. 1\. Short title amended; No. 32, 1918, s. 2\. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   1\. This Act may be cited as the Commonwealth Banks Act 1959-1973.*<lf> </lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name><longtitle>COMMONWEALTH BANKS ACT 1959</longtitle>- SECT. 2\. Commencement. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   2\. This Act shall come into operation on the day on which the Reserve Bank Act 1959 comes into operation.*<lf> Section 3 repealed by No. 117, 1973, s. 3.<lf>                          *   *   *   *   *   *   *   *<lf> </lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name><longtitle>COMMONWEALTH BANKS ACT 1959</longtitle>- SECT. 4\. Definitions. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Amended by No. 117, 1973, s. 5.<lf>   4\. In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears-<lf> <lf>   ''Australia'' includes the Territories;<lf> <lf>   ''bank'' means a person carrying on the business of banking, and includes the Trading Bank, the Savings Bank and the Development Bank;<lf> <lf>   ''Executive Committee'' means the Executive Committee of the Board for the Trading Bank, the Executive Committee of the Board for the Savings Bank or the Executive Committee of the Board for the Development Bank;<lf> <lf>   ''officer'' means an officer of the service;<lf> <lf>   ''repealed Act'' means an Act repealed by the Commonwealth Bank Act 1945 or by the Reserve Bank Act 1959;<lf> <lf>   ''statutory office'' means the office of Managing Director or Deputy Managing Director of the Corporation, General Manager of the Trading Bank, General Manager of the Savings Bank or General Manager of the Development Bank;<lf> <lf>   ''the Board'' means the Commonwealth Banking Corporation Board;<lf> <lf>   ''the Commonwealth Bank'' means the Commonwealth Bank of Australia;<lf> <lf>   ''the Corporation'' means the Commonwealth Banking Corporation;<lf> <lf>   ''the Deputy Managing Director'' means the Deputy Managing Director of the Corporation;<lf> <lf>   ''the Development Bank'' means the Commonwealth Development Bank of Australia; <lf> <lf>   ''the Managing Director'' means the Managing Director of the Corporation;<lf> <lf>   ''the Reserve Bank'' means the Reserve Bank of Australia;<lf> <lf>   ''the Savings Bank'' means the Commonwealth Savings Bank of Australia;<lf> <lf>   ''the Service'' means the Commonwealth Banking Corporation Service;<lf> <lf>   ''the Trading Bank'' means the Commonwealth Trading Bank of Australia.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>

### <name><longtitle>COMMONWEALTH BANKS ACT 1959</longtitle>- SECT. 5\. Application to Territories. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Substituted by No. 117, 1973, s. 4.<lf>   5\. Subject to section 5A, this Act extends to all the Territories.<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name><longtitle>COMMONWEALTH BANKS ACT 1959</longtitle>- SECT. 5A. Cessation of application to Territory. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Inserted by No. 117, 1973, s. 4.<lf>   5A. (1) The Treasurer may, by notice published in the Gazette, declare that, on a date specified in the notice, this Act shall cease to extend to an external Territory specified in the notice,* and, on and after the date specified in such a notice, this Act, other than sub-section (2) of this section, does not extend to the Territory so specified and a reference in this Act, other than this section, to a Territory does not include a reference to the Territory so specified.<lf> 

  (2) Section 8 of the Acts Interpretation Act 1901-1973 applies in relation to a notice published under this section as if the notice were an Act repealing this Act to the extent that, immediately before the date specified in the notice, this Act extended to the Territory specified in the notice.<lf> </lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name><longtitle>COMMONWEALTH BANKS ACT 1959</longtitle>- SECT. 6\. This Act subject to Banking Act. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   6\. This Act has effect subject to the Banking Act 1959 and to the regulations under that Act.<lf> </lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name><longtitle>COMMONWEALTH BANKS ACT 1959</longtitle>- SECT. 7\. The Commonwealth Banking Corporation. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>                 PART  II-THE  COMMONWEALTH  BANKING  CORPORATION<lf>   7\. (1) There shall be a Commonwealth Banking Corporation.<lf> 

  (2) The Corporation-<lf> <lf>   (a)  is a body corporate;<lf> <lf>   (b)  shall have a seal; and<lf> <lf>   (c)  is capable of acquiring, holding and disposing of real and personal property and of suing and being sued.<lf> <p>  (3) The Corporation shall provide and make available to the Trading Bank, the Savings Bank and the Development Bank such officers and employees as are necessary for efficiently conducting the business of each of those banks.<lf> </lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name><longtitle>COMMONWEALTH BANKS ACT 1959</longtitle>- SECT. 8\. Establishment of Commonwealth Banking Corporation Board. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   8\. There shall be a Commonwealth Banking Corporation Board, which shall be constituted as provided by Part III.<lf> </lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name><longtitle>COMMONWEALTH BANKS ACT 1959</longtitle>- SECT. 9\. Functions of Commonwealth Banking Corporation Board. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   9\. (1) Subject to this Act, the Board has power to determine the policy of the Corporation, of the Trading Bank, of the Savings Bank and of the Development Bank, in relation to any matter, and to control the affairs of the Corporation and of each of those banks.<lf> 

  (2) It is the duty of the Board, within the limits of its powers, to ensure that the policy of the Corporation, and the banking policy of the Trading Bank, of the Savings Bank and of the Development Bank, are directed to the greatest advantage of the people of Australia and have due regard to the stability and balanced development of the Australian economy.<lf> </lf>
</lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name><longtitle>COMMONWEALTH BANKS ACT 1959</longtitle>- SECT. 10\. Executive Committees. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   10\. (1) There shall be an Executive Committee of the Board for the Trading Bank, an Executive Committee of the Board for the Savings Bank and an Executive Committee of the Board for the Development Bank, each of which shall be constituted as provided by Part III.<lf> 

  (2) It is the duty of the Executive Committee for a bank to take such action as is necessary to ensure that effect is given by that bank to the policy of that bank as determined under this Part and to any directions given by the Board in relation to the affairs of that bank.<lf> </lf>
</lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name><longtitle>COMMONWEALTH BANKS ACT 1959</longtitle>- SECT. 11\. Differences of opinion on questions of policy. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   11\. (1) The Board shall, from time to time, inform the Government of-<lf> <lf>   (a)  any policy of the Corporation which, in the opinion of the Board, should be made known to the Government; and<lf> <lf>   (b)  the banking policy of the Trading Bank, the Savings Bank and the Development Bank.<lf> 

  (2) In the event of a difference of opinion between the Government and the Board whether any policy referred to in the last preceding sub-section is directed to the greatest advantage of the people of Australia and has due regard to the stability and balanced development of the Australian economy, the Treasurer and the Board shall endeavour to reach agreement.<lf> <p>  (3) If the Treasurer and the Board are unable to reach agreement, the Board shall forthwith furnish to the Treasurer a statement in relation to the matter in respect of which the difference of opinion has arisen.<lf> <p>  (4) The Treasurer may then submit a recommendation to the Governor-General, and the Governor-General, acting with the advice of the Federal Executive Council, may, by order, determine the policy to be adopted by the Corporation, the Trading Bank, the Savings Bank or the Development Bank, as the case may be, but so that the policy so determined is not inconsistent with the Banking Act 1959, with the regulations under that Act or with the monetary and banking policy of the Reserve Bank.<lf> <p>  (5) The Treasurer shall inform the Board of the policy so determined and shall at the same time inform the Board that the Government accepts responsibility for the adoption by the Corporation, the Trading Bank, the Savings Bank or the Development Bank, as the case may be, of that policy and will take such action (if any) within its powers as the Government considers to be necessary by reason of the adoption of that policy.<lf> <p>  (6) The Board shall thereupon ensure that effect is given to the policy determined by the order and shall, if the order so requires, continue to ensure that effect is given to that policy while the order remains in operation.<lf> Amended by No. 117, 1973, s. 5\. <p>  (7) The Treasurer shall cause to be laid before each House of the Parliament, within fifteen sitting days of that House after the Treasurer has informed the Board of the policy determined under sub-section (4)-<lf> <lf>   (a)  a copy of the order determining the policy;<lf> <lf>   (b)  a statement by the Government in relation to the matter in respect of which the difference of opinion arose; and<lf> <lf>   (c)  a copy of the statement furnished to the Treasurer by the Board under sub-section (3).<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name><longtitle>COMMONWEALTH BANKS ACT 1959</longtitle>- SECT. 12\. Management of the Corporation. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   12\. (1) There shall be a Managing Director and a Deputy Managing Director of the Corporation, who shall be appointed and hold office as provided by Part III. <lf> Amended by No. 117, 1973, s. 5\. 

  (2) Subject to sub-section (1) of section 9, the Corporation shall be managed by the Managing Director.<lf> <p>  (3) The Deputy Managing Director shall perform such duties as the Managing Director directs and, in the event of a vacancy in the office of Managing Director, the Deputy Managing Director shall perform the duties of the Managing Director and shall have and may exercise the powers and functions of the Managing Director.<lf> </lf></p></lf>
</lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name><longtitle>COMMONWEALTH BANKS ACT 1959</longtitle>- SECT. 13\. Managing Director and Secretary to Treasury to establish liaison. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   13\. The Managing Director and the Secretary to the Department of the Treasury shall establish a close liaison with each other and shall keep each other fully informed on all matters which jointly concern the Corporation, the Trading Bank, the Savings Bank or the Development Bank and the Department of the Treasury.<lf> </lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name><longtitle>COMMONWEALTH BANKS ACT 1959</longtitle>- SECT. 14\. Membership of Board. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>             PART  III-THE  COMMONWEALTH  BANKING  CORPORATION  BOARD<lf>   14\. (1) The Commonwealth Banking Corporation Board shall consist of-<lf> <lf>   (a)  the Managing Director;<lf> <lf>   (b)  the Deputy Managing Director;<lf> <lf>   (c)  the Secretary to the Department of the Treasury; and<lf> <lf>   (d)  eight other members, who shall be appointed by the Governor-General in accordance with this section.<lf> 

  (2) A member of the Board appointed under paragraph (d) of the last preceding sub-section-<lf> <lf>   (a)  shall, subject to this section, be appointed for a period of five years but is eligible for re-appointment; and<lf> <lf>   (b)  holds office subject to good behaviour.<lf> Sub-section (3) omitted by No. 216, 1973, s. 3.<lf>                          *   *   *   *   *   *   *   *<lf> Amended by No. 117, 1973, s. 5\. <p>  (4) In the event of a member of the Board appointed under paragraph (d) of sub-section (1) ceasing to hold office before the termination of his period of office, the period of appointment of a person appointed in his place shall be the remainder of the period of office of the member so ceasing to hold office.<lf> <p>  (5) A member of the Board shall be paid by the Corporation such remuneration (if any) as the Governor-General determines.*<lf> </lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>

### <name><longtitle>COMMONWEALTH BANKS ACT 1959</longtitle>- SECT. 15\. Declaration by members. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   15\. A member of the Board shall, before entering upon his duties or exercising any power under this Act, make, before a Justice of the Peace or a Commissioner for taking Affidavits, an oath or affirmation of allegiance in accordance with the form in the Schedule to the Constitution and a declaration of secrecy in accordance with the prescribed form.<lf> </lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name><longtitle>COMMONWEALTH BANKS ACT 1959</longtitle>- SECT. 16\. Disqualifications from membership. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Sub-section (1) amended by No. 117, 1973, s. 5.<lf>   16\. (1) A person who is-<lf> <lf>   (a)  a member of the Reserve Bank Board appointed under paragraph (d) of sub-section (1) of section 14 of the Reserve Bank Act 1959;<lf> <lf>   (b)  the Governor, the Deputy Governor or an officer or employee of the Reserve Bank;<lf> <lf>   (c)  an officer or employee of the Corporation (other than the Managing Director or the Deputy Managing Director);<lf> <lf>   (d)  the General Manager of the Trading Bank, of the Savings Bank or of the Development Bank;<lf> <lf>   (e)  an officer of the Public Service of the Commonwealth (other than the Secretary to the Department of the Treasury); or<lf> <lf>   (f)  a director, officer or employee of a corporation the business of which is wholly or mainly that of banking,<lf> is not capable of appointment, or of continuing to act, as a member of the Board.<lf> Added by No. 58, 1966, s. 2\. 

  (2) The last preceding sub-section does not disqualify a person from appointment, or from continuing to act, as a member of the Board by reason only that he is a member of the Development Bank Board established by the Ordinance of the Territory of Papua and New Guinea known as the Papua and New Guinea Development Bank Ordinance 1965.<lf> </lf>

### <name><longtitle>COMMONWEALTH BANKS ACT 1959</longtitle>- SECT. 17\. Vacation of office. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Sub-section (1) amended by No. 117, 1973, s. 5.<lf>   17\. (1) If a member of the Board appointed under paragraph (d) of sub-section (1) of section 14-<lf> <lf>   (a)  becomes permanently incapable of performing his duties;<lf> <lf>   (b)  becomes bankrupt, applies to take the benefit of any law for the relief of bankrupt or insolvent debtors, compounds with his creditors or makes an assignment of his remuneration for their benefit;<lf> <lf>   (c)  resigns his office by writing under his hand addressed to the Governor-General;<lf> <lf>   (d)  is absent, except on leave granted by the Treasurer, from all meetings of the Board held during two consecutive months or during any three months in any period of twelve months; or<lf> <lf>   (e)  fails to comply with his obligations under section 24,<lf> the Governor-General shall terminate his appointment.<lf> 

  (2) In this section, ''month'' means any of the twelve months of the year.<lf> </lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name><longtitle>COMMONWEALTH BANKS ACT 1959</longtitle>- SECT. 18\. Chairman and Deputy Chairman of the Board. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Sub-section (1) amended by No. 117, 1973, s. 5.<lf>   18\. (1) The Governor-General shall appoint one of the members of the Board appointed under paragraph (d) of sub-section (1) of section 14 to be Chairman of the Board, and another such member to be Deputy Chairman of the Board, for such period as the Governor-General determines.<lf> 

  (2) The Chairman or the Deputy Chairman of the Board holds office as Chairman or Deputy Chairman until the expiration of the period of his appointment or until he ceases to be a member of the Board, whichever first happens.<lf> <p>  (3) The Chairman or the Deputy Chairman of the Board may resign his office as Chairman or Deputy Chairman by writing under his hand addressed to the Governor-General.<lf> </lf></p></lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name><longtitle>COMMONWEALTH BANKS ACT 1959</longtitle>- SECT. 19\. Meetings of Board. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   19\. (1) The Board shall meet at such times and places as the Board determines or as the Chairman or the Deputy Chairman directs.<lf> 

  (2) The Chairman shall preside at all meetings of the Board at which he is present and, in the absence of the Chairman, the Deputy Chairman shall preside. <lf> <p>  (3) Six members, of whom the Chairman or the Deputy Chairman shall be one, form a quorum at a meeting of the Board.<lf> <p>  (4) Questions arising at a meeting of the Board shall be decided by a majority of the votes of the members present and voting.<lf> <p>  (5) The member presiding at a meeting of the Board shall have a deliberative vote and, in the event of an equality of votes, shall also have a casting vote. <lf> </lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name><longtitle>COMMONWEALTH BANKS ACT 1959</longtitle>- SECT. 20\. Membership of Executive Committees. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   20\. (1) Each Executive Committee shall consist of the Managing Director and four other members of the Board.<lf> 

  (2) A member of an Executive Committee (other than the Managing Director) shall be appointed by the Treasurer after consultation with the Board and shall hold office during the pleasure of the Treasurer or until he ceases to be a member of the Board.<lf> <p>  (3) The Chairman of the Board shall not be appointed as a member of an Executive Committee but may attend a meeting of an Executive Committee.<lf> <p>  (4) The Secretary to the Department of the Treasury shall not be appointed as a member of the Executive Committee for the Trading Bank or of the Executive Committee for the Development Bank.<lf> <p>  (5) If the Deputy Managing Director is not a member of an Executive Committee, he may attend a meeting of the Committee at which the Managing Director is not present as the deputy of the Managing Director and, while so attending, shall be deemed to be a member of the Committee.<lf> </lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name><longtitle>COMMONWEALTH BANKS ACT 1959</longtitle>- SECT. 21\. Meetings of Executive Committees. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   21\. (1) The Board may give such directions as it thinks fit with respect to the procedure to be followed at and in relation to meetings of an Executive Committee.<lf> 

  (2) Directions given by the Board under the last preceding subsection in relation to an Executive Committee may include directions with respect to-<lf> <lf>   (a)  the convening of meetings of the Executive Committee;<lf> <lf>   (b)  the number of members of the Executive Committee to constitute a quorum; <lf> <lf>   (c)  the appointment of a member of the Executive Committee to preside at meetings of the Executive Committee; and<lf> <lf>   (d)  the manner in which questions arising at a meeting of the Executive Committee shall be decided.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
