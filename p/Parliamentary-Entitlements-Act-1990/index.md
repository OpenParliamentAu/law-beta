###  PARLIAMENTARY ENTITLEMENTS ACT 1990<part>Part 1&#151;Preliminary </part>
**
###  PARLIAMENTARY ENTITLEMENTS ACT 1990- SECT 1  Short title [_see_ Note 1] 
<dl compact="">

		This Act may be cited as the _Parliamentary Entitlements Act 1990_.

 </dl>
###  PARLIAMENTARY ENTITLEMENTS ACT 1990- SECT 2  Commencement [_see_ Note 1] 
<dl compact="">

		This Act commences on the day on which it receives the Royal Assent.

 </dl>
###  PARLIAMENTARY ENTITLEMENTS ACT 1990- SECT 3  Definitions 
<dl compact="">

		In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:

 </dl>
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_amount_, in relation to a benefit, includes any quantity,

frequency or other limitation expressed in numbers (other than numbers of

persons). _benefit_ means a benefit provided by or under this Act. _cost of travel_ means the cost of fares, accommodation, meals and incidentals. _dependent child_, in relation to a Senior Officer, means:  </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a person under 16 who:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>is in the custody, care and control of the Officer or is a person to whom

the Officer has access; or</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>where no other person has the custody, care and control of the person&#151;is

wholly or substantially in the care and control of the Officer; or

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>a person who is:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>at least 16 but under 25;</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>receiving full-time education at a school, college or university; and</dd>

<dt>(iii)</dt><dd>wholly or substantially dependent upon the Officer.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_incidentals_ includes the cost of transport. _leader of a minority party_ means the leader of a recognised non-Government party of at least 5 members, but does not include an Opposition Office Holder, and _deputy leader of a minority party_ has a corresponding meaning. _member_ means a member of either House of the Parliament. _Opposition Office Holder_ means:  </dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the Leader or Deputy Leader of the Opposition in the House of

Representatives; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the Leader or Deputy Leader of the Opposition in the Senate.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_Parliamentary office-holder_ means a person holding a particular

office, or performing particular functions, in or in relation to the Parliament

or either House of the Parliament. _Presiding Officer_ means the President of the Senate or the Speaker of the House of Representatives. _Scheduled benefit_ means a benefit set out in Schedule 1\. _Senior Officer_ means a Minister, an Opposition Office Holder or a Presiding Officer. _spouse_, in relation to a member, includes a person who is living with the member as the spouse of the member on a genuine domestic basis although not legally married to the member. _year_ means a financial year, and _annual_ has a corresponding meaning.  </dl></dl>

###  PARLIAMENTARY ENTITLEMENTS ACT 1990- SECT 4  Entitlement to benefits 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Members are entitled to benefits as set out in Part 1 of Schedule 1.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Parliamentary office-holders and Ministers are entitled to benefits as set out in Part 2 of Schedule 1.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>Benefits under subsection (2) are in addition to benefits under subsection (1).</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>Benefits under this Act do not limit: </dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>benefits under any other law; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>benefits afforded by the Government to a Minister for the purpose of

carrying out functions as a Minister.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

**

###  PARLIAMENTARY ENTITLEMENTS ACT 1990- SECT 5  Entitlement to additional benefits 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Members, Parliamentary office-holders and Ministers are entitled to such

additional benefits as are:

</dd> </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>determined by the Remuneration Tribunal under section 7 of the

_Remuneration Tribunal Act 1973_; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>prescribed by the regulations.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>Subsection (1) does not extend to a benefit in the nature of remuneration.

</dd> </dl></dl>

###  PARLIAMENTARY ENTITLEMENTS ACT 1990- SECT 7  Calculation of benefits <center>[Back to Top](#top)</center> 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>A person is entitled to a benefit (other than a benefit for a

Parliamentary office-holder or a Minister) during the whole time when the

person is entitled to an allowance under the _Parliamentary Allowances Act 1952_.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>A person is entitled to a benefit for a Parliamentary office-holder only so long as the person is a Parliamentary office-holder.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>A person is entitled to a benefit for a Minister only so long as the person is a Minister.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>A person may make use of the full annual amount of a benefit before the end of the relevant financial year. </dd> </dl></dl>

**

###  PARLIAMENTARY ENTITLEMENTS ACT 1990- SECT 8  Validation 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		Where a benefit of a kind set out in Schedule 1 has been used by, or made

available to, a person at any time before the commencement of this Act, the

person is taken, by force of this section, to have been entitled to that

benefit as if this Act had been in force at that time.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  PARLIAMENTARY ENTITLEMENTS ACT 1990- SECT 9  Alteration of Schedule 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>A Scheduled benefit may be varied or omitted by determination by the

Remuneration Tribunal under section 7 of the _Remuneration Tribunal Act 1973_.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>A Scheduled benefit may be varied or omitted by the regulations.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>A determination or regulations made under this section may make such consequential and transitional provisions as are necessary. </dd> </dl></dl>

**

###  PARLIAMENTARY ENTITLEMENTS ACT 1990- SECT 10  Conflict between regulations and determination 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Where the regulations are inconsistent with a determination by the

Remuneration Tribunal under paragraph 5(1)(a) or subsection 9(1), the

regulations prevail and the determination is void to the extent of the

inconsistency.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Where the regulations are intended to cover the whole field in relation to a particular subject matter, then, for the purposes of subsection (1), any provision of a determination that deals with that subject matter is taken to be inconsistent with the regulations, whether or not they are capable of operating concurrently. </dd> </dl></dl>

**

###  PARLIAMENTARY ENTITLEMENTS ACT 1990- SECT 11  Appropriation 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		The costs of benefits are to be paid out of the Consolidated Revenue Fund,

which is appropriated accordingly.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  PARLIAMENTARY ENTITLEMENTS ACT 1990- SECT 12  Regulations 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

		The Governor-General may make regulations for the purposes of paragraph

5(1)(b) and section 9.

 </dl></dl>

**

###  PARLIAMENTARY ENTITLEMENTS ACT 1990- SECT 13  Amendment of Remuneration Tribunal Act 
**

 <dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The _Remuneration Tribunal Act 1973_ is amended as set out in

Schedule 2.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>A determination under section 7 of the _Remuneration Tribunal Act 1973_ and in force immediately before the commencement of this Act continues in force as if it had been made under that Act as amended by this Act. </dd> </dl></dl>
