### <name>PARLIAMENTARY ALLOWANCES ACT 1952-1973 - SECT. 1\. Short title. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> <lf> Short title amended; No. 32, 1918, s. 2.<lf>   1\. This Act may be cited as the Parliamentary Allowances Act 1952-1973.*<lf> </lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY ALLOWANCES ACT 1952-1973 - SECT. 2\. Commencement. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Amended by No. 14, 1973, s. 7.<lf>   2\. This Act shall be deemed to have come into operation on the 1st January, 1952.<lf> Section 3 repealed by No. 216, 1973, s. 3.<lf>                          *   *   *   *   *   *   *   *<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY ALLOWANCES ACT 1952-1973 - SECT. 3A. Interpretation. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Inserted by No. 22, 1970, s. 3.<lf>   3A. For the purposes of this Act, the day of the election or re-election of a Senator or a member of the House of Representatives shall be deemed to be-<lf> <lf>   (a)  where he was elected or re-elected as the case may be, as the result of a polling-the day fixed for that polling; or<lf> <lf>   (b)  in any other case-the day on which he was declared duly elected.<lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY ALLOWANCES ACT 1952-1973 - SECT. 4\. Allowances to Senators. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Sub-section (1) amended by No. 29, 1956, s. 3; No. 19, 1959, s. 3; No. 70, 1964, s. 3; No. 93, 1966, s. 3; No. 101, 1968, s. 4; and No. 14, 1973, s. 7.<lf>   4\. (1) Each senator shall receive an allowance at the rate of Fourteen thousand five hundred dollars a year.<lf> Amended by No. 29, 1956, s. 3; No. 19, 1959, s. 3; No. 70, 1964, s. 3; No. 93, 1966, s. 3; No. 101, 1968, s. 4; No. 22, 1970, s. 4; and No. 14, 1973, s. 7\. 

  (2) Each senator shall receive, in addition to the allowance referred to in the last preceding sub-section, an allowance, in respect of the expenses of discharging his duties, at the rate of Four thousand one hundred dollars a year. <lf> <p>  (3) The allowances to a senator shall be reckoned-<lf> <lf>   (a)  in the case of a senator chosen at the first election after a dissolution of the Senate-from and including the day of his election;<lf> <lf>   (b)  in the case of a senator chosen to fill a place which is to become vacant in rotation-from and including the first day of July following the day of his election; and<lf> <lf>   (c)  in the case of a senator chosen or appointed to fill a casual vacancy-from and including the day of his choice or appointment.<lf> Amended by No. 22, 1970, s. 4\. <p>  (4) The allowances to a senator who is a member of the Senate immediately before a dissolution of the Senate and is a candidate at the next following Senate election shall be reckoned to and including the day next before the day fixed for the polling at that election.<lf> </lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY ALLOWANCES ACT 1952-1973 - SECT. 5\. Allowances to members of House of Representatives. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Sub-section (1) amended by No. 29, 1956, s. 4; No. 19, 1959, s. 4; No. 70, 1964, s. 3; No. 93, 1966, s. 3; No. 101, 1968, s. 4; and No. 14, 1973, s. 7.<lf>   5\. (1) Each member of the House of Representatives shall receive an allowance at the rate of Fourteen thousand five hundred dollars a year.<lf> Substituted by No. 29, 1956, s. 4; amended by No. 19, 1959, s. 4; No. 70, 1964, s. 3; No. 93, 1966, s. 3; No. 101, 1968, s. 4; and No. 14, 1973, s. 7\. 

  (2) A member of the House of Representatives shall receive, in addition to the allowance referred to in the last preceding sub-section, an allowance, in respect of the expenses of discharging his duties, at the rate of Four thousand one hundred dollars a year.<lf> Sub-section (3) omitted by No. 29, 1956, s. 4.<lf>                          *   *   *   *   *   *   *   *<lf> <p>  (4) The allowances to a member of the House of Representatives shall be reckoned from and including the day of his election.<lf> Amended by No. 22, 1970, s. 5\. <p>  (5) The allowances to a member of the House of Representatives who is a member of that House immediately before the dissolution or expiration of that House and is a candidate for election as a member of the House of Representatives at the next following general election shall be reckoned to and including the day next before the day of his re- election or, if he is not re-elected, the day next before the day fixed for the polling at that general election.<lf> </lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY ALLOWANCES ACT 1952-1973 - SECT. 6\. Allowances to President, Speaker and Chairmen of Committees. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Sub-section (1) amended by No. 19, 1959, s. 5; No. 70, 1964, s. 3; No. 93, 1966, s. 3; No. 101, 1968, s. 4; and No. 14, 1973, s. 7.<lf>   6\. (1) There are payable to the President of the Senate and to the Speaker of the House of Representatives allowances at the rate of Ten thousand five hundred dollars a year each.<lf> Amended by No. 19, 1959, s. 5; No. 70, 1964, s. 3; No. 93, 1966, s. 3; No. 101, 1968, s. 4; and No. 14, 1973, s. 7\. 

  (2) There are payable to the President of the Senate and to the Speaker of the House of Representatives allowances, in respect of the expenses of discharging the duties of their respective offices, at the rate of Four thousand two hundred and fifty dollars a year each.<lf> Amended by No. 19, 1959, s. 5; No. 70, 1964, s. 3; No. 93, 1966, s. 3; No. 101, 1968, s. 4; and No. 14, 1973, s. 7\. <p>  (3) There are payable to the Chairman of Committees of the Senate and to the Chairman of Committees of the House of Representatives allowances at the rate of Four thousand dollars a year each.<lf> Substituted by No. 101, 1968, s. 3\. <p>  (4) The allowances to a President of the Senate or to a Chairman of Committees of the Senate who is re-elected at an election of the Senate shall be reckoned to and including the day next before the day on which his successor as President or Chairman, as the case may be, is chosen.<lf> Substituted by No. 101, 1968, s. 3\. <p>  (5) The allowances to a Speaker of the House of Representatives or to a Chairman of Committees of that House who is re-elected at a general election of that House shall be reckoned to and including the day next before the day on which his successor as Speaker or Chairman, as the case may be, is chosen.<lf> </lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY ALLOWANCES ACT 1952-1973 - SECT. 7\. Allowances to Leaders of Opposition. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Sub-section (1) amended by No. 19, 1959, s. 6; No. 70, 1964, s. 3; No. 93, 1966, s. 3; No. 101, 1968, s. 4; and No. 14, 1973, s. 7.<lf>   7\. (1) There is payable to the Leader of the Opposition in the Senate an allowance at the rate of Seven thousand five hundred dollars a year and to the Leader of the Opposition in the House of Representatives an allowance at the rate of Ten thousand five hundred dollars a year.<lf> Amended by No. 19, 1959, s. 6; No. 70, 1964, s. 3; No. 93, 1966, s. 3; No. 101, 1968, s. 4; and No. 14, 1973, s. 7\. 

  (2) There is payable to the Leader of the Opposition in the Senate an allowance, in respect of the expenses of discharging the duties of his office, at the rate of Four thousand two hundred and fifty dollars a year and to the Leader of the Opposition in the House of Representatives an allowance, in respect of the expenses of discharging the duties of his office, at the rate of Four thousand eight hundred and seventy-five dollars a year.<lf> </lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY ALLOWANCES ACT 1952-1973 - SECT. 7A. Allowances to Deputy Leader of Opposition in Senate. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Inserted by No. 29, 1956, s. 5.<lf> Sub-section (1) amended by No. 19, 1959, s. 7; No. 70, 1964, s. 3; No. 93, 1966, s. 3; No. 101, 1968, s. 4; and No. 14, 1973, s. 7.<lf>   7A. (1) There is payable to the Deputy Leader of the Opposition in the Senate an allowance at the rate of Three thousand two hundred dollars a year.<lf> Amended by No. 70, 1964, s. 3; No. 93, 1966, s. 3; No. 101, 1968, s. 4; and No. 14, 1973, s. 7\. 

  (2) There is payable to the Deputy Leader of the Opposition in the Senate an allowance, in respect of the expenses of discharging the duties of his office, at the rate of Nine hundred dollars a year.<lf> </lf>
</lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY ALLOWANCES ACT 1952-1973 - SECT. 8\. Allowances to Deputy Leader of Opposition in House of Representatives. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Sub-section (1) amended by No. 19, 1959, s. 8; No. 70, 1964, s. 3; No. 93, 1966, s. 3; No. 101, 1968, s. 4; and No. 14, 1973, s. 7.<lf>   8\. (1) There is payable to the Deputy Leader of the Opposition in the House of Representatives an allowance at the rate of Seven thousand five hundred dollars a year.<lf> Amended by No. 19, 1959, s. 8; No. 70, 1964, s. 3; No. 93, 1966, s. 3; No. 101, 1968, s. 4; and No. 14, 1973, s. 7\. 

  (2) There is payable to the Deputy Leader of the Opposition in the House of Representatives an allowance, in respect of the expenses of discharging the duties of his office, at the rate of Four thousand two hundred and fifty dollars a year.<lf> </lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY ALLOWANCES ACT 1952-1973 - SECT. 8A. Allowance to leader of Second non-Government party in Senate. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Inserted by No. 101, 1968, s. 5; amended by No. 14, 1973, s. 7.<lf>   8A. There is payable to the leader in the Senate of the second non-Government recognized political party in the Senate an allowance at the rate of Two thousand dollars a year, and a further allowance, in respect of the expenses of discharging the duties of his office, at the rate of Seven hundred and fifty dollars a year.<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY ALLOWANCES ACT 1952-1973 - SECT. 9\. Allowance to leader of third party in House of Representatives. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Sub-section (1) amended by No. 19, 1959, s. 9; No. 70, 1964, s. 3; No. 93, 1966, s. 3; No. 101, 1968, s. 4; and No. 14, 1973, s. 7.<lf>   9\. (1) There is payable to the leader in the House of Representatives (not being the Leader of the Opposition or the Deputy Leader of the Opposition) of a recognized political party not less than ten members of which are members of the House of Representatives and of which no member is a Minister of State an allowance at the rate of Five thousand dollars a year.<lf> Added by No. 19, 1959, s. 9; amended by No. 70, 1964, s. 3; No. 93, 1966, s. 3; No. 101, 1968, s. 4; and No. 14, 1973, s. 7\. 

  (2) There is payable to a member of the House of Representatives who is in receipt of an allowance under the last preceding sub-section an allowance, in respect of the expenses of discharging the duties of his office, at the rate of One thousand five hundred dollars a year.<lf> </lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY ALLOWANCES ACT 1952-1973 - SECT. 10\. Allowances to Whips. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Sub-section (1) amended by No. 19, 1959, s. 10; No. 70, 1964, s. 3; No. 93, 1966, s. 3; No. 101, 1968, s. 4; and No. 14, 1973, s. 7.<lf>   10\. (1) There are payable to the Government Whip in the Senate and to the Opposition Whip in the Senate allowances at the rate of Two thousand dollars a year each.<lf> Amended by No. 19, 1959, s. 10; No. 70, 1964, s. 3; No. 93, 1966, s. 3; No. 101, 1968, s. 4; and No. 14, 1973, s. 7\. 

  (2) There is payable to the Government Whip in the House of Representatives an allowance at the rate of Two thousand five hundred dollars a year.<lf> Amended by No. 19, 1959, s. 10; No. 70, 1964, s. 3; No. 93, 1966, s. 3; No. 101, 1968, s. 4; and No.14, 1973, s. 7\. <p>  (3) There is payable to the Whip in the House of Representatives of each recognized political party which has not less than ten members in that House, but not including the Government Whip, an allowance at the rate of Two thousand dollars a year.<lf> Added by No. 14, 1973, s. 4\. <p>  (4) There are payable to the senator specified by the Leader of the Government in the Senate as the senator assisting the Government Whip in the Senate and to the senator specified by the Leader of the Opposition in the Senate as the senator assisting the Opposition Whip in the Senate allowances at the rate of Five hundred dollars a year each.<lf> Added by No. 14, 1973, s. 4\. <p>  (5) There are payable to the member of the House of Representatives specified by the Prime Minister as the member assisting the Government Whip in that House and to the member of the House of Representatives specified by the Leader of the Opposition in that House as the member assisting the Opposition Whip in that House allowances at the rate of Five hundred dollars a year each.<lf> </lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY ALLOWANCES ACT 1952-1973 - SECT. 11\. Certain allowances to be additional to allowances as members. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Amended by No. 29, 1956, s. 6; and No. 14, 1973, s. 7.<lf>   11\. The allowances provided for by sections 6 to 10 are in addition to the allowances provided for by sections 4 and 5.<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY ALLOWANCES ACT 1952-1973 - SECT. 11A. Allowances for travelling expenses and expenses of living in Canberra. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Inserted by No. 14, 1973, s. 5.<lf>   11A. The Governor-General may make regulations providing for the payment to persons referred to in this Act (not being persons who are Ministers of State) of allowances in respect of travelling expenses and in respect of expenses of living in Canberra.<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY ALLOWANCES ACT 1952-1973 - SECT. 12\. Appropriation. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> Amended by No. 14, 1973, s. 6.<lf>   12\. The allowances provided for by this Act (other than any allowances provided for by regulations under section 11A) are payable out of the Consolidated Revenue Fund, which is appropriated accordingly.<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY ALLOWANCES ACT 1952-1973 - SECT. 13\. Unclaimed allowances. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   13\. All moneys payable under this Act not claimed within three months after becoming due shall revert to the Consolidated Revenue Fund.<lf> First Schedule repealed by No. 216, 1973, s. 3; Second Schedule repealed by No. 14, 1973, s. 7.<lf>                          *   *   *   *   *   *   *   *<lf> ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ -- <lf> </lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
