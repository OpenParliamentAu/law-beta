### <name>PASSENGER MOVEMENT CHARGE COLLECTION ACT 1978 - LONG TITLE </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   An Act relating to charge imposed in respect of the departure<lf>                   of persons from Australia<lf> </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PASSENGER MOVEMENT CHARGE COLLECTION ACT 1978 - SECT 1 Short title </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   1\. This Act may be cited as the Passenger Movement Charge Collection Act 1978.*1* SEE NOTES TO FIRST ARTICLE OF THIS CHAPTER . </sect>
**<b>

### <name>PASSENGER MOVEMENT CHARGE COLLECTION ACT 1978 - SECT 2 Commencement </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   2\. This Act shall come into operation on the day on which the Departure Tax Act 1978 comes into operation.*1* SEE NOTES TO FIRST ARTICLE OF THIS CHAPTER . </sect>
**<b>

### <name>PASSENGER MOVEMENT CHARGE COLLECTION ACT 1978 - SECT 3 Interpretation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   3\. In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:<lf>   "authorized officer" means a person who is appointed by the Minister by instrument in writing to be an authorized officer for the purposes of this Act;<lf>   "charge" means the charge, called "passenger movement", imposed by the Passenger Movement Charge Act 1978;<lf>   "charge stamp" means a charge stamp issued under this Act;<lf>   "exemption stamp" means a stamp issued under this Act for denoting exemption from liability to charge;<lf>   "exempt passenger", in relation to an international flight, means a passenger on the flight in respect of the departure of whom from Australia on the flight charge would be payable but for the operation of subsection 5 (1);<lf>   "external Territory" does not include:<lf>   (a) the Territory of Christmas Island; or<lf>   (b) the Territory of Cocos (Keeling) Islands;<lf>   "refund" means refund of charge under this Act;<lf>   "vessel" includes an aircraft. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PASSENGER MOVEMENT CHARGE COLLECTION ACT 1978 - SECT 4 Act binds the Crown </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   4\. This Act binds the Crown in right of the Commonwealth and of each State. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>PASSENGER MOVEMENT CHARGE COLLECTION ACT 1978 - SECT 5 Persons exempt from passenger movement charge </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   5\. (1) Charge is not payable in respect of the departure from Australia of a person who, at the time of his or her departure, is:<lf>   (a) a person who is not 12 years of age, that is to say, the 12th anniversary of whose birth has not occurred;<lf>   (b) a person who is a member of the crew of the vessel on which he or she departs from Australia;<lf>   (c) if the departure is before 1 September 1994 - a person referred to in paragraph (b) of the definition of "exempt non-citizen" in subsection 5 (1) of the Migration Act 1958 as in force at the time of the departure;<lf>   (d) if the departure is before 1 September 1994 - a person who, being a person who has arrived in Australia, is not, for the purposes of the Migration Act 1958 as in force at the time of the departure, deemed by subsection 5 (2) of that Act to have entered Australia;<lf>   (e) a person who is, for the purposes of the Agreement between Australia and the United States of America concerning the status of United States Forces in Australia signed on 9 May 1963:<lf>     (i) a member of the United States Forces or a member of the<lf> civilian component; or<lf>     (ii) a dependant of a person referred to in subparagraph<lf> (i);<lf>   (f) a person who is, for the purposes of the Agreement between Australia and Papua New Guinea regarding the Status of Forces of each country in the territory of the other country signed on 26 January 1977:<lf>     (i) a member of the Visiting Force of Papua New Guinea or a<lf> member of the civilian component; or<lf>     (ii) a dependant of a person referred to in subparagraph<lf> (i);<lf>   (g) a person being deported or extradited from Australia; or<lf>   (ga) a person being removed from Australia under section 54ZF of the Migration Act 1958; or<lf>   (h) a person, or a person included in a class of persons, prescribed for the purposes of this section. 

<lf>   (2) In paragraph (1) (b), "member of the crew" has the same meaning as in the Migration Act 1958\. </lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PASSENGER MOVEMENT CHARGE COLLECTION ACT 1978 - SECT 6 Liability to pay passenger movement charge </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   6\. (1) Charge in respect of the departure of a person from Australia is payable by the person before the departure. 

<lf>   (2) Where charge in respect of the departure of a person from Australia is not paid before the departure, the person is guilty of an offence punishable upon conviction by a fine not exceeding 1 penalty unit. <p><lf>   (3) Where a person is convicted of an offence against subsection (2), the court may, in addition to imposing a penalty under that subsection, order the person to pay to the Commonwealth the amount of charge not paid by the person. </lf></p></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PASSENGER MOVEMENT CHARGE COLLECTION ACT 1978 - SECT 7 Powers of authorised officers </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   7\. (1) Where an authorized officer has reasonable grounds for believing:<lf>   (a) that a person is about to depart from Australia for another country or for an external Territory; and<lf>   (b) that any charge payable in respect of the departure of the person has not been paid, and will not be paid, before the departure;<lf> the authorized officer may take such steps as are reasonably necessary to prevent the departure of the person, including, without limiting the generality of the foregoing, steps to prevent the person from going on board, or steps to remove the person from, any vessel which the authorized officer has reasonable grounds for believing is the vessel on which the departure will take place. 

<lf>   (2) An authorized officer may, for the purpose of ascertaining whether charge has been or will be paid in respect of the departure from Australia of a person who, in the opinion of the authorized officer, is about to depart from Australia for another country or for an external Territory, require that person, or any other person who may, in the opinion of the authorized officer, have information with respect to the matter, to answer questions or produce documents to him, or both. </lf>
</lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PASSENGER MOVEMENT CHARGE COLLECTION ACT 1978 - SECT 8 Offences </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   8\. (1) A person shall not, without reasonable excuse, refuse to answer a question or produce a document when required to do so in pursuance of subsection 7 (2).<lf> Penalty: 1 penalty unit. 

<lf>   (2) A person shall not, when required to answer a question or produce a document in pursuance of subsection 7 (2), make a statement, or produce a document, that is false or misleading in a material particular.<lf> Penalty: 5 penalty units. <p><lf>   (3) A person shall not, without reasonable excuse, obstruct or hinder an authorized officer acting in the performance of his functions or the exercise of his powers under this Act.<lf> Penalty: 5 penalty units. <p><lf>   (4) A person shall not assault or threaten an authorized officer acting in the performance of his functions or the exercise of his powers under this Act.<lf> Penalty: 10 penalty units or imprisonment for 6 months, or both. </lf></lf></p></lf></lf></p></lf></lf>
</lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PASSENGER MOVEMENT CHARGE COLLECTION ACT 1978 - SECT 9 Refunds of passenger movement charge </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   9\. (1) A person is entitled to a refund of charge paid by the person if:<lf>   (a) the departure in respect of which the charge was paid does not take place;<lf>   (b) the departure in respect of which the charge was paid takes place, but the person returns to Australia without having entered another country;<lf>   (c) by virtue of section 5, charge was not payable in respect of the departure in respect of which the charge was paid; or<lf>   (d) the person is entitled to a refund under the regulations. 

<lf>   (2) If an authorized officer, by instrument in writing, so directs, a refund to which a person is entitled by virtue of subsection (1) shall be applied in discharge of the liability of the person for charge payable by the person in respect of a departure specified in the instrument, and a refund that is so applied shall be deemed to have been paid to the person. <p><lf>   (3) A person shall not:<lf>   (a) obtain a refund that is not payable;<lf>   (b) make, in or in connection with a claim for a refund, a statement that is false or misleading in a material particular; or<lf>   (c) present, in connection with an application for a refund, a document that is false or misleading in a material particular.<lf> Penalty: 5 penalty units. <p><lf>   (4) Where a person is convicted of an offence against subsection (3), the court may, in addition to imposing a penalty under that subsection, order the person to repay to the Commonwealth the amount of any refund incorrectly paid to the person. </lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PASSENGER MOVEMENT CHARGE COLLECTION ACT 1978 - SECT 10 Special arrangements for payment of passenger movement charge </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   10\. (1) The Minister may make an arrangement with a person under which the person agrees to pay to the Commonwealth, in the manner provided in the arrangement, an amount equal to any charge that may become payable by any person to whom the arrangement applies. 

<lf>   (2) Where an arrangement under subsection (1) applies to a person who departs from Australia for another country, the person shall, for the purposes of this Act, be deemed to have paid any charge payable in respect of that departure. <p><lf>   (3) An amount payable by a person to the Commonwealth under an arrangement under subsection (1) may be recovered by the Commonwealth by action against the person in a court of competent jurisdiction. <p><lf>   (4) In spite of anything in this section, the Minister must not make an arrangement under this section that is to apply in respect of the departure from Australia, before 1 July 1995, of any person on an international flight. </lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PASSENGER MOVEMENT CHARGE COLLECTION ACT 1978 - SECT 11 Charge stamps and exemption stamps </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   11\. (1) The regulations may make provision for and in relation to the payment of charge by the production to authorized officers or such other officers as are prescribed of charge stamps by or on behalf of persons liable to pay charge and, in particular, without limiting the generality of the foregoing, may make provision for and in relation to:<lf>   (a) the issue of charge stamps by the Commonwealth;<lf>   (aa) the supply (whether by way of sale or otherwise) of charge stamps by the Commonwealth to approved agents;<lf>   (ab) the terms and conditions upon which charge stamps may be supplied by the Commonwealth to approved agents;<lf>   (ac) the sale of charge stamps by approved agents;<lf>   (ad) the terms and conditions upon which charge stamps may be sold by approved agents;<lf>   (b) refunds, by the Commonwealth or by approved agents specified in the regulations, of amounts paid for the purchase of charge stamps that are not produced for the purpose of payment of charge or in respect of which a refund is otherwise payable under the regulations; and<lf>   (c) the reimbursement of approved agents who pay refunds in accordance with regulations made for the purposes of paragraph (b). 

<lf>   (2) The regulations may make provision for and in relation to the issue by the Commonwealth of stamps (in this subsection referred to as "exemption stamps") to or in respect of persons in respect of the departure of whom from Australia charge would be payable but for the operation of subsection 5 (1) and, in particular, without limiting the generality of the foregoing, may make provision for and in relation to the production to authorized officers or such other officers as are prescribed of exemption stamps by or on behalf of such persons. <p><lf>   (3) Without limiting the generality of subsection (2), the regulations may make provision for and in relation to:<lf>   (a) the supply of exemption stamps by the Commonwealth to approved agents;<lf>   (b) the terms and conditions upon which exemption stamps may be supplied by the Commonwealth to approved agents;<lf>   (c) the supply of exemption stamps by approved agents; and<lf>   (d) the terms and conditions upon which exemption stamps may be supplied by approved agents. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PASSENGER MOVEMENT CHARGE COLLECTION ACT 1978 - SECT 12 Recovery of passenger movement charge </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   12\. Charge that a person has become liable to pay and has not paid is a debt due to the Commonwealth by the person by whom the charge is payable. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>PASSENGER MOVEMENT CHARGE COLLECTION ACT 1978 - SECT 13 Averment </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   13\. (1) In any prosecution for an offence against subsection 6 (2) or in any proceedings for the recovery of charge, a statement or averment of the prosecutor or plaintiff in the information, complaint, claim or declaration is prima facie evidence of the matter so stated or averred. 

<lf>   (2) This section shall apply to any matter so averred although:<lf>   (a) evidence in support or rebuttal of the matter averred or any other matter is given; or<lf>   (b) the matter averred is a mixed question of law and fact, but in that case the averment shall be prima facie evidence of the fact only. <p><lf>   (3) Any evidence given in support or rebuttal of a matter so averred shall be considered on its merits, and the credibility and probative value of such evidence shall be neither increased nor diminished by reason of this section. <p><lf>   (4) This section shall not lessen or affect any onus of proof otherwise falling on the defendant. </lf></p></lf></p></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PASSENGER MOVEMENT CHARGE COLLECTION ACT 1978 - SECT 14 Delegation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   14\. (1) The Minister may, either generally or as otherwise provided by the instrument of delegation, by instrument in writing, delegate to an officer of the Department any of his or her powers under this Act, other than this power of delegation. 

<lf>   (2) A power so delegated, when exercised by the delegate, shall, for the purposes of this Act, be deemed to have been exercised by the Minister. <p><lf>   (3) A delegation under this section does not prevent the exercise of a power by the Minister. </lf></p></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PASSENGER MOVEMENT CHARGE COLLECTION ACT 1978 - SECT 15 Regulations </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   15\. The Governor-General may make regulations, not inconsistent with this Act, prescribing all matters required or permitted by this Act to be prescribed or necessary or convenient to be prescribed for carrying out or giving effect to this Act, and, in particular:<lf>   (a) making provision for and in relation to claims for refunds, the allowance or payment of refunds and the recovery of refunds paid incorrectly;<lf>   (aa) making provision for and in relation to the appointment of bodies or persons as approved agents; and<lf>   (b) prescribing penalties not exceeding 5 penalty units for offences against the regulations. </lf></lf></lf></sect>
