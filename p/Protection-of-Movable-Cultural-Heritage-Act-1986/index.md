###  PROTECTION OF MOVABLE CULTURAL HERITAGE ACT 1986- SECT 1  Short title [_see_ Note 1] 
This Act may be cited as the _Protection of Movable Cultural Heritage Act 1986_.

 
###  PROTECTION OF MOVABLE CULTURAL HERITAGE ACT 1986- SECT 2  Commencement [_see_ Note 1] 
This Act shall come into operation on a day to be fixed by Proclamation.

###  PROTECTION OF MOVABLE CULTURAL HERITAGE ACT 1986- SECT 7  Movable cultural heritage of Australia 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>A reference in section 8 to the movable cultural heritage of Australia is

a reference to objects that are of importance to Australia, or to a particular

part of Australia, for ethnological, archaeological, historical, literary,

artistic, scientific or technological reasons, being objects falling within one

or more of the following categories:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>objects recovered from:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>the soil or inland waters of Australia;</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>the coastal sea of Australia or the waters above the continental shelf of

Australia; or</dd>

<dt>(iii)</dt><dd>the seabed or subsoil beneath the sea or waters referred to in

subparagraph (ii);

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>objects relating to members of the Aboriginal race of Australia and

descendants of the indigenous inhabitants of the Torres Strait Islands;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>objects of ethnographic art or ethnography;</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>military objects;</dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>objects of decorative art;</dd>

<dt>(f)</dt><dd>objects of fine art;</dd>

<dt>(g)</dt><dd>objects of scientific or technological interest;</dd>

<dt>(h)</dt><dd>books, records, documents or photographs, graphic, film or television

material or sound recordings;</dd>

<dt>(j)</dt><dd>any other prescribed categories.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>The generality of paragraph (1)(j) is not limited by any of the other

paragraphs of subsection (1).

</dd> </dl>

###  PROTECTION OF MOVABLE CULTURAL HERITAGE ACT 1986- SECT 8  National Cultural Heritage Control List 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Subject to subsection (2), the regulations shall prescribe a list, to be

known as the National Cultural Heritage Control List, of categories of objects

that constitute the movable cultural heritage of Australia and are to be

subject to export control.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>The Control List shall divide such objects into 2 classes, namely: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>Class A objects, being objects that are not to be exported otherwise than

in accordance with a certificate; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>Class B objects, being objects that are not to be exported otherwise than

in accordance with a permit or certificate.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>Subsection (2) does not prevent the division of Class A objects and Class

B objects into sub-classes and other divisions or categories.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>Nothing in this section shall be taken to limit the application of subsection 33(3A) of the _Acts Interpretation Act 1901_. </dd> </dl>

###  PROTECTION OF MOVABLE CULTURAL HERITAGE ACT 1986- SECT 9  Unlawful exports 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Where a person exports an Australian protected object otherwise than in

accordance with a permit or certificate, the object is forfeited.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Where a person attempts to export an Australian protected object otherwise than in accordance with a permit or certificate, the object is liable to forfeiture.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>A person is guilty of an offence if: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the person exports, or attempts to export, an object; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the object is an Australian protected object; and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>the person's conduct referred to in paragraph&#160;(a) is otherwise than

in accordance with a permit or certificate.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(3A)</dt><dd>A person is guilty of an offence if:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a permit or certificate relates to an Australian protected object; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the person engages in conduct; and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>the conduct contravenes a condition of the permit or certificate.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(3B)</dt><dd>A person who is convicted of an offence against subsection&#160;(3) or

<dt>(3A)</dt><dd>is punishable by:

</dd> </dd></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>if the person is an individual&#151;a fine not exceeding 1,000 penalty units

or imprisonment for a period not exceeding 5&#160;years, or both; or

</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>if the person is a body corporate&#151;a fine not exceeding 2,000 penalty

units.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Note:	See subsection 46(4) for penalties that may be imposed by a

court of summary jurisdiction.

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(4)</dt><dd>For the purposes of this section, an object shall be taken to be exported

at the time when:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the object has been placed on board a ship or aircraft at a particular

place in Australia with the intention that it be taken out of Australia by that

ship or aircraft and the departure of the ship or aircraft from that place has

commenced; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the object has been delivered as a postal article into the control of the

Australian Postal Corporation at a particular place in Australia with the

intention that it be sent out of Australia by post and the movement of the

postal article from that place has commenced.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(5)</dt><dd>Without limiting the meaning of references in this section to a person

attempting to export an object, a person shall be taken to attempt to export an

object if the person conveys, or has possession of, the object with intent to

export it or knowing that it is intended to be exported.</dd> <dt>(6)</dt><dd>For the purposes of this section, a person who exports or attempts to export an Australian protected object shall be taken to export, or attempt to export, the object otherwise than in accordance with a permit or certificate unless, before exporting or attempting to export the object, the person produces a permit or certificate authorising the export: </dd> </dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>where the export is not from an external Territory to an officer of

Customs; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>where the export is from an external Territory&#151;to an inspector performing

duties in relation to the export of Australian protected objects.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(7)</dt><dd>In this section:

</dd> </dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_engage in conduct_ means:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>do an act; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>omit to perform an act.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

###  PROTECTION OF MOVABLE CULTURAL HERITAGE ACT 1986- SECT 10  Grant of permits in respect of particular objects 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>A person may apply to the Minister for a permit to export a Class B

object.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>An application shall be made in writing in the prescribed form, or, if no form is prescribed, the form approved by the Minister.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>On receipt of an application, the Minister shall refer it to the Committee and the Committee shall refer it to one or more expert examiners.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>The expert examiner or examiners shall submit to the Committee a written report on the application, and the Committee shall forward the report to the Minister together with the written recommendations (if any) made by the Committee.</dd> <dt>(5)</dt><dd>The Minister shall consider the report and recommendations (if any) and: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>grant a permit to export the Class B object concerned, subject to such

conditions (if any) as the Minister specifies; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>refuse to grant a permit.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(6)</dt><dd>In considering the application, an expert examiner, the Committee and the

Minister:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>shall have regard, among other things, to the reasons referred to in

subsection 7(1) that are relevant to the object to which the application

relates; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>if satisfied that the object is of such importance to Australia, or a part

of Australia, for those reasons, that its loss to Australia would significantly

diminish the cultural heritage of Australia&#151;shall not recommend the grant of a

permit, or grant a permit, as the case may be, to export the object permanently.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(7)</dt><dd>If the Minister refuses to grant the permit, the Minister shall, within

the prescribed period after the decision is made, cause to be served on the

applicant notice in writing of the refusal, setting out the reasons for the

refusal.

</dd> </dl>

###  PROTECTION OF MOVABLE CULTURAL HERITAGE ACT 1986- SECT 14  Unlawful imports 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Where:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a protected object of a foreign country has been exported from that

country;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the export was prohibited by a law of that country relating to cultural

property; and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>the object is imported;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

the object is liable to forfeiture.

<dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>Where a person imports an object, knowing that:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the object is a protected object of a foreign country that has been

exported from that country; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the export was prohibited by a law of that country relating to cultural

property;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

the person is guilty of an offence punishable, on conviction, by:

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>if the person is a natural person&#151;a fine not exceeding $100,000 or

imprisonment for a period not exceeding 5 years, or both; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>if the person is a body corporate&#151;a fine not exceeding $200,000.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>This section does not apply in relation to the importation of an object if:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the importation takes place under an agreement between:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>the Commonwealth, a State, a Territory, a principal collecting institution

or an exhibition co-ordinator; and</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>any other person or body (including a government); and

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the agreement provides for the object to be loaned, for a period not

exceeding 2 years, to the Commonwealth, State, Territory, principal collecting

institution or exhibition co-ordinator, as the case may be, for the purpose of

its public exhibition within Australia.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(4)</dt><dd>In subsection (3):

</dd> </dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_exhibition co-ordinator_ means a body that arranges for the

conducting in Australia of public exhibitions of objects from collections

outside Australia, and that achieves this by, from time to time:

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>entering into an agreement with a person or body (including a government)

for the importation of such objects on loan; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>entering into an agreement with the Commonwealth, a State or a Territory

under which the Commonwealth, State or Territory agrees to compensate the

person or body referred to in paragraph (a) for any loss of or damage to the

objects arising from, or connected with, the carrying out of the agreement

referred to in that paragraph or the public exhibition of the objects in

Australia.

</dd>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>identifying the object and the date of the seizure;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>setting out the reasons for the seizure;</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>setting out the terms of the provisions of subsection 37(1); and</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>where subsection (5) applies&#151;setting out the terms of the provisions of

that subsection and specifying, and setting out the address of, an appropriate

person and stating that any notice under that subsection is to be given to that

person.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>An inspector is not required to serve a notice under subsection (2) if,

after making such inquiries as the inspector thinks appropriate, the inspector

does not have sufficient information to do so.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>Where: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a notice under subsection (2) has not been served in respect of an object

seized by an inspector under this Act; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>within 30 days after the day on which the object was seized, a person who

claims to be a person entitled to be served with such a notice gives an

appropriate person sufficient information, in writing, to enable the notice to

be served;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

the appropriate person shall forthwith convey that information to the inspector.

<dl compact="">

<dt>(5)</dt><dd>Where a protected object has been seized on the grounds that it is liable

to forfeiture by virtue of subsection 9(2) or 14(1) and a notice under

subsection (2) is served in respect of the object, the object is forfeited

unless, within 30 days after the service of the notice, the owner, or the

person who had possession, custody or control of the object immediately before

it was seized:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>gives notice, in writing, to the appropriate person specified in the

notice under subsection (2) claiming the object; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>brings an action for recovery of the object.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(6)</dt><dd>Where, at the end of the period of 30 days after the day on which a

protected object was seized under this Act:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a notice has not been served in respect of the object under subsection

(2); and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>a person claiming to be a person entitled to be served with such a notice

has not given an appropriate person sufficient information to enable the notice

to be served;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

the object is forfeited.

<dl compact="">

<dt>(7)</dt><dd>Where notice is given under subsection (5) to the appropriate person, the

appropriate person shall serve on the person who gave the notice a notice, in

writing, setting out the terms of the provisions of subsection (8).</dd> <dt>(8)</dt><dd>Where: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a notice is given to a person under subsection (7) in relation to an

object; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the person does not, within 4 months after the service of the notice,

bring an action for recovery of the object;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

the object is forfeited. 

**

###  PROTECTION OF MOVABLE CULTURAL HERITAGE ACT 1986- SECT 37  Court proceedings 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Where:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a protected object has been seized under this Act; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the object is not forfeited by section 36 or by or under this section;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

the owner, or the person who had the possession, custody or control of the object immediately before it was seized, may bring an action against the Commonwealth in a court of competent jurisdiction for the recovery of the object on the ground that the object is not forfeited or liable to be forfeited.

<dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>Where:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>a protected object has been seized on the grounds that it is liable to

forfeiture by virtue of subsection 9(2) or 14(1);</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>a person brings an action for recovery of the object; and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>the person discontinues the action;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

the object is forfeited.

<dl compact="">

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>Where an action is brought for recovery of a protected object, the court

shall determine, on a balance of probabilities, whether:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the object is forfeited by virtue of subsection 9(1); or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the object is liable to forfeiture by virtue of subsection 9(2) or 14(1);

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

and the court:

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>where paragraph (a) applies&#151;shall reject the claim for recovery;</dd>

<dt>(d)</dt><dd>where paragraph (b) applies&#151;shall order that the object is forfeited;

or</dd>

<dt>(e)</dt><dd>in any other case&#151;may order the return of the object.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(4)</dt><dd>Where a person is convicted of attempting to export a protected object in

contravention of subsection 9(3) or of an offence against subsection 14(2) in

relation to a protected object, the court shall order that the object is

forfeited.

</dd> </dl>

**

###  PROTECTION OF MOVABLE CULTURAL HERITAGE ACT 1986- SECT 38  Result of forfeiture 
**

 <dl compact="">

		Where a protected object is forfeited by or under this Act:

 </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>all title and interest in the object is vested in the Commonwealth without

further proceedings;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the object shall be dealt with and disposed of in accordance with the

directions of the Minister; and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>any costs incurred by the Commonwealth in transporting or disposing of the

object are a debt due to the Commonwealth by the person who was the owner of

the object immediately before it was forfeited and may be recovered by the

Commonwealth in a court of competent jurisdiction.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

**

###  PROTECTION OF MOVABLE CULTURAL HERITAGE ACT 1986- SECT 39  Production of permit 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>An inspector may require any person whom the inspector suspects on

reasonable grounds of intending to export, of exporting, or of having exported,

an Australian protected object to produce a permit or certificate authorising

the export or to produce evidence of the existence and contents of such a

permit or certificate.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>A person who fails to comply with a requirement made under subsection (1) is guilty of an offence punishable on conviction by a fine not exceeding $1,000.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>Subsection&#160;(2) does not apply if the person has a reasonable excuse. </dd> </dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Note:	A defendant bears an evidential burden in relation to the

matter in subsection&#160;(3) (see subsection 13.3(3) of the _Criminal Code_).

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(4)</dt><dd>Subsection&#160;(2) is an offence of strict liability.

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Note:	For _strict liability_, see section&#160;6.1 of

the _Criminal Code_.

 </dl></dl>

###  PROTECTION OF MOVABLE CULTURAL HERITAGE ACT 1986- SECT 40  Proof of authority 
**

 <dl compact="">

		An inspector (other than a member of a police force who is in uniform) is not

entitled to exercise any powers under section 32, 33, 34, 39 or 43 in relation

to a person unless:

 </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>if the inspector is a member of a police force&#151;the inspector produces,

for inspection by the person, written evidence of the fact that the inspector

is a member of that police force; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>in any other case&#151;the inspector produces his or her identity card for

inspection by the person.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

**

###  PROTECTION OF MOVABLE CULTURAL HERITAGE ACT 1986- SECT 41  Foreign country to request return of objects 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>A power conferred by this Part shall not be exercised by an inspector in

relation to a protected object of a foreign country unless the inspector

believes on reasonable grounds that the Commonwealth has received from the

Government of the country a request for the return of the object.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Proceedings for a contravention of section 14 in relation to a protected object of a foreign country shall not be instituted unless the Commonwealth has received from the Government of the country a request for the return of the object.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>In any proceedings, the mere production of a document purporting to be signed by the Secretary to the Department and stating that the Commonwealth has received a request from the Government of a specified foreign country for the return of a specified protected object is _prima facie_ evidence of the facts stated. </dd> </dl>

**

###  PROTECTION OF MOVABLE CULTURAL HERITAGE ACT 1986- SECT 43  Assistance of inspectors 
**

 <dl compact="">

		An inspector exercising or proposing to exercise powers pursuant to this Part

in relation to property, being any premises, structure, vessel, aircraft or

vehicle, may require the owner or person in charge of the property to provide

reasonable assistance to the inspector in the exercise of those powers in

relation to the property, and the person shall comply with the request.

 </dl>

Penalty:	$2,000 or imprisonment for 12 months, or both.

###  PROTECTION OF MOVABLE CULTURAL HERITAGE ACT 1986- SECT 46  Indictable offences 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>An offence against subsection 9(3), 9(3A) or 14(2) is an indictable

offence.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>An offence against section 43 is punishable on summary conviction.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>Notwithstanding that an offence referred to in subsection (1) is an indictable offence, a court of summary jurisdiction may hear and determine proceedings in respect of the offence if the court is satisfied that it is proper to do so and the defendant and the prosecutor consent.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>If, in accordance with subsection&#160;(3), a court of summary jurisdiction convicts a person of an offence referred to in subsection&#160;(1), the penalty that the court may impose is: </dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>if the person is an individual&#151;a fine not exceeding 50 penalty units or

imprisonment for a period not exceeding 2 years, or both; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>if the person is a body corporate&#151;a fine not exceeding 200 penalty units.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>
