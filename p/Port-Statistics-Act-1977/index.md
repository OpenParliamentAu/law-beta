###  Long Title 
**
###  PORT STATISTICS ACT 1977- SECT 1  Short title [_see_ Note 1] 
This Act may be cited as the _Port Statistics Act 1977_.

 
###  PORT STATISTICS ACT 1977- SECT 2  Commencement [_see_ Note 1] 
This Act shall come into operation on the date of commencement of the

_Stevedoring Industry Acts (Termination) Act 1977_.

 
###  PORT STATISTICS ACT 1977- SECT 3  Interpretation 
In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:

 
<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_document_ includes any record of information, however recorded or

stored, whether in written or printed form, on film, by electronic means or

otherwise. _port_ includes any place (including a place at sea) at or near which facilities are provided for or in connexion with the loading or unloading of cargo into or from ships. _Secretary_ means the Secretary to the Department. _ship_ includes any vessel.  </dl></dl>

**

###  PORT STATISTICS ACT 1977- SECT 3A  Application of the _Criminal Code_ 
**

 <dl compact="">

		Chapter&#160;2 of the _Criminal Code_ applies to all offences created by

this Act.

 </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Note:	Chapter&#160;2 of the _Criminal Code_ sets out the

general principles of criminal responsibility.

 </dl></dl>

###  PORT STATISTICS ACT 1977- SECT 4  Collection of statistics 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Secretary may collect statistics in relation to all or any of the

following matters:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the loading of cargo into, and the unloading of cargo from, ships,

including:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>the employment of persons in or in connexion with such loading or

unloading; and</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>the availability of persons for employment in or in connexion with such

loading or unloading;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the loading of cargo into, and the unloading of cargo from, vehicles at a

port, being cargo that has been unloaded from, or is to be loaded into, ships

at that port, including:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>the employment of persons in or in connexion with the first-mentioned

loading or unloading; and</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>the availability of persons for employment in or in connexion with the

first-mentioned loading or unloading;

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>the movement of ships into, from or within ports.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>Subject to subsection&#160;(3), the Secretary may, in such manner as he

thinks appropriate, cause to be published, or to be made available to other

persons, statistics collected in pursuance of subsection&#160;(1). </dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>Subject to subsection&#160;(4), subsection&#160;(2) does not authorize the publication or making available of statistics in a manner that enables the identification of a particular person or organization. </dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>Statistics may be published or made available in a manner that enables the identification of a particular person or organization if it is not practicable to publish or make available those statistics in a manner that does not enable the identification of that person or organization. </dd> </dl>

###  PORT STATISTICS ACT 1977- SECT 5  Furnishing of information 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>For the purpose of enabling the collection of statistics referred to in

section&#160;4, the Secretary may, subject to subsection&#160;(2), by notice in

writing, require a person:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>to furnish to the Secretary, or to a person specified in the notice acting

on behalf of the Secretary, such information as is specified in the notice,

being information relating to a matter specified in any of the paragraphs of

subsection 4(1); or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>to produce to the Secretary, or to a person specified in the notice acting

on behalf of the Secretary, books or documents specified in the notice, being

books or documents that contain information that relates, or may relate, to a

matter specified in any of the paragraphs of subsection 4(1);

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

within such reasonable time and in such manner as is specified in the notice. 

<dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>The powers of the Secretary under subsection&#160;(1) to require a person

to produce books or documents are exercisable only for the purpose of enabling

the Secretary to verify the accuracy of information furnished by the person in

pursuance of a requirement under paragraph&#160;(1)(a). </dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>A person must not fail to comply with a requirement under subsection&#160;(1). </dd> </dl>

Penalty:	$500.

<dl compact="">

<dt>(4)</dt><dd>It is a defence to a prosecution for an offence against

subsection&#160;(1) if the defendant complies with the requirement to the

extent to which he or she is capable.

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Note:	A defendant bears an evidential burden in relation to the

matter in subsection&#160;(4) (see subsection 13.3(3) of the _Criminal Code_).

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(5)</dt><dd>Strict liability applies to an offence under subsection&#160;(3).

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact="">

Note:	For _strict liability_, see section&#160;6.1 of

the _Criminal Code_.

 </dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(6)</dt><dd>A person commits an offence if:

</dd> </dl>

<center>Back to Top</center>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the person does an act with the intention of avoiding the requirements of

this section; and</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the act results in the destruction, mutilation, defacing, concealment or

removal of a book or document.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

Penalty:	$500\. 

**

###  PORT STATISTICS ACT 1977- SECT 6  Delegation 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>The Secretary may, either generally or as otherwise provided by the

instrument of delegation, by writing signed by him, delegate to a person any of

his powers under this Act other than this power of delegation and his powers

under section&#160;8.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>A power so delegated, when exercised by the delegate, shall, for the purposes of this Act, be deemed to have been exercised by the Secretary.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>A delegation under this section does not prevent the exercise of a power by the Secretary. </dd> </dl>

**

###  PORT STATISTICS ACT 1977- SECT 7  Officers to observe secrecy 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Subject to section&#160;9, a person who is or has been an officer shall

not, either directly or indirectly, except in the performance of a duty under

or in connexion with this Act or except as provided by the regulations:

</dd> </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>make a record of, or (except to, or as authorized by, the Minister)

divulge or communicate to any person, any information concerning the affairs of

any other person acquired by him in the performance of his duties, or in the

exercise of his powers or functions, under or in connexion with this Act; or</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>produce to any person a document furnished for the purposes of this Act.

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

Penalty:	$1,000 or imprisonment for 3 months.

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(2)</dt><dd>A person to whom information referred to in paragraph&#160;(1)(a) is

divulged as authorized by or under subsection&#160;(1) shall, in respect of

that information, be subject to the same obligations and liabilities under

subsection&#160;(1) as if the person were a person performing duties under this

Act and had acquired the information in the performance of those duties.</dd>

<dt>(3)</dt><dd>For the purposes of this section, any information acquired by an officer

in accordance with an arrangement entered into under section&#160;8 shall be

deemed to have been acquired by the officer in the performance of a duty under

this Act.</dd>

<dt>(4)</dt><dd>In this section:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<def><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

_officer_ means a person performing duties, or exercising powers or

functions, under or in connexion with this Act. _produce_ includes permit access to.  </dl></dl>

<dl compact="">

<dt>(5)</dt><dd>This section extends to persons who are officers or employees of a State.

</dd> </dl>

###  PORT STATISTICS ACT 1977- SECT 8  Arrangements with States 
**

 <dl compact="">

		The Minister may enter into arrangements with a relevant Minister of State of

a State with respect to:

 </dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(a)</dt><dd>the delegation by the Secretary of powers under this Act to:

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl>

<dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact=""><dl compact="">

<dt>(i)</dt><dd>officers and employees of the State; and</dd>

<dt>(ii)</dt><dd>persons who constitute, are members of, or are employed by, authorities

established by or under laws of the State;</dd>

<dt>(b)</dt><dd>the supply to the State and to authorities established by or under laws of

the State of information and statistics collected under this Act; and</dd>

<dt>(c)</dt><dd>the supply to the Commonwealth by the State and by authorities established

by or under laws of the State of information and statistics relating to matters

specified in any of the paragraphs of subsection 4(1).

</dd>

</dl></dl></dl></dl>

###  PORT STATISTICS ACT 1977- SECT 9  Australian Statistician 
**

 <dl compact="">

<dt>(1)</dt><dd>Nothing in this Act shall be taken to affect the exercise by the

Australian Statistician of powers under _the Census and Statistics Act 1905_.</dd> <dt>(2)</dt><dd>Notwithstanding anything contained in section&#160;7, an officer may divulge to the Australian Statistician any information acquired by the officer in the performance of his duties, or in the exercise of his powers or functions, under this Act.</dd> <dt>(3)</dt><dd>Any information furnished to the Australian Statistician in pursuance of subsection&#160;(2) shall, for the purposes of section&#160;19 of _the Census and Statistics Act 1905_, be deemed to have been furnished in pursuance of that Act.</dd> <dt>(4)</dt><dd>For the purpose of assisting the Australian Bureau of Statistics to perform the functions referred to in paragraphs 6(1)(c) and (d) of the _Australian Bureau of Statistics Act 1975_, the Secretary shall, from time to time, consult the Australian Statistician in relation to the exercise by the Secretary of powers under this Act. </dd> </dl>

**

###  PORT STATISTICS ACT 1977- SECT 10  Regulations 
**

 <dl compact="">

		The Governor-General may make regulations not inconsistent with this Act,

prescribing all matters required or permitted by this Act to be prescribed, or

necessary or convenient to be prescribed for carrying out or giving effect to

this Act.

 </dl>
