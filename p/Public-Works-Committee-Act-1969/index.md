### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 5 Interpretation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   5\. In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears-<lf>   "assessor" means a person appointed under section 26;<lf>   "authority of the Commonwealth" means-<lf>      (a)  a body corporate, or an unincorporated body, established for a public purpose by, or in accordance with the provisions of, an enactment;<lf>         (b)  a body established by the Governor-General or a Minister otherwise than in accordance with an enactment; or<lf>         (c)  an incorporated company over which the Commonwealth is in a position to exercise control,<lf>        but does not include an inter-governmental body;<lf>   "commence", in relation to a public work, includes enter into a contract for the carrying out of the whole or a part of the work, and "commencement" has a corresponding meaning;<lf>          "court of summary jurisdiction" includes a court of a Territory sitting as a court for the making of summary orders or the summary punishment of offences under the law of the Territory;<lf>          "enactment" means-<lf>             (a)  an Act;<lf>         (b)  an Ordinance of the Australian Capital Territory; or<lf>         (c)  an instrument (including rules, regulations or by-laws) made under an Act or under such an Ordinance;<lf>      "inter-governmental body" means a body corporate or an unincorporated body established by, or in accordance with the provisions of, an agreement between the Commonwealth and a State or States or between the Commonwealth and the Government of another country or the Governments of other countries;<lf>          "member" means a member of the Committee;<lf>          "public work" means-<lf>             (a)  a work-<lf>             (i)  that is proposed to be carried out by or for the Commonwealth, either within or outside Australia; and<lf>                  (ii)  in respect of the carrying out of which moneys appropriated by the Parliament are proposed to be expended by the Commonwealth;<lf>               (b)  a work-<lf>                   (i)  that is proposed to be carried out, either within or outside Australia, by or for an authority of the Commonwealth to which this Act applies by virtue of section 6A; and<lf>                  (ii)  in respect of the carrying out of which moneys appropriated by the Parliament, or moneys of the authority, are proposed to be expended by the authority; or<lf>               (c)  a work in respect of which a declaration referred to in sub-section (2) of section 6B is in force,<lf>        but does not include-<lf>         (d)  a work that is proposed to be carried out by or on behalf of the National Capital Development Commission-<lf>             (i)  for the Commonwealth; or<lf>                  (ii)  for an authority of the Commonwealth to which this Act applies (including that Commission),<lf>                 unless the work is, under the regulations, a prescribed work for the purposes of this definition; or<lf>         (e)  a work that is proposed to be carried out by or for the Commonwealth by way of assistance to an overseas country;<lf>      "State" includes the Northern Territory;<lf>          "the Chairman" means the Chairman of the Committee;<lf>          "the Committee" means the Parliamentary Standing Committee on Public Works for the time being constituted in accordance with this Act;<lf>          "work" includes a continuation, completion, repair, re-construction or extension of a work. </lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 6 Extension to Territories </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   6\. (1) This Act extends to every Territory not forming part of Australia. 

  (2) This Act does not extend to-<lf>   (a)  a work-<lf>              (i)  that is proposed to be carried out by or for the Northern Territory; and<lf>         (ii)  in respect of the carrying out of which moneys of that Territory are proposed to be expended by that Territory; or<lf>      (b)  a work-<lf>          (i)  that is proposed to be carried out by or for the Administration of Norfolk Island; and<lf>         (ii)  in respect of the carrying out of which moneys of that Administration are proposed to be expended by that Administration. <p></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 6A Bodies to which Act applies </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   6A. (1) Subject to sub-section (2), this Act applies to every authority of the Commonwealth. 

  (2) This Act does not apply to-<lf>   (a)  an authority of the Commonwealth in respect of which a declaration referred to in sub-section (3) is in force;<lf>          (b)  the Parliament House Construction Authority;<lf>         (ba)  the Australian Maritime College; or<lf>          (c)  an authority of the Commonwealth established for the purpose of providing, or for purposes which include the purpose of providing, tertiary education in the Australian Capital Territory. <p>  (3) Where the Governor-General is satisfied that an authority of the Commonwealth is engaging in trading or other activities, or is providing services, in competition with another body or other bodies, or with persons, the Governor-General may make regulations declaring that this Act does not apply to that authority. </p><p></p></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 6B Regulations may declare works to be public works </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   6B. (1) The regulations may declare a work that is proposed to be carried out by or on behalf of the National Capital Development Commission-<lf>   (a)  for the Commonwealth; or<lf>          (b)  for an authority of the Commonwealth to which this Act applies (including that Commission),<lf>        to be a prescribed work for the purposes of the definition of "public work" in section 5\. 

  (2) Notwithstanding that an authority of the Commonwealth is not an authority of the Commonwealth to which this Act applies, the regulations may declare a work-<lf>   (a)  that is proposed to be carried out by or for the authority; and<lf>          (b)  in respect of the carrying out of which moneys appropriated by the Parliament, or moneys of the authority, are proposed to be expended by the authority,<lf>        to be a public work to which section 18 applies. <p></p></lf></lf></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></sect>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - PART II  PART II-PARLIAMENTARY STANDING COMMITTEE ON PUBLIC WORKS </name>
</b>** 
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 7 Parliamentary Standing Committee on Public Works </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   7\. (1) As soon as practicable after the commencement of the first session of each Parliament, a joint committee of members of the Parliament, to be known as the Parliamentary Standing Committee on Public Works, shall be appointed. 

  (3) The Committee shall consist of nine members, namely-<lf>   (a)  three members of the Senate appointed by the Senate; and<lf>          (b)  six members of the House of Representatives appointed by that House. <p>  (4) The appointment of members by a House of the Parliament shall be in accordance with the practice of that House with respect to the appointment of members of that House to serve on joint select committees of both Houses of the Parliament. </p><p>  (5) A member of the Parliament is not eligible for appointment as a member of the Committee if he is-<lf>   (a)  a Minister;<lf>          (b)  the President of the Senate;<lf>          (c)  the Speaker of the House of Representatives; or<lf>          (d)  the Chairman of Committees of the Senate or of the House of Representatives. <p>  (6) A member holds office during the pleasure of the House of the Parliament by which he was appointed. </p><p>  (7) A member ceases to hold office-<lf>   (a)  when the House of Representatives expires by effluxion of time or is dissolved;<lf>          (b)  if he becomes the holder of an office specified in any of the paragraphs of sub-section (5);<lf>          (c)  if he ceases to be a member of the House of the Parliament by which he was appointed; or<lf>          (d)  if he resigns his office as provided by either of the next two succeeding sub-sections. <p>  (8) A member appointed by the Senate may resign his office by writing under his hand addressed to the President of the Senate. </p><p>  (9) A member appointed by the House of Representatives may resign his office by writing under his hand addressed to the Speaker of that House. </p><p>  (10) Either House of the Parliament may appoint one of its members to fill a vacancy amongst the members of the Committee appointed by that House. </p><p>  (11) Notice of the appointment of a member, and of a member ceasing to hold office otherwise than upon the expiry or dissolution of the House of Representatives, shall be published in the Gazette. </p><p>  (12) The exercise of a power or the performance of a function by the Committee, the Chairman or a member is not invalidated by reason only of there being a vacancy or vacancies in the membership of the Committee. </p><p></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 8 Declaration to be subscribed by members </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   8\. A member shall, before proceeding to discharge the duties of his office, make and subscribe a declaration of office in accordance with the form in the Schedule-<lf>   (a)  in the case of a member appointed by the Senate-before the President of the Senate or a person appointed by the President of the Senate to be a person before whom such a declaration may be made and subscribed; and<lf>          (b)  in the case of a member appointed by the House of Representatives-before the Speaker of that House or a person appointed by the Speaker of that House to be a person before whom such a declaration may be made and subscribed. </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 9 Chairman and Vice-Chairman </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   9\. (1) There shall be a Chairman and a Vice-Chairman of the Committee, who shall be elected by the members. 

  (2) The Chairman and Vice-Chairman hold office during the pleasure of the Committee. 

  (3) The Chairman or Vice-Chairman ceases to hold office if-<lf>   (a)  he ceases to be a member; or<lf>          (b)  he resigns his office as provided by the next succeeding sub-section. <p>  (4) The Chairman or Vice-Chairman may resign his office by writing under his hand presented to a meeting of the Committee. </p><p>  (5) Subject to this section, the Chairman or, in his absence, the Vice-Chairman shall preside at all meetings of the Committee. </p><p>  (6) Where-<lf>   (a)  the Chairman is absent from Australia or is, for any reason, unable to exercise his powers under this Act; and<lf>          (b)  the Vice-Chairman is absent from Australia or is, for any reason, unable to exercise the powers of the Chairman under this Act,<lf>        the members present at a meeting of the Committee may appoint one of their number to be temporary chairman of the Committee. <p>  (7) While the appointment of a temporary chairman remains in force, he shall preside at all meetings of the Committee at which he is present. </p><p>  (8) The appointment of a temporary chairman remains in force until-<lf>   (a)  he resigns the appointment by writing under his hand presented to a meeting of the Committee;<lf>          (b)  he ceases to be a member;<lf>          (c)  the absence or inability of the Chairman or Vice-Chairman ceases; or<lf>          (d)  the appointment is revoked by the Committee,<lf>        whichever first happens. <p>  (9) The Vice-Chairman and a temporary chairman may each exercise any of the powers of the Chairman under this Act. </p><p>  (10) Where the Chairman, the Vice-Chairman and, if the appointment of a temporary chairman is in force, the temporary chairman are absent from a meeting of the Committee, the members present may appoint one of their number to preside at the meeting and the member so appointed may exercise, in relation to that meeting, and in relation to any matter arising out of that meeting, any of the powers of the Chairman under this Act. </p><p>  (11) For the purpose of the exercise of the powers of the Chairman under this Act, a reference in this Act to the Chairman includes a reference to the Vice-Chairman, to a temporary chairman and to a member appointed under the last preceding sub-section. </p><p></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 10 Sectional Committees </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   10\. (1) The Committee may appoint three or more members to be a Sectional Committee and may appoint a member to fill a vacancy amongst the members of a Sectional Committee. 

  (2) A member of a Sectional Committee-<lf>   (a)  holds office during the pleasure of the Committee;<lf>          (b)  ceases to hold office if he ceases to be a member of the Committee; and<lf>          (c)  may resign his office by writing under his hand addressed to the Chairman of the Committee. <p>  (3) There shall be a Chairman and a Vice-Chairman of each Sectional Committee, who shall be elected by the members of the Sectional Committee. </p><p>  (4) The Chairman or Vice-Chairman of a Sectional Committee-<lf>   (a)  holds office during the pleasure of the Sectional Committee;<lf>          (b)  ceases to hold office if he ceases to be a member of the Sectional Committee; and<lf>          (c)  may resign his office by writing under his hand presented at a meeting of the Sectional Committee. <p>  (5) The Chairman, or, in his absence, the Vice-Chairman, of a Sectional Committee shall preside at all meetings of the Sectional Committee. </p><p>  (6) There shall not be more than two Sectional Committees at the one time. </p><p>  (7) A Sectional Committee or Sectional Committees may sit when the Committee is sitting. </p><p>  (8) At a meeting of a Sectional Committee, a majority of the members of the Sectional Committee form a quorum. </p><p>  (9) The exercise of a power by a Sectional Committee, the Chairman of a Sectional Committee or a member of a Sectional Committee is not invalidated by reason only of there being a vacancy or vacancies in the membership of the Sectional Committee. </p><p></p></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 11 Reference of matters to Sectional Committees </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   11\. (1) The Committee may refer to a Sectional Committee appointed under the last preceding section, for inquiry and report to the Committee, a matter connected with a public work that has been referred to the Committee under this Act. 

  (2) For the purposes of the last preceding sub-section, this Act (other than sections 7, 8, 9 and 10, this section, sub-sections (2) and (3) of the next succeeding section and sections 13, 16, 17, 18, 19, 24, 35, 38 and 39) applies in relation to a Sectional Committee in like manner as it applies in relation to the Committee and, for the purposes of this Act as so applying, a reference to the Chairman shall be read as a reference to the Chairman, or to the Vice-Chairman, of the Sectional Committee. 

  (3) The report of a Sectional Committee upon a matter referred to it under this section, together with the record of all evidence given before, and all documents and parts of documents produced in evidence to, the Sectional Committee shall be presented to the Committee by the Chairman, or by the Vice-Chairman, of the Sectional Committee. 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 12 Meetings of the Committee </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   12\. (1) The Committee may meet at such times and at such places within Australia or within an external Territory as the Committee, by resolution, determines or, subject to any resolution of the Committee, as the Chairman determines, but shall not meet at any place outside Australia and the external Territories. 

  (2) Five members may make a request in writing signed by them to the Chairman to convene a meeting of the Committee for a specified purpose. 

  (3) Upon receiving a request under the last preceding sub-section, the Chairman shall-<lf>   (a)  determine a time and place for the holding of the meeting, being a time on a day not later than fourteen days after the day on which he received the request; and<lf>          (b)  by such means as he thinks fit, inform each member of the time, date and place of the meeting and of the purpose of the meeting. <p>  (4) The Committee may meet and transact business notwithstanding any prorogation of the Parliament. </p><p>  (5) The Committee shall not meet or transact business on a sitting day of either House of the Parliament during the time of the sitting, except by leave of that House. </p><p></p></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 13 Quorum </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   13\. At a meeting of the Committee, five members form a quorum. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 14 Voting </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   14\. (1) A question arising at a meeting of the Committee shall be determined by a majority of the votes of the members present and voting. 

  (2) The member presiding at a meeting of the Committee has a deliberative vote and, in the event of an equality of votes, a casting vote. 

  (3) Where the members present at a meeting of the Committee do not vote unanimously on a question, there shall, if a member so requires, be recorded in the minutes and in the Committee's report-<lf>   (a)  the names of the members who voted and the manner in which each such member voted;<lf>          (b)  the names of the members who abstained from voting and the fact that they abstained from voting; and<lf>          (c)  if the question was determined by the casting vote of the member presiding-the name of the member presiding and the fact that the question was so determined. <p></p></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 15 Minutes </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   15\. The Committee shall keep full minutes of its proceedings. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 16 Annual report </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   16\. The Committee shall cause to be laid before each House of the Parliament, within fifteen sitting days of that House after each 31 December, a report of its proceedings, if any, during the year ended on that 31 December. </sect>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - PART III  PART III-REPORTS ON PUBLIC WORKS </name>

**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 17 Functions of the Committee </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   17\. (1) The Committee shall, as expeditiously as is practicable-<lf>   (a)  consider each public work that is referred to it in accordance with this Act; and<lf>          (b)  make a report to both Houses of the Parliament concerning the expedience of carrying out the work and concerning any other matters related to the work in respect of which the Committee thinks it desirable that the views of the Committee should be reported to those Houses,<lf>        and, for those purposes, shall do such things and make such inquiries as it thinks necessary. 

  (2) The Committee may, in its report on a public work, recommend any alterations to the proposals for the work that, in its opinion, are necessary or desirable to ensure that the most effective use is made of the moneys to be expended on the work. 

  (3) In considering and reporting on a public work, the Committee shall have regard to-<lf>   (a)  the stated purpose of the work and its suitability for that purpose;<lf>          (b)  the necessity for, or the advisability of, carrying out the work;<lf>          (c)  the most effective use that can be made, in the carrying out of the work, of the moneys to be expended on the work;<lf>          (d)  where the work purports to be of a revenue-producing character, the amount of revenue that it may reasonably be expected to produce; and<lf>          (e)  the present and prospective public value of the work. <p>  (4) In considering and reporting on a public work proposed to be carried out by an authority of the Commonwealth, the Committee shall have regard to the functions, powers and duties of the authority and to the powers conferred on a Minister in relation to the activities of the authority concerned. </p><p></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 18 Reference of public works to the Committee </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   18\. (1) A motion may be moved in either House of the Parliament that a public work be referred to the Committee for consideration and report. 

  (2) A motion under the last preceding sub-section may relate to a public work that has been referred to the Committee as constituted during a previous Parliament. 

  (3) Upon the moving of the motion, the Minister for Housing and Construction shall furnish to the House in which the motion is moved-<lf>   (a)  a statement in relation to the public work (including the purpose of the work); and<lf>          (b)  such plans, specifications and other particulars as the Minister thinks necessary. <p>  (4) The Governor-General may, at any time when the Parliament is not in session or the House of Representatives is adjourned for a period exceeding one month or for an indefinite period, being a time when the Committee is in existence, refer a public work to the Committee for consideration and report. </p><p>  (5) A public work that has been referred to the Committee in accordance with this section shall not be commenced before a report of the Committee concerning the work has been presented to both Houses of the Parliament. </p><p>  (6) If, after a report of the Committee concerning a public work has been presented to both Houses of the Parliament and before the work has been commenced, each House resolves that, for reasons or purposes stated in the resolution, the work be again referred to the Committee for consideration and report, the Committee shall further consider the work and the work shall not be commenced before a further report of the Committee concerning the work has been presented to both Houses. </p><p>  (7) A public work that has been referred to the Committee shall not be commenced unless, after the report of the Committee (or, if there has been a further reference of the work under the last preceding sub-section, the report of the Committee on the further reference) has been presented to both Houses of the Parliament, the House of Representatives has resolved that it is expedient to carry out the work. </p><p>  (8) A public work the estimated cost of which exceeds Two million dollars shall not be commenced unless-<lf>   (a)  the work has been referred to the Committee in accordance with this section;<lf>          (b)  the House of Representatives has resolved that, by reason of the urgent nature of the work, it is expedient that it be carried out without having been referred to the Committee;<lf>          (c)  the Governor-General has, by order, declared that the work is for defence purposes and that the reference of the work to the Committee would be contrary to the public interest; or<lf>          (d)  the work is a work that has been declared, by a notice under sub-section (8A), to be a repetitive work for the purposes of this sub-section. <p>  (8A) The Minister for Housing and Construction may, by notice published in the Gazette, declare a work to be a repetitive work for the purposes of sub-section (8) if-<lf>   (a)  he is satisfied that the work is substantially similar to other works that have been carried out, are being carried out or are likely to be carried out from time to time by or for the Commonwealth, or by or for an authority of the Commonwealth to which this Act applies; and<lf>          (b)  the Committee has agreed to the work being so declared. <p>  (9) The reference in sub-section (8) to the estimated cost of a public work shall be read as a reference to the estimate of cost made at the stage of design development at which all particulars of the work substantially affecting its cost have been determined. </p><p></p></lf></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 18A Inquiries by Committee </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   18A. (1) Subject to this section, where a public work is referred to the Committee for consideration and report, the Committee may direct that the inquiry by the Committee into the work shall take place in public or in private. 

  (2) Where the Committee directs that an inquiry by the Committee into a public work take place in private, the Committee may give directions as to the persons who may be present at the inquiry. 

  (3) Where a public work by way of the provision of services on land for the purpose of developing the land for use as urban land is referred to the Committee, the Committee may decide to consider and report on the work without holding any inquiry into the work. 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 18B Consideration of overseas public works </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   18B. Where a public work that is to be carried out outside Australia and the external Territories is referred to the Committee-<lf>   (a)  the Committee shall consider the work on the basis of plans, models and statements placed before it and of the evidence (if any) taken by it;<lf>          (b)  the Committee is not entitled to require or request the attendance before it of any person who is outside Australia; and<lf>          (c)  the Committee may receive statements, in writing, relevant to its consideration of the work, from any person who is in or outside Australia, and may take evidence from any person who is in Australia. </lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 19 Review of reports </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   19\. (1) Where the Committee has made a report concerning a public work, the Committee (including the Committee as constituted at any subsequent time, whether during the same or another Parliament) may, if it resolves, before the work is commenced, that the report be reviewed, review the report and make a further report in accordance with section 17 to both Houses of the Parliament concerning the work. 

  (2) Without prejudice to the operation of sub-section (7) of section 18, if the Chairman notifies the Minister for Housing and Construction in writing that the Committee has resolved, in accordance with this section, that a report concerning a public work be reviewed, the work shall not be commenced unless-<lf>   (a)  the Committee has resolved that it does not desire the commencement of the work to be deferred;<lf>          (b)  a further report has been made by the Committee concerning the work;<lf>          (c)  the House of Representatives has resolved that it is expedient that the work be commenced without awaiting the further report; or<lf>          (d)  the further report has not been made before the House of Representatives expires by effluxion of time or is dissolved. <p></p></lf></lf></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 20 Power to take evidence </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   20\. (1) The Committee may take evidence on oath or affirmation and the Chairman may administer an oath or affirmation to a witness appearing before the Committee. 

  (2) An oath or affirmation administered to a witness may be in accordance with the prescribed form. 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 21 Power to summon witnesses </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   21\. (1) The Chairman or a member authorized by the Committee by resolution may summon a person to appear before the Committee to give evidence and to produce such documents (if any) as are referred to in the summons. 

  (2) The summons may be in accordance with the prescribed form. 

  (3) The summons may be served upon the person to whom it is addressed either personally or by being left at, or sent by post to, his usual place of business or of abode. 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 22 Warrant for apprehension of witness </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   22\. (1) Where-<lf>   (a)  a summons under the last preceding section has been served upon a person; <lf>          (b)  a reasonable amount for the costs of his conveyance has been tendered to that person; and<lf>          (c)  that person fails to appear or, if he has appeared, fails, unless excused or released by a member from continuing in attendance, to continue in attendance as directed by the Committee or the Chairman,<lf>        the Chairman or a member authorized by the Committee may issue a warrant for his apprehension. 

  (2) The warrant may be in accordance with the prescribed form. 

  (3) The person executing the warrant may-<lf>   (a)  apprehend the person in respect of whom it is issued;<lf>          (b)  bring that person before the Committee; and<lf>          (c)  detain that person in custody until he is released by order of the Chairman or a member. <p>  (4) The warrant may be executed by the person to whom it is addressed or by a person appointed by him to assist in its execution and the person executing the warrant may break and enter a building, place, vehicle or vessel for the purpose of executing the warrant. </p><p></p></lf></lf></lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 23 Evidence to be given in public except in certain cases </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   23\. (1) Subject to this section, where the Committee directs that the inquiry by the Committee into a public work shall take place in public, any evidence taken by the Committee for the purpose of the inquiry shall be taken in public. 

  (2) Where, in the opinion of the Committee, any evidence proposed to be given before, or the whole or a part of a document produced or proposed to be produced in evidence to, the Committee relates to a secret or confidential matter, the Committee may, and at the request of the witness giving the evidence or producing the document shall-<lf>   (a)  take the evidence in private; or<lf>          (b)  direct that the document, or the part of the document, be treated as confidential. <p>  (3) Where a direction under the last preceding sub-section is applicable in respect of a document, or a part of a document, produced in evidence to the Committee, the contents of that document or part shall, for the purposes of this section and of section 37, be deemed to be evidence given by the person producing the document and taken by the Committee in private. </p><p>  (4) Where, at the request of a witness, evidence is taken by the Committee in private-<lf>   (a)  the Committee shall not, without the consent in writing of the witness; and<lf>          (b)  a person (including a member) shall not, without the consent in writing of the witness and the authority of the Committee under sub-section (6),<lf>        disclose or publish the whole or a part of that evidence. <p>  (5) Where evidence is taken by the Committee in private otherwise than at the request of a witness, a person (including a member) shall not, without the authority of the Committee under the next succeeding sub-section, disclose or publish the whole or a part of that evidence. </p><p>  (6) The Committee may, in its discretion, disclose or publish or, by writing under the hand of the Chairman, authorize the disclosure or publication of, evidence taken in private before the Committee, but this sub-section does not operate so as to affect the necessity for the consent of a witness under sub-section (4). </p><p>  (7) Nothing in this section prohibits-<lf>   (a)  the disclosure of evidence by a Sectional Committee to the Committee;<lf>          (b)  the disclosure or publication of evidence that has already been lawfully published; or<lf>          (c)  the disclosure or publication by a person of a matter of which he has become aware otherwise than by reason, directly or indirectly, of the giving of evidence before the Committee. <p>  Penalty: Four hundred dollars or imprisonment for one year. </p><p></p></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf></lf></p></lf></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 24 Continuance of evidence </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   24\. Where the Committee as constituted at any time, or a Sectional Committee as constituted at any time, has taken evidence in relation to a matter but the Committee as so constituted has ceased to exist before reporting on the matter, the Committee as constituted at any subsequent time, whether during the same or another Parliament, may consider that evidence as if it had taken that evidence. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 25 Privileges of witnesses </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   25\. A person summoned to appear or appearing before the Committee as a witness has the same protection and privileges, and is, in addition to the penalties provided by this Act, subject to the same liabilities in any civil or criminal proceeding, as a witness in proceedings in the High Court. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 26 Assessors </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   26\. (1) The Committee may appoint a person to be an assessor to assist it in relation to an inquiry under this Act. 

  (2) A person is not qualified for appointment as an assessor unless he possesses engineering or other technical knowledge or, in relation to the matter the subject of the inquiry, special local knowledge or experience. 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 27 Power to enter on land, &c. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   27\. For the purposes of this Act, a member or other person acting on behalf of the Committee may, with the authority of the Committee, upon notice given by him or the Committee in accordance with the regulations to the occupier of any land, building or place in Australia or an external Territory-<lf>   (a)  enter and inspect the land, building or place; and<lf>          (b)  inspect any material on the land or on or in the building or place. </lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 28 Failure of witness to attend </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   28\. A person upon whom a summons under section 21 has been served shall not, without reasonable excuse (proof whereof shall lie upon him)-<lf>   (a)  fail to appear; or<lf>          (b)  fail, unless excused or released by a member from continuing in attendance, to continue in attendance,<lf>        as required by the summons. 

  Penalty: Four hundred dollars or imprisonment for one year. 
</lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 29 Preventing witnesses from giving evidence </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   29\. A person shall not knowingly dissuade or prevent a person from obeying a summons under section 21\. 

  Penalty: Four hundred dollars or imprisonment for one year. 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 30 Refusal to be sworn, & c. </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   30\. A person upon whom a summons under section 21 has been served shall not, without reasonable excuse (proof whereof shall lie upon him), refuse or fail-<lf>   (a)  to be sworn or to make an affirmation;<lf>          (b)  to answer a question put to him by the Chairman or a member; or<lf>          (c)  to produce a document that he was, by the summons, required to produce. 

  Penalty: Four hundred dollars or imprisonment for one year. 
</lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 31 False evidence </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   31\. A person shall not wilfully give false evidence on oath or affirmation before the Committee. 

  Penalty: Imprisonment for five years. 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 32 Protection of witnesses </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   32\. A person shall not-<lf>   (a)  use violence to or inflict injury on;<lf>          (b)  cause or procure violence, damage, loss or disadvantage to; or<lf>          (c)  cause or procure the punishment of,<lf>        a person for or on account of his having appeared, or being about to appear, as a witness before the Committee or for or on account of any evidence lawfully given by him before the Committee. 

  Penalty: Four hundred dollars or imprisonment for one year. 
</lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 33 Prosecution of offences </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   33\. (1) An offence against this Act may be prosecuted summarily or upon indictment, but an offender is not liable to be punished more than once in respect of the same offence. 

  (2) Subject to the next succeeding sub-section, where proceedings in respect of an offence against this Act are brought in a court of summary jurisdiction, the court may commit the defendant for trial or determine the proceedings. 

  (3) Where proceedings in respect of an offence against section 31 are brought in a court of summary jurisdiction, the court shall not determine the proceedings except with the consent of the defendant and, where the court determines the proceedings, it shall not impose a penalty exceeding imprisonment for a period of one year. 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 34 Proceedings to be instituted by Attorney-General </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   34\. Proceedings in respect of an offence against this Act shall not be instituted except by the Attorney-General or with his consent in writing. </sect>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 35 Allowances </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   35\. (1) The Chairman and other members shall be paid such allowances as are prescribed. 

  (2) The allowances are payable, upon the certificate of the Chairman, out of the Consolidated Revenue Fund, which is appropriated accordingly. 
</sect>

</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   36\. The total amount paid out of the Consolidated Revenue Fund in respect of allowances payable under the last preceding section shall not exceed Thirty thousand dollars in any financial year. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 37 Application of Parliamentary Papers Act </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   37\. (1) Section 23 has effect notwithstanding section 2 of the Parliamentary Papers Act 1908-1963\. 

  (2) Where evidence taken by the Committee in private is disclosed or published in accordance with section 23, section 4 of the Parliamentary Papers Act 1908-1963 applies to and in relation to the disclosure or publication as if it were a publication of that evidence under an authority given in pursuance of section 2 of that Act. 
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 38 Evidence given before former Committee </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   38\. For the purposes of this Act and of the Parliamentary Papers Act 1908-1963, evidence taken by the Parliamentary Standing Committee on Public Works as constituted at any time under the Commonwealth Public Works Committee Act 1913 or under that Act as amended and in force at any time, or by a Sectional Committee of that Committee within the meaning of that Act, shall be deemed to be evidence taken by the Committee under this Act and, if it was taken in private at the request of the witness giving it, to have been taken under this Act in private at the request of that witness. </sect>
**<b>

### <name>PUBLIC WORKS COMMITTEE ACT 1969 - SECT 40 Regulations </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   40\. The Governor-General may make regulations, not inconsistent with this Act, prescribing all matters that by this Act are required or permitted to be prescribed or are necessary or convenient to be prescribed for carrying out or giving effect to this Act and in particular providing for the payment of fees and expenses to assessors and to witnesses appearing before the Committee.
