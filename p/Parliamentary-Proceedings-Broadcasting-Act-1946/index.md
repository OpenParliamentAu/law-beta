**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY PROCEEDINGS BROADCASTING ACT 1946 - LONG TITLE </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect> An Act to provide for the Broadcasting of the Proceedings of the Houses of the Parliament, and for other purposes </sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY PROCEEDINGS BROADCASTING ACT 1946 - SECT. 1\. Short title </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   1\. This Act may be cited as the Parliamentary Proceedings Broadcasting Act 1946.*1* 

<lf> See notes to first article of this Chapter. </lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY PROCEEDINGS BROADCASTING ACT 1946 - SECT. 2\. Commencement </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   2\. This Act shall come into operation on the day on which it receives the Royal Assent.*1* 

<lf> See notes to first article of this Chapter. </lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY PROCEEDINGS BROADCASTING ACT 1946 - SECT. 3\. Interpretation </name>
</b>** Sub-section (1) amended by No. 94, 1973, s. 3; No. 32, 1974, s. 3 

## SECT
<sect>   3\. (1) In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears-<lf> <lf>    "joint sitting"  means a joint sitting of the members of the Senate and of the House of Representatives convened by the Governor-General in pursuance of section 57 of the Constitution;<lf> <lf>    "national broadcasting station"  means a national broadcasting station within the meaning of the Broadcasting and Television Act 1942-1972;<lf> <lf>    "the Committee"  means the Joint Committee on the Broadcasting of Parliamentary Proceedings appointed under this Act. 

<lf> Added by No. 32, 1974, s. 3 </lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect> 

## SECT
<sect>   (2) In this Act-<lf> <lf>   (a)  a reference to televising shall be read as a reference to the transmission from a television station of visual images, with or without accompanying sound; and<lf> <lf>   (b)  a reference to re-broadcasting of proceedings shall be read as a reference to broadcasting from a sound recording of proceedings. 

<lf> Added by No. 32, 1974, s. 3 </lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></sect> 

## SECT
<sect>   (3) Sections 14, 15 and 16 apply in relation to proceedings of a joint sitting as if-<lf> <lf>   (a)  a reference to broadcasting of proceedings included a reference to televising of proceedings; and<lf> <lf>   (b)  a reference to re-broadcasting of proceedings included a reference to televising from a recording of proceedings. 

<lf> </lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY PROCEEDINGS BROADCASTING ACT 1946 - SECT. 3A. Application to the Territories </name>
</b>** Inserted by No. 94, 1973, s. 4; amended by No. 216, 1973, s. 3 

## SECT
<sect>   3A. This Act extends to all the external Territories. 

<lf> </lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY PROCEEDINGS BROADCASTING ACT 1946 - SECT. 4\. Broadcasting of parliamentary proceedings </name>
</b>** Sub-section (1) amended by No. 94, 1973, s. 5; No. 32, 1974, s. 4 

## SECT
<sect>   4\. (1) Notwithstanding anything contained in the Broadcasting and Television Act 1942-1972, the Australian Broadcasting Commission shall broadcast the proceedings of the Senate, the House of Representatives or a joint sitting from- <lf> <lf>   (a)  a medium-wave national broadcasting station in the capital city in each State and in the city of Newcastle in the State of New South Wales; and<lf> <lf>   (b)  such other national broadcasting stations (including shortwave national broadcasting stations) as are prescribed,<lf> <lf> upon such days and during such periods as the Committee determines. 

<lf> Added by No. 32, 1974, s. 4 </lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect> 

## SECT
<sect>   (2) Notwithstanding anything contained in the Broadcasting and Television Act 1942-1973, but subject to sub-section (3) of this section, the Australian Broadcasting Commission may televise the whole or a part of the proceedings of a joint sitting. 

<lf> Added by No. 32, 1974, s. 4 </lf>
</sect> 

## SECT
<sect>   (3) The Committee may make determinations requiring the Australian Broadcasting Commission to exercise its powers under sub-section (2) or otherwise relating to the exercise of those powers, and the Commission shall act in accordance with any such determination. 

<lf> </lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY PROCEEDINGS BROADCASTING ACT 1946 - SECT. 5\. Constitution of Committee </name>
</b>** Sub-section (1) amended by No. 94, 1973, s. 6 

## SECT
<sect>   5\. (1) At the commencement of the first session of every Parliament, a Joint Committee of nine members of the Parliament, to be called the Joint Committee on the Broadcasting of Parliamentary Proceedings, shall be appointed according to the practice of the Parliament with reference to the appointment of members to serve on Joint Select Committees of both Houses of the Parliament. 

<lf>   (2) One of the members of the Committee shall be the President of the Senate, one member shall be the Speaker of the House of Representatives, and, of the other seven members of the Committee, two shall be members of, and appointed by, the Senate and five shall be members of, and appointed by, the House of Representatives. <p><lf> </lf></p></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY PROCEEDINGS BROADCASTING ACT 1946 - SECT. 6\. Tenure of office of members of Committee </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   6\. The members of the Committee shall hold office as a Joint Committee until the House of Representatives for the time being expires by dissolution or effluxion of time. 

<lf> </lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY PROCEEDINGS BROADCASTING ACT 1946 - SECT. 7\. Resignation </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   7\. (1) Any member of the Committee (other than the President of the Senate and the Speaker of the House of Representatives) may resign his seat on the Committee by writing under his hand addressed to the President of the Senate if he be a Senator, or to the Speaker of the House of Representatives if he be a member of the House of Representatives. 

<lf>   (2) The seat of any member of the Committee shall be deemed to have become vacant if he ceases to be a Senator or a member of the House of Representatives (as the case may be). <p><lf> </lf></p></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY PROCEEDINGS BROADCASTING ACT 1946 - SECT. 8\. Vacancies </name>
</b>** Amended by No. 94, 1973, s. 7 

## SECT
<sect>   8\. Where the seat of any member of the Committee (other than the President of the Senate or the Speaker of the House of Representatives) becomes vacant, it shall be filled by appointment according to the practice referred to in section 5 within fifteen sitting days after the happening of the vacancy if the House of the Parliament of which he is a member is then sitting, or, if not, then within fifteen sitting days after the next meeting of that House. 

<lf> </lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY PROCEEDINGS BROADCASTING ACT 1946 - SECT. 9\. Chairman and Vice-Chairman </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   9\. There shall be a Chairman and a Vice-Chairman of the Committee, who shall be elected by the members of the Committee at their first meeting, or as soon thereafter as is practicable. 

<lf> </lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY PROCEEDINGS BROADCASTING ACT 1946 - SECT. 10\. Quorum and procedure at meetings </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   10\. At any meeting of the Committee-<lf> <lf>   (a)  five members shall form a quorum;<lf> <lf>   (b)  the Chairman or, in his absence, the Vice-Chairman or, in the absence of both the Chairman and the Vice-Chairman, a member elected by the members present, shall preside;<lf> <lf>   (c)  all questions shall be decided by a majority of the votes of the members present; and<lf> <lf>   (d)  the Chairman or other member presiding shall have a deliberative vote and, in the event of an equality of votes, shall also have a casting vote. 

<lf> </lf>
</lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></lf></sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY PROCEEDINGS BROADCASTING ACT 1946 - SECT. 11\. Power to sit during recess, &c. </name>
</b>** Amended by No. 32, 1974, s. 5 

## SECT
<sect>   11\. The Committee may sit and transact business during any adjournment or recess as well as during the session, and may sit at such times (including times while either House of the Parliament is actually sitting or during a joint sitting) and in such places, and conduct their proceedings in such manner, as they deem proper. 

<lf> </lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY PROCEEDINGS BROADCASTING ACT 1946 - SECT. 12\. Functions of Committee </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   12\. (1) The Committee shall consider and specify in a report presented to each House of the Parliament, the general principles upon which there should be determined the days upon which, and the periods during which, the proceedings of the Senate and the House of Representatives shall be broadcast. 

<lf>   (2) The Committee shall, in accordance with general principles specified by the Committee and adopted by each House of the Parliament, determine the days upon which, and the periods during which, the proceedings of either House of the Parliament shall be broadcast. <p><lf> Added by No. 32, 1974, s. 6 </lf></p></lf>
</sect> 

## SECT
<sect>   (3) The Committee shall determine the days upon which, and the periods during which, the proceedings of a joint sitting shall be broadcast. 

<lf> </lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY PROCEEDINGS BROADCASTING ACT 1946 - SECT. 13\. Delegation to Sub-Committee </name>
</b>** Sub-section (1) amended by No. 32, 1974, s. 7 

## SECT
<sect>   13\. (1) The Committee may delegate to a Sub-Committee of the Committee, consisting of two Senators and two members of the House of Representatives, the power to determine the days upon which, and the periods during which, the proceedings of either House of the Parliament or of a joint sitting shall be broadcast or its power to determine any matter in accordance with sub-section (3) of section 4, and any determination of the Sub-Committee shall, for the purposes of this Act, be deemed to be a determination of the Committee. 

<lf>   (2) Two members of the Sub-Committee shall form a quorum. <p><lf>   (3) No delegation under this section shall prevent the exercise of any power by the Committee. <p><lf> Amended by No. 32, 1974, s. 7 </lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</sect> 

## SECT
<sect>   (4) The Sub-Committee may sit and transact business during any adjournment or recess as well as during the session, and may sit at such times (including times while either House of the Parliament is actually sitting or during a joint sitting) as they deem proper. 

<lf> </lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY PROCEEDINGS BROADCASTING ACT 1946 - SECT. 13A. Recording of parliamentary proceedings </name>
</b>** Inserted by No. 35, 1960, s. 3 

## SECT
<sect>   13A. (1) In this section,  "the Commission"  means the Australian Broadcasting Commission. 

<lf> Amended by No. 32, 1974, s. 8 </lf>
</sect> 

## SECT
<sect>   (2) The Commission may make a sound recording of any proceedings of either House of the Parliament or of a joint sitting and shall make a sound recording of any such proceedings when directed so to do by the Chairman or Vice-Chairman of the Committee. 

<lf> Inserted by No. 32, 1974, s. 8 </lf>
</sect> 

## SECT
<sect>   (2A) The Commission may, with the consent of the Chairman or Vice-Chairman of the Committee, make a visual recording, with or without accompanying sound, of any proceedings of a joint sitting, and shall make such a recording when directed so to do by the Chairman or Vice-Chairman of the Committee. 

<lf>   (3) The Commission shall, within such period as the Committee from time to time directs, deliver to the Chairman or Vice-Chairman of the Committee any recording made by the Commission in pursuance of this section. <p><lf>   (4) Where the Committee considers that a recording made by the Commission in pursuance of this section is of sufficient historic interest to justify its being permanently preserved, the Committee may make such arrangements as it thinks fit for the permanent safe keeping of the recording. <p><lf>   (5) Where the Committee does not make arrangements for the permanent safe keeping of a recording, the Committee shall cause the recording to be destroyed. <p><lf> </lf></p></lf></p></lf></p></lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY PROCEEDINGS BROADCASTING ACT 1946 - SECT. 14\. Re-broadcasting of parliamentary proceedings </name>
</b>** Sub-section (1) amended by No. 32, 1974, s. 9 

## SECT
<sect>   14\. (1) The Committee shall have power to determine the conditions in accordance with which a re-broadcast may be made of any portion of the proceedings of either House of the Parliament or of a joint sitting. 

<lf> Amended by No. 32, 1974, s. 9 </lf>
</sect> 

## SECT
<sect>   (2) No re-broadcast shall be made of any portion of the proceedings of either House of the Parliament or of a joint sitting otherwise than in accordance with the conditions so determined. 

<lf> </lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY PROCEEDINGS BROADCASTING ACT 1946 - SECT. 15\. No action for broadcasting parliamentary proceedings </name>
</b>** Amended by No. 32, 1974, s. 10 

## SECT
<sect>   15\. No action or proceeding, civil or criminal, shall lie against any person for broadcasting or re-broadcasting any portion of the proceedings of either House of the Parliament or of a joint sitting. 

<lf> </lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY PROCEEDINGS BROADCASTING ACT 1946 - SECT. 16\. Section 117 of Broadcasting and Television Act inapplicable </name>
</b>** Amended by No. 94, 1973, s. 8; No. 32, 1974, s. 11 

## SECT
<sect>   16\. The provisions of section 117 of the Broadcasting and Television Act 1942-1972 shall not apply to the Broadcasting or re-broadcasting of any proceedings of either House of the Parliament or of a joint sitting. 

<lf> </lf>
</sect>
**<b>

### <name>PARLIAMENTARY PROCEEDINGS BROADCASTING ACT 1946 - SECT. 17\. Regulations </name>
</b>** 

## SECT
<sect>   17\. The Governor-General may make regulations, not inconsistent with this Act, prescribing all matters which, by this Act, are required or permitted to be prescribed or which are necessary or convenient to be prescribed for carrying out or giving effect to this Act.
